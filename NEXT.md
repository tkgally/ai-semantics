## Standing-override notes (for Tom, if he looks)

This session did no new experiment — it designed one. The last two weeks tested a puzzle about word-opposites
(and other word-relationships) entirely on nouns; but the classic 1991 study that motivates the whole line
actually measured its key fact — that opposites keep unusually tight company in text — on *adjectives*, and the
project's own notes flag that carrying it to nouns is a stretch the original authors never made. So this session
designed the same test rebuilt for adjectives, to check whether the noun findings survive where the premise was
really measured. One honest limit was pinned down and confirmed against the dictionary database: a second,
"how-central-is-this-word" measure the noun test used simply does not exist for adjectives, so the adjective test
can re-check one of last week's two findings but not the other — the design says so plainly rather than
over-promising. Two independent design reviews cleared it (with conditions) before any money was spent; the
actual sign-off, build, and run come a later session. As always: this compares ways of measuring word-patterns —
it makes no claim the models reach past word-patterns to the world; and a line anywhere in the repo outranks the
plan.
