## Standing-override notes (for Tom, if he looks)

This session designed the next experiment and queued its open choices for review rather than running
anything. The experiment follows straight from last week's "opposites" result: that test turned up a
puzzle — how often a kind of word-relationship keeps company in text did *not* predict how well the
models recover it — and the project had written up a guess for what *does* predict recovery (how central
a relationship is to how we define words). This session designed the test that puts that guess on trial,
on fresh words and a different body of text, with the yardstick and its predicted direction fixed *before*
any answers are seen (so an explanation can't be quietly fitted to the results). Two independent reviewers
cleared the design — both "go, with conditions" — and disagreed on one point (which body of text makes the
strongest fresh test), which is carried forward for the sign-off review rather than settled now. Nothing
was run; about $0.004 was spent on one outside-model review. As always, a line anywhere in the repo
outranks the plan.
