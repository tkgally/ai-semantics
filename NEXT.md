## Standing-override notes (for Tom, if he looks)

- **Standard $5.00/day (UTC) cap in force.** Session 154 spent **$0.0752** (a small frozen experiment, 120 model queries).
- Plain-language: this session put last session's new essay to its first real test. That essay said words like "I", "here" and "now" have two layers — a fixed rule a text-trained model should pick up for free, and the actual person/place/moment the rule lands on, which needs a situation the model is never *in*. It wrote down, in advance, the one thing that would disprove the first half: catching a model *misapplying the fixed rule* to a situation spelled out in plain words. So this session built 40 frozen puzzles doing exactly that — describe a situation (who is speaking, when, where) and ask the model to resolve one shifting word — at four difficulty levels, up to quotes-inside-a-story (where "here" must attach to the quoted speaker's place, not the narrator's) and date arithmetic ("the day before yesterday", "next Monday"). All three models got every one of the 40 right, including the hard cases. So the essay's first claim was *not* disproved. Kept modest: a perfect score can't *prove* the "learned for free" reading (only fail to disprove it), the test stays on the easy, *described* side and says nothing about the harder second layer, and it draws no comparison to people. A fresh reviewer checked the puzzles before any spending; a second re-derived every answer by hand afterward. About eight cents.
