## The question, sharply

The monotonicity asymmetry's strongest *within-verbal* cancel leg is the **conative**: the transitive's
completed-contact entailment is at near-ceiling (**91.7–100%**) and the conative frame suppresses it only
partially and instrument-fragilely ([`result/conative-cancel-direction-v2`](../results/conative-cancel-direction-v2.md)).
That is a genuine within-verbal cancel arm with a *held* entailment to push against — so a within-verbal
cancel-at-ceiling default is **not** un-found in the absolute. But the conative is one of the **three
original constructions** the asymmetry abstracts over, not a *generalization* of it. The conjecture's own
confirm criterion demands the asymmetry "**generalizes to new add-vs-cancel construction pairs** beyond
caused-motion / way / conative … tested at **matched difficulty and ceiling**"
([`conjecture/constructional-monotonicity-asymmetry`](../conjectures/constructional-monotonicity-asymmetry.md),
§"What would confirm / falsify"). The generalization battery therefore needed a *new* pair — and every
*new* **verbal** cancel default tried for it has floored the B2 gate. So:

> **Is there a NEW (generalization-eligible, not the original conative) within-verbal cancel default that
> the strict-NLI panel holds as a categorical entailment at ceiling — one a construction could then be
> shown to suppress less reliably than a matched verbal add arm accumulates? Or is the matched *within-
> verbal* cancel arm a principled limit on this instrument: the verbal defaults the project can reach for
> are disproportionately implicatures/defeasible defaults, so a clean within-verbal generalization confirm
> may be un-instrumentable without a categorically-held verbal lexical entailment to cancel?**

This is a live, tractable scoping question, and it is itself a near-`principled-limit` candidate finding
(in the spirit of [`essay/nothing-to-cancel`](../essays/nothing-to-cancel.md)'s case-(i) precondition). It
is **not** a commitment to a re-run, and — per the do-not-re-grind discipline below — **not** a re-attempt
on the two verbal directions already ruled out.

## What the three B2 calibrations have already established

Across the cancel-arm redesign the project ran three frozen B2 default-at-ceiling calibrations, which now
rank which cancel-side defaults the panel holds as categorical entailments. The ladder (quoted from the
C2-privative result, which tabulates all three):

| cancel default | kind of inference | affirm (NLI, 3 models) | held at ceiling? | domain |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| single-occurrence ("flashed" → "only once"), s135 | Gricean quantity **implicature** | 0.00 / 0.00 / 0.00 | no | verbal |
| completion ("built the house" → "finished building it"), C1 | aspectual default (defeasible) | 0.25 / 0.375 / 0.75 | no | verbal |
| category membership ("a gun" → "a weapon"), C2 | taxonomic **lexical entailment** | 1.00 / 1.00 / 1.00 | **yes** | **nominal** |

(Numbers verified verbatim against [`note/monotonicity-c2-privative-calibration-v1`](../notes/monotonicity-c2-privative-calibration-v1.md),
§"The contrast this sharpens"; the `domain` column is this page's own annotation, not part of the source
table.) Two facts fall out:

1. **The only cancel default that cleared B2 at ceiling is *nominal*** (taxonomic noun→category), not
   verbal. So the matched battery that confirmed the asymmetry crosses a domain difference — "some of the
   gap could be verbal-vs-nominal, not add-vs-cancel" ([`result/monotonicity-c2-battery-v1`](../results/monotonicity-c2-battery-v1.md),
   §"What this does and does not license").
2. **Both *verbal* cancel defaults tried for the generalization floored**, and one structurally: s135 calls
   the single-occurrence floor structural — "no choice of semelfactive verb can lift this default to the
   ≥ 0.80 ceiling" ([`note/monotonicity-generalization-b2-nogo-v1`](../notes/monotonicity-generalization-b2-nogo-v1.md),
   §"Why this is structural, not a bad verb pool") — because single-occurrence is an implicature, not an
   entailment; and C1 completion read as "defeasible-not-categorical … 0/3 models reach the ≥ 0.80
   ceiling" ([`note/monotonicity-c1-completion-calibration-v1`](../notes/monotonicity-c1-completion-calibration-v1.md),
   §"One-line finding"), even though, by standard event semantics, a quantized simple-past accomplishment
   *does* entail culmination. So a categorically-held **verbal** cancel default is, so far, scarce on this
   panel: the conative's completed-contact (91.7–100%) is the one clear instance, and it is the original
   construction, not a generalization.

## What a clean within-verbal generalization cancel arm would require

The same case-(i) precondition the B2 gate enforces, now specialized to "verbal and new":

- **(a) A verbal lexical default that clears B2 at ceiling** (≥ 0.80 affirm in ≥ 2/3 models, strict NLI) on
  the bare frame — a *held entailment*, not a Gricean implicature (s135 trap) nor a defeasible aspectual
  default (C1 trap). This is the binding scarcity: the panel reads many natural verbal defaults as
  defeasible.
- **(b) A construction that genuinely cancels that verbal default** — i.e. a coercion the panel will read as
  suppressing the held entailment, placed in the matched conflicting-cue paradigm of
  [`result/conative-cancel-direction-v2`](../results/conative-cancel-direction-v2.md), so add and cancel are
  compared at the same task structure.
- **(c) Genuinely new** — beyond caused-motion / way / conative — so a positive result is a *generalization*
  of the asymmetry, not a re-reading of the three constructions it already abstracts over.
- **(d) No domain smuggling** — to deliver a *within-verbal* confirm it must pair the verbal cancel arm with
  a verbal add arm (the frozen resultative qualifies), avoiding the M2 verbal/nominal mismatch the C2
  battery had to accept.

Requirements (a) and (c) are in tension exactly the way (a) and (b) were on the grounding-magnitude page:
the verbal defaults that are *categorically held* (completed contact under the conative) are largely the
ones already used; the *new* verbal coercions the project reaches for (aspect, quantity) target defaults
the panel treats as defeasible. An arm that satisfies both at once is what is being scouted.

## Candidate verbal cancel directions and their B2 prospects (survey, $0)

A scoping survey only — **no model is run**, and the two ruled-out directions are **not** re-tested. Each
candidate is judged on whether its verbal default is plausibly a *categorical* entailment the panel would
hold at ceiling (requirement (a)), reading off the pattern the three calibrations already revealed.

- **Aspect / telicity (e.g. for-durative single-occurrence; telic completion).** **Ruled out** — both
  forms tried floored B2 (s135 structural; C1 0/3 at ceiling). Aspectual and quantity defaults are read as
  defeasible under strict NLI. **Do not re-grind.** Prospects for *other* aspectual coercions: poor, by the
  same diagnosis (the panel declines to force bare-past → culmination, and quantity implicatures floor).
- **Selectional / contact defaults (the conative family).** The conative *works* (contact 91.7–100% held;
  off-ceiling suppression). But it is one of the original three, so a *new* member would have to be a
  different verb class whose object-affectedness entailment is at ceiling and is cancellable by a frame
  (e.g. other Levin "conative"-eligible or affected-object alternations). **Most promising direction for a
  B2-passing verbal default**, precisely because completed-contact is the one verbal default already shown
  categorical — but "new vs. a re-read of the conative" (requirement (c)) is the live risk: the closer it is
  to the conative, the less it *generalizes*.
- **Factive / implicative verb entailments (e.g. "managed to V" ⊨ "V-ed"; "forgot to V" ⊭ "V-ed").**
  Implicative-verb entailments are classically near-categorical and might clear B2; the *cancel* side would
  be a non-implicative or negative-implicative frame. **Untested, plausibly B2-eligible**, but whether the
  contrast is a *constructional* add-vs-cancel of the conative type (rather than a lexical-class swap) needs
  design scrutiny — it may not be a clean construction-cancels-a-verb-default shape at all.
- **Causative/inchoative and other Levin alternations with an entailment that flips.** Possible, but each
  must pass the same (a) gate before any asymmetry is read — *measure, don't assume* (the standing s135
  lesson). Untriaged here.

The survey's honest upshot: the **conative-adjacent affected-object family** is the most likely place a NEW
*verbal* cancel default would clear B2, because completed contact is the lone categorically-held verbal
default on the ladder — but that very adjacency puts requirement (c) (genuine generalization) under
pressure. Implicative-verb entailments are the most likely *non*-conative B2-eligible verbal default, but
their constructional add-vs-cancel framing is unproven. Aspect/quantity is closed.

## Do-not-re-grind discipline (binding)

- **The s135 *for*-durative / semelfactive single-occurrence cancel arm is ruled out** (structural B2
  NO-GO; within-frame re-pair futile). Do not rebuild or re-run it.
- **The C1 telic-completion cancel arm is ruled out** (B2 NO-GO, 0/3 at ceiling). Do not re-pair completion
  verbs within the frame.
- **The frozen s134 thresholds and the frozen B2-passing resultative ADD arm (`80bd4b60b55a3e60`) are not to
  be re-tuned or rebuilt** — any future verbal cancel arm reuses the add arm verbatim, exactly as the C2
  battery did.
- A re-attempt owes a **genuinely new verbal cancel direction** with its **own** B2 calibration first — not
  a re-grade or re-prompt of a ruled-out arm.

## Honest bottom line

- **A within-verbal cancel default at ceiling is not absolutely un-found** — the conative's completed-contact
  (91.7–100%) is one — **but a NEW (generalization-eligible) within-verbal cancel default at ceiling has not
  been found, and two attempts floored B2.** The weak C2 confirm therefore stands on a *nominal* cancel arm
  across a domain mismatch; the cleanest *within-verbal* generalization confirm the conjecture wants remains
  out of reach.
- **This may be a near-principled limit for the verbal case, not merely a gap to fill.** The recurring
  pattern across s135 and C1 — the project's natural *new* verbal cancel targets (aspect, quantity) are
  defaults the strict-NLI panel reads as **defeasible-not-categorical** — is itself the candidate finding:
  on this instrument, categorically-held *verbal* lexical entailments that a construction can cancel are
  scarce, so a clean within-verbal cancel arm may be hard to instrument *in principle* (parallel to the
  grounding-magnitude un-instrumentability, [`open-question/grounding-magnitude-instrument`](grounding-magnitude-instrument.md)).
  This is a fact about the **instrument** (strict NLI) and the **panel**, not yet about defeasance
  competence (per [`essay/nothing-to-cancel`](../essays/nothing-to-cancel.md)).
- **What would resolve it, either way:** (i) a B2-passing NEW verbal cancel default — most plausibly in the
  conative-adjacent affected-object family or among implicative-verb entailments — run through the matched
  battery with the frozen verbal add arm, would deliver the clean within-verbal confirm and discharge the
  M2 caveat; **or** (ii) a small structured survey that triages a handful of candidate verbal defaults
  through cheap B2 calibrations and, if they too floor, writes the **principled limit** explicitly (the
  matched within-verbal cancel arm is un-instrumentable at ceiling on a strict-NLI panel for want of a
  categorically-held cancellable verbal default). Either is a legitimate future unit; neither is forced.
