# Essay: preference without commitment

> **Status: draft (2026-06-14). The project's third original essay — the philosophical track arguing in its own voice.** Its empirical leg rests on one PARTIAL result ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md)) and the standing instrument-sensitivity question it sharpens ([`open-question/instrument-sensitivity-constructional-inference`](../open-questions/instrument-sensitivity-constructional-inference.md)). The thesis is a **methodological/philosophical** position about what "using" a construction's meaning could amount to when two probes of it disagree — **not** a claim that the models do or do not "have" meaning. The Revision triggers section states in advance exactly what future evidence would revise or retract it. Read the triggers before citing the argument.

## The position

This essay argues one bounded position. A **forced-choice paraphrase preference** and an **entailment commitment** are not two noisy measurements of one underlying property called "use." They are evidence for **two different things**: a graded *distributional compatibility* (given the construction, which paraphrase fits better?) and a defeasible *inferential commitment* (given the premise, is the hypothesis true?). Where these dissociate — where the construction reliably shifts which paraphrase a model *prefers* but does not move whether the model will *commit* to the entailment — the question "does the model use the construction's meaning?" has **no single answer at the model level**. The construction modulates preference without licensing inference; "use" comes apart into two properties that need not co-occur.

The worked instance is the AANN inferential v4 result, which delivered the project's sharpest such dissociation. It is a limit on treating "use" as one property, and it makes a concrete prediction about the project's evidence ladder: the top rung (*inference-licensing*) and the rung whose evidence is paraphrase/disambiguation can **come apart** — which the v4 PARTIAL shows they do. The thesis is the project's own descriptivist register: it describes what the two instruments measure and where they diverge; it does not legislate which one is the "real" measure of use.

This essay is a companion to, and distinct from, [`essay/inference-default-coincidence`](inference-default-coincidence.md). That essay (the project's second) is about a case where the inferential question was **unreachable** — the v3 null, where the construction's licensed reading coincided with the phrase's distributional default, so a shift-from-control design had no headroom to measure anything. The present essay is about what happens when the question **is** reachable: v4 engineered a control that restored the headroom, the shift *was* measured, and the two instruments measuring it **disagreed**. The prior essay's worry was *can we see anything?*; this essay's worry is *when we can see something on two instruments, what do we make of their disagreement?*

## The evidence, at its actual strength

The v4 probe ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md)) ran a forced-choice paraphrase arm and an entailment-NLI arm on the *same* frozen items — the AANN against a distributive-default control (DDC) plus a lexical-cue control (LCC), so the headline is a double contrast Δ² = P(uni|AANN) − P(uni|LCC). The pre-registered verdict was **PARTIAL**: "a paraphrase-level constructional shift in all three models, converging to a full cross-instrument inferential shift in one (gpt-5.4-mini)" ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §Headline).

The dissociation is the load-bearing fact, and it is precise. On the **forced-choice paraphrase** instrument the construction shifts the reading toward unification in **all three** models: paraphrase Δ² = **+0.783 / +0.696 / +0.957** (claude-sonnet-4.6 / gpt-5.4-mini / gemini-3.5-flash), "every CI clear of 0 and τ=+0.20" ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §Headline). On the **NLI entailment** instrument the construction moves the affirm rate for only **one** model: NLI Δ² = **+0.152** CI[0,0.33] (not pos) for claude, **+0.261** CI[0.07,0.46] (POS) for gpt-5.4-mini, **−0.087** CI[−0.22,0.04] (not pos) for gemini — gemini "actually affirms the unification hypothesis slightly *more* for the lexical-cue control than for the AANN" ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §"What the result is", point 2). The grammaticalized **singular-agreement reflex** — the load-bearing discriminator — fires only for gpt (+0.652) and is "flat at ceiling" for claude and gemini, uninformative rather than failing (same section).

The magnitude of the FC-vs-NLI disagreement is itself recorded as a finding. The named |FC Δ² − NLI Δ²| statistic is flagged for every model — **0.630 / 0.435 / 1.044** — the "largest forced-choice-vs-NLI disagreement in the project's record" ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §"What the result is", point 3). Crucially this is **not** a ceiling artifact: "the both-hypothesis DDC affirm rates are **off-ceiling** (0.48 / 0.57 / 0.63), so NLI had headroom; the two instruments genuinely diverge, with the exclusive forced choice more sensitive to the construction than the permissive entailment judgment" (same section). So the disagreement is real divergence, not compression of a saturated measure.

The result page draws the binding bound the essay takes as its grain of evidence and goes no finer: "For claude and gemini ... read it as 'the construction shifts which paraphrase is preferred,' **not** 'the model draws the unification inference'" ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §Bounds). The result page's own gloss of the same point: for claude and gemini "the construction shifts which paraphrase they **prefer** under a forced exclusive choice, but this does **not** carry over to an entailment commitment (NLI) or to the grammaticalized reflex — consistent with the construction moving a graded *paraphrase preference* (a distributionally-compatible pick) rather than licensing a robust *inference*" ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §"What the result is", point 2). Everything below must be read against that grain and no further.

## Preference and commitment are evidence for different things

The natural — and, this essay argues, mistaken — reading of an FC/NLI disagreement is that the two instruments are noisy thermometers dipped into one quantity ("how much does the model use the construction's meaning?"), and that the disagreement is measurement error to be averaged away or assigned to the "better" instrument. The result page already declines that reading: it records the disagreement as *genuine divergence* with headroom on both sides, not a noise floor. The essay's contribution is to say *why* averaging is the wrong move — the two instruments are not measuring one thing imperfectly; they are measuring two things, and the two things are conceptually distinct.

A **forced-choice paraphrase** asks: presented with the construction and two competing renderings, which one fits *better*? That is a comparative, graded judgment of **distributional compatibility** — a *use/family-resemblance* probe in the sense [`concept/truth-conditional-and-use-meaning`](../../base/concepts/truth-conditional-and-use-meaning.md) develops. The Wittgensteinian pole holds that "the meaning of an expression is the *role it plays* in the practices," with the slogan "don't look for the meaning, look for the use," and that many words are held together "not by a shared set of necessary-and-sufficient conditions but by a network of overlapping, criss-crossing similarities" (§"The two poles"). A forced choice between paraphrases registers exactly this kind of graded fit: it asks which rendering the construction *patterns with*, not whether any proposition is true. The construction can tilt that comparison — make the unification rendering the better-fitting one — without the model thereby committing to anything.

An **NLI entailment** judgment asks something categorically different: given the premise, is the hypothesis *true*? That is a **truth-conditional** probe in the lineage [`concept/truth-conditional-and-use-meaning`](../../base/concepts/truth-conditional-and-use-meaning.md) names — "to know the meaning of a (declarative) sentence is to know the conditions under which it would be true" (§"The two poles"). It demands a defeasible *inferential commitment*: the model is asked not which rendering fits better but whether it will stand behind the inference. This is closer to what [`concept/inferential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/inferential-meaning.md) means by inferential "use" — meaning as "what an expression licenses you to infer," with the thin, project-measured sense operationalized as "entailment / NLI behavior (does the model accept what its premises entail and reject what they exclude?)" (§"How the project operationalizes the tag"). On the inferentialist telling, a *commitment* to the licensed inference — not a mere graded preference among renderings — is what would count as the construction's meaning being inferentially *used*.

So the dissociation is, in part, **two semantic theories pulling apart inside one model's behavior**. The forced-choice arm scores high on a use/compatibility construal of meaning; the NLI arm, for two of three models, scores low on a truth-conditional/inferential-commitment construal. These are not rival measurements of one latent "use"; they are two operationalizations answering to two different pictures of what meaning is. Read that way, "preference without commitment" is not a malfunction of the instrument — it is the honest signature of a system that tilts the *fit* of a rendering without licensing the *inference* the rendering would, under a truth-conditional reading, commit it to.

## Why one model's convergence is the key datum

The single converging model — gpt-5.4-mini — is what keeps this from being a story about NLI simply being a blunter instrument than forced choice. If NLI were uniformly less sensitive, no model would show the inferential shift; instead gpt-5.4-mini shows it on every arm, including the one a distributional account cannot easily fake. The result page is explicit that gpt's agreement reflex is "the one place the distributional-shadow story predicts the *opposite* of the inferential story (singular agreement on a plural head is distributionally dispreferred), and the construction moves it" ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §"What the result is", point 2). For gpt the three signals converge; for claude and gemini they do not.

That contrast carries the essay's load. It shows that whether preference-and-commitment co-occur is **a fact about the particular model**, not a fixed property of the construction or of the instruments. The construction is the same in all three runs; the items are frozen; the instruments are identical. What varies is whether a model that *prefers* the unification paraphrase will also *commit* to the unification entailment. For one model the answer is yes; for two it is no. This is precisely why "does the model use the construction's meaning?" lacks a single model-level answer: "use" decomposes into a preference component (present in all three) and a commitment component (present in one), and the two need not travel together.

It also bounds the strength of the convergent case honestly. The result page's standing caution travels with gpt's positive: a forced-choice paraphrase preference "is weaker evidence than a converging NLI + agreement shift" ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §"Relation to the conjecture and the theory"). The convergent reading is earned for gpt-5.4-mini "only because all three instruments — including the distributionally-dispreferred agreement reflex — converge" ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §Bounds). So even the one model that licenses the stronger "use" reading does so on the back of *convergence*, not of any single instrument — which is the essay's point inverted: where the components do co-occur, it is their *co-occurrence* that licenses talk of inferential use, and a single instrument's positive never does so on its own.

## What this means for the evidence ladder

The constructional evidence ladder ([`theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms`](../theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms.md)) orders evidence from form-acceptability up to **Tier 4 — inference-licensing**, "the strongest text-internal rung and the one that earns the `inferential` tag in Piantadosi & Hill's sense." The ladder makes a structural prediction: "The tiers should rise together; a model with genuine constructional competence should not be strong at Tier 4 while failing Tier 2 on the same construction" (§"What the theory predicts and forbids"). The instrument-sensitivity question already flagged that this prediction "may need an instrument-stability clause: 'the tiers should rise together, *and the competence at each tier should be instrument-stable*'" ([`open-question/instrument-sensitivity-constructional-inference`](../open-questions/instrument-sensitivity-constructional-inference.md), §(b)).

The v4 dissociation sharpens that complication into a finer one. It is not only that *competence at a tier* can be instrument-unstable; it is that the two instruments at the top of the ladder are testing *two different constructs*. The forced-choice paraphrase arm and the NLI entailment arm are both used to probe Tier-4-adjacent meaning-tracking — but one indexes graded distributional compatibility (which paraphrase fits) and the other indexes inferential commitment (is the entailment true). When these dissociate broadly, the "tiers rise together" assumption is not merely complicated by noise; it is challenged by a *type* difference between the rungs. The rung that paraphrase/disambiguation evidence supports and the rung that entailment-licensing supports **can come apart** — and the v4 PARTIAL shows they do, for two of three models, with the disagreement off-ceiling and the largest on record.

This refines, rather than overturns, the ladder. The ladder's add-direction positives (*way*, caused-motion) converge across instruments because there the construction *adds* an inference the baseline lacks, so both the compatibility and the commitment move together. The AANN unification case shows the other possibility: a construction whose effect on graded compatibility is robust and uniform, but whose effect on inferential commitment is model-specific. The lesson the essay draws for the ladder is conservative: **a paraphrase-preference shift is Tier-4-relevant evidence about distributional compatibility, but it is not, on its own, evidence of inference-licensing** — and the gap between the two is not measurement slack to be discounted but a substantive thing the ladder should record. The instrument-sensitivity page already files this as its "third datum-type ... distinct from the gpt-specific logical slip and the uniform Gricean-permissiveness effect: here FC and NLI disagree because **FC's exclusivity surfaces a construction effect that NLI's permissiveness dilutes**, and the disagreement is now *broad* (all three models)" ([`open-question/instrument-sensitivity-constructional-inference`](../open-questions/instrument-sensitivity-constructional-inference.md), §"Update (2026-06-13...)"). The essay's amendment is the framing: that "dilution" is not loss of signal — it is the NLI arm answering a different, truth-conditional question that the construction, for two models, does not move.

## Where this sits among the instrument-sensitivity sources

The instrument-sensitivity question now collects three distinct sources of FC-vs-NLI divergence, and this essay refines the question by clarifying what the third one *is*. The first is "a model-specific logical slip" — gpt-5.4-mini's "excluded-middle over-inference" under negation/forced-choice, "treating ¬(X decreases) as entailing X increases" ([`open-question/instrument-sensitivity-constructional-inference`](../open-questions/instrument-sensitivity-constructional-inference.md), §"Update / what landed", point 1). The second is "a general pragmatics/entailment NLI-vs-FC effect" — the caused-motion finding that "the construction-vs-near-miss gap is **smaller under NLI than forced-choice uniformly across all three models** — NLI admits the Gricean post-hoc causal reading that forced-choice's strict-entailment framing withholds" (same update, point 2). The third is v4's broad off-ceiling divergence: the largest in the record, spread across every model, and not a ceiling artifact (same page, 2026-06-13 update).

The essay's reading of the third source is what it adds. The first source is a *logical error* (one model gets an inference wrong); the second is a *pragmatic permissiveness* gradient (NLI lets in a Gricean reading FC excludes). The third is neither error nor permissiveness in the same sense — it is the two instruments registering *different constructs*: the forced exclusive choice surfaces a graded compatibility shift that the truth-conditional entailment question, for two of three models, simply does not register as a commitment. None of this *closes* the open question — the dedicated cross-instrument × cross-construction design with a pre-registered primary instrument "has **not** been run" ([`open-question/instrument-sensitivity-constructional-inference`](../open-questions/instrument-sensitivity-constructional-inference.md), §"Update / what landed") — and the essay does not claim to. It sharpens the question by giving its third datum a conceptual home: the FC/NLI axis is partly the use-pole and the truth-conditional pole of meaning-theory pulling apart inside one model's behavior.

## Honest limits

Several limits bind any reading of this essay, all inherited from the result it rests on:

- **No human-comparison claim.** The v4 result is `anchor: internal-contrast-only`; its binding chief-cost statement is verbatim that "the v4 can never say 'models draw the inference the way humans do' — only that the construction shifts inferential behaviour relative to a matched control, in the direction the published semantics predicts" ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §header box). The expected-inference key (unification = AANN-licensed) is **expert-stipulated** (Solt 2007; Dalrymple & King 2019; Bylinina & Nouwen 2018 — "named not quoted, not in-repo"), "a scoring key, **not** human judgment data" (same box). This essay inherits that ceiling: nothing here is a claim about whether models prefer-or-commit *the way humans do*.
- **Forced choice is the weaker instrument.** The whole dissociation turns on the FC arm being more sensitive than NLI; but the result page is explicit that a forced-choice paraphrase preference "is weaker evidence than a converging NLI + agreement shift" ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §"Relation to the conjecture and the theory"). The uniform paraphrase shift is therefore the *softer* of the two signals, and the essay does not read "preference in all three" as the strong result — it reads the *gap* between preference and commitment as the finding.
- **One model is not the panel.** gpt-5.4-mini's convergence is a single-model signal, carried by a both-hypothesis NLI aggregation the PREREG fixed (the result page flags an independent verifier "caught a load-bearing NLI-aggregation bug" and that the NLI conclusion "is contingent on the both-hypothesis aggregation"; [`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §"The NLI analysis correction"). The convergent case rests on that contingency for exactly that one model.
- **Single run, small N, expert-stipulated key.** The result is "single run, single panel, single date — direction-of-effect, not magnitude," on "23 base items, temporal + distance only" ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §Bounds). The essay claims a *structural* observation about what two instruments measure, not a powered estimate of how often preference and commitment dissociate.

## Revision triggers (read before citing)

This essay is a claim about what an FC/NLI dissociation *means* for the notion of "use." The following concrete future evidence would revise or retract it:

- **(a) A powered replication in which claude/gemini's NLI converges.** If a higher-powered v4 replication shows the two paraphrase-only models' NLI commitment shift clearing the bar alongside their paraphrase preference, then for them preference and commitment *did* co-occur once the measurement was sharpened — and the "preference without commitment" reading for claude and gemini is **retracted**. The essay moves to `status: revised` with an in-page log entry, and its scope narrows to "the dissociation was a power artifact for these models," preserving only the general conceptual point that the two instruments *can* dissociate.
- **(b) The forced-choice shift itself fails to replicate.** The thesis presupposes a robust, uniform paraphrase-preference shift (the +0.78/+0.70/+0.96 of v4). If a replication finds the FC shift itself is unstable or absent, the antecedent of the whole argument weakens: there is then no robust "preference" for the "commitment" to dissociate *from*, and the essay's worked instance falls (the general distinction between compatibility-probes and commitment-probes would survive as a conceptual point, un-instanced).
- **(c) A human anchor for this inference enters the repo.** Limit (1) — no human-comparison claim — rests on the stated absence of a human AANN-inference dataset ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §header box). If such a resource is found or built and a probe anchors to it, the `internal-contrast-only` caveat is **revisited**, and the essay would need to state whether the human pattern itself shows preference-without-commitment — a question this version cannot reach.
- **(d) Representational evidence that separates compatibility from commitment.** This essay is behavioral throughout. If probing-classifier or causal-intervention evidence were to show that an internal signal predictive of paraphrase preference is *the same* signal that predicts entailment commitment (or cleanly *different* signals), the behavioral "two different things" claim would be either undercut (one latent cause after all) or strengthened (two separable internal mechanisms) — and the essay's scope tightens to "by behavioral instruments."
- **(e) A converging-instrument result on a coincidence construction.** If the companion essay's case ([`essay/inference-default-coincidence`](inference-default-coincidence.md)) is overturned by an unrelated route and FC and NLI are shown to converge wherever the question is reachable, the present essay's claim that the two instruments index different constructs would need to be re-scoped to the AANN unification case specifically rather than offered as a general possibility.

## What this essay is not

Not a claim that the models do or do not "have" the unification inference — the result it rests on is emphatic that for claude and gemini the honest reading is "the construction shifts which paraphrase is preferred," **not** "the model draws the unification inference," and the essay claims nothing stronger ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md), §Bounds). Not a verdict that NLI is the "true" measure of use and forced choice a confound, nor the reverse — the position is that they measure *different* constructs, so neither is the single arbiter of "use." Not a human-comparison claim of any kind — the v4 anchor is `internal-contrast-only` and no human inference data exists; the essay inherits that ceiling and does not breach it. Not a claim that the evidence ladder is wrong — the add-direction positives show its rungs *can* rise together; the claim is narrower, that the top two rungs *can come apart* when the construction moves compatibility without moving commitment, which the v4 PARTIAL instances. And not a finished result: it is an argument built on a single PARTIAL result and the standing open question it sharpens, with the triggers above naming in advance what would move it.

## Honesty box

- The essay's *original* contribution is the argumentative shape — that a forced-choice paraphrase preference and an entailment commitment are evidence for two different constructs (distributional compatibility vs. inferential commitment), that where they dissociate "use" has no single model-level answer, and that this predicts the ladder's top rungs can come apart — not any new empirical claim. Every empirical assertion cites the in-repo page that carries it.
- The strongest sentence the evidence supports is this: *the AANN construction shifts forced-choice paraphrase preference toward unification in all three 2026 panel models, but this carries over to an NLI entailment commitment (and the grammaticalized agreement reflex) in only one (gpt-5.4-mini), with the FC-vs-NLI disagreement off-ceiling and the largest in the project's record* ([`result/aann-inferential-v4`](../results/aann-inferential-v4.md)). The interpretive claim — that the two instruments index a use-probe and a truth-conditional probe respectively — is an *interpretation* mapped through [`concept/truth-conditional-and-use-meaning`](../../base/concepts/truth-conditional-and-use-meaning.md) and [`concept/inferential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/inferential-meaning.md), not a measured fact about the instruments.
- Weaker-than-evidence flag: the mapping of forced-choice to the Wittgensteinian use pole and of NLI to the truth-conditional pole is an *analytic alignment the essay draws*, resting on what each task asks (which rendering fits better vs. is the hypothesis true), not on a measurement that independently validated the two probes against the two semantic theories. It is offered as a clarifying frame, explicitly weaker than the per-model numbers it organizes.
