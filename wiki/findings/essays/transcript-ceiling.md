# Essay: the transcript ceiling

> **Status: draft (2026-06-17). A philosophical-track essay arguing in the project's own voice.** Its
> original contribution is *conceptual*: it asks whether the project's recurring
> **single-reader-recoverability** criterion — the cut [`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md)
> draws between a *thin* (merely updated) and a *rich* (constituted) convention — is best read as a
> fact about *these models* or as a fact about *the medium the project probes them in*. The thesis it
> defends, as a **conjecture about the project's own method** (not a proven impossibility): the cut may
> track the **text record itself**, so a text-only probe is **structurally ceilinged at the thin
> side** — it can show updating, integration, even non-commuting operations, but the rich-side surplus
> (a live two-party normative trajectory; arrival-order information not encoded in the final state) is
> constitutively *not in any transcript* to be found. If so, the relational program's most likely
> honest terminus is a **documented structural closure**, exactly as the just-ratified rung-(iii)
> decision allows. **No empirical claim here is new**; the relational findings it leans on are cited at
> their stated (thin, `internal-contrast-only`) strength, and there is **no human comparison** anywhere
> in this essay. The thesis is a *conjecture*, hedged throughout with "may," and carries explicit
> puncture conditions (see Revision triggers). Read those before citing.

## The position

The project keeps discovering the same shape. On the relational axis it has firm, replicated evidence
that both panel models recover a reassigned coined term by its most-recent binding
([`claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment`](../claims/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment.md);
[`result/relational-spontaneous-recency-a`](../results/relational-spontaneous-recency-a.md)) and
*compose* a compatible earlier turn rather than discarding it
([`result/relational-integration-rung-ii`](../results/relational-integration-rung-ii.md)) — two rungs
of a second ladder, **both on the thin side** of a single decidable cut. That cut, stated verbatim in
[`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md):

> A convention is merely **updated** (thin) if its recovered interpretation is a function of the
> record's *content + stamps*, computable by **any single reader** of that record. It is
> **constituted** (rich) only if recovery requires a surplus that **no single reader of the record
> could reconstruct** — something carried by the live, ordered, two-party trajectory and not encoded
> in the final content+stamps.

This essay presses on a feature of that criterion the ladder essay states but does not develop: the cut
is defined *against the record*. Thin means recoverable-by-a-single-reader-*of-the-record*; rich means
requiring-a-surplus-*not-in-the-record*. So the obvious question is whether the project's failure to
reach the rich side is a fact about the *models* (they happen to compute only single-reader functions)
or a fact about the *record* it hands them (the rich-side surplus is not in any record, so there is
nothing there for any model to compute). The thesis defended here is the second: **single-reader-
recoverability is most likely a property of the medium.** A text-only stimulus simply *is* a final
content+stamps record — the just-ratified rung-(iii) decision states the trap in one line:

> But a text-only stimulus *is* a final content+stamps record.
> ([`decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence`](../../decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence.md), §"The crux")

If that is right, then a text-only probe cannot, even in principle, place a model on the rich side of
the criterion — not because the model is impoverished, but because the rich side is *defined* as the
surplus a record omits, and a transcript is a record. The probe's instrument and the criterion's
denominator are the same object. That coincidence is the **transcript ceiling**.

## The argument

Lay it out as plainly as the ladder essay laid out the cut.

1. **The rich side is defined by what the record lacks.** Constitution (rung iv) requires "content that
   exists *in the interaction and not antecedently in either party*"
   ([`concept/relational-meaning`](../../base/concepts/relational-meaning.md)) — commitments,
   entitlements, challenges *instituted and tracked between* interlocutors. Path-dependence proper
   (rung iii) requires information about the *order of arrival* that "the final content+stamps do not
   encode" ([`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md), rung iii). In both, the
   defining feature is a surplus *over* what the record states.

2. **A transcript states a record and nothing more.** Whatever a model is shown as text — turns,
   stamps, repairs, even an ordered list of operations — is, by the time it reaches the model, a fixed
   string a single reader can read in full. There is no "live, ordered, two-party trajectory" attached
   to a string; there is only the string's depiction of one. The interaction that *produced* the
   transcript had a between-party surplus; the transcript of it does not carry that surplus forward,
   any more than a chess score-sheet carries forward the players' deliberation. The prior relational
   essays already name the deflationary default this implies: a convention "whose interpretation any
   fresh agent can recover from the content-bag of the record is exactly what a shared distributional
   substrate predicts" — recoverable "*without* anything existing 'in the interaction and not
   antecedently in either party'" ([`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md),
   quoting [`essay/aggregation-not-constitution`](aggregation-not-constitution.md); the embedded phrase
   "the content at issue exists in the interaction and not antecedently in either party" is the
   relational concept page's, [`concept/relational-meaning`](../../base/concepts/relational-meaning.md)).

3. **Therefore a text-only probe's reachable space is the thin side, entire.** Anything a model
   recovers from a transcript is, by (2), recoverable from the record; by the criterion, that is thin.
   The rich side is not a harder rung the model failed to climb; it is *outside the probe's range*. The
   project has not been measuring how close these models are to constitution. It has been measuring,
   with increasing sharpness, that they sit at the top of the thin region — which is a different and
   more modest thing.

The two occupied rungs corroborate the reading rather than straining it. Latest-binding-wins is "order-
sensitive *and* single-reader-recoverable, so it is thin by definition, not by interpretive charity"
([`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md), criterion section); integration
"is exactly the kind a single reader of the record performs (intersect the stated constraints)" (ibid.).
Each climb moved the models to a stricter dependence *on the same side of the cut*. That is precisely
the signature a ceiling produces: progress that never crosses, because the crossing is not in the
instrument's gift.

## What this is not — the escape hatch the ratified decision keeps open

The thesis must not be inflated into a proof. The rung-(iii) decision was ratified
(2026-06-17, [`decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence`](../../decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence.md))
to adopt **Option C — non-commuting operation semantics** under a pre-run adjudication gate, precisely
because there is one way a text record might *appear* to carry arrival-order load: make the turns
**operations that do not commute**, so two orders of the same operation-set reach genuinely different
end states and a shuffle is not a no-op. The honest status of that escape hatch, which this essay
adopts wholesale rather than dodging, is that an operation-order gap "may still be *thin*
(single-reader-recoverable from content+order, where order is now part of content)" — it "may measure
*function composition*, not a between-agent surplus"
([`decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence`](../../decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence.md),
Option C). That is the transcript ceiling restated from the other side: smuggling order *into the
content* of the record (an ordered operation list) keeps the answer single-reader-recoverable, hence
thin. The decision's adjudication gate exists to refuse, *before any run*, the relabeling of such a gap
as rich. So the ceiling is a **conjecture with a named test**: if a certified Option-C design produced
a gap that the gate (biased against the rich reading) judged genuinely *not* single-reader-recoverable,
the ceiling would be punctured. The decision's own most-likely-outcome language — that the honest
terminus is "text-only stimuli cannot cross to the rich side; the rung-(iii) program is bounded on
current resources" — is this essay's thesis arrived at independently, by an adversarial reviewer, as
the *expected* result. This essay only argues *why* that is the expected result, and what it would mean.

## What the ceiling is a ceiling on (and what it is not)

Three disciplines keep the thesis from over-reaching.

- **It bounds the medium, not the models.** The claim is emphatically *not* that these models cannot
  represent order, trajectory, or social content — the relational results show they track recency, read
  stamps on demand, and compose compatible turns, all robustly. The claim is that *a text record* does
  not contain the rich-side surplus, so probing them with text records cannot reveal whether they could
  handle it if it were present. A model fed a genuine interaction log with normative observables
  (commitments made and challenged, entitlements tracked) — a *different medium* — is untested and out
  of this thesis's scope. The ceiling is on the project's instrument, not on the models' competence.

- **It is the project's recurring discriminating move, not a new one.** The single-reader-recoverability
  question is the relational face of a criterion the project applies everywhere: *can the answer be
  recovered from the cheap channel alone, or does it need the expensive surplus?* The constructional
  line asks whether acceptability is recoverable from form-distribution or needs the licensed inference;
  the lexical line asks whether sense-relatedness is recoverable from co-occurrence or needs perceptual
  grounding. Naming the relational version as a *medium* property simply makes explicit what the
  deflationary spine of the project has always tested ([`concept/distributional-meaning`](../../base/concepts/distributional-meaning.md)).

- **It is `internal-contrast-only`; no human leg.** Whether *human* conventions are constituted by a
  surplus a transcript omits is a separate, currently-undischargeable question — the in-repo human
  evidence (Brennan & Clark, order-*insensitive*; Hawkins, convergence-only; Clark & Wilkes-Gibbs,
  unfetched) cannot ground it, and the rung-(iii) decision ratified `internal-contrast-only` for exactly
  this reason. This essay asserts no human/model comparison. It is a claim about what a text probe of an
  LLM can and cannot reach.

## A cross-axis note (a contrast, not a confirmation)

It is tempting to read the project's grounding nulls as the same ceiling, and worth saying carefully why
they are *not* a clean confirmation — the discipline matters more than the rhetorical win. On the
grounding axis the first image-input probe found a **redundancy** null: showing the depicting picture did
not improve the panel's sense-separation because "the text-only panel **already separates them
perfectly**" ([`result/multimodal-grounding-image-v1`](../results/multimodal-grounding-image-v1.md)),
and that page is explicit that "a null here is 'no detectable image effect on an already-saturated
separation,' **not** evidence that grounding is impossible." That points the *opposite* way from the
transcript ceiling: there, the text record carried *more* than expected (the perceptual surplus was
**redundant**, not **absent**); here, the text record carries *less* than the rich side needs (the
constitutive surplus is **absent**). What the two axes share is not a verdict but the *criterion*: both
turn on whether the answer is recoverable from the text channel alone, and both gave the project its most
informative findings exactly where that recoverability was the whole story — surplus redundant in one,
surplus absent in the other. So the grounding axis does not *confirm* the transcript ceiling; it shows
the same single-channel question yielding a different answer, which is a reason to hold the relational
thesis as a conjecture about *that* axis rather than a law of text. (The clean case for a *symbol*-
grounding ceiling — where text genuinely cannot supply a perceptual distinction — lives in the deferred
fine-polysemy / abstract-sense cell the grounding result names as untested, not in the saturated
homonym cell it actually ran; see [`concept/grounding`](../../base/concepts/grounding.md).)

## Why naming the ceiling is itself knowledge

If the thesis is right, the relational program does not fail by stalling; it *completes* by documenting
its own bound. That is the posture the ratified decision already encodes — a structural closure is "a
fully promotable outcome, as is a null; neither is a reason to keep spending"
([`decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence`](../../decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence.md)).
The transcript ceiling tells the project three concrete things. First, that further text-only relational
probes are likely to keep landing on the thin side, so the marginal value of another one is low — the
next genuinely new evidence would require a *different medium* (an interaction log with normative
observables; the rung-(iv) instrument the ladder essay calls "entirely unbuilt"). Second, that the
distance the ladder essay says is "measurable — and large" is, on this reading, not merely unmeasured
but *uncrossable with the current instrument* — no contradiction with that essay, since its "measurable"
referred to the gap's *size* being characterizable (we can say how far the rich side is), not to the
*crossing* being achievable; the transcript ceiling sharpens only the latter into "not crossable by a
text probe," leaving the former intact.
Third, that the right thing to look for is not a cleverer text stimulus but the **one design that puts a
between-party surplus into the record** — which, by the argument of §2, is a contradiction in terms for
a transcript, and so points outside text entirely. A ceiling you can name is a map of where to stop
digging.

## Revision triggers (read before citing)

This essay is a conjecture about the project's method and is explicitly revisable. Each trigger names the
evidence that would puncture or bound it.

- **(a) A certified Option-C gap the adjudication gate judges genuinely non-single-reader-recoverable.**
  If a rung-(iii) operation-semantics design survives shortcut certification and produces a live-vs-
  shuffled gap that the pre-run gate (biased against the rich reading) rules is *not* recoverable by a
  single reader of the final record, the ceiling is **punctured for the relational axis** — a text
  record would have carried a rich-side surplus after all, and the thesis would be wrong as stated. (The
  decision's honest expectation is that this will not happen, but the gate exists to allow it.)
- **(b) A between-agent (rung iv) instrument in a different medium.** A relational instrument that records
  commitments/entitlements/challenges — necessarily *not* a text transcript of the final state — would
  move the rich side from "outside the probe's range" to "measured," and would bound this essay to
  *text-only* probes explicitly (which is in fact its intended scope; such a result would confirm the
  scoping rather than overturn the thesis).
- **(c) A grounding result that genuinely needs the non-text channel.** If the deferred fine-polysemy /
  abstract-sense grounding probe found a sense distinction text-only models *cannot* recover but image-
  native models can, that would be a clean second instance of a medium ceiling (this time perceptual),
  strengthening the general "single-channel recoverability is a medium property" reading the cross-axis
  note holds open — and would let that note be upgraded from contrast to corroboration.
- **(d) A human order/path anchor.** A fetched human resource bearing on whether human conventions carry a
  transcript-omitted surplus would, for the first time, license a human comparison — currently forbidden
  by the `internal-contrast-only` status of every cited result, and currently undischargeable in-repo.

## What this essay is not

Not a new empirical claim — its original contribution is the argument that the thin/rich cut may be a
medium property, a conceptual move; every empirical sentence cites the in-repo page that carries it at
that page's stated strength. Not a proof of impossibility — it is a conjecture with a named puncture
condition (trigger (a)), adopting the ratified decision's own escape hatch rather than denying it. Not a
claim that the models are impoverished — the opposite: it locates the limit in the *transcript*, and
notes the models track everything a transcript *can* carry. Not a cross-axis law — the grounding note is
flagged as a *contrast*, not a confirmation, because the grounding null ran the other way (redundancy,
not absence). Not a human comparison — the cited results are `internal-contrast-only`, and the human leg
of every order/path/grounding contrast in the repo is unanchored. Not a metaphysical verdict — it does
not litigate whether meaning *is* constitutively social or grounded; it argues only about what a text
probe of an LLM can and cannot reach.

  criterion of [`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md) — is plausibly a
  property of the **text medium**, so a text-only probe is structurally ceilinged at the thin side. No
  empirical claim is original here.
- The strongest empirical sentences leaned on, at their stated strength: both panel models recover a
  reassigned term by recency and compose a compatible earlier turn, both **thin** and
  `internal-contrast-only` ([`result/relational-spontaneous-recency-a`](../results/relational-spontaneous-recency-a.md);
  [`result/relational-integration-rung-ii`](../results/relational-integration-rung-ii.md)); the image-
  input probe's null is a **redundancy** null, explicitly "**not** evidence that grounding is impossible"
  ([`result/multimodal-grounding-image-v1`](../results/multimodal-grounding-image-v1.md)). The essay's
  thesis does **not** rest on the grounding leg, which is presented as a contrast.
- The thesis is a **conjecture**, hedged with "may" throughout, and is **falsifiable** by trigger (a): a
  certified Option-C gap the adjudication gate judges genuinely non-single-reader-recoverable would show a
  text record carrying a rich-side surplus, contradicting the essay. The ratified decision's expectation
  that this will not occur is the essay's main external support, but it is an expectation, not a result.
- **No human comparison** is made or owed: every cited result is `internal-contrast-only`, and the human
  leg of the order/path/grounding axes is unanchored in-repo. The claim is strictly about the reach of a
  text-only probe of an LLM.
