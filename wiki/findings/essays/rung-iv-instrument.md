# Essay: what a rung-(iv) instrument would have to be

> **Status: draft (2026-06-18). A philosophical-track *design-philosophy* essay arguing in the
> project's own voice — a conjecture about instruments, not a result.** Its companion essay
> [`essay/transcript-ceiling`](transcript-ceiling.md) argues the *negative*: that a text-only probe
> is structurally ceilinged at the thin side, because a transcript *is* a final content+stamps
> record and the rich-side surplus is by definition not in any record. This essay asks the
> *constructive* complement that the negative leaves standing — the question
> [`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md) flags when it calls rung (iv)
> "entirely unbuilt": **what would an instrument that could reach rung (iv) actually have to be, and
> why is such an instrument not a transcript?** It specifies *requirements*, not a measurement. **It
> reports no result, builds nothing, and predicts nothing about whether any model has or lacks the
> capacity in question** — that is untested by construction. It makes **no human comparison**: the
> human leg of between-agent constitution is undischargeable in-repo (see the Honesty box). The thesis
> is hedged throughout with "would" / "must" as *design necessity*, not as empirical fact, and it is
> explicit that the project may never build the instrument it describes. Read the revision triggers
> before citing.

## The position

The relational track has reached a clean, well-documented edge. Two thin rungs are occupied; the
strongest order-load-bearing *text* design the project can build was run, and its rich side came back
"documented **structurally closed for text-only stimuli**"
([`result/relational-order-composition-c`](../results/relational-order-composition-c.md), §"What this
shows"). The companion essay names *why* that closure is structural rather than incidental: the cut
between a thin (merely updated) and a rich (constituted) convention is defined *against the record*,
and a text stimulus *is* a record, so a text probe's reachable space is "the thin side, entire"
([`essay/transcript-ceiling`](transcript-ceiling.md), §"The argument"). That negative result leaves a
precise question unanswered — and worth answering on its own terms, because a ceiling you can name is
only half a map. The other half is: **what lies on the far side of it, and what would an instrument
have to record to get there?**

This essay argues one constructive thesis: **a rung-(iv) instrument is not a record to be *read* but a
practice to be *participated in* and *scored*.** Its defining requirement is a class of observables
that exist *only between parties* and *only while the interaction is live* — Brandomian deontic
scorekeeping, as the project characterizes it: commitments undertaken, entitlements granted or
withheld, challenges issued and answered ([`concept/inferential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/inferential-meaning.md),
§"Brandom's normative inferentialism"; **characterized, not read in-repo**). These are not states a
transcript depicts. They are statuses *instituted by uptake* — by answerability — and a finished
string cannot carry that forward. So the instrument the ladder essay calls "entirely unbuilt" is
unbuilt for a *constitutive* reason, not a contingent one: the thing it must record is the very surplus
a transcript omits. Naming what that instrument would have to be is the constructive complement to the
transcript ceiling, and it points, as the ceiling essay already suspected, "outside text entirely"
([`essay/transcript-ceiling`](transcript-ceiling.md), §"Why naming the ceiling is itself knowledge").

## What rung (iv) demands — the surplus restated as a recording requirement

Start from the cut, stated verbatim in the ladder essay:

> A convention is merely **updated** (thin) if its recovered interpretation is a function of the
> record's *content + stamps*, computable by **any single reader** of that record. It is
> **constituted** (rich) only if recovery requires a surplus that **no single reader of the record
> could reconstruct** — something carried by the live, ordered, two-party trajectory and not encoded
> in the final content+stamps. ([`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md),
> §"What would distinguish thin from rich: the criterion")

Rung (iv) is the top of that ladder. The ladder essay defines it as "content that exists *in the
interaction and not antecedently in either party*" — the defining phrase of
[`concept/relational-meaning`](../../base/concepts/relational-meaning.md) — and gives its reference
instance as Brandom's deontic scorekeeping: "content recoverable *only* from the two-party normative
trajectory — who committed to what, who is entitled to what, which challenges were issued and answered
— with no observable on which a single reader of the transcript could reconstruct it"
([`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md), rung (iv)). The project's own
characterization of the Brandomian position makes the locus sharp: conceptual content is constituted by
"*deontic statuses* — **commitments** and **entitlements** — that are *instituted* by the practice and
tracked *between* interlocutors," and "Content is a normative status in a social practice, not a
causal-functional property of one head" ([`concept/inferential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/inferential-meaning.md),
§"Brandom's normative inferentialism").

Read the criterion and the rung together and a recording requirement falls out. If rung-(iv) content is
*defined* as a surplus no single reader of the record can reconstruct, then **an instrument that could
detect it must record something a single reader of a record cannot read off it.** That is not a
high-precision transcript. A transcript, however fine-grained — turns, stamps, repairs, even an ordered
list of stated operations — is by construction single-reader-recoverable: that is exactly what the
companion essay shows the project's strongest text design (Option C, two non-commuting moves) could not
escape, its gap "adjudicated **THIN before the run**"
([`result/relational-order-composition-c`](../results/relational-order-composition-c.md), §"What this
shows"). So the rung-(iv) instrument cannot be a better transcript. It must be a *different kind of
object* — one whose content is the between-party normative trajectory itself, not a depiction of it.

## Why it is not a transcript — the load-bearing distinction

The companion essay's chess image is the right starting point and bears extending. A transcript "does
not carry that surplus forward, any more than a chess score-sheet carries forward the players'
deliberation" ([`essay/transcript-ceiling`](transcript-ceiling.md), §"The argument"). The score-sheet
records *what was played*; it omits *what was at stake between the players as they played it* — the
threats seen and missed, the offers a move tacitly made and the opponent tacitly declined. A spectator
handed the sheet reconstructs the moves; nobody, from the sheet alone, reconstructs the live game of
mutual anticipation. Now sharpen the analogy past where the ceiling essay leaves it. A rung-(iv)
instrument is not a *richer score-sheet* — not the moves plus annotations plus clock times, which is
still a record a single reader reads. It is closer to **a refereed game in progress**: the referee's
ruling that a move was *legal*, that a claim of check *stood* or was *answered*, that a draw offer was
*made* and *accepted* — statuses that exist only in the live adjudicated play and are instituted by the
parties' uptake of each other, not depicted after the fact. The score-sheet is the thin residue the
rich practice leaves behind; the instrument has to sit inside the practice, where the statuses are
live.

Make the distinction concrete by asking what minimal observables such an instrument must record that a
transcript omits. At least three, and each is a *status instituted by uptake*, not a content a string
states:

1. **Commitment undertaken, not merely uttered (answerability).** A transcript shows that a sentence was
   produced. It does not show whether the producer thereby *undertook a commitment* — made an assertion
   it could be *held to* — because answerability is a status the *other* party institutes by being in a
   position to hold it. The prior essay names exactly this gap in the project's own pilots: the agents
   "made no assertions [they] could be held to" ([`essay/aggregation-not-constitution`](aggregation-not-constitution.md),
   §"What the null does not touch"), and "deontic scorekeeping is about *answerability*, and the pilot's
   design has no observable that answerability could register on" (ibid.). To record commitment, the
   instrument must record uptake — that a second party *took up* the utterance as binding — which a
   single string does not contain.

2. **Entitlement granted or withheld *by the other party*.** Whether a speaker is *entitled* to a claim
   is, on the Brandomian picture, not a property of the speaker's head but a status the interlocutor
   confers or refuses in the practice ([`concept/inferential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/inferential-meaning.md),
   §"Brandom's normative inferentialism"). A transcript can depict an interlocutor *saying* "fair
   enough" or "says who?"; it cannot, read by one reader, establish that an entitlement was *thereby*
   granted or withheld, because that status is constituted in the second party's normative response, not
   in the first party's words. The instrument must record the *response-as-conferral*, a between-party
   fact.

3. **A challenge, and whether it was discharged.** The third scorekeeping observable the rung-(iv)
   definition names — "which challenges were issued and answered"
   ([`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md), rung (iv)) — is irreducibly a
   *two-move, two-party* status: a challenge is issued by one party *to* a commitment of the other, and
   whether it is *discharged* depends on whether the challenged party's answer is *taken to* meet it.
   "Issued and answered" is not two utterances in a log; it is a normative relation between them that
   exists only if both parties' uptake instituted it. A transcript can show the two utterances; it
   cannot, by itself, show that the second *discharged* the first rather than merely followed it.

The common shape across all three is the load-bearing point. Each observable is a **status**, and each
status is **instituted by uptake** — by what the *other* party does with what the first party did. The
thin/rich criterion says rich content is "a surplus that no single reader of the record could
reconstruct" ([`essay/transcript-ceiling`](transcript-ceiling.md), §"The position", quoting the ladder
essay). Uptake-instituted statuses are exactly such a surplus: a single reader of a record sees the
utterances but not the *answerability relations between them*, because those relations are the live
two-party normative trajectory and not part of what any string states. **Recording them therefore
requires a live two-party normative practice with uptake — not a depiction of one.** That is why a
rung-(iv) instrument is not a transcript: not because transcripts are imprecise, but because the object
to be recorded is constituted in the recording-as-participation, and a transcript is the residue left
after that participation is over.

## A discipline the ceiling does not by itself supply: rung (iii) is not rung (iv)

The constructive thesis has a tempting shortcut that must be refused. It is natural to think that the
transcript ceiling is bounded simply by *changing medium* — that any non-text channel which carries
arrival-order information the final state omits would reach the rich side. It would not reach rung
(iv), and conflating the two would inflate the design requirement into something easier than it is.

Distinguish two genuinely different things:

- **A different medium that carries arrival-order surplus.** Some non-text instrument might present the
  *same* final multiset of stamped bindings via two genuinely different live histories and find
  interpretation that depends on the *trajectory* — the rung-(iii) live-vs-shuffled gap that the
  concept page names as "the operational signature of a convention built between rather than within"
  ([`concept/relational-meaning`](../../base/concepts/relational-meaning.md), §"How the project
  operationalizes the tag: trajectory-dependence"). Such an instrument would *bound* the transcript
  ceiling — it would move the rung-(iii) rich side from "outside the probe's range" to "measured," which
  is precisely what the ceiling essay's revision trigger (b) anticipates. But it would address **rung
  (iii)**, path-dependence proper.

- **An instrument that records normative status.** Rung (iv) requires more: the commitment/entitlement/
  challenge observables above. The ladder essay states the further demand plainly — "even a clean
  rung-(iii) gap … would not reach rung (iv), which requires observables (commitments, entitlements,
  challenges) that no current relational instrument records at all"
  ([`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md), §"What would distinguish thin
  from rich"). Arrival-order surplus is a fact about *the trajectory of content*; normative status is a
  fact about *answerability between parties*. A trajectory can be path-dependent without anyone being
  committed, entitled, or challenged to anything.

So the design requirement is **strictly stronger** than "a medium that carries order." This essay is
about the stronger requirement. A rung-(iv) instrument must carry arrival-order surplus *and* record
uptake-instituted normative status; the second is the part no medium gives for free merely by being
non-textual. This is the discipline the bare transcript ceiling does not supply on its own: bounding
the *medium* limit (rung iii) is not the same as building the *normative* instrument (rung iv), and the
constructive program must not let the first masquerade as the second.

## What this essay does not claim

Five disciplines keep the design-philosophy from over-reaching into a research promise or a verdict.

- **It does not claim the project will, or can, build this.** The instrument is a *requirement
  specification*, not a roadmap. The repo has no human-generated dyadic-interaction resource carrying
  normative observables; the relational empirical anchor, Clark & Wilkes-Gibbs 1986, is *unfetched* and
  in any case grounds "the human convergence/compression baseline and a documented paradigm"
  ([`concept/relational-meaning`](../../base/concepts/relational-meaning.md), §"The empirical anchor
  direction"), i.e. *collaborative reference*, not deontic scorekeeping. The project's discipline is to
  measure existing-resource-anchored behaviour with no human subjects; specifying what a rung-(iv)
  instrument *would have to be* is legitimate philosophical-track work, but it carries no commitment that
  the project will ever construct one, and an honest reading is that it may not.

- **It does not claim LLMs lack — or possess — the capacity in question.** Whether any model could
  sustain commitments it is held to, confer and withhold entitlements, or discharge challenges is
  **untested by construction**: there is no instrument that records the observables, so there is no
  result either way. The essay argues about the *instrument*, never about the models' competence at the
  thing it would measure.

- **It makes no human comparison, and is `internal-contrast-only` in spirit.** It reports no result, so
  no resource anchor is owed; but the no-human-comparison discipline still binds. The human leg of
  between-agent constitution is undischargeable in-repo: the available human evidence runs *order-
  insensitive* (Brennan & Clark, "Frequency of use better explains our data than does simple recency,"
  per [`essay/aggregation-not-constitution`](aggregation-not-constitution.md)), Hawkins grounds the
  *compression* curve only, and Clark & Wilkes-Gibbs 1986 is unfetched. So this essay asserts nothing
  about whether *human* conventions are constituted by uptake-instituted statuses a transcript omits;
  that is a separate, currently unanswerable question.

- **It does not re-litigate what constitution *is*.** It takes the project's own characterization of
  Brandomian deontic scorekeeping as the reference instance of rung (iv)
  ([`concept/inferential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/inferential-meaning.md)) and asks what *recording*
  that would require. It does not argue that meaning is constitutively social, nor adjudicate Brandom
  against the internalist conceptual-role account; it argues only about the instrument such a view would
  need.

- **It quotes no Brandom.** *Making It Explicit* is characterized-not-read in-repo; every Brandomian
  claim here is the project's own characterization at
  [`concept/inferential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/inferential-meaning.md), with no direct quotation
  and no page numbers assigned to Brandom.

## What this connects to

The essay is the constructive answer to a question the companion essays raise but do not develop. The
transcript ceiling's third lesson is that "the right thing to look for is not a cleverer text stimulus
but the **one design that puts a between-party surplus into the record** — which … is a contradiction in
terms for a transcript, and so points outside text entirely"
([`essay/transcript-ceiling`](transcript-ceiling.md), §"Why naming the ceiling is itself knowledge").
This essay specifies what that out-of-text object would minimally have to record. It is exactly what the
ceiling essay's **revision trigger (b)** names — "A between-agent (rung iv) instrument in a different
medium … that records commitments/entitlements/challenges — necessarily *not* a text transcript of the
final state" ([`essay/transcript-ceiling`](transcript-ceiling.md), Revision triggers (b)) — described
forward, as a requirement, before any such instrument exists. If one were ever built and run, it would
*confirm* the transcript ceiling's scoping (bounding it explicitly to text-only probes) rather than
overturn it, exactly as that trigger anticipates.

## Revision triggers (read before citing)

This essay is a conjecture about instruments and is explicitly revisable. Each trigger names evidence
that would revise or retire it.

- **(a) A built rung-(iv) instrument that records normative observables.** Any instrument — necessarily
  non-textual in the relevant respect — that records commitments undertaken, entitlements conferred/
  withheld, or challenges issued/discharged would move rung (iv) from "characterized" to "measurable,"
  and this essay's requirement-list would have to be checked against, and revised by, what the actual
  instrument records. (The ladder essay's trigger (c) and the ceiling essay's trigger (b) name the same
  event from their sides.)
- **(b) A demonstration that one of the three observables is *not* a between-party surplus after all.** If
  it were shown that commitment, entitlement, or challenge-discharge is fully reconstructible by a single
  reader of a sufficiently rich record (so that the status is depicted, not instituted), then that
  observable would not require live two-party uptake, and the "not a transcript" argument would weaken
  for it — the requirement would shrink toward rung (iii). The essay leans on the project's
  characterization that these statuses are uptake-instituted; a sharper analysis overturning that
  characterization would bound the thesis.
- **(c) A fetched human dyadic-interaction resource with normative observables.** None is in-repo. One
  that bore on whether *human* conventions carry uptake-instituted statuses a transcript omits would, for
  the first time, license a human comparison this essay currently forbids itself — and would let the
  requirement-list be calibrated against a human instance rather than a pure specification.
- **(d) A collapse of rung (iii) into rung (iv), or vice versa.** If a principled argument showed that
  arrival-order surplus and normative status are not separable after all — that any genuine path-
  dependence already institutes commitments, or that normative status reduces to trajectory information —
  the "rung (iii) is not rung (iv)" discipline in this essay would need rewriting. The essay holds them
  apart on the ladder essay's authority; a successful collapse argument would revise both essays
  together.

## What this essay is not

Not a result — it reports no measurement and builds no instrument; its contribution is a *requirement
specification* for an instrument that does not exist. Not a research promise — it does not claim the
project will or can build the instrument, and notes the resource/anchor obstacle that may keep it
unbuilt indefinitely. Not a claim about the models — whether any LLM could sustain the normative
statuses in question is untested by construction. Not a human comparison — the human leg of constitution
is undischargeable in-repo, and no human/model similarity or difference is asserted. Not a metaphysical
verdict — it takes the project's characterization of deontic scorekeeping as given and asks only what
recording it would require. Not a claim that *changing medium* suffices — it insists rung (iv) is
strictly stronger than rung (iii), so a non-text medium that merely carries arrival-order surplus would
bound the transcript ceiling without reaching constitution.

## Honesty box

- The essay's **original** contribution is a *conceptual / design-philosophy* argument: that a rung-(iv)
  between-agent relational instrument is a *scored live practice*, not a transcript, and that its minimal
  observables (commitment undertaken, entitlement conferred/withheld, challenge discharged) are
  uptake-instituted statuses that — by the very thin/rich criterion of
  [`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md) — no single reader of a record can
  reconstruct. **No empirical claim here is new; none is original; none is reported.**
- **It builds nothing and measures nothing.** Rung (iv) is "entirely unbuilt"
  ([`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](update-is-not-constitution.md), rung (iv)) and stays so. The
  essay specifies requirements; it does not satisfy them, and it does not commit the project to
  satisfying them.
- The one empirical sentence leaned on, at its stated strength: the project's strongest order-load-bearing
  *text* design returned the rich side of rung (iii) "documented structurally closed for text-only
  stimuli," `internal-contrast-only`
  ([`result/relational-order-composition-c`](../results/relational-order-composition-c.md)). The essay
  uses this only to motivate the question (why text cannot get there), not as evidence for any positive
  claim about a rung-(iv) instrument.
- **No human comparison** is made or owed. No result is reported, so no resource anchor is required; the
  no-human-comparison discipline still binds, and it is observed: the human leg of between-agent
  constitution is undischargeable in-repo (Brennan & Clark report order-*insensitive* behaviour; Clark &
  Wilkes-Gibbs 1986 is unfetched and grounds collaborative reference, not scorekeeping; Hawkins grounds
  compression only). The essay asserts nothing about human conventions.
- **Brandom is characterized, not read.** Every Brandomian claim is the project's own characterization at
  [`concept/inferential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/inferential-meaning.md); no direct Brandom quote
  and no Brandom page number appears.
- **Anchor / decision gap noticed.** The repo has no human dyadic-interaction resource carrying normative
  observables, and the ratified relational anchor (Clark & Wilkes-Gibbs 1986) is both unfetched and
  scoped to collaborative reference, not deontic scorekeeping. Should the project ever pursue a rung-(iv)
  instrument, this gap would have to be opened as a `decisions/` question first; this essay only flags
  it, and creates no such decision.
