# Essay: updating a convention is not constituting one

> **Status: revised (2026-06-17). A philosophical-track essay arguing in the project's own voice.** Its original contribution is *conceptual*, not empirical: it builds the structure the relational concept page and the two prior relational essays said was missing — a *ladder* of distinct rungs between a thin update-rule and a convention genuinely *constituted between* agents, each with a candidate behavioral signature, and a sharp **thin/rich criterion** for telling them apart. The project now has firm, replicated evidence for **two** rungs — rung (i), the bottom (overwrite on conflict; [`claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment`](../claims/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment.md), supported), and, as of **2026-06-17, rung (ii)** (the earlier turn *survives* when compatible — composition, not blind overwrite; [`result/relational-integration-rung-ii`](../results/relational-integration-rung-ii.md)). **Both are on the *thin* side of the criterion.** Rungs (iii)–(iv) remain **proposed discriminating designs, not reported results.** The thesis is unchanged and better-instanced: even with two rungs climbed, both sit below the thin/rich cut, so the *distance* to constitution stays measurable — and large. There is **no human comparison** anywhere in this essay (the cited results are `internal-contrast-only`; the human leg of any order/path contrast is unanchored in-repo). Read the revision triggers before citing.

## The position

The relational track now has a defined floor. When a coined referential term is reassigned across stamped rounds so that the content-set is symmetric and only recency disambiguates, both panel models recover the term by its most-recent binding, spontaneously and at ceiling ([`claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment`](../claims/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment.md); [`result/relational-spontaneous-recency-a`](../results/relational-spontaneous-recency-a.md)), and that behavior survives removal of the explicit reassignment flag ([`result/relational-implicit-reassignment-control`](../results/relational-implicit-reassignment-control.md)). The [`concept/relational-meaning`](../../base/concepts/relational-meaning.md) page records the consequence in a single phrase: the second ladder's "**bottom rung is partly climbed**," but only the bottom rung, because the order-sensitivity is "**thin** (a *latest-binding-wins / convention-update* rule)," not "a convention *constituted between* agents."

This essay argues the thesis compressed in its title: **updating a convention is not constituting one.** An order-sensitive result is not, on its own, evidence of between-agent constitution — and the gap between the two is not a vague "more research needed," but a *structured* gap with nameable rungs and a decidable criterion. The prior relational essays asserted the bound ("thin, not rich / bottom rung"); none of them said what the rungs *above* the bottom one are, or what observable surplus each would require. The [`concept/relational-meaning`](../../base/concepts/relational-meaning.md) page describes the relational axis as "a *second ladder* whose bottom rung is not yet defined" — naming the ladder but leaving its higher rungs unspecified. This essay builds that structure. It is a map of unbuilt terrain, offered precisely so the project does not mistake the firm floor for nearness to the top.

## The deflationary core of the positive: why order-sensitivity is not constitution

Start with what the bottom rung actually is, stated as deflationarily as the evidence permits. Latest-binding-wins is order-sensitive — but it is order-sensitive in the weakest possible way: the recovered interpretation is a **function of the single maximum-stamp binding**. Given the content of the record (which figures the term was used for) and the round stamps (when), the answer is the figure with the largest stamp. Nothing else in the trajectory bears on it. The first binding, the middle bindings, the *path* by which the term moved from one figure to another — none of it changes the output. The result page says exactly this: order-sensitivity here "is consistent with a thin **convention-update / overwrite** rule ('the latest agreement supersedes earlier ones')," which "is **not** the deep [`concept/relational-meaning`](../../base/concepts/relational-meaning.md) notion of a convention *constituted between* agents through the live trajectory" ([`result/relational-spontaneous-recency-a`](../results/relational-spontaneous-recency-a.md), §"What this shows").

Here is the move that makes "order-sensitivity ≠ constitution" precise. An overwrite rule is fully **recoverable by a single reader from the record**. The relational concept page names the deflationary default that any constitution claim must beat: a convention "whose interpretation any fresh agent can recover from the content-bag of the record is exactly what a shared distributional substrate predicts," and predicts "*without* anything existing 'in the interaction and not antecedently in either party'" ([`essay/aggregation-not-constitution`](aggregation-not-constitution.md), §"What the null deflates", quoting [`concept/relational-meaning`](../../base/concepts/relational-meaning.md)). Latest-binding-wins barely escapes the *cleanest* version of that default — the order-*insensitive* bag of turns — but it does not escape the default's deeper point. Take the bag of turns and add to each turn its stamp. A single reader, handed that augmented record, computes max-stamp and reads off the answer. No second party is required; no live exchange is required; the ordering is recoverable from content+stamps that sit inert in the text. This is why the finding co-tags `model-internal` and is consistent with `distributional` structure: a next-token predictor conditioned on a linear, stamped context can produce a recency-weighting "for free" from the prompt geometry and the training objective ([`concept/distributional-meaning`](../../base/concepts/distributional-meaning.md)), with no constitution anywhere in the loop. Order-sensitivity raised the floor above the *order-free* bag; it did not lift the result out of the space of things one reader can compute from the record alone. That space — *single-reader-recoverable* — is the home of mere updating. Constitution, if it exists, must live outside it.

## A ladder of rungs on the second ladder

With that core in hand, the rungs become legible. Each is a *stricter* demand on what the recovered interpretation must depend on; each comes with a candidate behavioral signature and a discriminating design. **The project has evidence for rungs (i) and (ii)** (the latter in its *survival* sense, as of 2026-06-17), **both on the thin side.** Rungs (iii)–(iv) are conjectural — the essay is *proposing* their signatures, not reporting them. Where a rung connects to a real open backlog unit, that is noted.

**Rung (i) — order-sensitive OVERWRITE (latest-binding-wins). The rung now climbed.** Interpretation is a function of the maximum-stamp binding. Signature: in a reassignment design where only recency disambiguates, recovery tracks the latest binding above the shortcut chance floor. This is the established positive — SPONT latest-binding rate 1.000 in both models, replicated under the implicit control ([`result/relational-spontaneous-recency-a`](../results/relational-spontaneous-recency-a.md); [`result/relational-implicit-reassignment-control`](../results/relational-implicit-reassignment-control.md)). It is order-sensitive and it is real. It is also, by the argument above, single-reader-recoverable: thin.

**Rung (ii) — order-sensitive INTEGRATION / non-overwrite repair. The rung now occupied (in its *survival* sense).** The recovered convention reflects the *combination* of earlier and later agreements, not just the last state. A later turn that *refines* an earlier one ("the DAX — the spiky one — actually, the spiky one *on the left*") should yield an interpretation that carries information from *both* turns, not one that simply discards the earlier. Signature: recovery that is a function of more than the max-stamp binding — measurably sensitive to a non-terminal turn whose content an overwrite rule would throw away. Proposed discriminating design: hold the final turn fixed and vary an *earlier* refining turn; an overwrite reader (rung i) is invariant to that change, an integrating reader is not. **This is now tested ([`result/relational-integration-rung-ii`](../results/relational-integration-rung-ii.md), 2026-06-17): when the latest turn (a pattern) is compatible with an earlier turn (a shape) and only their conjunction is unique, both panel models recover the both-match figure at ceiling (INTEG target 1.000; the pure-overwrite "latest only" reading taken 0.000), beating the 0.50 single-attribute ceiling that any non-integrating reader is pinned to. The earlier, non-terminal turn *survives*; agreements *compose*. With rung (i) established for *conflicting* turns, the rule is: supersede-on-conflict, compose-on-compatibility.** Two limits the result carries (both binding on this paragraph): the design's constraints are *symmetric*, so the result shows the earlier turn **survives**, not that the composition is itself *order-sensitive* (a commutative AND of two compatible facts passes the test equally) — so the "order-sensitive" in this rung's title remains the *aspiration*, with only the survival half occupied; and the conjunction is trivially easy (the on-demand check is also at ceiling), so the result is decisive evidence against *blind overwrite* but only weak evidence of genuinely *spontaneous* composition. One horn of that harder-load worry — "more turns" — was tested 2026-06-17 and **bounded one step in the claim's favour**: with *three* compatible turns and the earliest buried under *two* later ones, both models still retain it at ceiling and the "drop-the-oldest" reading is taken 0.000 of the time ([`result/relational-integration-depth`](../results/relational-integration-depth.md)) — so integration is not a depth-1 artifact, though that result is itself saturated (robust to *one* further turn of depth, not arbitrary depth) and leaves the spontaneity reading exactly as weak (DIRECT still at ceiling). Integration is in any case *still* single-reader-recoverable — a single reader given the full record could combine the turns — so rung (ii) is a stricter form of dependence but, even occupied, not by itself constitution. It matters because it was the first rung an overwrite rule fails: it separates "the latest agreement supersedes" from "the agreements compose," and the models fall on the compose side.

**Rung (iii) — PATH-dependence proper.** Interpretation depends on the *trajectory* of repairs — the ordered sequence by which the convention was reached — and not merely on the final multiset of bindings plus their stamps. Two records with the *same* set of stamped turns but a *different order of arrival* should yield different interpretations. Signature: a **live-vs-shuffled gap** — hold the content and the stamps of prior turns constant, destroy their ordering, and ask whether interpretation survives unchanged ([`concept/relational-meaning`](../../base/concepts/relational-meaning.md), §"How the project operationalizes the tag: trajectory-dependence"). The relational concept page names this as the operational surplus over distributional convergence: "a live-vs-shuffled gap is the operational signature of a convention built between rather than within; its absence is a relational *deflation*." Crucially, rung (iii) is where single-reader-recoverability begins to *fail*: if the answer depends on a trajectory that the final content+stamps do not encode, no single reader of the record can reconstruct it. **This is a proposed contrast, not a result** — and the project does not currently have an instrument that separates rung (i) from rung (iii) (see the criterion section). It is the threshold rung: below it, updating; at it, something the record alone cannot carry.

**Rung (iv) — genuine BETWEEN-agent constitution.** Content that exists *in the interaction and not antecedently in either party* — the defining phrase of [`concept/relational-meaning`](../../base/concepts/relational-meaning.md). The reference instance is Brandom's deontic scorekeeping: conceptual content as commitments and entitlements *instituted and tracked between* interlocutors, "a normative status in a social practice, not a causal-functional property of one head" ([`concept/inferential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/inferential-meaning.md), §"Brandom's normative inferentialism", characterized-not-read in-repo). Signature: content recoverable *only* from the two-party normative trajectory — who committed to what, who is entitled to what, which challenges were issued and answered — with no observable on which a single reader of the transcript could reconstruct it. **Nothing in the project measures this.** As the prior essay records, the relational pilots "made no assertions [the agents] could be held to, issued and faced no challenges, kept no score of anyone's entitlements" ([`essay/aggregation-not-constitution`](aggregation-not-constitution.md), §"What the null does not touch"). Rung (iv) is the top of the ladder and is entirely unbuilt.

The shape of the climb is the point. Rungs (i) and (ii) are firm (rung ii in its survival sense, as of 2026-06-17). Rungs (iii) and (iv) are each strictly more demanding, and the project has run *neither* of them — and crucially, both occupied rungs sit on the **thin** side of the criterion below. The two rungs being real does not foreshorten the rest of the ladder; it makes the unclimbed length of it — the *rich* side, where constitution would live — visible.

## What would distinguish thin from rich: the criterion

The ladder needs a single decidable cut, and the deflationary core supplies it.

> **Thin/rich criterion.** A convention is merely **updated** (thin) if its recovered interpretation is a function of the record's *content + stamps*, computable by **any single reader** of that record. It is **constituted** (rich) only if recovery requires a surplus that **no single reader of the record could reconstruct** — something carried by the live, ordered, two-party trajectory and not encoded in the final content+stamps.

Rungs (i) and (ii) sit on the thin side: an overwrite rule and an integration rule are both single-reader-recoverable (one reads off max-stamp; the other composes the turns), even though (ii) is a stricter dependence than (i). **The 2026-06-17 rung-(ii) result confirms this placement empirically** — the models compose compatible turns, but the composition the design measures is exactly the kind a single reader of the record performs (intersect the stated constraints), so occupying rung (ii) moved the models to a stricter rung *on the same side of the cut*, not across it. Rungs (iii) and (iv) cross to the rich side: path-dependence proper requires information about the *order of arrival* that the final multiset+stamps does not contain, and between-agent constitution requires normative status that no transcript-reader can reconstruct alone. The criterion is what makes "order-sensitivity ≠ constitution" non-negotiable: latest-binding-wins is order-sensitive *and* single-reader-recoverable, so it is thin by definition, not by interpretive charity — and so is the integration just observed.

Honesty demands stating plainly what the current instrument can and cannot do. **It cannot separate rung (i) from rung (iii).** This is the claim's own scope limit 3, stated verbatim: "'Latest-binding-wins' may be a shallow update heuristic; this claim does not separate thin order-sensitivity from deep path-dependence" ([`claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment`](../claims/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment.md), scope limit 3). The reassignment design makes recency the sole disambiguator, but it does not vary the *trajectory* while holding the final state fixed, so a max-stamp reader and a genuinely path-dependent reader produce the same answer on it. The design that *could* separate them is the live-vs-shuffled contrast the concept page names: present the *same* final multiset of stamped bindings via two different ordered histories and test whether interpretation differs. A null there (interpretation invariant to the shuffle) would confirm the convention is thin even where one might have hoped for path-dependence; a clean gap would be the first evidence for rung (iii). That design is **proposed here, not run** — and its operationalization is now a surfaced open decision ([`decisions/open/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence`](../../decisions/open/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence.md)), which states honestly that the rung may be **structurally unable** to produce a rich-side result with text-only stimuli (any difference a single reader of the final record could reconstruct is thin by construction) — so a documented closure is a live, promotable outcome alongside a positive or a null. And even a clean rung-(iii) gap would not reach rung (iv), which requires observables (commitments, entitlements, challenges) that no current relational instrument records at all.

## Revision triggers (read before citing)

This essay's empirical floor is firm (two thin rungs occupied) but its *rich* upper ladder is a proposal; specific evidence would force a rewrite.

- **(a) A non-overwrite-repair result showing integration (rung ii). — FIRED 2026-06-17.** A design in which a later turn *refines* rather than *replaces* an earlier one showed recovery sensitive to the earlier turn's content — an integrating, not overwriting, convention ([`result/relational-integration-rung-ii`](../results/relational-integration-rung-ii.md)): both models compose at ceiling, overwrite rate 0.000. Rung (ii) is now **occupied** (survival sense), and the "single function of the max-stamp binding" characterization of the positive is relaxed to **supersede-on-conflict, compose-on-compatibility** above. The essay is `revised`. (The *null* that would have *sharpened* the thin overwrite verdict — recovery insensitive to the non-terminal turn — did not occur; the result instead moved the models up one thin rung.) The trigger now stays live only in its stronger form: a result showing the composition is itself *order-sensitive* (non-commutative), which this symmetric design did not test.
- **(b) A thin/rich separation result (rung iii).** A live-vs-shuffled design that holds the final stamped multiset constant and varies trajectory: a clean gap would be the first crossing to the rich side and would falsify the essay's central deflationary reading of the *current* positive's neighborhood (though not the positive itself, which is scoped to rung i); a null would confirm single-reader-recoverability where path-dependence was the alternative. Either outcome rewrites the criterion section's "cannot currently separate (i) from (iii)" into a settled separation.
- **(c) Any between-agent observable (rung iv).** A relational instrument that records commitments/entitlements/challenges (a scorekeeping observable) — currently absent in-repo — would let rung (iv) be tested rather than only characterized, and would require replacing the "entirely unbuilt" description with whatever it finds.
- **(d) A human order/path anchor.** The human leg of every order/path contrast here is unanchored in-repo; fetching a human resource that bears on order- or path-sensitivity (none is in-repo) would, for the first time, license a human comparison — currently forbidden by the `internal-contrast-only` status of the cited results.

## What this essay is not

Not a new empirical claim — its original contribution is the **ladder and the thin/rich criterion**, a conceptual scaffold; every empirical assertion in it cites the in-repo page that carries it, at that page's stated strength. Not a claim that the project is near constitution — it argues the opposite, that the firm bottom rung makes the *distance* to constitution measurable and that the distance is large and mostly unmeasured. Not a report of rungs (iii)–(iv) — those are *proposed* discriminating designs and signatures, explicitly not run; treating either as a result would be fabrication (rung (ii) *is* now reported, in its survival sense, and only that sense). Not a human comparison — the cited results are `internal-contrast-only`, and Brennan & Clark 1996 (cited via [`essay/aggregation-not-constitution`](aggregation-not-constitution.md)) report an order-*insensitive* frequency>recency pattern, so the human leg of any order/path contrast is unanchored and no human/LLM difference or similarity is asserted. Not a metaphysical verdict — it locates a behavioral floor and maps the steps above it; it does not litigate what constitution *is*, only what would *count* as observing it.
