# Essay: the "no" on LLM reference is not one boundary

> **Status: draft (2026-06-16). A philosophical-track essay arguing in the project's own voice.** Its contribution is a *structural diagnosis*, not a new empirical result. It examines the project's own habit of recording "an LLM's words do not refer" as a *single* boundary — anchored, where the project states it, to Bender & Koller's in-principle argument — and shows that the repo now holds **three structurally distinct denials** of LM reference, resting on mutually incompatible metasemantics. They agree on the present verdict and disagree about almost everything else that matters: *what kind of thing* reference is, *where* the lack lives, and — the load-bearing point — *what would have to change for an LM's words to refer.* The essay introduces **no new empirical claim**; every textual assertion cites the in-repo page that carries it verbatim. It is the complement of [`essay/reference-as-premise-bound`](reference-as-premise-bound.md): that essay anatomizes the *yes-vs-no* disagreement (and finds it premise-bound); this one anatomizes the **internal disunity of the "no" camp** and draws the consequence for two of the project's own pages.

> **Revision note (2026-06-25, session 110; trigger (c) partially fired, thesis unchanged).** [`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md) entered the repo at primary strength — the first of the externalist primaries trigger (c) names (Evans 1973, Kripke remain not-in-repo). The externalist frame Denial 3 runs is now primary-anchored. But trigger (c)'s *consequence* — that Denial 3's counterfactual "stops being premise-open and the table's row 3 must be rewritten" — does **not** follow on reading Putnam: his text fixes reference to "the sociolinguistic state of the collective linguistic body" (p. 146) and a division of linguistic labor (p. 144), but says nothing about whether a corpus-trained system has "the required status in our communities," so it does **not** decide whether a built world-relation of the historical kind counts as membership. Denial 3's row 3 therefore stands as written (conditionally-yes / premise-bound antecedent). The complementary essay [`essay/stereotype-without-the-expert`](stereotype-without-the-expert.md) (session 110) uses Putnam's apparatus to sharpen the *terms* of that antecedent — the model occupies neither the expert nor the deferring-layperson role — without settling it. Honest-limits bullet 4 and trigger (c) updated accordingly.

> **Revision note (2026-06-25, session 111; trigger (c) fired further — Evans 1973 + Burge 1979 in; Denial-3 row 3 sharpened, not rewritten; thesis unchanged).** The remaining named externalist primaries entered: [`source/evans-1973-causal-theory-of-names`](../../base/sources/evans-1973-causal-theory-of-names.md) and [`source/burge-1979-individualism-and-the-mental`](../../base/sources/burge-1979-individualism-and-the-mental.md) (only **Kripke** now remains queued). Both bear on **Denial 3** (the externalist-membership "no"), and neither rewrites its row. *Burge* reinforces row 3's community-embedding requirement and **broadens it** — on social externalism the missing off-board status withholds not just reference but the *content of attitudes* (the arthritis case), via *deference* to a corrigible community (pp. 77-85); so Denial 3's "missing off-board status" is, in Burgean terms, a missing *deference-anchored community membership*, and a built world-relation lifts it only if it confers that — still the premise-bound antecedent, now stated for content as well as reference. *Evans* is the one that most directly tests row 3's "*world-relation of the **right historical kind***" clause, because Evans *specifies* the historical kind: name-reference tracks the **dominant causal source of a body of information** (pp. 198-199), and a corpus *is* a body of information with item-sources, "source" being satisfied by *reading a person's works* (p. 199). So the "right historical kind" of world-relation in row 3 now has a concrete candidate — an information-source relation a corpus arguably instantiates — which makes row 3's "conditionally yes" **less hypothetical**; but Evans's knowledge-aptness (p. 200) and intentions/common-knowledge (p. 202) conditions keep whether the corpus relation *counts* an open, off-board question. **Denial 3's row 3 therefore stands (conditionally yes / premise-bound antecedent), with its antecedent sharpened from "some historical world-relation" to "a knowledge-apt information-source relation of the kind Evans names."** The counterfactual-divergence thesis is unchanged: Denials 1, 2, 3 and Ostertag still answer "what would make an LM's words refer?" incompatibly (Evans sits inside Denial 3's externalist family; Burge inside it too). Honest-limits bullet 4 and trigger (c) updated.

## The position

This essay argues one bounded thesis. The project records "an LLM's words do not refer" as if it were **one** boundary with **one** ground. It is not. The repo now carries at least three published or in-repo denials of LM reference that are **jointly inconsistent in their grounds and in their counterfactuals** — they would not survive being merged into a single "no." Once that is seen, two of the project's own pages need a small, honest correction: the deflationary concept page's anchoring of the existence-question "no" to a single argument, and the situating-the-map theory page's prediction about *what* could ever move the referential cell.

The original contribution is the **counterfactual divergence**: asked "what would it take for a language model's words to refer?", the in-repo denials give answers that contradict one another — a missing *signal*, a missing *social-historical status*, or a missing *mind* — and one of those answers says the other two would not be enough. A camp that agrees on a verdict while disagreeing on its truthmaker, and on the counterfactual that would overturn it, is not one boundary. It is several boundaries that happen, on today's models, to fall in the same place.

## The single boundary the project currently records

Two in-repo pages state the referential "no" as a settled edge of what the project's evidence can reach, and both lean on **one** argument when they do.

The [`concept/deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning`](../../base/concepts/deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning.md) page makes Bender & Koller the *rigorous* form of the denial — "the **rigorous** version of the eliminativist line — the one stated as an argument rather than a slogan — is Bender & Koller 2020" ([`concept/deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning`](../../base/concepts/deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning.md), §"Eliminativism") — and treats its in-principle gap as *the* boundary behavioral evidence cannot cross: "no behavioral result this project can obtain settles the form-vs-meaning question in principle" (same page, §"A note on scope"). Bender & Koller's denial is signal-based: meaning is "the relation M ⊆ E × I which contains pairs (e, i) of natural language expressions e and the communicative intents i they can be used to evoke" ([`source/bender-koller-2020-climbing`](../../base/sources/bender-koller-2020-climbing.md), p. 5187), and "a system trained only on form has a priori no way to learn meaning" ([`source/bender-koller-2020-climbing`](../../base/sources/bender-koller-2020-climbing.md), p. 5185, abstract) — reconstructing the intents "from the forms alone is hopeless" ([`source/bender-koller-2020-climbing`](../../base/sources/bender-koller-2020-climbing.md), p. 5189).

The [`theory/situating-llm-meaning`](../theory/situating-llm-meaning.md) map carries the same boundary forward into a *prediction* about what could move it: "no text-only probe, however much it climbs, reaches the referential/externalist or richly-grounded cell — those move only with a **built world-relation**, not a cleverer text task" ([`theory/situating-llm-meaning`](../theory/situating-llm-meaning.md), §"What this synthesis predicts and forbids"). That sentence presupposes that the referential cell has *one* truthmaker missing (a world-relation) whose construction would lift it. Hold onto that presupposition; it is exactly what the disunity below breaks.

## Three denials, at full strength

Take each denial in its own words. They are not three statements of one position; they are three positions that share a conclusion. A point that strengthens the thesis rather than weakening it: Denials 2 and 3 below come from the *same paper* — Baggio & Murphy run an internalist frame-rejection at §4 and an externalist-membership antecedent-denial at §5, on metasemantics incompatible with each other. So the disunity is not only across documents; it is *intra-paper*, with one rejoinder housing two grounds for "no" that point to different missing ingredients. The "three" here is a count of **frames** (metasemantics), not of documents.

**Denial 1 — the signal/grounding "no" (Bender & Koller).** The lack is in the *training signal*. The corpus is form, the meaning relation M ties form to communicative intent, and the signal needed to fix M is simply not in form — so "a system trained only on form has a priori no way to learn meaning" ([`source/bender-koller-2020-climbing`](../../base/sources/bender-koller-2020-climbing.md), p. 5185, abstract). This denial is *architectural and binary* (per [`concept/deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning`](../../base/concepts/deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning.md), §"Eliminativism"): the question is what the system had access to, not what metaphysics of reference is correct. It is officially silent on the externalist/internalist quarrel — it would bind whichever theory of reference one held.

**Denial 2 — the internalist frame-rejection (Baggio & Murphy, §4).** The lack is the absence of a *mind*. Baggio & Murphy reject the externalist machinery M&L (and the project's [`concept/referential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/referential-meaning.md) page) run, holding that "there appears to be nothing natural, nor historical, about linear strings of tokens, as considered by current tokenisation algorithms" ([`source/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder`](../../base/sources/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder.md), §4), and that instead "Facts about the mind-brain must be invoked to explain how words refer: not what they refer to, but what determines what they refer to" ([`source/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder`](../../base/sources/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder.md), §4). Their verdict is the flattest of the three: "Neither language models themselves can refer, and nor can 'their words' refer" ([`source/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder`](../../base/sources/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder.md), §4) — granting only that an LM's outputs can be *about* subject matters: "Aboutness is more directly applicable to LMs than reference" ([`source/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder`](../../base/sources/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder.md), §5).

**Denial 3 — the in-principle externalist "no" (the project's concept page; Baggio & Murphy, §5).** *Granting* externalism, the lack is the missing *off-board status* — the causal-historical, community embedding externalism requires. The [`concept/referential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/referential-meaning.md) page runs externalism to "the strongest *in-principle* case that an LLM does not *refer*" because the model "has no causal-historical chain to a baptismal event, no membership in a community of experts, no environment it is embedded in independently of the corpus" ([`concept/referential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/referential-meaning.md), §"Internalism vs. externalism — and the LLM case"). Baggio & Murphy voice the same move from inside the dispute: extending "'linguistic community member' to include LMs … does not address the issue whether LMs have the required status in our communities, such that some of their 'words' can refer" ([`source/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder`](../../base/sources/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder.md), §5). This is the denial [`essay/reference-as-premise-bound`](reference-as-premise-bound.md) diagnoses as *premise-bound*: it turns on the antecedent of M&L's community-membership conditional ("If they are part of a linguistic community which uses 'Peano' to refer to Peano, then their use of 'Peano' refers to Peano" — [`source/mandelkern-linzen-2024-do-words-refer`](../../base/sources/mandelkern-linzen-2024-do-words-refer.md), §4.3).

**The asymmetric fourth voice — the route-critic who leaves the verdict open (Ostertag).** Ostertag is not a fourth *denial of reference*; he is the published reason the "no" cannot even be treated as *closed*. An externalist himself, he faults only M&L's particular shortcut — "the upshot is that externalism, as understood by Mandelkern and Linzen, cannot ground reference" ([`source/ostertag-2025-externalist-reply`](../../base/sources/ostertag-2025-externalist-reply.md), §3) — while explicitly accepting the broader conditional: "if we can make sense of LMs possessing referential intentions, or if deference to usage is not essential to reference, then there is no principled barrier to LMs' outputs referring. In this case, my concern is not with the conditional, which I accept, but with the antecedent, about which I have doubts" ([`source/ostertag-2025-externalist-reply`](../../base/sources/ostertag-2025-externalist-reply.md), §4). His closing register is *open*, not negative: "the path to the claim that LMs' outputs refer remains daunting—a far cry from the easy route that the naïve externalist promised" ([`source/ostertag-2025-externalist-reply`](../../base/sources/ostertag-2025-externalist-reply.md), §4). A daunting-but-open path is not a boundary at all. Including him is not padding the count of "no"s; it is the cleanest single piece of evidence that the in-repo "no" is a chorus of disagreeing voices, one of whom does not even sing the note.

## Why they are not allies: the counterfactual test

The way to see that these are different boundaries, not one, is to ask each the same forward question — **what would have to change for a language model's words to refer?** A genuine single boundary answers once. These answer incompatibly.

| Denial | Where the lack lives | What would lift it | Does a *built world-relation* suffice? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Signal / grounding (Bender & Koller) | the training signal — form lacks the form→intent relation M | give the system the missing signal: access to communicative intent / a non-form grounding channel | **Plausibly yes** — a grounding channel beyond form is exactly the kind of thing that supplies M |
| 2. Internalist frame-rejection (Baggio & Murphy §4) | the absence of a mind-brain that does the referring | a mind-brain whose cognitive states fix reference — which a token-string never is | **No** — no external relation suffices; the bar is mental, not relational |
| 3. Externalist membership (concept page; B&M §5) | the missing off-board status (causal-historical / community embedding) | acquire the historical-community embedding externalism requires | **Conditionally yes** — but only a *world-relation of the right historical kind*, and whether it counts is the premise-bound antecedent |
| 4. Route-critic (Ostertag) | not a settled lack — the easy *argument* fails | make sense of referential intentions, or show deference is inessential | **Verdict left open** — he neither asserts nor denies a sufficient fix |

Read down the last column. Denial 1 and Denial 3 would both, in their different ways, be *moved* by building some world-relation into the system (a grounding/intent channel for 1; a historical-community embedding for 3). Denial 2 says flatly that **no** world-relation would move it — because on internalism the truthmaker of reference is a fact about a mind, and you do not get a mind by adding a relation to a string of tokens. And Ostertag holds the whole question open. So the four parties do not merely disagree about *why* today's models don't refer; they disagree about whether the very thing the project names as the cell's potential mover — a built world-relation — could ever be the mover at all.

This is the precise sense in which the "no" is not one boundary. Two disputes are the *same* boundary when the parties would converge on the same counterfactual — agree on what, if added, would flip the verdict. These parties do not. They agree the verdict is "no" for the systems in front of us, and they part company immediately on its truthmaker and on its repair. A conclusion shared by arguments that disagree about their own defeasibility conditions is an *overlap*, not a *consensus*; the agreement is in the verdict's extension today, not in its grounds or its modal profile.

## The two corrections this forces

The diagnosis is not idle; it constrains two of the project's own pages.

**Correction 1 — the deflationary page must not anchor the existence-question "no" to a single argument.** [`concept/deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning`](../../base/concepts/deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning.md) is right that *some* in-principle boundary stands behind the project's descriptivist refusal to litigate, and right that Bender & Koller's is the cleanest signal-based statement of one. But "the rigorous version of the denial" overstates uniqueness: Bender & Koller's is the rigorous version of the *signal-based* denial (Denial 1). The internalist (Denial 2) and the externalist-membership (Denial 3) boundaries are independently rigorous, rest on different metasemantics, and — crucially for a *descriptivist* program — would be lifted (or not) by different things. The honest statement is that the project's "describe, don't litigate" stance is over-determined: it is supported by *several* in-principle boundaries that do not agree with each other, which makes the refusal-to-litigate *stronger*, not weaker (it would take refuting all of them, on incompatible terms, to force the existence question). The page should record the denial as a *family*, citing Denial 1 as the signal-based member, not as the boundary.

**Correction 2 — the map's "only a built world-relation moves the referential cell" is frame-relative, and must be restated.** [`theory/situating-llm-meaning`](../theory/situating-llm-meaning.md) predicts the referential cell moves "only with a built world-relation, not a cleverer text task." On Denials 1 and 3 that is true (a grounding channel; a historical embedding). On Denial 2 it is **false**: an internalist holds that *no* world-relation lifts the cell, because the missing ingredient is a mind, not a relation — so on that frame the cell is not "move it with a world-relation," it is "unmovable for a token-string, world-relation or not." And on Ostertag's route-critique the cell's mover is left open (referential intentions; deference-independence), neither of which is straightforwardly "a built world-relation." So the map's prediction is correct *within the externalist/grounding frames it tacitly assumes* and silent-or-wrong outside them. To be fair to the page: it is *not* blind to the internalist frame — its positioning section already describes the model "in **narrow-content / internalist** terms" and notes externalist reference is "structurally un-probeable" ([`theory/situating-llm-meaning`](../theory/situating-llm-meaning.md), §"The positioning"). The defect is narrower than frame-blindness: it is confined to the lone *unconditioned* "Predicts" sentence, which states the world-relation counterfactual frame-neutrally even though the page elsewhere holds internalism as a live description. The fix is one clause: state that prediction as frame-relative — *on the externalist and signal-based readings*, only a built world-relation moves the cell; on the internalist reading nothing the project could build does; and the choice among frames is itself off-board (the premise-bound point).

Both corrections have the same shape, and it is the shape of the whole essay: the project has been treating a verdict shared across frames as if it were a fact within one frame. The discipline that follows is a citation rule — **never cite "LLMs don't refer" monolithically; name the frame, because the frame fixes both the ground and the counterfactual.**

## What this essay is not

- **Not a verdict on which denial is right.** It ranks none of the three (and does not adjudicate Ostertag's open question). It claims only that they are *incompatible in grounds and counterfactuals*, so they cannot be merged into one boundary.
- **Not a claim that the denials disagree on today's verdict.** They agree the systems in front of us don't refer (Ostertag excepted, who holds it open). The disunity is entirely in the *why* and the *what-would-change-it* — which is exactly what a descriptivist program that talks about *moving cells* needs to get right.
- **Not a duplicate of [`essay/reference-as-premise-bound`](reference-as-premise-bound.md).** That essay takes the *yes-vs-no* externalist disagreement and shows it premise-bound. This essay brackets the "yes" entirely and dissects the *internal* structure of the "no" — its disunity across frames and the counterfactual divergence — which the premise-bound essay explicitly set aside (it scoped itself to "the *externalist* dispute" and treated Baggio & Murphy's §4 frame-rejection as *outside* its claim). The two are siblings: one on the boundary between the camps, one within the larger camp.
- **Not a new empirical claim.** It produces no number and overturns no result. Its force is locating a disunity and disciplining two pages' wording.

## Honest limits

- **The denials are not symmetric in standing, and Denial 1's target is not strictly "reference."** Bender & Koller deny *meaning* M (the form→intent relation), of which word-to-world reference is a part, not the whole; the essay treats their denial as *bearing on* LM reference (no M ⇒ no reference, on their picture), not as a denial pitched at reference specifically. Baggio & Murphy is an arXiv preprint whose internalist premises are "themselves contested" ([`source/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder`](../../base/sources/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder.md), §"Known limits"); Ostertag is peer-reviewed (*Computational Linguistics* 51(2)) but leans on "one framework (contrastive explanation / Lipton)" ([`source/ostertag-2025-externalist-reply`](../../base/sources/ostertag-2025-externalist-reply.md), §"Known limits"); the concept page's "no" is the project's own reading. The essay treats them as *frames whose counterfactuals diverge*, not as equally-weighted authorities.
- **"Three" is a count of frames in-repo, not of all possible denials.** Other denials exist in the literature (e.g. teleosemantic, deflationary-pragmatic) and are not in-repo; the claim is about the disunity *already present in the repo*, which is enough to force the two corrections.
- **The counterfactual table is interpretation, not the sources' own words.** Each "what would lift it" cell is the essay's reconstruction of a frame's commitments, anchored to the verbatim quote in the row's source but not stated by that source as a counterfactual. The internalist "no world-relation suffices" reading, in particular, follows from "Facts about the mind-brain must be invoked" (§4) but is the essay's inference about the modal consequence, flagged as such.
- **The externalist primaries are now substantially in-repo.** [`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md) (session 110), [`source/evans-1973-causal-theory-of-names`](../../base/sources/evans-1973-causal-theory-of-names.md), and [`source/burge-1979-individualism-and-the-mental`](../../base/sources/burge-1979-individualism-and-the-mental.md) (session 111) are all in-repo at primary strength, so the externalist frame Denials 2–3 and Ostertag contest is anchored to its founding primaries, not only to worked applications + the project's characterization; only **Kripke** (the baptism-chain account Evans rejects) remains in [`wanted.md`](../../base/wanted.md). The disunity diagnosis is robust to this either way (it is about the *structure* of the in-repo denials), and reading the three primaries does not collapse the counterfactual divergence — they fix reference/content by off-board facts (information-source, community, deference) without deciding the corpus-membership antecedent, so Denial 3's "conditionally yes" row is **sharpened** (Evans names the "right historical kind" of world-relation — a knowledge-apt information-source — see the session-111 Revision note) but **unchanged in verdict**.
- **It is a structural diagnosis, not a measurement.** Its whole force is locating a disunity and correcting two pages' wording. That is a real but modest contribution.

## Honesty box

- The essay's *original* contribution is the **counterfactual-divergence diagnosis** — that the in-repo denials of LM reference (signal-based, internalist-frame-rejecting, externalist-membership) share today's verdict but give *incompatible* answers to "what would make an LM's words refer?", one of which (internalism) denies that the very mover the project's map names — a built world-relation — could ever lift the cell — together with the two page-corrections that follow (the deflationary page's single-anchor overstatement; the map's frame-relative counterfactual). This shape is the essay's own; the source/concept pages state the *ingredients* (the three denials, their grounds, Ostertag's open conditional) but not the disunity diagnosis or its two consequences.
- **Every textual assertion cites its in-repo page.** The Bender & Koller quotes (M-relation p. 5187; "a priori no way" p. 5185; "from the forms alone is hopeless" p. 5189) are taken from [`source/bender-koller-2020-climbing`](../../base/sources/bender-koller-2020-climbing.md) (as carried verbatim on [`concept/deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning`](../../base/concepts/deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning.md)). The Baggio & Murphy quotes (§4 token-strings, §4 mind-brain, §4 verdict, §5 community-membership, §5 aboutness) are from [`source/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder`](../../base/sources/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder.md), verified there character-for-character against the arXiv PDF (section-level locators). The Ostertag quotes (§3 "cannot ground reference", §4 accepted-conditional, §4 "daunting") are from [`source/ostertag-2025-externalist-reply`](../../base/sources/ostertag-2025-externalist-reply.md), with printed-page locators (CL 51(2):651–659) verified against the publisher PDF. The M&L §4.3 conditional is from [`source/mandelkern-linzen-2024-do-words-refer`](../../base/sources/mandelkern-linzen-2024-do-words-refer.md). The "strongest in-principle case" and narrow-access quotes are from [`concept/referential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/referential-meaning.md); the "rigorous version" and "no behavioral result … settles" quotes from [`concept/deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning`](../../base/concepts/deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning.md); the "only with a built world-relation" quote from [`theory/situating-llm-meaning`](../theory/situating-llm-meaning.md).
- **The strongest thing the essay asserts** is that *the in-repo denials are jointly inconsistent in grounds and counterfactuals, so the referential "no" is not one boundary.* That is an argued structural claim about the in-repo texts, not a proof that no unifying meta-theory of the denials exists; the limits bound it to the in-repo frames and to this project's descriptivist register.

## Revision triggers (read before citing)

- **(a) If a single meta-theory enters the repo that subsumes the three denials** — showing they are facets of one underlying boundary with one counterfactual — then the "not one boundary" thesis weakens to "not *obviously* one boundary," and the two corrections must be re-examined.
