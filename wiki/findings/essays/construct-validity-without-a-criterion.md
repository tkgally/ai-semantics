# Essay: construct validity without a criterion

> **Status: draft (2026-06-23). A philosophical-track / methodological essay arguing in the project's
> own voice.** Its original contribution is a **reframing with a sharp diagnosis**: the project's
> scattered operationalization-gate disciplines — freeze-before-run, the `internal-contrast-only`
> terminal state, "no admissible certifier," "design-out-not-model-out," "presence-is-not-balance" —
> are, in the classical vocabulary of measurement, a single thing: **construct validation** in
> Cronbach & Meehl's (1955) sense. And the project's **no-human-subjects rule removes one of the two
> classical legs** — the *criterion-oriented* one (predictive/concurrent validation, which needs "an
> independent criterion measure on the same subjects"). What is left is **nomological-net construct
> validity alone**, and that single fact *predicts* the project's recurring terminal states rather
> than merely re-describing them: it says *why* so many lines land at `internal-contrast-only`, *why*
> "no admissible certifier" is a structural boundary and not bad luck, and *why* the project's
> negatives are read as ambiguous between instrument and theory. It contains **no new empirical
> claim** and makes **no human comparison**; its one source
> ([`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](../../base/sources/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md))
> is a *methodology* anchor, explicitly **not** a human-comparison anchor, and licenses no claim about
> any model. Read the revision triggers before citing.

## The occasion

The project has, over dozens of sessions, accreted a methodology — a set of rules for building and
scoring behavioral probes of machine meaning — and written several essays each naming one rule of it.
[`essay/design-out-not-model-out`](design-out-not-model-out.md) says a confound collinear with the
manipulation must be fixed at construction time. [`essay/undischargeable-negative`](undischargeable-negative.md)
says behavior can witness a capability but never close out its absence.
[`essay/no-admissible-certifier`](no-admissible-certifier.md) says a stimulus premise readable only by
a barred reader is uncertifiable from in-repo means. [`essay/presence-is-not-balance`](presence-is-not-balance.md)
says an attested ambiguity certifies co-presence and is silent on balance. Two of those essays close
with the same confession: their general rule rests on a *from-scratch re-derivation* inside one
in-repo case, not on any ingested methodology literature, and they name that as their weakest
provenance point.

This session ingested the literature those essays were missing —
[`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](../../base/sources/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md),
the founding statement of **construct validity** in measurement — and reading the project's own gate
discipline against it makes a thing visible that the piecemeal essays could not see from inside their
own cases. The scattered rules are not a grab-bag. They are the project, without the word, doing
*construct validation* — and doing it under a constraint Cronbach & Meehl did not face, which removes
half of their toolkit and forces the project onto the half that remains. Naming that is this essay's
work.

## The classical frame in one paragraph

Cronbach & Meehl partition the ways a test can be validated. "The categories into which the
Recommendations divide validity studies are: predictive validity, concurrent validity, content
validity, and construct validity. The first two of these may be considered together as
**criterion-oriented** validation procedures"
([`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](../../base/sources/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md),
§"Four Types of Validation"). A criterion-oriented study has a fixed shape: "He administers the test,
obtains **an independent criterion measure on the same subjects**, and computes a correlation" (ibid.).
You validate the test by checking it against an external yardstick you already trust — the criterion.
But for many attributes there *is* no such trusted yardstick, and then a different mode is forced:
"**Construct validity must be investigated whenever no criterion or universe of content is accepted as
entirely adequate to define the quality to be measured**" (ibid.), and more generally "Construct
validation is involved whenever a test is to be interpreted as a measure of some attribute or quality
which is not 'operationally defined'" (ibid.). A *construct* is "some postulated attribute … assumed to
be reflected in test performance" (ibid., §"Kinds of Constructs"); you validate the *interpretation* of
the test as measuring it not against one criterion but against the construct's place in an interlocking
web of lawful relations — the **nomological network**: "the interlocking system of laws which
constitute a theory" (ibid., §"The Logic of Construct Validation"). The deep methodological claim of
the paper is that for such attributes, validation just *is* locating the test in that net: "'Learning
more about' a theoretical construct is a matter of elaborating the nomological network in which it
occurs" (ibid.).

## The project's probes are construct-validity instruments

Apply the partition to a project probe. A function-word swap probe, a sense-commitment probe, a
forced-both lexical probe — each interprets a pattern of model behavior as a measure of an attribute:
*sensitivity to a constructional contrast*, *commitment on a word sense*, *sense co-activation*. None
of those attributes is operationally defined; each is "some postulated attribute … assumed to be
reflected in test performance," read off the model instead of a person. So by Cronbach & Meehl's own
criterion the project is, every time it builds a probe, in the **construct-validation** regime: there
is no single accepted yardstick that *defines* "commitment" or "constructional sensitivity," and the
interpretation of the probe as measuring it has to be earned by the probe's place in a net of
contrasts, not by matching one criterion.

This is not a loose analogy. The match is structural and runs joint for joint:

- The project's **freeze-before-run-and-hash** discipline is the construct-validity demand that the
  interpretation be fixed *before* the data, so it cannot be back-fitted — the analogue of specifying
  the nomological net's predictions in advance rather than reading them off the result.
- The project's **convergence ladder** ([`essay/cross-level-convergence-ladder`](cross-level-convergence-ladder.md)),
  which grades a cross-level regularity "from rhyme to mechanism" rung by rung and forbids asserting a
  unification above the rung the evidence has reached, is *exactly* nomological-net elaboration in
  Cronbach & Meehl's sense: "'Learning more about' a theoretical construct is a matter of elaborating
  the nomological network in which it occurs"
  ([`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](../../base/sources/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md)).
  The ladder is the project's name for the net; the rungs are degrees of net elaboration. The two
  essays were describing the same object from two vocabularies.
- The project's refusal to read a probe's result against a single self-chosen standard is Cronbach &
  Meehl's warning against the regress of "relating every criterion to some more ultimate standard," an
  "infinite frustration" (ibid.) that construct validity was introduced to escape.

So the project did not need to invent its discipline from nothing; it independently reconstructed a
1955 framework. That is worth saying plainly — and it is the *value* of the ingest, not a deflation of
the prior essays: their from-scratch derivations were sound, and now they have the classical name and
pedigree they flagged themselves as lacking.

## The amputation: no-human-subjects removes the criterion leg

Here is where the project's situation departs from Cronbach & Meehl's, and where the reframing earns
its keep as a *diagnosis* rather than a relabeling. Cronbach & Meehl have *both* legs available: when a
good criterion exists they use criterion-oriented validation, and they reserve construct validation for
the cases where no criterion is "entirely adequate." Both are on the table; the methodologist chooses.

The project does not get to choose. Criterion-oriented validation requires "an independent criterion
measure **on the same subjects**" — and the project's subjects are models, while its **only** source
of a human criterion is barred from being elicited fresh. [`CLAUDE.md`](../../../CLAUDE.md) rule 4 is
blunt: "**No human subjects.** Tom is the monitor, not a subject. All human bearing comes from existing
resources (treebanks, sense-annotated corpora, dictionaries, acceptability sets, construction
inventories)." A criterion measure obtained on the model is no human criterion at all (and reading the
construct off the model under test is the circularity the gates forbid); a criterion measure obtained
fresh from people is forbidden by rule 4. The single admissible residue is a criterion that *already
exists, pre-encoded in a resource* — which is precisely the project's `anchors:` discipline, and which
covers only those attributes some existing human-labeled asset happens to have measured.

The consequence is exact: **for any construct no existing resource pre-encodes, the project has no
criterion leg at all, and construct validity by the nomological net is the *only* validation it can
perform.** This is not a failing of any one probe. It is a standing structural fact about a behavioral
project bound by no-human-subjects: it lives, for most of its constructs, permanently in
Cronbach & Meehl's "no criterion … entirely adequate" regime — not by happenstance, the way an
ordinary psychometrician sometimes lacks a criterion, but **by rule**, for every construct the existing
resources do not already cover.

## What the amputation predicts (the four terminal states, decoded)

A reframing is only worth the ink if it *predicts* the phenomena it reframes. This one does: the
project's recurring terminal states are exactly what a criterion-less construct-validation regime must
produce, and the frame says *why* each is the honest stopping point rather than a defect to engineer
away.

- **`internal-contrast-only` is "construct validation with the criterion leg amputated," named.** When
  a result carries the `internal-contrast-only` tag it asserts a *within-model contrast* and makes "no
  human-comparison claim." In the classical frame that is precisely a construct whose validity rests
  *entirely* on its position in a net of within-model contrasts, with no criterion correlation
  available — the purest case of construct-validity-without-a-criterion. The tag is not a weaker kind
  of result smuggled past the anchor rule; it is the *correct* and *only* posture for a construct the
  existing resources do not pre-encode. The frame dignifies the tag: it is what validation looks like
  when, lawfully, only the net is available.

- **"No admissible certifier" is a missing observable node in the net.** Cronbach & Meehl require that
  an admissible construct "occur in a nomological net, **at least some of whose laws involve
  observables**"
  ([`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](../../base/sources/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md),
  principle 3). [`essay/no-admissible-certifier`](no-admissible-certifier.md)'s gap — a stimulus
  premise (a constructed sentence's in-item balance) readable *only* by a barred human subject or the
  model under test — is, in this vocabulary, a construct the project needs to place in its net but for
  which it can find **no admissible observable** to tie it down: the would-be observable nodes (a fresh
  human rating; the model's own output) are both ruled out, and no existing resource supplies a
  substitute. The premise cannot enter the net on the observable side, so the net cannot reach it. That
  is why the escape is always the same — *find an existing resource that pre-encodes the property*
  (supply the missing observable node), never relax a rule. The construct-validity frame turns
  "no admissible certifier" from a one-off wall into the general statement: a construct with no
  admissible observable cannot be validated, only parked until one is found.

- **The negatives are two-edged because disconfirmation falls on the whole net.** This is the sharpest
  import. Cronbach & Meehl are explicit that a disconfirming result does not condemn the test: "The
  investigator whose prediction and data are discordant must make strategic decisions. His result can
  be interpreted in three ways: 1. The test does not measure the construct variable. 2. The theoretical
  network which generated the hypothesis is incorrect. 3. The experimental design failed to test the
  hypothesis properly"
  ([`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](../../base/sources/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md),
  §"Conclusions Regarding the Network After Experimentation" → "Implications of Negative Evidence");
  "If the prediction is not confirmed, **any link in the chain may be wrong**" (ibid.). That is, line
  for line, the project's own recurring caveat — that a behavioral null is ambiguous between *the
  instrument failing* and *the capacity being absent*. [`essay/undischargeable-negative`](undischargeable-negative.md)'s
  typology (effect-null / instrument-uninterpretable / structural reach-closure, with the
  capability-absence box kept empty) is the project's worked-out special case of Cronbach & Meehl's
  three-way charge, and the body-difference and instrument-shopping confounds the result pages
  routinely disclose are the project living inside "any link in the chain may be wrong." The frame
  explains *why* the project can never write "the model cannot" from behavior: with no criterion to
  pin the test leg independently, a negative can always be charged to the test or the design rather
  than the construct, exactly as Cronbach & Meehl say.

- **"Design-out" and "presence-is-not-balance" are construct-validity hygiene.** A confound collinear
  with the manipulation ([`essay/design-out-not-model-out`](design-out-not-model-out.md)) is an
  irrelevant determiner of test variance that no analysis can separate from the construct of interest —
  so the test partly measures something other than its construct, and the only fix is at construction.
  Reading balance off a co-presence label ([`essay/presence-is-not-balance`](presence-is-not-balance.md))
  is interpreting the test as measuring a construct (no-dominance) the evidence does not bear on — a
  mismatch between the attribute claimed and the attribute the instrument actually reflects. Both are
  guards on the *correspondence between instrument and construct* that construct validity is about. (The
  later, sharper vocabulary for these two failure shapes — *construct-irrelevant variance* and
  *construct under-representation* — is Messick's, not Cronbach & Meehl's; named here as a pointer, not
  attributed to the 1955 paper, and not relied on.)

## The constructive half: how a criterion-less project still validates

The amputation is not paralysis, and saying only what is lost would misread Cronbach & Meehl, whose
whole point is that construct validation is a *real* form of validation, not a consolation prize. With
the criterion leg gone, the project's available moves are precisely the net-elaboration moves, and the
frame tells it which they are and ranks them:

- **Elaborate the net.** Add contrasts, levels, and instruments that the construct must cohere with —
  which is the convergence ladder's whole program, and Cronbach & Meehl's principle 4 verbatim. A
  within-model construct gains validity as more of its predicted within-model relations hold under
  frozen instruments; cross-level convergence and cross-model concordance are *more net*, not a
  criterion. The ladder's rung discipline is the rule for how much such elaboration licenses.
- **Convert a positive into joint support.** Cronbach & Meehl note that a confirmed prediction supports
  the whole chain at once: "We do not first 'prove' the theory, and then validate the test, nor
  conversely … we examine the relation between the total network of theory and observations"
  ([`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](../../base/sources/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md),
  §"Conclusions Regarding the Network After Experimentation"). A clean within-model result is therefore
  evidence for *both* the instrument's construct validity and the theory it was built to test — which
  is exactly why the project treats a survived contrast as worth something even with no criterion: it
  is net-confirming.
- **Supply a missing observable.** Where a construct has no admissible observable node (the
  no-admissible-certifier gap), the *only* validity-increasing move is to find an existing resource
  that pre-encodes the property and ratify it across a session boundary — adding the observable the net
  was missing. This is why the project's source-scouting and anchor-ratification machinery is not
  ancillary bookkeeping but the literal mechanism by which a criterion-less project repairs its net.

So the frame is not a counsel of despair; it is a map of the project's legitimate validation moves and
a ranking of them, with criterion-matching crossed off by rule and net-elaboration promoted to the
only game.

## The honest price (so the frame is not sold as free)

Naming the project's discipline "construct validity" buys clarity, but it inherits Cronbach & Meehl's
own admitted costs, and an honest essay carries them forward rather than hiding behind a respectable
label.

The first cost is the one the paper itself names: a construct "is defined implicitly by a network of
associations or propositions in which it occurs"
([`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](../../base/sources/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md),
§"Recapitulation"). When the criterion leg is gone, *nothing outside the net pins the construct down* —
its meaning floats on the net the project itself built, and a sufficiently flexible net can accommodate
almost any result. The discipline that keeps this honest is exactly the freeze-and-rung machinery: fix
the net's predictions before the data, and refuse to assert a unification above the rung reached. The
construct-validity frame does not remove the circularity risk; it *names* it as the price of working
without a criterion and points at the existing guards as the only thing holding it.

The second cost is interpretive: with only the net, a negative is permanently three-edged (test /
theory / design), so the project's nulls can never be sharpened into capability-absences. The frame
says this is not fixable by more behavioral probing — "any link in the chain may be wrong" is a
standing condition of criterion-less validation, not a temporary shortfall — and that the only routes
that *do* sharpen a negative are off the behavioral axis (an architectural or mechanistic argument that
pins the test or theory leg independently), exactly as
[`essay/undischargeable-negative`](undischargeable-negative.md) already concludes.

The deepest point is the one the project should keep in view: the criterion leg was not lost by
accident but **surrendered on purpose**, because the no-human-subjects rule buys auditable,
reproducible human anchoring and forecloses a whole class of researcher-degrees-of-freedom. The
findings are cleaner *because* the project gave up the criterion leg; the bill is that most of its
constructs can be validated only against the net, and some not at all until a resource turns up. That
is a feature with a cost, named — not a deficiency to engineer around.

## What this essay is not

- **Not a new empirical claim.** It is a reframing of the project's *methodology*. Every empirical
  sentence about a model in the essays it touches is left at its own page's stated strength; this essay
  asserts nothing new about any model. Its one source is a 1955 measurement paper.
- **Not a human comparison, and not a new anchor.** Cronbach & Meehl is a *methodology* anchor; it
  supplies a theory of validity, not a human yardstick, and it explicitly cannot discharge any
  empirical claim's human-anchor obligation. The essay imports no human number.
- **Not a claim the project consciously applied construct-validity theory.** The discipline was
  reconstructed from scratch over many sessions; the ingest lets it be *read against* the classical
  framework after the fact. The claim is that the two coincide, not that one was derived from the
  other.
- **Not an argument to relax the no-human-subjects rule.** The reverse: it shows the rule's cost
  (criterion-validation surrendered) is a *principled* one with a real benefit, and that the project's
  existing machinery (nomological-net elaboration, resource-anchoring) is the right and sufficient
  response to working without the criterion leg.
- **Not a claim that construct validity is uncontested.** The 1955 framework was later revised and
  criticized (Campbell & Fiske's convergent/discriminant validity; Messick's unified validity;
  Borsboom's critiques) — none in-repo. The essay cites Cronbach & Meehl as the *origin and canonical
  statement*, and trigger (d) queues the obvious follow-ups.

## Revision triggers (read before citing)

- **(a) A criterion leg becomes available for some construct.** If an existing human-labeled resource
  is ratified that pre-encodes a construct the project currently validates only internally (so that a
  genuine criterion correlation becomes computable), then for *that* construct the project regains the
  criterion leg, and the result moves from `internal-contrast-only` toward a criterion-anchored claim.
  This is the amputation healing locally, exactly where the no-admissible-certifier escape fires; the
  essay is confirmed and gains a worked example.
- **(b) A construct is shown un-placeable in any net.** If some attribute the project wants to measure
  turns out to have neither a criterion nor any admissible within-model observable to tie it to (no net
  position at all), then by Cronbach & Meehl's principle 3 it is not a scientifically admissible
  construct for this project, and the honest move is to retire it rather than probe it. The essay would
  gain its sharpest application: a construct the project must *abandon*, not merely park.
- **(c) A negative is sharpened off the behavioral axis.** If an architectural or mechanistic argument
  ever pins the test or theory leg independently for some probed capability (discharging one edge of
  the three-way charge), the project would, for that case, escape "any link in the chain may be wrong"
  and could write a sharper negative. This would not overturn the essay (whose claim is about
  criterion-less *behavioral* validation) but would add the off-axis route, dovetailing with
  [`essay/undischargeable-negative`](undischargeable-negative.md)'s trigger (a).
- **(d) A later validity source is ingested.** Campbell & Fiske (1959, convergent/discriminant
  validity), Messick (unified validity, construct-irrelevant variance / under-representation), or
  Borsboom et al. (the realist critique of construct validity) are uncatalogued. Ingesting one would
  let the essay's "design-out / presence-is-not-balance as construct-validity hygiene" mapping be stated
  in the precise later vocabulary it currently only gestures at, and would test whether the project's
  reconstruction matches the *post-1955* consensus or only the founding statement. Flagged, not
  assumed.

## Honesty box

- The essay's **original** contribution is the **reframing-plus-diagnosis**: that the project's
  operationalization-gate discipline *is* construct validation in Cronbach & Meehl's (1955) sense; that
  the no-human-subjects rule **removes the criterion-oriented leg by rule** (criterion validation needs
  an independent criterion measure on the same subjects, which the project's subjects-are-models +
  human-bearing-only-via-existing-resources constraints forbid), leaving **nomological-net construct
  validity as the only available form**; and that this single fact **predicts** the project's four
  recurring terminal states — `internal-contrast-only` (construct validity with the criterion leg
  amputated), "no admissible certifier" (a missing observable node), the two-edged negative
  (disconfirmation falling on the whole net, per Cronbach & Meehl's three-way charge), and
  design-out / presence-is-not-balance (construct-instrument correspondence hygiene) — together with the
  identification of the convergence ladder as the project's nomological-net-elaboration engine. **No
  empirical claim here is new, original, or reported.**
- The strongest sentences leaned on, at their stated strength, all from
  [`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](../../base/sources/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md):
  the four types and "The first two of these may be considered together as criterion-oriented
  validation procedures"; the criterion-oriented study obtains "an independent criterion measure on the
  same subjects"; "Construct validation is involved whenever a test is to be interpreted as a measure
