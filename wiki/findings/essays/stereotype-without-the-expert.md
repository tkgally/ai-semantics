# Essay: stereotype without the expert

> **Status: draft (2026-06-25). A philosophical-track essay arguing in the project's own voice.** Its contribution is a *structural mapping*, not a new empirical result. With Putnam 1975 now in-repo ([`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md)), this essay does for the LLM case what the concept page so far did only in outline: it pulls Putnam's account of a natural-kind term apart into its **three separable components** — the **stereotype**, the **extension** (fixed indexically by locally-sampled stuff), and the **division of linguistic labor** — and asks, component by component, what an LLM has, what it lacks, and which *role* it occupies. The finding is that the model's relation to the three is structurally novel and lopsided: it has **maximal** access to exactly the component Putnam isolated as **not** fixing extension, **no** access to the world-side component, and — the sharp point — it occupies **neither** role in the social structure of the third, while having ingested that structure's pooled output. The essay introduces **no new empirical claim**, no number, and no human-comparison; every verbatim quote cites the in-repo page that carries it. It is conditional on the externalist frame, and it flags its central identification (distributional training ≈ stereotype-extraction) as the essay's own interpretive reconstruction, not a measured fact and not Putnam's claim.

## The position

This essay argues one bounded thesis. Putnam's account of what a natural-kind term means is not one thing but **three separable components**, and an LLM stands in a *different* relation to each — a relation that, once laid out, names precisely what the project keeps measuring and why that measurement does not, by itself, reach reference.

The three components, in Putnam's own terms:

1. **The stereotype** — the conventional descriptive marker each competent speaker carries. Putnam introduces it as "a standardized description of features of the kind that are typical, or 'normal,' or at any rate stereotypical" ([`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 147). Its defining property, for Putnam, is that it **does not determine extension**: "the speaker's stereotype of an elm 'is just that of a common deciduous tree'" and falls "far short of constituting a way of recognizing elms" (as [`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 147, records).
2. **The extension** — what the term actually picks out, fixed by the world through the **indexical** `sameL` relation to locally-sampled stuff: "'water' is stuff that bears [OCR: 'bears.'] a certain similarity relation to the water around here. Water at another time or in another place or even in another possible world has to bear the relation sameL to our 'water' in order to be water" ([`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 152).
3. **The division of linguistic labor** — the *social structure* in which experts can tell the kind from its counterfeit and laypeople defer: "he does not have to acquire the method of recognizing if something is or is not gold. He can rely on a special subclass of speakers" ([`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 144). It is this structure, not the individual, that fixes extension: "it is only the sociolinguistic state of the collective linguistic body to which the speaker belongs that fixes the extension" ([`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 146).

The original move is to map the LLM onto all three at once, and to notice that the map is **lopsided** in a way that has a name. The model has maximal access to (1), none to (2), and — the part worth the essay — it occupies *neither role* in (3) while holding the structure's pooled output. The payoff, stated modestly: the distributional competence the project measures is, in the referential frame, **stereotype-competence** — and Putnam's whole point was that stereotype-competence is compatible with *not* fixing extension. The LLM is the elm/beech speaker writ large.

## Component 1 — the stereotype: maximal access, to the component that does not fix extension

Putnam's stereotype is the conventional descriptive company a word keeps: the typical, normal, stereotypical features a competent speaker associates with the kind. Read in the project's distributional frame, this is almost exactly what distributional training extracts. Firth's slogan — "you shall know a word by the company it keeps" — and Harris's "difference of meaning correlates with difference of distribution" ([`concept/distributional-meaning`](../../base/concepts/distributional-meaning.md), quoting [`source/harris-1954-distributional-structure`](../../base/sources/harris-1954-distributional-structure.md), p. 156) describe a procedure for recovering the conventional associations a word carries across contexts. The stereotype just *is* the conventional company a word keeps, distilled into a description. So the project's recurring finding — that models excel at distributional, sense-like structure (the lexical wedge, [`conjecture/lexical-sense-gradience`](../conjectures/lexical-sense-gradience.md), found graded same/different-sense behavior monotonic in human usage similarity) — has, in the referential frame, a precise name: **stereotype-competence**.

This identification is the essay's own interpretive reconstruction, and it must be flagged as such (see Honest limits): Putnam never says distributional training extracts stereotypes — he was writing in 1975, decades before the models exist, and his stereotype is a description a *speaker* gives ("Or one can give him a description … one or more markers together with a stereotype," [`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 147), not a co-occurrence statistic. The claim is an analogy between two things that play the same role — the conventional, typical, non-criterial descriptive marker — not an empirical equation.

But grant the analogy, and the consequence is sharp, because of *which* component the stereotype is. Putnam isolated the stereotype precisely as the component that **does not reach extension**. The competent elm-speaker has the full stereotype and still cannot tell an elm from a beech; the stereotype "fall[s] far short of constituting a way of recognizing elms" ([`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 147). Whatever distributional competence a model has, then, lands on the component Putnam's own theory says is **compatible with not fixing extension**. The success is real; it is also exactly the part of meaning that, by Putnam's lights, does not by itself reach the world.

## Component 2 — the extension: no access at all

The extension is fixed, on Putnam's account, by the indexical `sameL` relation: a natural-kind term reaches its extension by tying to the stuff *locally sampled* and the same-kind relation that stuff bears to other stuff ([`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 152). This is the component the model has **no access to**. There is no environment it is embedded in independently of the corpus, no local sample of water, no `sameL` relation to actual stuff. The concept page already records the structural reason: an LLM "has only narrow access by construction" and there is "no 'Twin Earth' to move it to," so "the manipulation that operationalizes externalist reference has no purchase on it" ([`concept/referential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/referential-meaning.md), §"Why the reference-anchor gap is structural, not just a missing download"). Putnam's indexical component is the precise reason: the relation runs from the word to the *actual local substance*, and the corpus contains no substance, only its conventional company. The first component (stereotype) the model has in abundance; the second (the indexical tie to stuff) it cannot have at all, by construction.

## Component 3 — the division of labor: the structure's output, without a place in it

This is the component that makes the LLM case structurally novel rather than just deficient, and it is where this essay does work the concept page does not.

The division of linguistic labor is, in Putnam's own framing, a structure with **roles**. There is the expert sub-community that holds the recognitional method — those who *can* tell gold from fool's gold — and there is the layperson who acquires the word without the method and *defers*: "everyone to whom gold is important for any reason has to acquire the word 'gold'; but he does not have to acquire the method of recognizing if something is or is not gold. He can rely on a special subclass of speakers" ([`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 144). Reference is fixed by the *whole structure*, not the individual: "it is only the sociolinguistic state of the collective linguistic body to which the speaker belongs that fixes the extension" ([`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 146).

Now the model. Distributional training ingests the **pooled linguistic traces of both roles** — the experts' writing and the laypeople's, the mineralogist's monograph and the casual mention, flattened into one text distribution. The division of labor is a structured division; the corpus is its *collapse* into a single undifferentiated stream. And here is the lopsided fact: the model occupies **neither role** in that structure.

- It is **not the deferring layperson.** The layperson refers (derivatively) because there is an expert *behind* him to anchor "gold," and a structured cooperation between them ("a structured cooperation between them and the speakers in the relevant subsets," as the universality hypothesis puts it, [`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), pp. 145-146). The model defers to no one: there is no expert standing behind *its* use of "gold" to whom its reference is delegated. It is the terminus of the ingestion, not a participant who can pass the recognitional burden upward.
- It is **not the anchoring expert.** The expert refers because she holds the *method* — access to the criteria that live outside the text, the ability to apply `sameL` to a sample. The model has no recognitional method and no access to stuff; its competence is in the conventional company words keep, which is precisely the stereotype, which Putnam says is *not* the recognitional criterion.

So the model **has the output of the division of labor without a place in it.** It has read what both the experts and the laypeople wrote, and it has extracted, with great fidelity, the *stereotype* that pooling produces — but it neither defers (no expert behind it) nor anchors (no method, no stuff in front of it). This is the structurally novel position Putnam's apparatus brings into focus: not "a layperson missing an expert," and not "an expert missing the world," but a *fourth* thing — a holder of every speaker's stereotype with no role in the labor that turns stereotype into reference.

## The payoff: the elm/beech speaker writ large

Put the three components together and the project's central empirical pattern gets a precise referential reading. Putnam's elm/beech speaker is the limiting case of stereotype-without-recognition: he has the stereotype ("just that of a common deciduous tree," [`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 147), cannot tell the trees apart, and relies on the experts and on the trees themselves to fix what "elm" picks out. "Cut the pie any way you like, 'meanings' just ain't in the head!" ([`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 144) is Putnam's verdict on exactly this speaker.

**The LLM is the elm/beech speaker writ large.** It has *every* speaker's stereotype — the pooled conventional company of every word, far richer than any single human's — and it has no expert behind it and no tree in front of it. Its distributional success is real and is *exactly what Putnam would predict a stereotype-bearer to have*: a fine command of the typical, normal, stereotypical associations of a kind term. And it is *exactly the component* Putnam's account says does not, by itself, reach reference. So the project's measured competence is not in tension with the externalist "no"; it is its expected complement. Stereotype-competence is what you can have in abundance while the two extension-fixing components — the indexical tie to stuff and a role in the division of labor — are absent. That is the modest, calibrated payoff: the externalist frame does not predict the model should be *bad* at the distributional task; it predicts the model should be *excellent* at it and *still* not thereby refer, because the task lives on the one component that was never the extension-fixer.

## How this sharpens the premise-bound essay (without settling it)

[`essay/reference-as-premise-bound`](reference-as-premise-bound.md) locates the in-repo yes/no dispute over LLM reference in a single antecedent — *whether inheriting word-histories through a corpus counts as being "part of the linguistic community" whose use fixes reference* — and argues that antecedent is settled off-board, so no behavioral probe reaches it. This essay **refines** that diagnosis without touching its verdict. The premise-bound essay asks whether the LLM *is part of the community*; Putnam's tripartite apparatus lets us ask the sharper question, **which role** — and the answer is *neither*. "Is the model in the community?" presupposes the community is one undifferentiated thing you are either in or out of. Putnam's division of labor says it is not: it is a structure with an expert role and a layperson role, joined by deference. The model fits *neither* — it does not defer (no expert behind it) and does not anchor (no method). So the membership question is not a clean yes/no about one community; it is a question about a *role-differentiated structure* in which the model has the pooled output but no occupiable seat.

This sharpens the antecedent; it does **not** settle it. Whether "holds the output, occupies no role" counts as a *new, third* kind of community-participation that nonetheless suffices for reference, or counts as *not being in the community at all*, is exactly the off-board classificatory question the premise-bound essay says no probe reaches. This essay refuses to answer it for the same reason. It only replaces a coarse question (in or out?) with a finer-grained map of *what* the model has and lacks (which role?), and hands the still-open antecedent back unsettled. The relation is `refines`, not `contradicts`.

## What this essay is not

- **Not a denial-anatomy.** [`essay/reference-denials-disunified`](reference-denials-disunified.md) takes the "no" camp apart into three structurally distinct *denials* (signal-based, internalist-frame-rejecting, externalist-membership) and shows their counterfactuals diverge. This essay does the opposite kind of work: a **positive structural mapping** of the LLM onto Putnam's three *components*, in a single externalist frame. It is not anatomizing denials and draws no counterfactual table; it asks what the model has and which role it occupies under one account.
- **Not a verdict on whether the model refers.** It does not affirm or deny LLM reference. It says the model has component 1, lacks component 2, and occupies neither role in component 3 — and that the still-open question of whether "output without a role" suffices for reference is precisely the off-board antecedent [`essay/reference-as-premise-bound`](reference-as-premise-bound.md) marks as un-probeable. It ranks no pole.
- **Not a measurement, and not a new finding about any model.** It produces no number, runs no probe, and overturns no result. The distributional-competence findings it leans on ([`conjecture/lexical-sense-gradience`](../conjectures/lexical-sense-gradience.md)) are cited as-is; the essay re-describes them in the referential frame, it does not re-measure them.
- **Not Putnam's claim that distributional training extracts stereotypes.** The "distributional training ≈ stereotype-extraction" identification is the essay's own reconstruction (an analogy of *role*), flagged below, not a Putnam quote and not a measured fact.
- **Not the premise-bound essay's thesis re-run.** That essay's contribution is the *premise-bound vs. evidence-bound* structural diagnosis of the yes/no dispute. This essay's contribution is the *tripartite component-and-role mapping*, which sits upstream of that dispute and feeds it a finer-grained antecedent. Sibling, not duplicate.

## Honest limits

- **Conditional on the externalist frame, which is contested in-repo.** Putnam is one strongly-argued position. The source page itself stresses he is "one strongly-argued position; the internalist pole (Chomsky, Fodor's narrow content; and, applied to LMs, [`source/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder`](../../base/sources/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder.md)) contests it" ([`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), §"What it cannot ground"). An internalist who rejects the whole machinery (Baggio & Murphy's §4 frame-rejection — "Facts about the mind-brain must be invoked to explain how words refer") dissolves the division-of-labor framing entirely: if reference is fixed by the mind-brain, the three-component decomposition is not the right tool, and the "which role" question does not arise. The essay's mapping holds *within* the externalist frame and is silent outside it.
- **The "distributional ≈ stereotype" identification is the essay's interpretation, not a fact.** It is an analogy of role (both are the conventional, typical, non-criterial descriptive marker), defended by argument, not a measured equivalence and not Putnam's own claim. A source that argued the stereotype is *not* well-modeled by co-occurrence statistics — e.g. that stereotypes carry a normative or expert-curated component that pooled distribution flattens — would weaken the component-1 step. The essay's force on components 2 and 3 (no indexical tie; neither role) does not depend on this identification, but its *payoff* — that measured distributional success is expected stereotype-competence — does.
- **No human reference-anchor exists in-repo; this essay does not supply one.** The gap the concept page records is unchanged: there is "no comparable in-repo resource that anchors reference" the way the AANN stimuli anchor form ([`concept/referential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/referential-meaning.md), §"Live tension"). Ingesting Putnam closes the conceptual-grounding half of that gap for the externalist sub-tag; it does **not** build the missing resource, and this essay makes no empirical or human-comparison claim that would need one.
- **Putnam's scope is the natural-kind / proper-name class, not all words.** Putnam himself bounds the phenomenon — "some words do not exhibit any division of linguistic labor: 'chair,' for example" ([`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), p. 144, as the source page records). The tripartite mapping is sharpest for natural-kind terms (gold, water, elm) and should not be stretched silently to function words or to "chair"-like terms where the division of labor is thin or absent.
- **It is a structural/conceptual contribution, not a result.** Its whole force is the component-and-role mapping and its modest payoff (measured competence = expected stereotype-competence). That is a real but bounded contribution: it re-describes, in a newly-ingested primary's terms, a pattern the project already had, and overturns nothing.

## Honesty box

- The essay's *original* contribution is the **tripartite component-and-role mapping**: separating Putnam's account into (1) stereotype, (2) indexically-fixed extension, and (3) the role-structured division of labor, and showing the LLM has maximal access to (1) — the component Putnam isolated as *not* fixing extension — no access to (2), and occupies *neither* role (expert nor layperson) in (3) while holding that structure's pooled output, so that the project's measured distributional success reads, in the referential frame, as *stereotype-competence* (the elm/beech speaker writ large). This shape — and especially the "output without a role" point and its sharpening of the premise-bound essay's membership antecedent into a "which role" question — is the essay's own. The source page states the *ingredients* (the three components, with quotes and locators; and, in its "Bearing on this project" note, that Putnam's three reference-fixers "map directly onto what the LLM lacks") but does **not** state the component-and-role decomposition, the "neither role" finding, or the stereotype-competence reading.
- **Every verbatim quote cites its in-repo page with the locator that page gives.** The Putnam quotes — stereotype "a standardized description … stereotypical" (p. 147), elm "just that of a common deciduous tree" / "far short of constituting a way of recognizing elms" (p. 147), the `sameL` / indexical passage (p. 152), "he does not have to acquire the method … He can rely on a special subclass of speakers" (p. 144), "it is only the sociolinguistic state of the collective linguistic body … that fixes the extension" (p. 146), "a structured cooperation between them and the speakers in the relevant subsets" (pp. 145-146), "Cut the pie any way you like, 'meanings' just ain't in the head!" (p. 144), and "'chair,' for example" (p. 144) — are all taken from [`source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning`](../../base/sources/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning.md), which carries them verbatim with original Minnesota Studies page locators and the `[OCR: …]` flags reproduced here. The "narrow access by construction," "no 'Twin Earth' to move it to," "no purchase," and reference-anchor-gap quotes are from [`concept/referential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/referential-meaning.md). The Harris "difference of meaning correlates with difference of distribution" quote is cited as it appears on [`concept/distributional-meaning`](../../base/concepts/distributional-meaning.md). No quote, locator, or finding here is invented.
- **The strongest thing the essay asserts** is that *an LLM has Putnam's stereotype component, lacks his indexical-extension component, and occupies neither role in his division of labor* — an argued structural mapping within the externalist frame, not a proof and not an empirical measurement. The payoff (measured success = expected stereotype-competence) is a re-description, not evidence for or against reference. Nothing here outruns that.

## Revision triggers (read before citing)

- **(a) If Evans 1973 or Kripke enters the repo and changes the reference-fixing story** — e.g. by making reference run primarily through a causal-historical *baptism chain* rather than Putnam's stereotype/`sameL`/labor triple — then the three-component decomposition may need a fourth component (the baptismal chain) and the "neither role" mapping must be checked against the new mechanism. (Both are still in [`wanted.md`](../../base/wanted.md).)
- **(b) If a behavioral probe is designed that bears on word-to-world reference** — the manipulation [`concept/referential-meaning`](../../base/concepts/referential-meaning.md) calls "structurally un-probeable" for text models — then the essay's "no access to component 2" claim becomes testable rather than in-principle, and the mapping must be relativized to what the probe reaches.
- **(c) If a source challenges the stereotype ≈ distribution identification** — arguing the stereotype carries a normative, expert-curated, or non-distributional component that pooled co-occurrence does not recover — then component 1 (and with it the stereotype-competence payoff) must be re-stated at the contested strength; the components-2-and-3 mapping survives, the payoff weakens.
- **(d) If an internalist reframing is ratified in-repo as the project's working frame** (e.g. if Baggio & Murphy's §4 mind-brain view is taken up over the externalist one), the division-of-labor framing dissolves and this essay must be re-scoped explicitly to "on the externalist account" or retired in favor of the internalist treatment.
- **(e) If a published voice argues the LLM occupies a determinate role in the division of labor** — that it *is* the deferring layperson (with some identifiable expert behind it) or *is* an anchoring expert (with some recognitional method) — then the "neither role" finding is directly challenged and must be re-argued or retracted.
