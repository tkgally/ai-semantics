# Essay: from rhyme to mechanism — a convergence ladder for cross-level pattern claims

> **Status: revised (2026-06-22). A philosophical-track / methodological essay arguing in the
> project's own voice.** Its original contribution is a **general evidence standard** for one
> recurring move the project is now positioned to make repeatedly: the upgrade from *"we describe
> several findings with one phrase"* to *"those findings share one underlying mechanism."* That
> upgrade is not free; it must be **earned**, rung by rung. The essay proposes a graded
> **convergence ladder** — abstracted from one motivating instance (the cross-level
> "graded-in-the-population, discrete-in-the-moment" shape) but applicable to **any** future
> cross-level or cross-instrument family-resemblance the project finds — and the rule that a
> cross-level unification may be **asserted only at the rung the evidence has reached**. It contains
> **no new empirical claim** and makes **no human comparison**: the motivating instance is cited at
> exactly the strength its own page states, and every empirical sentence cites the in-repo page that
> carries it. Read the revision triggers before citing.

## The occasion

The project has just matured an open question into a conjecture, and in doing so it wrote, without
naming it, an evidence standard. The open question
[`open-question/gradience-population-not-moment`](../open-questions/gradience-population-not-moment.md)
observes a shape that recurs at **three semantic levels** — lexical sense, constructional preference,
relational coordination: a **graded / structured** property recoverable from the *aggregate* (the
population of a model's judgments, or the content-bag of a record) sitting beside a **discrete /
ungraded / uncommitted** handling of the *single moment or item*. It then asks, sharply, whether that
shape is "a **general constraint on distributional competence** … or … **three separate,
level-specific facts that merely rhyme**." The companion conjecture
[`conjecture/cross-level-gradience-aggregate-not-moment`](../conjectures/cross-level-gradience-aggregate-not-moment.md) makes the specific bet and names the
shared-instrument probe that would test it; the decision
[`decisions/open/cross-level-shared-instrument-operationalization`](../../decisions/open/cross-level-shared-instrument-operationalization.md) will fix that probe's yardstick.

This essay is **none of those three things**. It is not the question (the OQ owns that), not the bet
or its test (the conjecture and its decision own those). It is the **reusable standard** the
conjecture's confirm/falsify criteria silently instantiate: *what licenses upgrading a cross-level
family-resemblance to a single-mechanism claim?* The cross-level gradience shape is the motivating
instance, cited here as one application of the standard, not re-argued. The pairing mirrors an
existing one in the project's record —
[`conjecture/preference-commitment-generality`](../conjectures/preference-commitment-generality.md)
makes a specific generality bet, while
[`essay/preference-without-commitment`](preference-without-commitment.md) states the position the bet
serves. Here the conjecture makes the cross-level bet; this essay states the *general bar* every such
bet must clear.

## The thesis: a family resemblance among phrasings is the weakest possible evidence

When the project sees the same shape recur across levels or instruments, the inference from "we
describe N findings with one phrase" to "one underlying mechanism" is not a discovery — it is a
**hypothesis**, and the weakest-supported one available. The OQ says this against its own pattern,
and the candour is worth quoting because the essay only generalizes it. The unification there is, in
the OQ's own words, "at present a *family resemblance among phrasings*, not a demonstrated
mechanism," and the OQ flags the structural reason the suspicion is acute: a pattern that spans levels
"is also the pattern most likely to be an **artifact of how the project phrases its findings** rather
than of the models." Stated at its starkest by the same page: "the three legs *rhyme*; whether they
*share a cause* is unshown."

That self-skepticism is the seed of a general rule, because it is not special to gradience. **A shared
phrase is a fact about the project's prose, not about the models.** The project writes its findings in
one voice, reaches for memorable glosses, and re-uses a vocabulary ("aggregate vs. moment," "graded
vs. committed," "add-easy vs. cancel-hard") across pages precisely because that vocabulary is what
makes the wiki readable page-to-page. The very habit that makes the findings legible is the habit most
likely to manufacture a spurious cross-level unity: the same words get laid over genuinely different
measurements, and the eye reads kinship into the overlay. So the default posture toward *any*
"we keep seeing this shape" observation must be the deflationary one — the shape is a rhyme until
something stronger than the project's own phrasing distinguishes it from one.

## The convergence ladder

The standard is graded, not binary, for the same reason the project's construction evidence-ladder is
graded: a yes/no verdict throws away exactly the information — *how strong is the cross-level evidence
right now?* — that the project most needs to report honestly. The rungs, weakest to strongest:

- **R0 — shared vocabulary.** The project describes N findings with one phrase. This is the weakest
  rung; it is pure rhyme, and the default suspicion is **artifact-of-phrasing**. R0 licenses an
  open-question, never a claim — it is the rung at which the OQ correctly sits.

- **R1 — same direction under each level's own native instrument.** Each leg independently exhibits
  the pattern, *but each in its own measure*: lexical via self-reported confidence + categorical
  decline + forced-pick dispersion; constructional via NLI entailment commitment; relational via
  content-bag recoverability. This is **the current state of the cross-level gradience shape** — and
  it is *not yet a mechanism*. R1 is genuinely more than R0 (three independent instruments is more
  than one phrase), but it cannot tell a shared cause from three instrument-specific facts that happen
  to point the same way, because the instruments are confounded with the levels: nothing has been held
  fixed *across* the legs. (One within-leg qualifier: the lexical leg has *itself* already had one
  break-test — its output channel was widened and, for the models that took it up, the commitment null
  largely survived
  ([`result/lexical-bridging-context-working-surface-v1`](../results/lexical-bridging-context-working-surface-v1.md))
  — so on its own that leg sits marginally above bare R1; but the *cross-level* shape stays at R1,
  because widening the channel *within* one leg equalizes no instrument *across* the legs.) The OQ's
  own "Where the parallel is loose" section is, read at this height,
  a catalogue of why R1 is not R2 — "**Three different instruments measure the three 'moments,'**" and
  "**The 'aggregate' is not one thing across legs.**"

- **R2 — one shared instrument applied at ≥2 levels (instrument equalized).** The same model shows the
  shape under *one* commitment instrument applied at more than one level, scored the same way. This is
  **the first rung that distinguishes mechanism from instrument-artifact**, because it removes the
  confound R1 cannot: if the shape survives equalizing the instrument, the instrument is no longer a
  rival explanation for the cross-level kinship. The OQ names exactly this discriminator — "hold *one*
  commitment instrument fixed and apply it at more than one level … If the same model shows
  graded-aggregate / discrete-moment under *one* instrument across levels, that is evidence for a
  general constraint; if the shape appears under each level's *own* instrument but dissolves when the
  instruments are equalized, that is evidence for three rhyming instrument-specific facts." R2 is the
  floor for asserting a cross-level unification at all.

- **R3 — the shape survives a break-test.** A manipulation that *should* dissolve the shape if it were
  instrument-specific is applied, and the shape **persists where the unified reading predicts and
  dissolves exactly where the deflationary reading predicts**. The cleanest break-tests are the ones
  the project already owns: a channel-widening the model genuinely takes up, more resamples, paraphrase
  variants. This rung borrows its logic from
  [`essay/output-channel-confound`](output-channel-confound.md), which establishes that a forced-format
  capability-negative is "**channel-bounded**, never … a capacity verdict" until the channel is varied
  *and taken up* — its working-surface re-run of the lexical leg is the in-repo precedent for a
  cross-level break-test, and that essay's three-state grid (channel-bounded / channel-controlled /
  channel-not-taken-up) is exactly the kind of differential prediction R3 demands: a real mechanism
  survives the manipulation that a confound would not. A shape that clears R3 is a cross-level
  *finding*, not a conjecture.

- **R-ceiling — representational / mechanistic / training-level evidence of a shared cause.** Direct
  evidence that one mechanism produces the shape at every level — shared representational structure, a
  mechanistic-interpretability result, a demonstrable training-time origin. **This rung is out of reach
  for a purely behavioral project**, and naming it so is part of the standard, not a deferral of it.
  Every leg of every cross-level shape the project can build is behavioral, on n=3 commercial models to
  which the project has no mechanistic access; the ceiling is a *true* out-of-reach, like the
  "transcript ceiling" the project already names as an honest boundary, not a rung the project can
  climb with more behavioral probes. A cross-level unification asserted at R-ceiling would require a
  method the project does not have.

The rungs are cumulative in the same way the construction ladder's tiers are: each higher rung
subsumes the evidential force of the ones below and removes one more rival explanation for the
cross-level kinship. The progression is **phrasing → independent-instrument agreement →
instrument-equalized agreement → break-test survival → mechanistic identity**, and only the top three
rungs are evidence for a *shared mechanism* as opposed to a *suggestive coincidence of vocabulary*.

## This is a different ladder from the construction evidence-ladder

The project already has a five-rung **evidence ladder**, on
[`theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms`](../theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms.md): Tier 0
form-acceptability < Tier 1 surprisal-contrast < Tier 2 gradient semantic tracking < Tier 3
generalization < Tier 4 inference-licensing, compressed by that page as "**form-acceptability <
surprisal-contrast < gradient semantic tracking < generalization < inference-licensing**." The
convergence ladder borrows that page's *format* — graded rungs, each subsuming those below, placements
made explicit — and the borrowing is deliberate. But the two ladders grade **different things**, and
conflating them would be a category error this essay must forbid up front.

- The **construction evidence-ladder** grades, for **one model on one construction**, how strong the
  evidence is that the model has internalized that construction's *meaning* rather than only its form.
  Its rungs climb from formal competence toward inference-licensing; its question is *meaning vs. form
  in a single model*.
- The **convergence ladder** grades, for **a shape already observed across levels or instruments**,
  how strong the evidence is that the shape reflects *one cause* rather than *rhyming coincidence*. Its
  rungs climb from shared vocabulary toward mechanistic identity; its question is *one mechanism vs.
  many that rhyme*.

They are orthogonal. A finding can sit at Tier 4 of the construction ladder (strong single-model
inference-licensing) and at R0 of the convergence ladder (its kinship to some *other* level's finding
is, so far, only a shared phrase). A cross-level shape can reach R2 of the convergence ladder while
each of its legs sits at a different tier of the construction ladder. The construction ladder asks
*how deep is this one competence?*; the convergence ladder asks *is this recurring description one
thing or several?* Different ladders, different questions — said plainly so no reader imports one's
rungs into the other.

## The standard, and what it supports

The rule the ladder delivers is one sentence: **a cross-level unification may be asserted only at the
rung the evidence has reached.** Below R2 it is not a claim; it stays a *rhyme* — a conjecture-shaped
question, filed as an open-question, never written as a finding. At R2 it may be asserted as an
instrument-equalized cross-level regularity, scoped to the levels actually tested. At R3 it earns the
status of a cross-level finding with a survived break-test. R-ceiling, for this project, is a boundary,
not an available assertion.

This standard does not strain against the project's existing commitments; it **discharges** them in a
new place. It is the **deflationary default** ([`concept/distributional-meaning`](../../base/concepts/distributional-meaning.md)
is the null these cross-level claims are about) applied to the project's own synthesizing impulse: when
the wiki notices a shape spanning its pages, the burden of proof sits on the unification, not on the
deflation. It is the **no-overclaim rule** ([`CLAUDE.md`](../../../CLAUDE.md) rule 6 — "Keep every
claim calibrated to its evidence; never inflate for novelty"; "When in doubt, under-claim") in the
specific register where overclaim is most tempting, because a cross-level mechanism is the most
*novel-sounding* thing the project could announce and therefore the thing rule 6 most needs to fence.
And it is the project's **describe-don't-litigate** stance: the ladder says describe the rhyme at the
rung the evidence reaches, and do not litigate it up into a mechanism the behavior has not earned.

The cleanest way to see the standard's shape is as the **symmetric twin** of
[`essay/undischargeable-negative`](undischargeable-negative.md). That essay argues you cannot prove
"M *cannot* do X" from behavior — a capability-absence is "a universal over an open class of
elicitations that no finite behavioral battery reaches." The mirror holds here: **you cannot prove
"these legs share *one* mechanism" from rhyme.** Both are inferences that behavior *underdetermines* —
the negative because no finite battery exhausts the elicitations, the unification because no shared
phrasing rules out the rival that the legs are instrument-specific facts that merely point the same
way. The undischargeable-negative essay turns its asymmetry into a typology and a reporting discipline;
this essay turns its underdetermination into a ladder and an assertion discipline. The same honesty
governs both: a behavioral project states only what behavior licenses, and "one mechanism across
levels" is, below R2, exactly as unlicensed as "M cannot." (This is also why a re-run of a leg under
its *own* instrument cannot move the cross-level claim, just as the parent essay notes "more of the
same probe cannot" move a negative: another R1-style observation adds another rhyme, never an
instrument-equalized cross-leg test.)

## Where the ladder meets the external literature

The ladder was abstracted from scratch off the OQ's ad-hoc reasoning, and naming an external
methodology literature that it informally reinvents is the honest next step — it tells us whether the
project re-derived a real bar or merely a private one. The relevant literature is the philosophy of
**common-cause inference**: when two events are correlated and neither causes the other, what licenses
inferring that they **share a cause** rather than merely coincide. That is, abstractly, the ladder's
own question — "the three legs *rhyme*; whether they *share a cause* is unshown." The canonical
statement is Reichenbach's Common Cause Principle, summarized in
[`source/hitchcock-redei-2020-reichenbach-cc`](../../base/sources/hitchcock-redei-2020-reichenbach-cc.md):
"Reichenbach's Common Cause Principle says that when such a probabilistic correlation between *A* and
*B* exists, this is because one of the following causal relations exists: *A* is a cause of *B*; *B* is
a cause of *A*; or *A* and *B* are both caused by a third factor, *C*."

The mapping is exact at one point and instructive at another. The **exact** point is the
**screening-off / independence** condition: Reichenbach's common cause must render its effects
"*conditionally independent*, given *C*" — "In Reichenbach's terminology, *C* screens *A* off from
*B*." The ladder's R1→R2 step is the behavioral form of precisely this requirement: under R1 the legs
agree only under each level's *own* instrument, so their agreement could flow through the shared method
rather than a shared cause; R2 equalizes the instrument so that, if the shape survives, the instrument
is removed as a non-independent route — the behavioral analogue of demanding that the witnesses screen
off, not co-vary through a common method. The ladder's standing caveat that shared priors are not
independent witnesses is the same caveat under another name.

The **instructive** point is that the external literature locates the licensing bar *more permissively*
than the ladder does, and this sharpens rather than refutes the deflationary default. Reichenbach's
principle is "significant because it posits a connection between causal structure and probabilistic
correlations, thus facilitating causal inference from observed correlations" — i.e. a stable
correlation, on the principle, already *motivates* positing a common cause, whereas the ladder
withholds the mechanism verdict until the instrument is equalized at R2. The gap is deliberate, not a
correction of Reichenbach: the ladder operates over n=3 commercial models whose instruments are
confounded with the levels they measure, so the project's higher bar is a behavioral conservatism that
*operationalizes* Reichenbach's independence condition rather than relaxing it. And the literature
carries its own deflationary residue: "A number of authors have proposed counterexamples to the Common
Cause Principle" — correlations that resist a clean screening-off common cause — which is the external
mirror of the ladder's insistence that a rhyme can dissolve at R2. So the external grounding *confirms*
the family of inference (independence is the real discriminator) while *relocating* the bar (a robust
correlation licenses more, sooner, in the unconstrained case than the project's behavioral one allows).
The ladder stands; it is now read as a deliberately conservative behavioral specialization of a
recognized common-cause inference, not a from-scratch private yardstick.

## Why a ladder and not a binary

A binary — "is this shape one mechanism, yes or no?" — would force the project to either over-assert
(call R1 a mechanism) or under-report (call R1 nothing). The graded ladder lets the project say
**exactly how strong its cross-level evidence is**: *"the gradience shape is at R1 — same direction
under each leg's native instrument, instrument-equalized test not yet run."* That sentence is both
informative and modest, and it is the one the OQ already writes in longhand. The ladder also makes an
**honest negative a first-class result**: a shape that looked unified at R1 and then **dissolves at
R2** (appears under each native instrument but vanishes when the instrument is equalized) is not a
failed session — it is a clean finding that the cross-level kinship *was* an artifact of phrasing or
instrument, exactly the deflationary outcome the OQ flags as the parsimonious default. Without graded
rungs, that dissolution has nowhere to be recorded except as the absence of a positive; with them, "the
shape dissolves at R2" is a reportable result that *confirms the ladder's diagnostic value* and
sharpens the project's map of which of its recurring phrases are real and which are prose. This is the
same reason the construction evidence-ladder grades rather than gates: a graded instrument turns a null
into information instead of a non-event.

## Scope and limits

- **Behavioral only.** Every cross-level shape the project can build is read off what the models *do*;
  the convergence ladder is an evidence standard for *behavioral* cross-level claims. The R-ceiling rung
  (representational / mechanistic / training-level identity) is named precisely because it is the rung
  this discipline **cannot** reach behaviorally — the standard includes its own ceiling honestly, rather
  than pretending the top rung is in play.

- **An evidence standard, not a claim about any shape.** This essay asserts nothing about whether the
  gradience shape — or any other cross-level shape — *is* one mechanism. It says only at which rung such
  an assertion would become licit. The motivating instance is cited at its own page's strength: the
  gradience legs "*rhyme*; whether they *share a cause* is unshown," and a "general constraint" reading
  is "licensed only if the shape **survives equalizing the instrument**." The essay's original
  contribution is the ladder; the empirical facts it stands on are all the OQ's, at the OQ's strength.

- **n=3 commercial models throughout.** Like every behavioral finding in the project, any cross-level
  claim is scoped to three 2026 commercial models, direction-of-shape only, never a coverage claim.
  R2/R3 evidence on three models is R2/R3 evidence *on those three models*.

- **Scoped to distributional competence.** The ladder governs cross-level claims about *distributional
  competence* specifically — the OQ's own framing, since the candidate "general constraint" is a
  property "a next-token-trained model carries across levels because of how that training stores
  structure." [`concept/distributional-meaning`](../../base/concepts/distributional-meaning.md)'s
  standing caution therefore binds every rung: distributional structure is "by itself … **silent on
  reference and on truth**," so even an R3-confirmed cross-level shape would be a statement *about
  distributional competence*, not about meaning proper. A unification high on this ladder buys a fact
  about how distributional competence is organized across levels — not a claim about reference, truth,
  or grounding.

- **The ladder fixes a yardstick, never a result.** Adopting this standard fixes *how strong the
  evidence must be* to assert a cross-level mechanism; it does not make any particular shape a
  mechanism. Per [`CLAUDE.md`](../../../CLAUDE.md) rule 6, ratifying a yardstick "fixes the *yardstick*,
  never the *result*."

## Revision triggers (read before citing)

- **(a) The shared-instrument probe lands and reaches R2/R3.** If the conjecture's test
  ([`conjecture/cross-level-gradience-aggregate-not-moment`](../conjectures/cross-level-gradience-aggregate-not-moment.md), via
  [`decisions/open/cross-level-shared-instrument-operationalization`](../../decisions/open/cross-level-shared-instrument-operationalization.md)) holds one commitment instrument
  fixed across levels and the gradience shape **survives** instrument-equalization (R2) or a break-test
  (R3), then for *that* shape a cross-level unification becomes assertable at the reached rung — and the
  "deflationary default" stance should be revised *for that shape specifically*, logged as a worked
  climb. The ladder is unaffected; one shape moves up it.

- **(b) A shape unified at R1 dissolves at R2.** If a cross-level shape that looked unified under each
  leg's native instrument **vanishes** when the instrument is equalized, that does not weaken this essay
  — it **confirms the ladder's value**, and should be logged as the worked example the "honest negative
  is first-class" section anticipates: a rhyme correctly *not* promoted to a mechanism.

- **(c) The project adopts a representational method.** If the project ever gains
  mechanistic-interpretability or representational access to its panel (retiring the R-ceiling
  out-of-reach), the ceiling claim must be revised: R-ceiling would become a rung the project can
  *climb*, not merely name, and the standard would extend to license a mechanistic-identity assertion it
  currently forbids. Nothing in-repo licenses that today; the panel is behavioral, n=3 commercial.

- **(d) A cross-instrument (not cross-level) family resemblance.** The ladder is written for cross-*level*
  claims, but the same underdetermination afflicts a shape claimed across *instruments at one level*
  (e.g. the recurring FC-vs-NLI divergence). If such a case is pressed toward a single-mechanism reading,
  this essay should be checked for whether its rungs transfer unchanged (they appear to: R1 = each
  instrument's native reading, R2 = a third instrument equalizing the contrast) or need a sibling. The
  present scope is cross-level; the cross-instrument extension is flagged, not asserted.

- **(e) A methodological source that locates the bar differently — *partially discharged 2026-06-22*.**
  The ladder began as a from-scratch abstraction of the OQ's ad-hoc disanalogy-and-discriminator
  reasoning. As of 2026-06-22 (session 81) this trigger is **partially discharged**: the essay now
  ingests [`source/hitchcock-redei-2020-reichenbach-cc`](../../base/sources/hitchcock-redei-2020-reichenbach-cc.md),
  the SEP entry on Reichenbach's Common Cause Principle, as the external methodology literature on
  mechanism-vs-coincidence inference (see "Where the ladder meets the external literature"). The source
  **confirmed** the ladder's discriminator — the R1→R2 step is the behavioral form of Reichenbach's
  **screening-off / conditional-independence** condition — and **relocated** the bar: the principle is
  *more permissive* than the ladder's deflationary default (a robust correlation already licenses
  positing a common cause), so the ladder is now read as a deliberately conservative behavioral
  specialization, not a refuted one. Residual weakness: one encyclopedia entry is not the whole
  literature (robustness analysis / triangulation / consilience proper — e.g. Keyser on robustness, not
  reachable open-access at ingest — remain un-ingested), and the ladder stays a *behavioral adaptation*
  of these ideas, not a derivation from them. If a future source locates the bar elsewhere again,
  re-derive.

## What this essay is not

- **Not a restatement of the open question.** The question — is the gradience shape one constraint or
  three rhyming facts? — lives at
  [`open-question/gradience-population-not-moment`](../open-questions/gradience-population-not-moment.md).
  This essay does not re-ask it; it states the general bar any answer to it (or to any like it) must
  clear.

- **Not the conjecture or its test.** The specific bet and the shared-instrument probe live at
  [`conjecture/cross-level-gradience-aggregate-not-moment`](../conjectures/cross-level-gradience-aggregate-not-moment.md) and its decision. This essay's standard
  *supports holding that conjecture to the right rung*; it does not make the bet.

- **Not a claim that any cross-level shape is (or is not) one mechanism.** It is an evidence standard.
  It asserts only at which rung such a claim would become licit, and cites the gradience instance at its
  own page's strength.

- **Not a new ladder for single-model meaning.** It is explicitly distinguished from the construction
  evidence-ladder ([`theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms`](../theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms.md)):
  that ladder grades meaning-vs-form in one model; this one grades one-mechanism-vs-rhyme across levels.
  Different questions.

- **Not a human comparison.** The standard governs the project's own cross-level claims about its own
  LLM panel. The `human-comparison` tag is carried because the **anchor-strength** point is part of the
  standard — a cross-level unification can be stated only at the *weakest common anchor strength* of its
  legs (the OQ notes one gradience leg is human-anchored-but-capped while two are
  `internal-contrast-only`, so "a unified statement would have to be stated at the weakest common
  strength," and the two internal-contrast-only legs "forbid any human comparison"). The essay imports
  no human result and asserts none.
