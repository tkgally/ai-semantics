## What this table is

The lexicon–grammar continuum ([`theory/lexicon-grammar-continuum-v2`](lexicon-grammar-continuum-v2.md))
runs one test across the whole word↔construction cline: *does the model track a meaning gradient that
beats the distributional shadow?* The [`essay/shadow-depth-cross-cuts-grain`](../essays/shadow-depth-cross-cuts-grain.md)
argues that this test does not sort phenomena by **grain** (word vs construction) but by
**shadow-depth** — how much of the phenomenon is already written into surface co-occurrence — and that
shadow-depth **cross-cuts** grain: each pole holds *both* a demonstrated shadow-**beater** and a
shadow-**saturated corner**.

This page turns that reading into a table. Each **beater** row is a probe on the project's panel with a
**named distributional control that strips the shadow** and a **measured residual over that control with a
95% CI**. Each **saturated** row is a phenomenon the essay places at the deep-shadow end by an argued
reading; its status is now, at both poles, **controlled-and-weakened** rather than a bare bet — the
matched controls the first edition marked as owed have since run, and neither corner came back a clean
uncontrolled reading (see the saturated section). The table's claim is the **structure**, at the essay's
weak evidential strength — not a measured shadow-depth scalar per phenomenon.

## The two row forms (read before the table)

The beater rows do not share one statistical form, and the table never silently equates them
(decision default, question 1):

- **Matched-material residual (form i):** a **within-model** contrast — the model's behavior on the
  phenomenon minus its behavior on a control built from the *same surface material*, so a
  distribution-only account predicts no gap. The comparative-correlative gap is the paradigm; the dative's
  within-item shift is control-**by construction** (the two phrasings are identical across an item's two
  discourse contexts, so length/order/position cancel); the **genitive's** within-frame animacy shift on
  the **nonce/rare-possessor firewall** is control-by-construction in a third way (a no-animacy reader with
  only per-lemma corpus statistics scores zero, because nonce possessors carry none).
- **Partialled correlation (form ii):** a **model–human** gradient correlation (Spearman ρ against a human
  gradient) that **survives partialling out a distributional covariate** — the model's own topic-similarity
  rating (lexical) or Zipf word-frequency (AANN). The residual is the *partial* correlation: rank
  information about the human gradient carried *over and above* the shadow.

Form (i) is a magnitude in the model's own behavior; form (ii) is agreement with humans net of a shadow.
They point the same way — a residual the distributional shadow does not explain — but they are **not
commensurable**, so **no cross-row comparison of magnitudes is made or implied.** The saturated section
carries a **third** measured form, kept strictly apart from both beater forms: an **internal-contrast
predictor comparison** (form iii) — *which corpus/lexicon statistic best rank-predicts recovery*, with no
beat-the-shadow residual and no human comparison at all. It is displayed as a marked reading of an ordering
axis, never as a beater row. The grammar-side BLiMP sweep adds a **fourth** measured form (iv) — a
within-panel difficulty gradient by structural depth plus a human-profile-alignment correlation — also kept
apart from the beater rows.

## The production-side alternation pair — dative (givenness) + genitive (animacy)

The two flagship probabilistic-grammar alternations of the Bresnan school now **both** have a row on this
table, and together they form the strongest single reading the beater side carries: **the panel tracks a
soft, human-attested production constraint in the human direction across *both* alternations, and the
constraint is a different kind in each.**

- The **dative** alternation (DOC vs prepositional dative) turns on a **discourse-structural** constraint —
  *givenness* (given-before-new). Promoted `claim` (3/3 at powered N; magnitudes attached).
- The **genitive** alternation (s-genitive vs of-genitive) turns on a **semantic/referential** constraint —
  *possessor animacy* (animate → s-genitive). Result-cited, now a **replicated pair** (s218 + rep2 s220,
  promotion earnable — a cross-session review is the successor); the animacy shift **survives the nonce
  firewall 3/3 on both disjoint runs** (a no-animacy surface reader scores zero), so the direction is not
  merely a possessor-propensity shadow.

That the same "tracks the soft constraint in the human direction" behavior appears for a **semantic** driver
(animacy) as well as a **discourse-structural** one (givenness) is the reason the genitive row belongs on
the beater side: it shows the dative pattern is **not specific to information structure**. The reading is
still fenced by the row's status — the genitive leg's gpt member was the marginal firewall leg at s218
(16/24 frames, sign-p 0.076) though the powered rep2 arm made it decisive (25/36, sign-p 0.014), and the
human anchor is **direction-only**. So the pair-reading is stated as: *both alternations show the
human-direction production shift; the dative's is thrice-observed and promoted, the genitive's is now a
twice-observed controlled pair whose promotion review is the next-session successor.* The parallel is real;
its two legs are not yet at equal evidential strength (the genitive still awaits its promotion review), and
the table shows that asymmetry rather than averaging over it.

## The lexical relation-recovery gradient's measured ordering axis (s193 — read at its exact strength)

One measured object sits under the lexical saturated corner, and it must be read at precisely the strength
its source page licenses — the do-not-over-read constraint is load-bearing.

When the antonymy bet lost (s186), the lexical pole kept a shadow-depth *gradient across relations* but lost
its named saturated corner: the project no longer knew which relation, if any, sat at the saturated end. The
fresh probe ([`result/lexical-relation-recovery-taxonomic-proxy-v1`](../results/lexical-relation-recovery-taxonomic-proxy-v1.md);
design ratified s193, C4 web corpus, fresh cues disjoint from the s186 lemmas, IS-A depth pre-registered
before recovery) did **not** re-run a beat-the-shadow control. It ran a **predictor comparison** — form
(iii) — asking which of two corpus/lexicon statistics rank-predicts across-relation recovery, on a shared
WordNet target that cancels in the head-to-head. Two things came back:

1. **The cue-strength–recovery decoupling is robust.** Raw contrastive-frame co-occurrence cue-strength
   again fails to predict recovery rank on a *different corpus family* (C4 web text, not s186's Simple
   English Wikipedia) and *fresh cues*: ρ_cue = **+0.14 / +0.09 / +0.09**, near-zero, 3/3
   ([`essay/cue-strength-recovery-decoupling`](../essays/cue-strength-recovery-decoupling.md) H1).
2. **Taxonomic hierarchical position out-predicts it, at the relation grain, on 2/3.** The pre-registered
   primary proxy — relation-mean IS-A path depth, predicted **negative** (more-superordinate/shallower cue
   sets recover better) — clears the pre-registered 0.20 margin on gpt and gemini (ρ_depth −0.37/−0.37) but
   not claude (−0.20; hypernymy being both deep *and* best-recovered caps that model's depth correlation).
   So relations whose cues sit higher in the taxonomy tend to be better recovered (H2 fired-**for**, 2/3).

Three honest limits travel with this row, and none may be dropped when it is cited:

- **It is `internal-contrast-only` and within-distributional.** IS-A depth is itself a form-internal
  structural statistic (WordNet's taxonomy has a dense corpus footprint). H2 winning means *a different
  form-internal statistic out-ranks contrastive-frame co-occurrence* — **never** that recovery escapes
  distribution. Reference and grounding are untouched.
- **Between-relation, not within-cue.** A cue's *own* depth does not predict its *own* recovery
  (item-level ρ ≈ +0.06 / +0.01 / +0.04, near-zero). The depth story is about **relation categories**, not
  individual words — a real limit on how far the ordering generalizes.
- **The Hearst-frame arm lost.** The corpus definitional-frame proxy correlated in the sign *opposite* its
  prediction (ρ_hearst −0.54/−0.49/−0.49), so the surviving taxonomic answer is *structural hierarchy*, not
  *corpus genus-naming frequency*.

This is therefore **not** a beater row (no residual over a matched control was measured), **not** a promoted
claim (a single 2/3 run; the adjective-antonymy replication or a within-family ladder would be owed first),
and **not** a human comparison. It is the measured *ordering axis* of a saturated-side gradient — the
strongest state this table has reached on the deep side at the lexical pole, and still a reading rather than
a claim.

## The grammar-side depth axis, measured directly across 40 paradigms (s205 BLiMP)

The beater/saturated columns order the **grammatical pole** by shadow-depth using two arranged *endpoints* —
the comparative-correlative beater (shallow) and the presupposition corner (deep). The s205 BLiMP
forced-choice sweep ([`result/blimp-forced-choice-sweep-v1`](../results/blimp-forced-choice-sweep-v1.md);
design ratified s205, 40 on-axis paradigms selected **human-agreement-blind** by a whole-category structural
rule, 30 pairs × both 2AFC orders × 3 models, 7,200 calls, verifier-REPRODUCED) fills that axis in *between*
the two poles with a direct 40-paradigm measurement. It carries **two readings that must be read at their
exact stated strength**, and it is a **fourth measured form (iv)** — a within-panel difficulty gradient by
structural depth, plus a human-profile-alignment correlation — kept **strictly apart** from the beater rows
