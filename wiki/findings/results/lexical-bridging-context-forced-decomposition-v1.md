# Result: forced-decomposition gpt leg of the lexical bridging-context channel check

> **Status: proposed (2026-06-22, session 82).** An **uptake-INDUCING** follow-up to
> [`result/lexical-bridging-context-working-surface-v1`](lexical-bridging-context-working-surface-v1.md),
> where **gpt declined** the *offered* working surface (≥85% bare one-token answers), leaving
> its channel leg of the load-bearing ungraded-COMMITMENT posture untested. This **forces**
> uptake with a mandatory, construction-neutral, answer-blind **3-step decomposition** before
> the `FINAL:` answer — the **single manipulated variable** is *offered → forced* (cf.
> [`result/scivetti-let-alone-forced-decomposition-v1`](scivetti-let-alone-forced-decomposition-v1.md)).
> **gpt-only** (claude and gemini already took up the offered surface). Built via the audited
> v1→offered→forced chain (task text **byte-identical to v1**; only the forcing clause differs;
> `verify_format_only.py`); instrument frozen, sha256 `dceafa9d…`. **No new operationalization
> decision owed** — an instrument extension under the resolved lexical gates
> ([`decisions/resolved/lexical-bridging-context-operationalization`](../../decisions/resolved/lexical-bridging-context-operationalization.md),
> [`…-anchor`](../../decisions/resolved/lexical-bridging-context-anchor.md)), exactly as the
> Scivetti forced-decomposition was. Independent fresh-agent **pre-run critic GO** with six
> binding disclosure/process conditions (none relaxes the instrument; all honored below).
> **352 calls (4 framings × 88 items), $0.24002 billed, 0 missing cost, 100% FINAL-tag parse,
> 0 errors** (+ a $0.01057 16-call calibration).

## Lead with the cap (binding, read first)

Every human comparison below is **about usage-similarity intermediacy ONLY** (anchor condition
6, inherited from v1). A DWUG mid-scale (DURel 2–3, ≥3-rater) "bridging" pair is a **human-rated
usage-similarity midpoint**, **not** a certified within-sense bridge. This is **behavioral, not
representational**, and small, lemma-clustered N (**24 bridging pairs / 17 lemmas**). "Commitment"
means the confidence/decline posture of a behavioral verdict, never a window into representation.

## Headline

**Forcing gpt to actually externalize the judgment INDUCED UPTAKE decisively (median ~10 → ~110
completion tokens; 85–99% bare → 0% bare; 100% worked), and with the channel now genuinely
exercised gpt's lexical posture comes out: graded SCALE intact, commitment MIXED/WEAK — a weak,
underpowered softening, NOT the clean channel-controlled null.**

Read against the frozen v1 per-axis rule, gpt under forced decomposition:

- **Position (the graded SCALE) — REPLICATES, robustly.** Bridging sits CI-strict between the
  clear classes and inside the frozen [40,60] band (clear-diff 38.9 / **bridging 56.9** /
  clear-same 82.5). The graded scale is **channel-robust** for gpt across all three channels
  (v1 narrow, offered, forced).
- **Commitment — MIXED/WEAK (B and C disagree), underpowered.** The two commitment instruments
  give *different* verdicts on whether the posture cracked, and **neither crack is robust**:
  self-reported **confidence** dips only *pointwise* on bridging (91.5 vs clear-same 92.7 /
  clear-diff 95.5) with a CI that **overlaps** clear-same — **no robust crack** (the v1-claude
  pattern); categorical **decline** ticks up to **8.3% (2/24)** vs ~3% on the clear classes —
  a *weak, thin* elevation, partly inside a **general** rise of decline under the forced surface
  (clears went 0% → ~3%).

So gpt's ungraded-commitment null **does not cleanly survive a genuinely-used channel** the way
**gemini's did** (channel-controlled), but it **does not cleanly flip to graded commitment**
either: it **weakly softens**, and the signal is too thin to call. This is the **third** model's
leg of the channel check, and it lands closest to **claude's offered-surface MIXED/WEAK** —
except claude's confidence cracked *CI-strict* while its decline held, whereas gpt's confidence
barely moves while its decline weakly rises. Different instruments soften for different models;
none reaches a clean graded-commitment positive.

gpt now genuinely exercises the surface (median ~110 tokens, 0% bare, 100% ≥40 pre-FINAL chars,

**visible** channel, effort held constant). It externalizes in **prose** about as often as in
"1./2./3." numbered form (≈73–76% numbered), so the uptake gate is content volume, not format.
gpt's residual posture is now read off a channel it **actually used** — the precondition a clean
channel test requires.

### Verdict (frozen v1 rule, gpt)

- **Position graded:** YES (CI-strict, in band).
- **Commitment:** **MIXED/WEAK.** Confidence shows **no robust crack** (pointwise lower, CI
  overlaps); decline shows a **weak elevation** (8.3%, 2/24). B and C **disagree** on whether the
  commitment cracked → per the frozen modification 3 this is a **MIXED/WEAK** result, reported as
  such — **not** the clean null and **not** a clean graded-commitment positive. Both signals are
  **underpowered** at this N.

## Q3 context control (bound, weak — disclosed)

On bridging items the model's relatedness rating tracks its own topic rating to a degree
(bridging `b_rel` 56.9 vs `topic` 27.5; the topic control separates the clear classes:
clear-diff topic 26.6 / clear-same 57.1), qualifying the **position** reading exactly as v1/ws;
it bears little on the commitment finding.

## What this resolves (and its honest limits)

- **The three-model lexical channel check is now COMPLETE.** gemini (offered, taken up):
  commitment null **survived** (channel-controlled). claude (offered, taken up): **MIXED/WEAK** —
  confidence cracked CI-strict, decline held. gpt (forced, **now** taken up): **MIXED/WEAK** —
  confidence no robust crack, decline weakly elevated. So v1's clean **3/3** ungraded-commitment
  null does **not** cleanly replicate under genuinely-used channels: it is **channel-controlled
  for one model (gemini)** and **weakly softens for two (claude, gpt)**, on *different*
  instruments. The **graded SCALE** (position), by contrast, replicates for all three across all
  channels.
- **For [`claim/lexical-graded-scale-ungraded-commitment`](../claims/lexical-graded-scale-ungraded-commitment.md):**
  the claim's "largely channel-controlled" reading is **sharpened, not overturned** — the
  position/graded-scale half is channel-robust; the ungraded-commitment half is **model-specific
  and uptake-sensitive**, clean only for gemini, with claude and gpt showing weak (non-robust)
  softening once they genuinely use the surface. The gpt leg the working-surface result flagged
  "untested" is now **tested** → weak softening.
- **For [`open-question/gradience-population-not-moment`](../open-questions/gradience-population-not-moment.md):**
  the lexical "discrete-moment" leg is **firmer on position** but **more textured on commitment**
  — under genuine uptake the moment-pole is not uniformly discrete across models; it softens
  weakly for two of three. This is exactly the kind of model- and instrument-specificity the
  newly-resolved cross-level gate's condition **C4** (a confidence shift unaccompanied by a
  decline shift is a self-report effect, not graded-moment evidence) and **C1** (categorical
  decline load-bearing) were written to handle.

## What this does and does NOT license

**Does license:** a project-owned, revisable statement that, with uptake **forced** (gpt now
works the steps 88/88, median ~110 tokens, 0% bare), gpt's lexical bridging posture is **graded
in position** but shows a **weak, underpowered softening of commitment** under a genuinely-used
working surface — pointwise-lower confidence (not CI-strict) and a thin decline elevation (2/24)
— landing as **MIXED/WEAK**, neither gemini's clean channel-controlled null nor a clean positive.

**Does NOT license:**
- **"gpt has graded commitment."** The softening is **non-robust** (confidence CI overlaps;
  decline is 2/24 amid a general rise) and is observed **under a forced, structured scaffold**
  that, per [`result/scivetti-let-alone-forced-decomposition-v1`](scivetti-let-alone-forced-decomposition-v1.md)'s
  own Limits, **conflates uptake with structure**: instructing the model to describe each use
  separately could itself scaffold a softer posture. A **free-form minimum-length** uptake-forcer
  (which isolates uptake from structure) is the cleaner next axis. So this is **graded-under-a-
  forced-structured-scaffold**, never an unscoped trait.
- **A clean cross-model comparison.** gpt ran under the **forced** surface while claude/gemini
  ran under the **offered** surface (their legs already answered there), so gpt-vs-others is
  **not** apples-to-apples. The force of this result is the **within-gpt forced-vs-offered/-v1
  contrast** plus the clear-class precondition, not a panel ranking.
- **A sense-co-presence claim.** Usage-similarity intermediacy only (the cap).
- **A tight magnitude.** n=24 bridging / 17 lemmas; CIs wide; the decline elevation is 2 items.

## Scope and limits (lead with these)

- **Behavioral, not representational; usage-similarity, not sense co-presence** (v1 caps).
- **Small, lemma-clustered N** (24 bridging / 17 lemmas); single model (gpt-5.4-mini), single
  forced-decomposition design, single date; direction-of-effect with wide CIs; no coverage claim.
- **Uptake conflated with structure** (the structured-scaffold caveat above); a free-form
  min-length forcer is the cleaner follow-up.
- **gpt-only; not a clean cross-model comparison** (forced vs others-offered).
- **Decline elevation is thin** (2/24) and partly a general rise under the forced surface.
