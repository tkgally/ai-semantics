## Lead with the cap (binding, read first)

This is a **within-model noise-floor measurement**, the licit same-instrument re-run
([`essay/undischargeable-negative`](../essays/undischargeable-negative.md): re-running to *measure
the floor* is licit; to "firm a null" is not). It makes **NO new human-comparison claim** beyond
pinning the magnitude of the already-reported, descriptive gpt leg. The DWUG anchor
([`resource/dwug-usage-graphs`](../../base/resources/dwug-usage-graphs.md)) and the
**usage-similarity-only** cap are inherited from
[`result/lexical-bridging-context-forced-decomposition-v1`](lexical-bridging-context-forced-decomposition-v1.md):
a DWUG mid-scale "bridging" pair is a **human-rated usage-similarity midpoint**, not a certified
within-sense bridge. **Behavioral, not representational**; "commitment" means the confidence/decline
posture of a behavioral verdict. Small, lemma-clustered N (**24 bridging / 17 lemmas**); single model
(gpt-5.4-mini); single forced-decomposition design; single date; direction-of-effect only.

## Headline

**The disagreement RESOLVES to the channel-controlled NULL (s82b).** Across five byte-identical
temp-0 runs, gpt's **bridging** decline sits at the jitter floor — **1–2 of 24 per run (4.2 %–8.3 %),
de-noised majority-vote 1/24 (4.2 %)** — and is **not preferentially elevated**: the **clear-same**
class actually declines **more** (per-run 6.9 %–17.2 %; de-noised majority-vote **3/29 = 10.3 %**).
And the **confidence** axis shows **no robust crack** (de-noised bridging − clear-same difference CI
**[−4.64, +1.68]**, includes 0). So **both** commitment instruments de-noise to "no bridging-preferential
crack" — gpt's ungraded-commitment null is **channel-controlled too, like gemini's**. The session-82
**MIXED/WEAK** reading (bridging 8.3 % over clears ~3 %) was reading a **high jitter draw**: the 8.3 %
recurs here as the *top* of the bridging band, not an elevation over the clear classes.

| bridging decline | 8.3 % (2/24) | 0.0 % (0/24) | per-run {4.2, 8.3, 8.3, 4.2, 8.3}; **majority 1/24 = 4.2 %** | jitter floor, 1–2/24 |

bootstrap CI **[0.0 %, 13.0 %]** does not exceed the clear-class de-noised decline of 10.3 %); the
confidence CI includes 0. The s82 2/24 elevation was run-to-run temp-0 jitter; the s82b null is the
de-noised truth.

## The decline axis, de-noised (the disputed axis)

Per-run `%UNCLEAR` by class (5 byte-identical temp-0 draws):

| class (n) | run1 | run2 | run3 | run4 | run5 | range | **de-noised majority decline** |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| bridging (24) | 4.2 | 8.3 | 8.3 | 4.2 | 8.3 | **4.1** | **1/24 = 4.2 %** |
| clear-same (29) | 10.3 | 6.9 | 10.3 | 10.3 | 17.2 | **10.3** | **3/29 = 10.3 %** |
| clear-different (35) | 2.9 | 2.9 | 5.7 | 2.9 | 0.0 | 5.7 | low |

- **Bridging is the *least*-declined of the two ambiguous-adjacent classes, not the most.** The
  de-noised bridging decline (1/24) is **below** the clear-same decline (3/29). Whatever low-level
  decline gpt shows under the forced surface is **not bridging-preferential** — it is, if anything,
  slightly concentrated on **clear-same** this session.
- **Only one bridging item is persistently declined** (de-noised majority-UNCLEAR): `contemplation`
  (`lx-3-111`, UNCLEAR in 3/5 runs); its sibling `contemplation` (`lx-3-112`) is UNCLEAR 2/5, and
  `bar`/`heel`/`lane` once each. **Churn 5/24 (20.8 %)**; mean pairwise UNCLEAR-label agreement
  **0.90** over the C(5,2)=10 run-pairs — i.e. the bridging decline label is *mostly* stable but
  jitters on ~1/5 of items, which is exactly the 1-vs-2/24 swing that produced the s82↔s82b gap.
- **The jitter band brackets both prior point estimates' neighbourhood.** The five bridging draws
  are {1,2,2,1,2}/24; s82's **8.3 % (2/24) lands inside the band** (it is the *high* draw, not an
  elevation). s82b's exact **0/24 did not recur** (the low draw here is 1/24), so s82b caught a low
  draw — but its *verdict* (no bridging-preferential decline) is the de-noised truth, because
  bridging (1/24) sits below clear-same (3/29). The disagreement was **run-to-run temp-0 jitter** in
  the 1–2/24 band ([`essay/point-estimate-is-a-draw`](../essays/point-estimate-is-a-draw.md)).

## A disclosed cross-session shift (lead with it): clear-same decline is higher this session

The **clear-same** decline this session (per-run 6.9 %–17.2 %; de-noised 10.3 %) is materially higher
than session 82's clear-same figure (**3.4 %, 1/29**). This is a **session-to-session shift**, not a
within-this-run effect, and it is exactly the kind of cross-session instability
[`essay/point-estimate-is-a-draw`](../essays/point-estimate-is-a-draw.md) flags as a **lower bound**
on true run-to-run jitter (a single session's spread understates it). Three honest consequences:

1. It **reinforces** the NULL reading: gpt's forced-surface decline is a **general low-level
   phenomenon** (a few items across all classes), **not** a bridging-specific crack — and this
   session it falls *more* on clear-same than on bridging.
2. The **clear-class precondition still passes every run** (clear-same and clear-different decline
   both **< 20 %** on all five runs; mean confidence **91.4–96.3 > 70**), so the instrument read
   cleanly and the bridging comparison is interpretable. (Run 5's clear-same 17.2 % is the closest
   approach to the 20 % bar, disclosed.)
3. It is a **caution against over-reading any single decline cell** — including this run's. The
   right object is the **band and the cross-class comparison**, not a third-decimal rate.

## What this does and does NOT license

**Does license:** a project-owned, revisable measurement statement that, on this forced-decomposition
lexical instrument at temperature 0, gpt's **bridging decline de-noises to the jitter floor (1/24
majority over 5 runs), is not elevated over the clear classes, and is accompanied by no robust
confidence crack** — so the session-82/82b disagreement resolves to the **channel-controlled NULL**,
and gpt joins gemini in showing no clean commitment crack on the bridging midpoint (de-noised).

**Does NOT license:**
- **"gpt cannot decline" / "gpt never hedges."** It declines a few items per run across all classes;
  the claim is only that the decline is **not bridging-preferential** and **not robust**.
- **A precise clear-same decline rate.** The 10.3 % is this session's de-noised figure; the
  cross-session shift from 3.4 % shows it is itself a draw — report the **band**, not the point.
- **A clean cross-model comparison.** gpt ran under the **forced** surface; claude/gemini answered
  under the **offered** surface in the parent result. The force here is the **within-gpt** de-noised
  measurement.
- **A sense-co-presence or representational claim.** Usage-similarity-capped, behavioral.
- **A transferable jitter constant.** This is gpt's bridging-decline jitter on *this* instrument.
