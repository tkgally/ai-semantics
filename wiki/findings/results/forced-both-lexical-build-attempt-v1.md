## Headline

## What was attempted, gate question by question

| gate question | what it asks | status this attempt |
|---|---|---|
| **Q1-i** structural forced-both frame | a zeugma/co-predication frame yoking one token to two complements that each demand a different unrelated sense | **BUILDABLE** — 8 author-built frames (see below) |
| **Q2-i** forced-single-application instrument | a downstream task only one reading licenses, so "it means both" can't complete it | **BUILDABLE** — one per item; *not* the blocker |
| **Q3** frozen reading rule | response-class → (A)-commit / (B)-decline map, `answer-both` its own class, higher bar on commit, C1/C3/C4 | **INHERITABLE** verbatim from the sibling gates; not the blocker |
| **Q1-ii** independent balance check | certify, *independent of model output*, that neither sense dominates (a lean suppresses `UNCLEAR` → spurious commit/A) | **NOT SATISFIABLE** — the wall |

**Q1-i is buildable.** Eight co-predication frames over homonyms, each yoking one token to two
complements that demand a different *unrelated* sense — e.g. *"The bank was slick with mud and badly
short on cash"* (`slick with mud` selects river-edge, `short on cash` selects finance; a single-sense
reading makes one complement anomalous). The structural criterion is checkable before any model call.

**Q2-i is buildable.** Each item carries a task that *only one reading licenses* — e.g. *"treat the
bank as a physical place: can a person walk along its edge?"* A model treating the item as
both-at-once must either refuse (→ decline (B)) or force one reading and lose the other's content
(→ commit (A)); "it means both" cannot complete the task. This is the strongest part of the gate and
is **not** the blocker.

## Why Q1-ii fails — the load-bearing finding

The gate requires certifying, *independent of model output*, that neither sense **dominates**, because
a lean **suppresses** `UNCLEAR` and so an under-certified "pun" that is really a leaning homonym
"would bias toward commit and spuriously support (A)"
([`result/within-lexical-scalar-vs-disjunctive-v1`](within-lexical-scalar-vs-disjunctive-v1.md),
caveat 2). Two routes to that certification exist, and **both are closed under autonomy**:

1. **An independent human annotator on the constructed sentence.** Closed: the project takes **no
   human subjects** ([`CLAUDE.md`](../../../CLAUDE.md) rule 4); all human bearing comes from
   *existing* resources, none of which rate these author-constructed sentences.

2. **A corpus/frequency check that the two senses are comparably available.** The only reachable
   corpus signal that decomposes word frequency **by sense** is the SemCor sense-tagged corpus via
   WordNet lemma tag counts ([`resource/wordnet-sense-inventory`](../../base/resources/wordnet-sense-inventory.md)).
   ([`resource/subtlex-us-frequency`](../../base/resources/subtlex-us-frequency.md) is word-level
   only — it "aggregates over senses and PoS"; [`resource/dwug-usage-graphs`](../../base/resources/dwug-usage-graphs.md)
   "does not tag pairs as polysemy vs. homonymy" and gives no per-sense frequency.) Applied to the
   eight candidates ([`balance_check.py`](../../../experiments/designs/forced-both-lexical-v1/balance_check.py)):

   | word | sense-A tags | sense-B tags | balance (min/total) | powered (≥5 each)? |
   |------|---:|---:|---:|:--:|
   | bank | 25 | 20 | 0.444 | **yes** |
   | club | 3 | 9 | 0.250 | no |
   | pen | 1 | 8 | 0.111 | no |
   | file | 17 | 1 | 0.056 | no |
   | pitch | 0 | 3 | 0.000 | no |
   | seal | 1 | 3 | 0.250 | no |
   | bark | 1 | 4 | 0.200 | no |
   | spring | 4 | 25 | 0.138 | no |

   **Only 1/8 (bank)** has ≥5 SemCor tags on *both* senses; the rest are statistically powerless
   (SemCor is a ~200k-word corpus and most homonym senses are tagged 0–4 times). This is a real
   regularity, not bad luck: WordNet orders senses by frequency, and homonyms characteristically
   have a **dominant** sense, so a frequency-balanced homonym is the rare exception — and the corpus
   has too few tags to certify even those.

**The deeper, structural defect (holds even for `bank`).** A SemCor balance ratio measures
**general-usage** sense balance across the corpus. It does **not** measure the balance of the
*specific constructed co-predication sentence*. A co-predication frame forces both senses precisely
by yoking **each sense to its own complement**, so the in-item balance is set by the *relative pull
of the two complements* (`slick with mud` vs `short on cash`) — a property of the constructed item
no general-frequency statistic can see. The only instruments that could read the constructed item's
balance directly are (i) a human annotator on that sentence (closed) or (ii) the model's own response
(forbidden — the circular operationalization-tuning failure the gate exists to prevent). So **even a
perfectly frequency-balanced homonym leaves Q1-ii's in-item balance uncertified.**

**Net.** At most **N=1** (bank) clears even the wrong (general-frequency) proxy, and *no* candidate's
constructed-sentence balance can be certified without a human annotator (unavailable) or model output
(forbidden). N=1 is also far too thin to read a per-model pattern. All three clauses of the essay's
trigger (c) are met — the item **cannot be certified forced-both rather than a leaning homonym**
without leaning on model output, **and** N is too thin — so no verdict on (A) vs (B) is licensed and
the fork **stays at R1**.

## Independent adversarial review (VERDICT-SOUND)

A fresh independent reviewer (not the builder) read the gate, the essay, the sibling result, the
build artifacts, and the four resource pages, and checked for any reachable independent balance
instrument the build missed. Verdict **VERDICT-SOUND**:

- **Q1-ii genuinely not constructible.** No reachable in-item balance instrument was missed: SUBTLEX
  is word-level; DWUG/WiC/Usim/CoSimLex rate pre-existing corpus usages or word pairs, never an
  author-constructed co-predication sentence; `wanted.md` and the resources index contain **no**
  pun/zeugma/sense-co-activation/sense-balance resource. The SemCor counts were reproduced and are
  genuine.
- **Structural argument sound** — the in-item-balance point makes the failure *principled* (holds at
  any N), not an N-too-thin accident.
- **Not result-motivated and not a lazy dodge** — trigger (c) makes a spurious (A) win *harder*; no
  band was relaxed; Q1-i and Q2-i were actually built and shown buildable; the blocker is specifically
  Q1-ii's independence requirement.
- **Quote integrity PASS** — the DWUG and SUBTLEX characterizations verify against the resource pages.

The reviewer flagged one synset-id NIT (the `club` cudgel sense is `club.n.03`, not the team sense),
which was corrected before freeze — sharpening the table to 1/8 powered.

## What would lift it off R1 (for a later session)

The fork becomes testable only if the Q1-ii independence wall is removed — which requires a **new
resource**, separately and cross-session ratified, that certifies **sense co-activation / in-item
balance** for forced-both items **without** model output:

- a released corpus of human-annotated puns/zeugmas with per-item balance or co-activation labels
  (none in-repo or on `wanted.md` today); **or**
- a sense-frequency resource dense enough that *general*-balance is defensible as a proxy **and** a
  separate argument that general balance transfers to the constructed frame (the structural defect
  above suggests this transfer is itself the hard part). Such a resource would *also* discharge the
  gate's Q4 anchor (lifting the class off `internal-contrast-only`), which is why the matched-ambiguity
  sibling held its co-activation anchor open. Scout reachability before committing time; do **not**
  relax the balance band to force a runnable item.
