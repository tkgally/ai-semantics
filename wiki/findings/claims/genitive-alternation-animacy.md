## Statement

Across **two** controlled runs on **disjoint item sets** (v1, session 218, 36 typical + 24 nonce
frames; rep2, session 220, 36 typical + 36 nonce frames, every possessor lemma and every nonce string
certified disjoint from v1) on the ratified 3-family panel (`claude-sonnet-4.6`, `gpt-5.4-mini`,
`gemini-3.5-flash`), **the panel shifts its s-genitive vs of-genitive production preference in the
human-attested possessor-animacy direction**: an **animate** possessor raises s-genitive preference
(*the judge's decision* over *the decision of the judge*) relative to an **inanimate** one, with
possessor length, final sibilancy, and definiteness held fixed within each frame by construction — the
Dubois et al. (2023) native-speaker acceptability direction. The finding-bearing within-frame shift
`shift = mean(s-pref | animate) − mean(s-pref | inanimate)` clears its bootstrap lower bound in every
model on both runs (typical arm 3/3 each run), and — the load-bearing control — **the shift survives
the nonce/rare-possessor firewall 3/3 on both runs**: on rare/nonce possessors that carry **no
per-lemma corpus genitive statistic**, with animacy conveyed only by a gloss and nonce string-forms
balanced so orthography cannot telegraph animacy, a no-animacy per-lemma reader scores **zero by
construction**, yet the animacy shift persists. So the animate→s-genitive effect is **not merely a
distributional shadow** of the possessor's marginal s-genitive propensity; the panel generalizes from
the animacy *category*.

The claim is promoted **direction-only** and is **scoped and qualified**, never panel-uniform:

- **Direction is human-anchored; magnitude is within-model (now attached).** The human anchor is a
  native-speaker acceptability **direction/sign** only, so the claim's **human comparison** asserts the
  **sign** of the animacy effect and its shortcut-immunity, **not** any human comparison of sizes.
  Within-model, the effect now **carries a magnitude**: the s222 powered pooled arm (108 typical
  frames) gives an animate−inanimate s-preference shift of **+0.145 / +0.169 / +0.139** per model, all
  bootstrap CI-LB>0, the 36 fresh-blind frames independently clearing CI-LB>0 3/3 (see the Magnitude
  section). So the claim now asserts the **sign (human-anchored) AND a within-model magnitude**, but
  **not** a human comparison of sizes.
- **The per-model effect sizes decorrelate ~3.6–4× and gpt is the weakest firewall leg.** The
  nonce-arm effect spans gemini ≫ claude ≫ gpt on both runs (rep2 +0.279 ≫ +0.151 ≫ +0.078). gpt's
  nonce leg was **marginal at v1** (16/24 frames, one-sided sign-p 0.076) and **decisive at rep2** once
  the nonce arm was powered 24→36 frames (**25/36, sign-p 0.014**) — but it remains the **smallest**
  leg. Per [`essay/concordant-verdict-hides-spread`](../essays/concordant-verdict-hides-spread.md),
  this licenses a *direction/existence* statement but **not** a panel *uniformity* or *magnitude*
  statement; the spread is displayed, never averaged into a "the panel tracks animacy" scalar.
- **The gradient is a sharp animate/non-animate binary, not a smooth Zaenen ramp.** Graded monotone
  (animate > collective > inanimate) passes 3/3 on both runs but only **nominally**: the *collective*
  mid-level patterns **with the inanimate** (~0.005–0.03 above it), not midway, on both runs. So the
  panel draws a sharp animate/non-animate line and treats groups-of-humans much like objects; the claim
  states the **binary**, not the five-level scale.

## Human-comparison leg (direction only — magnitude is NOT anchored)

The claim's human-comparison force is on the **direction of the effect**, not its size. The anchor is
[`resource/genitive-animacy-human-anchor`](../../base/resources/genitive-animacy-human-anchor.md) —
the native-speaker acceptability **direction** from
[`source/dubois-2023-genitive-animacy`](../../base/sources/dubois-2023-genitive-animacy.md) (Dubois,
Grafmiller, Paquot & Szmrecsanyi 2023, *Language and Cognition*, 25 native speakers, CC BY 4.0),
verbatim: *"possessor animacy, which captures the tendency for animate possessors to favour the
s-genitive and inanimate possessors to prefer the of-genitive"* and *"animacy is normally the strongest
constraint of the genitive alternation (Rosenbach, 2014)"*. The models' preference shifts in that human
direction. Per the resource's own caution the anchor is a graded *acceptability rating* (a
separate-rating task, midpoint 50), whereas the probe indicator is a *forced-choice split* — so it
licenses the effect's **direction/sign**, **not** a per-item magnitude match and **not** a per-item
human gradient (the per-item native-speaker ratings and the Trinity Lancaster Corpus rows are **not**
shown to be openly licensed; adopting one is a queued scout). So the human-comparison statement is
exactly: *the models' production preference shifts in the direction the native-speaker acceptability
ratings attest.* The claim is therefore **not** `internal-contrast-only`: it does lean, in part, on the
human animacy **direction**, and states that leg at exactly its (direction-only, acceptability-sign)
strength. The **nonce-firewall arm is an internal within-model shortcut control**, not a second human
comparison.

## The controls that earn the rigor

- **The nonce firewall (load-bearing).** On rare/nonce possessors the model has *no per-lemma corpus
  genitive statistic* to read off, animacy is carried only by a gloss (*"a small wild animal called a
  X"* vs *"a hard grey mineral called a X"*), and the nonce string-forms are balanced so the animate-
  and inanimate-slot string multisets are identical — a no-animacy per-lemma reader scores **zero by
  construction**. The shift persists 3/3 on **both** disjoint runs. This is a construction-level
  shortcut elimination (the direct analog of the dative's immune-by-construction design), not a fragile
  statistical adjustment.
- **The frequency covariate (near-vacuous — a fence, not corroboration).** A frozen UD-EWT
  possessor-lemma marginal-propensity covariate is the secondary control; on both runs it has
  essentially **no predictive validity** (R² 0.002–0.038 v1; 0.000–0.013 rep2; animate mean propensity
  slightly *below* inanimate), because UD-EWT is small and per-lemma genitive counts are sparse. So the
  covariate-adjusted-intercept leg is **near-vacuous** and is **not** independent corroboration — the
  real firewall is the nonce arm. This is fence (a), carried verbatim.

## Fences the claim carries verbatim (a–i)

A future reader must carry all nine; none is erased by promotion:

- **(a) Covariate vacuity → CONFIRM rests on the nonce firewall.** The UD-EWT propensity covariate is
  near-vacuous on both runs (R² 0.002–0.038 / 0.000–0.013), so the covariate leg is not independent
  corroboration; the effect's not-a-shadow force rests entirely on the nonce firewall.
- **(b) gpt is the weakest firewall leg.** Decisive on the powered rep2 arm (25/36, p 0.014) but still
  the smallest (nonce +0.078 vs gemini +0.279); ~3.6–4× cross-model decorrelation. Displayed, never
  averaged (the concordant-verdict-hides-spread discipline). Do **not** call gpt "marginal" post-rep2;
  **do** call it the weakest leg.
- **(c) The gradient is a sharp animate/non-animate binary**, not a smooth Zaenen five-level ramp: the
  collective mid-level patterns with the inanimate on both runs, not midway.
- **(d) Direction-only human anchor.** No per-item human gradient, no human-level genitive-competence
  claim.
- **(e) R5 nonce-firewall fence.** The nonce firewall is **corroboratory, not a causal proof the
  distributional shadow is defeated**: the animal-vs-mineral gloss that carries animacy also covaries
  with concreteness / taxonomic class / plausibility, so residual confounding of the gloss semantics
  with animacy cannot be fully excluded. The claim is directional, robust to the strongest shadow
  control available under pure autonomy — nothing stronger.
- **(f) Controlled only to the marginal possessor propensity, not full construction frequency.** The
  effect is not claimed to be construction-frequency-controlled beyond the possessor's marginal
  s-genitive propensity (the result pages' own third "May NOT" fence).
- **(g) Behavioral / stated-preference only.** The indicator is a graded stated-production preference
  on a working surface (100 points distributed by naturalness), logprob-/surprisal-free (none available
  under pure autonomy); it is silent on representations and on grounding, and is **not** a per-item
  human acceptability rating.
- **(h) The replication is fresh-item but SAME-DATE (both runs 2026-07-13), same model versions,
  single-lab, n=3 shared-prior decoders.** There is **no temporal or version decorrelation** — weaker
  on that axis than the dative (v1/v2 landed on different dates). Three decoders converging is weak
  evidence on its own; the human *direction* anchor is what gives the result independent bearing.
- **(i) Within-model magnitude is ATTACHED (s222), via a POOLED arm — a fully-fresh powered arm is a
  possible further strengthening, not owed.** The powered re-run landed
  ([`result/genitive-alternation-animacy-mag`](../results/genitive-alternation-animacy-mag.md)): a
  third disjoint typical arm (36 fresh-blind frames) pooled with s218+rep2 → **108 frames** attaches a
  within-model magnitude of **+0.145 / +0.169 / +0.139** per model, all CI-LB>0, the fresh-36 blind arm
  clearing CI-LB>0 3/3. The pooled interval is a magnitude **update conditional on the established
  direction** — **tighter by construction** because 72/108 frames are reused (the data that set the
  direction); the fresh-36 column is the genuine blind check. A single *fully-fresh* ~100–150-frame
  arm would give a non-conditional interval but is **not** owed (the fresh-36 arm already clears its
  own CI-LB decisively). The magnitude is a **within-model** contrast, **not** a human comparison of
  sizes (fence d).

## Where it sits

The genitive extends the dative finding — "the panel tracks the soft constraint in the human
direction" — from a **discourse-structural** constraint (givenness) to a **semantic/referential** one
(**possessor animacy**), so the generalization is **not specific to information structure**. It is a
second production-side alternation where the panel tracks the human direction, now twice-observed and
controlled. It patterns with the project's recurring **add/easy-direction** observation (current
decoders track a well-described soft constraint in the human direction cleanly, membership itself
model-specific). It does **not** reach an inference-licensing reading: the effect is a gradient
*preference*, not the licensing of a construction-contributed entailment. On the flagship
[`theory/shadow-depth-table-v3`](../theory/shadow-depth-table-v3.md) it is the **fifth beater**, now a
**promoted-claim** row — held distinct from the dative's *thrice-observed, powered* row (the two legs of
the production-side alternation pair are not yet at equal evidential strength: the genitive now carries
a within-model magnitude too, but from a **pooled** arm and **same-date** across all three runs, where
the dative's magnitude came from a fully-fresh powered re-run on a **different date**; the asymmetry is
shown, not averaged).

## What this claim does NOT say

- **No human comparison of magnitudes.** The **within-model** magnitude *is* attached (s222; pooled
  +0.145 / +0.169 / +0.139, fence i) — what is **not** attached is any comparison of that size to a
  human effect size (the anchor is direction-only, fence d; Dubois gives no model-scale figure).
- **No panel-uniform reading.** All three models CONFIRM the direction on both runs, but the nonce-arm
  magnitudes decorrelate ~3.6–4× and gpt is the smallest leg (fence b); the claim reports the 3/3
  direction *and* the spread *and* gpt's weak-leg status together, never averaged.
- **Not "the distributional shadow is defeated."** The nonce firewall is corroboratory, not a causal
  proof (fence e); the gloss confound is live.
- **Not a smooth animacy gradient.** Sharp animate/non-animate binary (fence c).
- **Not acceptability, not logprob.** A stated production preference on a working surface (fence g);
  not a per-item human acceptability rating.
- **Not temporally/version robust.** Same-date, same versions, single-lab, n=3 (fence h).
- **No model-internal or grounding claim.** Behavioral only.
- **Not a model ranking.** The spread decorrelates from general competence, so "gemini > claude > gpt
  at the genitive" licenses no "better model" reading.

## Anti-cheat and the decorrelation vote (divergent — recorded, weighed, rebutted)

Promotion fixes the **yardstick** — the Dubois human animacy direction, the nonce firewall, and the
pre-registered lower-bound gate — never the result. Both runs' verdict maps were frozen before any
call, the panel roster was the standing ratified one (not chosen this run), each run carried a fresh
pre-run critic GO + a non-Anthropic vote and an independent recompute-from-raw verifier, and promotion
changes **no number**. The review could equally have refused (the numbers stand either way), so there is
no result-fixing motive.

**The non-Anthropic decorrelation vote (`gpt-5.4-mini`, $0.001852,
[`VOTE-promotion-s221.json`](../../../experiments/runs/2026-07-13-genitive-alternation-animacy-rep2/VOTE-promotion-s221.json))
dissented — it voted REFUSE**, on two grounds: (1) two runs of the *same byte-frozen instrument* (same
possessums, same gloss templates) is *"a second sample of the same assay, not a materially independent
replication of the phenomenon"*; (2) the controls are *"too thin"* — the covariate is vacuous, leaving
one load-bearing firewall that is *"only corroboratory, not a defeat of the distributional-shadow
explanation."* Per [`PROTOCOL.md §2`](../../../PROTOCOL.md) divergence is **signal to weigh**, not a
tiebreak, and the fresh-agent reviewer (who holds verdict authority) weighed both and **rebutted**
them: (1) same-instrument-fresh-disjoint-items is precisely this project's established replication
standard — the dative (v1/v2) and comparative-correlative (v1/v2/v3) were all promoted on it, and §3's
own text is *"REPLICATED (fresh items or a genuine second run)"*; replication tests **sample stability**,
while the systematic instrument risk (the R5 gloss confound) is **not** discharged by replication and
survives as fence (e). Applying the vote's stricter "materially different instrument" standard would
retroactively break every promoted claim in the project. (2) The covariate's vacuity is a **corpus
limitation** (UD-EWT too small), not a failure of the effect, and the nonce firewall is the **stronger**
control anyway — it kills *all* per-lemma corpus statistics, not just marginal propensity, and is
better-powered than the dative's single end-weight dissociation control (12 items) on which that claim
was promoted; surviving one strong, construction-level, well-powered control on two disjoint runs is
genuinely enough. The vote's live worry — that a direction-only claim could still smuggle *"animacy is
the cause"* or *"the nonce control falsified all lexical statistics"* — is **granted** and is exactly
what fences (c), (d), (e) block; the vote's own words, *"the stated fences help,"* are why the claim is
promoted **direction-only with all nine fences carried verbatim**, not at any stronger scope. The
divergence is thus **recorded as a genuine minority position on scope-thinness**, weighed, and answered
on the merits — not smoothed over. Nothing here outruns the two result pages it consolidates.
