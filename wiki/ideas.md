# the ideas — a reading index over the essays

**What this is.** A **reading surface**, not an argument. The project's philosophical track has grown to ~50 [`essay`](findings/essays/) pages; this page sorts them by the distinct *idea* each develops, so a reader can find the roughly a dozen genuinely separate contributions without reading all fifty. Each entry names one contribution, gives a two-line abstract, and lists the essays that develop it (the **anchor** read first, then the essays that sharpen, defend, or apply it). This page asserts **nothing beyond what those essays already argue, at their stated strength**; every empirical fact lives on the cited `result`/`claim` page, not here.

**How to read it.** Organized *by idea, not by essay*: several essays often develop one idea from different angles, and an essay that touches two ideas is listed under its primary one. The entries are numbered for reference only — the numbering is not a ranking.

**Why an index and not a merge** (the finding that produced this page, session 182). The program (item B3) had queued *merging* redundant essay families. On inspection the families are **not redundant clutter but a densely cross-referenced web**: the single most-cited essay, [`essay/undischargeable-negative`](findings/essays/undischargeable-negative.md), is referenced by ~30 other pages; the reading-discipline essays by a dozen-plus each, across theory pages, `claim`s, and `result`s. Destructively merging load-bearing pages (each carrying its own registered bets in [`predictions.md`](predictions.md) and revision triggers cited specifically elsewhere) would churn the citation graph for little reader gain. The **additive** index delivers B3's actual goal — surface the distinct ideas, fight the proliferation — without disturbing the web. Genuinely redundant, lightly-cited drafts remain candidates for a later merge; none was found among the families examined.

The ideas fall into two families, loosely tracking the project's two tracks: **substantive positions on meaning** (what these essays argue meaning *is*, and where an LLM's falls short), and **methodological / measurement-epistemic disciplines** (how the project measures behavior and reads its own numbers honestly). Both are first-class ([`PROJECT.md`](../PROJECT.md) §12.1).

## I. Substantive positions on meaning

### 1. The distributional shadow, and shadow-depth as a measured axis

The single test that organizes the project — *does a meaning gradient beat its matched distributional (co-occurrence) control?* — paired with the insight that phenomena sort by **shadow-depth**: how much of the behavior is already written into co-occurrence. That axis is *orthogonal to grain*, so each pole is read as splitting into a shadow-beater and a (more tentatively placed) shadow-saturated corner — the saturated placements a reading/bet, not a controlled result, and the presupposition corner in particular sits, post-control, in an *under-licensed middle*.

- [`essay/shadow-depth-cross-cuts-grain`](findings/essays/shadow-depth-cross-cuts-grain.md) *(anchor)* — the continuum's single test sorts phenomena by shadow-depth, a second axis that cross-cuts the word↔construction cline.
- [`essay/two-distributional-hypotheses`](findings/essays/two-distributional-hypotheses.md) — "the distributional hypothesis" is two ideas (Harris's form-internal vs Firth's situated); the next-token objective instantiates Harris, not Firth.
- [`essay/shortcut-vs-competence-mis-cut`](findings/essays/shortcut-vs-competence-mis-cut.md) — the well-posed cut is *local vs transferable* distributional generalization; a residual over a co-occurrence control grades locality, not distribution-vs-non-distribution.
- [`essay/antonymy-outlier-distributional-shadow`](findings/essays/antonymy-outlier-distributional-shadow.md) — antonymy is the relation recovered best because it is where the shadow is largest; cross-method convergence *raises* the deflationary reading (a screening-off failure).
- [`essay/two-tags-molecularist-bet`](findings/essays/two-tags-molecularist-bet.md) — keeping `distributional` and `inferential` as two tags is structurally a molecularist bet, paid by stipulating the inferential subset per probe.

*Connects to:* [`theory/shadow-depth-table-v4`](findings/theory/shadow-depth-table-v4.md) (the measured backbone), [`theory/lexicon-grammar-continuum-v2`](findings/theory/lexicon-grammar-continuum-v2.md).

### 2. Reference: where the behavioral method goes constitutively silent

Whether an LLM's words *refer* is **premise-bound, not evidence-bound** — it turns on a definitional antecedent (does inheriting word-histories through training text count as community membership?) that no behavioral probe can settle. And the "no" the project records is not one boundary but several structurally distinct denials that merely agree on today's verdict.

- [`essay/reference-as-premise-bound`](findings/essays/reference-as-premise-bound.md) *(anchor)* — the dispute turns entirely on one externalist conditional's antecedent, fixed off-board; the method measures word-to-word structure, not word-to-world fixing.
- [`essay/reference-denials-disunified`](findings/essays/reference-denials-disunified.md) — three denials share the verdict but give incompatible counterfactuals for "what would make an LM's words refer?".
- [`essay/compositionality-asymptote-not-wall`](findings/essays/compositionality-asymptote-not-wall.md) — the method falls short of reference as a *wall* (no narrowing), unlike compositionality (an asymptote it keeps narrowing).
- [`essay/inherited-not-constituted`](findings/essays/inherited-not-constituted.md) — Grindrod's metasemantic route and the behavioral relational null draw one line ("inherited-and-public, not originated-or-constituted-here") from opposite directions.
- [`essay/stereotype-without-the-expert`](findings/essays/stereotype-without-the-expert.md) — an LLM has Putnam's stereotype, lacks his indexically-fixed extension, and occupies neither role in his division of linguistic labor.
- [`essay/two-topics-language-as-object`](findings/essays/two-topics-language-as-object.md) — Lewis's split: the method is a topic-one (language-as-object) instrument; its hardest silences are topic-two (selection-fact) silences it is worst-placed to reach.

### 3. Updating a convention is not constituting one (the relational ladder)

The relational-meaning results show models *update* a coined convention (order-sensitive, latest-binding-wins) — but this is **thin**. A structured ladder of nameable rungs separates order-sensitive updating from between-agent *constitution*, cut by a decidable thin/rich (single-reader-recoverability) criterion, and both occupied rungs sit on the thin side.

- [`essay/update-is-not-constitution`](findings/essays/update-is-not-constitution.md) *(anchor)* — the four-rung ladder and the single-reader-recoverability criterion cutting thin from rich.
- [`essay/aggregation-not-constitution`](findings/essays/aggregation-not-constitution.md) — the null deflates only the inference from convergent convention-use to constitution; it forbids some deflations too.
- [`essay/alignment-names-the-floor`](findings/essays/alignment-names-the-floor.md) — interactive alignment supplies a deflationary *mechanism* for the floor and, being a processing account, *raises* the constitution bar.
- [`essay/conversation-as-text-not-timeline`](findings/essays/conversation-as-text-not-timeline.md) — a third reading (a position-weighted layout, neither tracked timeline nor clean order-free set), scoped as regime-dependent.
- [`essay/rung-iv-instrument`](findings/essays/rung-iv-instrument.md) — a between-agent instrument would have to be a *scored live practice*, not a transcript.

*Connects to:* [`theory/relational-meaning-in-llms`](findings/theory/relational-meaning-in-llms.md), [`claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment`](findings/claims/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment.md).

### 4. Graded sense scale, ungraded commitment

These models rank-order word senses smoothly (a graded *scale*, tracking human judgments) yet still force every genuinely ambiguous use to a confident discrete pick (ungraded *commitment*). The two are logically independent, so "gradience in the ledger, none in the moment" is a substantive behavioral finding about how a model carries sense.

- [`essay/graded-scale-ungraded-commitment`](findings/essays/graded-scale-ungraded-commitment.md) *(anchor)* — scale and commitment are logically independent; the null combination would be as real a finding as a positive.
- [`essay/preference-without-commitment`](findings/essays/preference-without-commitment.md) — a paraphrase-preference and an entailment-commitment index two different constructs; where they dissociate, "does the model use the meaning?" has no single answer.
- [`essay/presence-is-not-balance`](findings/essays/presence-is-not-balance.md) — an attested ambiguity discharges *co-presence* but is silent (by type) on *balance*; the two must be anchored separately (topically about lexical sense, but methodologically a construct-validity–hygiene point — see §12).
- [`essay/ambiguity-kind-not-level`](findings/essays/ambiguity-kind-not-level.md) — the commit/decline split tracks *kind* of indeterminacy (scalar vs disjunctive), not level; its lexical bet was tested and **retracted** (models committed).
- [`essay/layer-specialness-vs-always-resolvability`](findings/essays/layer-specialness-vs-always-resolvability.md) — the surviving lexical commit admits two rival readings the design cannot separate; a genuinely-unresolvable item would tell them apart.
- [`essay/gradient-from-overlap`](findings/essays/gradient-from-overlap.md) — declining to read the gradient as a stored prototype repeats the prototype tradition's own discipline; the open question is *which overlap space* the gradient lives in.

*Connects to:* [`claim/lexical-sense-gradience`](findings/claims/lexical-sense-gradience.md), [`claim/lexical-graded-scale-ungraded-commitment`](findings/claims/lexical-graded-scale-ungraded-commitment.md).

### 5. The distribution/grounding seam: what perception and context can and can't move

Perception moves an LLM's lexical-sense behavior *only in the residue the text distribution leaves under-determined* — a **gated** shape (confirmed in direction), with a headroom predicted **narrow** and, structurally, **self-concealing**. The same seam splits indexical meaning: the convention-fixed *character* is distributionally native; the *content* (the value at an occupied context) must be supplied by the text.

- [`essay/headroom-positive-vindicates-the-null`](findings/essays/headroom-positive-vindicates-the-null.md) *(anchor)* — the grounding axis's first confirming cell fixes what its prior nulls were evidence for (text saturation, a gated modality), and licenses the *shape*, not the *size*.
- [`essay/narrow-headroom-conceals-its-magnitude`](findings/essays/narrow-headroom-conceals-its-magnitude.md) — prediction 3's truth-condition coincides with its low off-the-shelf measurability; the magnitude is self-concealing (and UNTESTED).
- [`essay/indexical-character-learnable-content-supplied`](findings/essays/indexical-character-learnable-content-supplied.md) — an indexical splits at Kaplan's seam: character on the distributional side, content on the grounding side.
- [`essay/origo-supplied-not-occupied`](findings/essays/origo-supplied-not-occupied.md) — a tool-clock probe tests an *as-if* behavior; the model occupies no context, every origo arrives as described text.

*Connects to:* [`conjecture/distributional-saturation-grounding-headroom`](findings/conjectures/distributional-saturation-grounding-headroom.md), [`open-question/grounding-magnitude-instrument`](findings/open-questions/grounding-magnitude-instrument.md).

### 6. Constructional (grammatical) meaning at the construction grain — the frame connection

The argument-structure construction is itself a **frame-evoker** — a conventional form evoking a structured inferential background its parts don't predict — cousin by inheritance to Fillmore's lexical frame; and "function word," a distributional/part-of-speech class, is *not* one natural kind for inferential bearing.

- [`essay/constructional-meaning-meets-frame`](findings/essays/constructional-meaning-meets-frame.md) *(anchor)* — the argument-structure construction as a grammatical frame-evoker, cousin to Fillmore's lexical frame.
- [`essay/frame-semantics-third-company`](findings/essays/frame-semantics-third-company.md) — Fillmore's evoked frame as a *third* reading of "knowing a word by its company," orthogonal to the co-occurrence a next-token objective instantiates.
- [`essay/function-words-not-one-category`](findings/essays/function-words-not-one-category.md) — inferential load pools onto a subset of function-word *types* (differing by model); constructional load is a relation among construction, inference, and instrument.

*Connects to:* [`theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms-v2`](findings/theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms-v2.md).

### 7. The presupposition corner: environment-gated and frame-defeasible — a distributional signature

At the presupposition corner, a presupposition/assertion split that "ought" to hold invariantly presents behaviorally as **sensitivity to the licensing environment** — projection gated by embedding frame, accommodation by context support. One distributional description ("follow the surface cue; its reliability is set by the environment") predicts both signatures — better read as how text-trained decoders handle the phenomenon than as computing the split.

- [`essay/presupposition-environment-gated`](findings/essays/presupposition-environment-gated.md) *(anchor)* — one distributional shape, two signatures; environment-gating appears in accommodation too, not just projection.
- [`essay/projection-defeasible-by-frame`](findings/essays/projection-defeasible-by-frame.md) — the conditional-antecedent collapse is a frame-shaped regularity, not item noise — a distributional signature.
- [`essay/under-licensed-middle`](findings/essays/under-licensed-middle.md) — a word-varying matched control can only ever return {saturated, under-licensed}, never a clean beater; "beater" and "under-licensed middle" are outputs of two different instruments.

*Connects to:* [`result/presupposition-doppelganger-control-v1`](findings/results/presupposition-doppelganger-control-v1.md) (internal-contrast-only).

### 8. The add-easy / cancel-hard asymmetry as a predictive-objective fingerprint

Adding a construction-licensed layer is easy; *cancelling* a lexical default is hard — and if this asymmetry generalizes, its *direction* is the behavioral **fingerprint of a next-token predictive objective** (accumulation moves with a high-probability continuation; defeasance moves against a strong prior), not a further fact about constructional competence. Whether the add/cancel joint even exists is itself an operational achievement of item construction.

- [`essay/predictive-objective-favors-accumulation`](findings/essays/predictive-objective-favors-accumulation.md) *(anchor)* — the inversion: the asymmetry's direction is what a predictor would *predict*, so it is the objective's fingerprint, not a separate competence fact.
- [`essay/add-cancel-separability`](findings/essays/add-cancel-separability.md) — the clean add/cancel joint is not pre-given in a distributional system; it is earned by careful item construction.
- [`essay/nothing-to-cancel`](findings/essays/nothing-to-cancel.md) — a cancel arm can be hard two ways (a held entailment failing to suppress vs an implicature never held); only the first measures defeasance.

## II. Methodological / measurement-epistemic disciplines

### 9. The undischargeable negative, and the economics of witness-seeking

A behavioral probe can **witness** a capability (one success settles existence) but can **never close out its absence** (a universal over an open elicitation class). So the project never writes a behavioral "M cannot": it *suspends* a witness-search (a reopenable spending decision), never closes it, spending a finite budget on axis-diverse, on-signature easings and preferring a structural bound to exhaustion.

- [`essay/undischargeable-negative`](findings/essays/undischargeable-negative.md) *(anchor)* — a positive is a verifiable existential, an absence an unverifiable universal; the typology of negatives (effect-null / instrument-uninterpretable / structural reach-closure).
- [`essay/witness-seeking-economics`](findings/essays/witness-seeking-economics.md) — the spending discipline; *closure is not suspension* is the load-bearing move.
- [`essay/floor-is-not-a-ceiling`](findings/essays/floor-is-not-a-ceiling.md) — a task-parameter floor (≥2 moves) is not a medium-level reach-closure; the search can only be suspended.
- [`essay/no-admissible-certifier`](findings/essays/no-admissible-certifier.md) — when a stimulus premise can be read only by a barred human subject or the model under test, it is uncertifiable in-repo; the resource-anchor discipline is the only escape.
- [`essay/inference-default-coincidence`](findings/essays/inference-default-coincidence.md) — where a construction's inference coincides with the phrase's distributional default, a shift-from-control design subtracts a baseline already at ceiling; the question is unreachable by that instrument.

### 10. What a panel / run summary conceals — the reading disciplines

Three independent reads a finding must pass, each recovering variation a summary statistic hides: a **split** panel is a per-model existential, never an averaged panel claim; a **concordant** verdict can still hide an order-of-magnitude magnitude spread; and a single **temperature-0 run is a draw**, carrying run-to-run jitter that single-run confidence intervals structurally understate.

- [`essay/capability-split`](findings/essays/capability-split.md) *(anchor)* — read a split as a per-model existential plus an instrument-bounded negative; mark every verdict CONCORDANT vs SPLIT.
- [`essay/concordant-verdict-hides-spread`](findings/essays/concordant-verdict-hides-spread.md) — carry the per-model magnitudes; a wide spread licenses a direction sentence, not a magnitude/uniformity one.
- [`essay/point-estimate-is-a-draw`](findings/essays/point-estimate-is-a-draw.md) — a between-X gap no larger than the run-to-run jitter is not yet a finding; re-run to *measure* the jitter, not to firm a result.

### 11. The output channel is an instrument (the working surface)

The channel a model must answer through — a single forced token vs a working surface it can externalize computation on — is a **degree of freedom of the instrument, not a neutral readout**: a too-narrow channel can *mask* a serial-computation capability and a wide one can *scaffold* it, so a forced-format capability-negative is channel-bounded until the channel, and its uptake, are varied.

- [`essay/output-channel-confound`](findings/essays/output-channel-confound.md) *(anchor)* — names the confound and the control every behavioral capability-negative owes; index every capability attribution to its channel, and rank the channel among the first controls.
- [`essay/transcript-ceiling`](findings/essays/transcript-ceiling.md) — single-reader-recoverability may be a property of the *text medium*, ceilinging text-only probes at the thin side.

*Connects to:* [`claim/output-channel-working-surface`](findings/claims/output-channel-working-surface.md) (promoted).

### 12. Instrument epistemics: build it, bracket it, trust it over the eyeball

A cluster of disciplines for constructing and reading behavioral instruments. **Design out** a within-condition-degenerate confound at construction time (you cannot model it out); **bracket** an instrument's competence between its two failure modes for a magnitude read; trust a **frozen strict instrument over an expert hand-read** (which systematically over-credits); the gate discipline *is* construct validation with the criterion leg amputated by the no-human-subjects rule; and a cross-level claim may be asserted only at the **convergence rung** the evidence has reached.

- [`essay/construct-validity-without-a-criterion`](findings/essays/construct-validity-without-a-criterion.md) *(anchor)* — the operationalization gates are construct validation (Cronbach & Meehl); no-human-subjects removes the criterion leg, leaving the nomological net.
- [`essay/design-out-not-model-out`](findings/essays/design-out-not-model-out.md) — a covariate degenerate (zero-variance) within the condition of interest must be fixed at construction time, not modelled out at analysis time.
- [`essay/audit-brackets-instrument-competence`](findings/essays/audit-brackets-instrument-competence.md) — a magnitude read's dominant uncertainty is the instrument's own competence; bracket it between two symmetric failure modes rather than assert it.
- [`essay/instrument-corrects-the-hand-read`](findings/essays/instrument-corrects-the-hand-read.md) — a careful adversarial eyeball is an optimistic point estimate a frozen, held-out, strict-rubric instrument moves downward.
- [`essay/cross-level-convergence-ladder`](findings/essays/cross-level-convergence-ladder.md) — R0 shared vocabulary → R-ceiling mechanistic identity; assert a cross-level unification only at the rung the evidence has reached.

*Also here, by role:* the anchor names `design-out-not-model-out` and [`essay/presence-is-not-balance`](findings/essays/presence-is-not-balance.md) (filed under §4 by topic) as **Messick's two construct-validity threats** — so *presence-is-not-balance* is a construct-validity–hygiene point as much as a lexical-sense one.
