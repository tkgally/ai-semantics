# Borsboom, Mellenbergh & van Heerden 2004 — The Concept of Validity

## What it is

Denny Borsboom, Gideon J. Mellenbergh, and Jaap van Heerden's 2004 *Psychological Review* article (111(4): 1061–1071), "The Concept of Validity." It is the **realist / causal critique** of the post-1955 construct-validity tradition — the paper that argues validity is a matter of **ontology, reference, and causality**, not of epistemology, meaning, and correlation. Its thesis is deliberately austere: "a test is valid for measuring an attribute if and only if (a) the attribute exists and (b) variations in the attribute causally produce variations in the outcomes of the measurement procedure" (p. 1061). On this view, the nomological-network account of [`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md) and the unified-validity account of [`source/messick-1995-validity-of-psychological-assessment`](messick-1995-validity-of-psychological-assessment.md) both get validity backward — they make it a property of *test-score interpretations* judged against a web of correlations, when it is a property of the *test* fixed by whether a real attribute causally produces the scores. Like the Cronbach & Meehl and Messick pages, it is a philosophy-of-measurement source — **not** a meaning-theory source and **not** a human-labeled empirical resource.

It was fetched for one specific in-repo purpose. The essay [`essay/construct-validity-without-a-criterion`](../../findings/essays/construct-validity-without-a-criterion.md) argues the project is barred from **two** of the three classical validation routes Freiesleben distinguishes — the *criterion* leg (no human subjects) and the *causal/realist* leg (no ontological commitment that the construct lives in the model) — leaving the nomological net as the only one available. That essay's "second amputation" leg has, until now, rested entirely on Borsboom **as characterized by Freiesleben** (a secondary channel); its revision trigger (e) explicitly asks for a *primary* Borsboom so the causal/realist leg can be stated from Borsboom's own text. This page ingests that primary source. It also lets the project check whether Freiesleben's rendering of Borsboom is faithful (it is — see "How it bears on the project") and supplies, as a bonus, Borsboom's own one-line verdict on the **multitrait–multimethod matrix** of Campbell & Fiske, the other still-uncatalogued want.

**This is a methodology anchor, NOT a human-comparison anchor.** It licenses no claim about any LLM. It supplies a *theory of validity* (specifically, the realist/causal alternative the project's deflationary commitment bars it from adopting); it cannot ground any empirical finding about a model, and (being a 2004 measurement paper) it says nothing about language models, lexical gradience, or Construction Grammar.

(The three "Versus" sections carry the paper's tripartite contrast — ontology/epistemology, reference/meaning, causality/correlation — and "Where to Look for Validity" states the implications for validation research and test construction.)

## Key positions & quotes (verbatim, with page locators)

**Abstract — the core realist/causal thesis (p. 1061):**

> "This article advances a simple conception of test validity: A test is valid for measuring an attribute if (a) the attribute exists and (b) variations in the attribute causally produce variation in the measurement outcomes."

**Abstract — ontology/reference/causality versus epistemology/meaning/correlation (p. 1061):**

> "In particular, the emphasis in the proposed conception is on ontology, reference, and causality, whereas current validity theory focuses on epistemology, meaning, and correlation."

**Opening argument — the thesis as an "if and only if" (p. 1061):**

> "a test is valid for measuring an attribute if and only if (a) the attribute exists and (b) variations in the attribute causally produce variations in the outcomes of the measurement procedure."

**Opening argument — the critique of the construct-validity / nomological-net tradition as concerning interpretations, not tests (p. 1061):**

> "They are not about a property of tests but about a property of test score interpretations. They are not about the simple, factual question of whether a test measures an attribute but about the complex question of whether test score interpretations are consistent with a nomological network involving theoretical and observational terms (Cronbach & Meehl, 1955) or with an even more complicated system of theoretical rationales, empirical data, and social consequences of testing (Messick, 1989)."

**Opening argument — the locus of evidence is response processes, so correlation tables are only circumstantial (p. 1062):**

> "That the crucial ingredient of validity involves the causal effect of an attribute on the test scores implies that the locus of evidence for validity lies in the processes that convey this effect. This means that tables of correlations between test scores and other measures cannot provide more than circumstantial evidence for validity."

**"Ontology Versus Epistemology" — validity is a property, validation is an activity (p. 1063):**

> "validity is a property, whereas validation is an activity. … Validity is about ontology; validation is about epistemology. The two should not be confused."

**"Ontology Versus Epistemology" — Messick's definition explicitly rejected (p. 1063):**

> "Validity is not a judgment at all. It is the property being judged."

**"Reference Versus Meaning" — reference and realism, not meaning, at the center (p. 1063):**

> "the central point in validity is one of reference: The attribute to which the psychologist refers must exist in reality; otherwise, the test cannot possibly be valid for measuring that attribute."

**"Reference Versus Meaning" — the nomological network as a logical-positivist relic (p. 1063):**

> "one of the relics of the approach has plagued validity theory to this day. This is the nomological network."

**"Reference Versus Meaning" — the operationist/positivist move that construct validity inherits: meaning without reference (p. 1064):**

> "Thus, in this view, one can have meaning without reference and can invoke theoretical terms without automatically engaging in ontological claims, which always introduce a lot of metaphysics."

**"Reference Versus Meaning" — construct validity depends crucially on the nomological network (p. 1064):**

> "This idea was used by Cronbach and Meehl (1955) to put forward their idea of construct validity. Construct validity theory depends crucially on the availability of a nomological network."

**"Reference Versus Meaning" — the decisive objection (the line the essay had only via Freiesleben) (p. 1064):**

> "Few, if any, theoretical terms in psychology can be unambiguously identified in this way."

**"Where to Look for Validity" — the constructive restatement: validity IS a property of the test, fixed by causation (p. 1067):**

> "Validity is a property of tests: A valid test can convey the effect of variation in the attribute one intends to measure. … A test is valid for measuring an attribute if variation in the attribute causes variation in the test scores."

**"Where to Look for Validity" — validate by theorizing response processes, not by correlation tables (p. 1067):**

> "the primary objective of validation research is not to establish that the correlations go in the right directions but to offer a theoretical explanation of the processes that lead up to the measurement outcomes."

**"Where to Look for Validity" — validity must be put into the test at construction (p. 1067):**

> "A large part of test validity must be put into the test at the stage of test construction—a stage of the testing process that has received little attention compared with the enormous emphasis that has been placed on test analysis."

**"Where to Look for Validity" — Borsboom's verdict on the multitrait–multimethod matrix (Campbell & Fiske) (p. 1068):**

> "we consider it impossible to argue for test validity solely on the basis of a multitrait, multimethod matrix. Such a matrix is helpful, but a favorable matrix configuration is not constitutive of validity. What is constitutive of validity is the existence of an attribute and its causal impact on scores."

**"Discussion" — the proposal as a viable realist alternative to the consensus (p. 1069):**

> "We do not see the need for a unified validity concept (Ellis & Blustein, 1991; Messick, 1989; Moss, 1992; Shepard, 1993) because we think there is nothing to unify. … We therefore think that a realist, causation-based concept of validity is a viable alternative to the current consensus in validity theory."

## How it bears on the project (the realist/causal validation leg the no-human-subjects rule does *not* bar — and a *second* commitment does)

- **It supplies the primary text for the essay's "second amputation."** [`essay/construct-validity-without-a-criterion`](../../findings/essays/construct-validity-without-a-criterion.md) argues the project lands on the nomological net not by preference but because its constraints bar the two alternatives: the *criterion* leg (removed by the no-human-subjects rule, CLAUDE.md rule 4) and the *causal/realist* leg. Borsboom's causal account is exactly that second leg, now sourced directly: a test is valid iff the attribute "exists and … variations in the attribute causally produce variation in the measurement outcomes" (p. 1061), and "What is constitutive of validity is the existence of an attribute and its causal impact on scores" (p. 1068). The project's deflationary commitment — its gates forbid reading the construct off the model under test, and it declines to assert a model *possesses* an attribute as an internal state that causally produces the behavior — is precisely a refusal to make Borsboom's ontological/causal claim about the model. So the realist/causal route is barred to the project **by its own commitment, not by a budget or subjects rule**; that is the structural point the essay names, and it can now be stated from Borsboom rather than from Freiesleben's secondary characterization.
- **It confirms Freiesleben's rendering of Borsboom is faithful.** The essay (via [`source/freiesleben-2026-construct-validity-llm-benchmarks`](freiesleben-2026-construct-validity-llm-benchmarks.md)) attributes to Borsboom two conditions — "the construct must exist, and variations in measurement outcomes must be caused by variations in the state of that construct" — and the verbatim quotation "Few, if any, theoretical terms in psychology can be unambiguously identified in this way." Both check out against the primary text: the two-condition thesis is Borsboom's own iff statement (p. 1061), and the quoted sentence appears verbatim on p. 1064. Trigger (e)'s contingency ("if the primary Borsboom diverges from Freiesleben's rendering, this section is revised") is therefore **not** triggered — the rendering is accurate, so the essay's "second amputation" argument stands as written, now with primary backing.
- **It sharpens the contrast Cronbach & Meehl and Messick are read through.** Where [`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md) makes validity a matter of a test's place in a nomological net and [`source/messick-1995-validity-of-psychological-assessment`](messick-1995-validity-of-psychological-assessment.md) makes it "an overall evaluative judgment" of score-meaning, Borsboom flatly denies both: "Validity is not a judgment at all. It is the property being judged" (p. 1063), and the nomological network is "one of the relics" of logical positivism that "has plagued validity theory to this day" (p. 1063). This is why the essay treats the nomological net as a *contested* account, not the settled theory of validity — Borsboom is the standing objection that the net-elaboration program "is bound to fail" because few theoretical terms can be identified by their place in a net. The project relies on the net because its constraints bar the alternatives, not because Borsboom is refuted.
- **Its "put validity into the test at construction" thesis rhymes with — but does not ground — the project's design-time discipline.** Borsboom's "A large part of test validity must be put into the test at the stage of test construction" (p. 1067) and "the primary objective of validation research is … to offer a theoretical explanation of the processes that lead up to the measurement outcomes" (p. 1067) are structurally close to the project's "design-out-not-model-out" rule (fix confounds at construction, not after the run) and its preference for mechanism over correlation. The resemblance is worth noting as convergence, but it is **not** a licensing relation: Borsboom's "processes" are *causal* response processes in a realist sense the project's deflationary stance does not assert about models. Cite the rhyme; do not import the realism.
- **It supplies a primary one-liner on the multitrait–multimethod matrix (Campbell & Fiske).** Borsboom's "a favorable matrix configuration is not constitutive of validity" (p. 1068) is a usable primary statement on MTMM logic while [`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md)'s and the essay's note that **Campbell & Fiske 1959 itself** remains uncatalogued still stands. Borsboom gives the *critic's* verdict on MTMM, not Campbell & Fiske's own convergent/discriminant framework; the primary Campbell & Fiske is still a separate want if a finding needs the matrix logic on its own terms.

## What it cannot ground

- **Any claim about any model.** It predates the project's questions, reports no measurement of any LLM, and is not a human-labeled empirical asset (no treebank, sense inventory, acceptability norm, or annotation). It cannot be an `anchors:` resource. Its role is methodology framing only — specifically, naming the realist/causal account the project's commitments place off-limits.
- **A human-comparison anchor.** It supplies a *theory of validity*, not a *human yardstick*. Citing it discharges no empirical claim's human-anchor obligation; it discharges the *primary-source* gap the essay's trigger (e) flagged for the causal/realist leg.
- **A licence to adopt the realist/causal account for models.** The page records Borsboom's position; it does **not** endorse applying it to LLMs. The essay's whole point is that the project *declines* the ontological commitment Borsboom's account requires. Do not cite this page as warrant for asserting that a model possesses an attribute that causally produces its behavior — that is the very claim the project's gates refuse.
- **Anything about lexical gradience or Construction Grammar.** The paper is general measurement methodology (its examples are IQ, intelligence, Extraversion, the balance-scale task, SES); it says nothing about graded word sense, form–meaning pairing, or acceptability methodology.

## Known limits

- **A contested position, not the consensus.** Borsboom's realist/causal account is a *minority critique* of the dominant construct-validity tradition, by the authors' own framing ("almost diametrically opposed to the currently endorsed conceptions of validity," p. 1069; "The philosophical assumptions involved … are strong—stronger, perhaps, than in any previous discussion of validity," p. 1069). Cite it as the **realist alternative** and the standing objection to the nomological net, not as the settled theory of validity. The project uses it to *name* the account it is barred from, and to mark the net as contested — not to declare the net wrong.
- **2004 measurement methodology.** Its examples and apparatus (latent-variable models, confirmatory factor analysis, the WAIS, the balance-scale task) are psychometric and predate language models. The *logic* of the ontology/epistemology and causality/correlation contrasts transfers to how the project reasons about validation; the examples are domain-specific.
- **Underlying text is © 2004 APA** (see Provenance/access note); the author's own site provides open access but not a public-domain license. Quotes kept short and attributed.
- **MTMM is mentioned, not developed.** Borsboom gives only the critic's one-line verdict on the multitrait–multimethod matrix; the convergent/discriminant framework of **Campbell & Fiske 1959** is still uncatalogued and remains a separate want.
