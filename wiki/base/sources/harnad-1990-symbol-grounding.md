# Harnad 1990 — The Symbol Grounding Problem

## What it is

Journal article in *Physica D: Nonlinear Phenomena* 42 (1990) 335–346. The canonical statement of the symbol grounding problem in cognitive science and philosophy of mind. Harnad asks how a purely symbolic system can have meaning that is intrinsic to it rather than borrowed from an external interpreter. Proposes a hybrid nonsymbolic/symbolic architecture in which elementary symbols are grounded bottom-up in iconic and categorical (nonsymbolic) representations derived from sensory input. The paper is the philosophical ancestor of the multimodal-grounding question and anticipates the core of Bender & Koller's form-vs.-meaning argument by three decades.

## Summary

Harnad's central argument has three moves:

1. **The symbol grounding problem stated.** Pure symbol systems manipulate tokens by their shapes alone; the semantic interpretations attached to those tokens are supplied by an external interpreter, not by the system itself. The meanings are therefore not intrinsic but *parasitic* on meanings in the interpreter's head. A purely symbolic model of mind faces the same problem: its symbols' interpretations are extrinsic.

2. **The Chinese/Chinese dictionary-go-round.** The analogy that motivates the problem: a learner trying to acquire Chinese using only a Chinese/Chinese dictionary would spin in an endless "merry-go-round" of symbol-to-symbol substitutions, never landing on what any symbol means in non-symbolic terms. The same regress afflicts any system whose only resource for interpretation is other symbols.

3. **The grounding proposal.** The solution is a hybrid architecture in which elementary symbols are connected bottom-up to two kinds of nonsymbolic representation: (i) *iconic representations* (analog sensory transforms of proximal projections of distal objects/events) and (ii) *categorical representations* (learned/innate feature-detectors that pick out the invariant features distinguishing category members from non-members). Once elementary category names are grounded in these nonsymbolic representations, higher-order symbolic strings inheriting from them can ground compositionally without further sensory reduction.

## Key passages

Page numbers refer to the original *Physica D* 42 (1990) pagination where determinable; the continuous text is preserved verbatim from arXiv HTML cs/9906002 (https://arxiv.org/html/cs/9906002, re-verified 2026-05-31). The "p. 335 (abstract)" labels are inferred (the abstract is the first printed page); the HTML carries no journal pagination, so treat any specific page number as a bound, not a confirmed locator.

**p. 335 (abstract) — problem statement, Part 1:**

> "How can the semantic interpretation of a formal symbol system be made intrinsic to the system, rather than just parasitic on the meanings in our heads?"

**p. 335 (abstract) — problem statement, Part 2:**

> "How can the meanings of the meaningless symbol tokens, manipulated solely on the basis of their (arbitrary) shapes, be grounded in anything but other meaningless symbols?"

**p. 335 (abstract) — dictionary analogy:**

> "The problem is analogous to trying to learn Chinese from a Chinese/Chinese dictionary alone."

**p. 335 (abstract) — candidate solution:**

> "A candidate solution is sketched: Symbolic representations must be grounded bottom-up in nonsymbolic representations of two kinds: (1) iconic representations, which are analogs of the proximal sensory projections of distal objects and events, and (2) categorical representations, which are learned and innate feature-detectors that pick out the invariant features of object and event categories from their sensory projections."

**Body — Chinese/Chinese dictionary-go-round (difficult version):**

> "Suppose you had to learn Chinese as a second language and the only source of information you had was a Chinese/Chinese dictionary. The trip through the dictionary would amount to a merry-go-round, passing endlessly from one meaningless symbol or symbol-string (the definientes) to another (the definienda), never coming to a halt on what anything meant."

**Body — impossible version and core question:**

> "Suppose you had to learn Chinese as a first language and the only source of information you had was a Chinese/Chinese dictionary! … How can you ever get off the symbol/symbol merry-go-round? How is symbol meaning to be grounded in something other than just more meaningless symbols?"

**Body — the problem named:**

> "This is the symbol grounding problem."

**Body — hybrid solution:**

> "What will be proposed is a hybrid nonsymbolic/symbolic system, a "dedicated" one, in which the elementary symbols are grounded in two kinds of nonsymbolic representations that pick out, from their proximal sensory projections, the distal object categories to which the elementary symbols refer."

**Body — sensorimotor extension:**

> "In addition, motor and sensory-motor grounding will no doubt be as important as the sensory grounding that is being focused on here."

**Body — compositional grounding:**

> "Once one has the grounded set of elementary symbols provided by a taxonomy of names (and the iconic and categorical representations that give content to the names and allow them to pick out the objects they identify), the rest of the symbol strings of a natural language can be generated by symbol composition alone, and they will all inherit the intrinsic grounding of the elementary set."

## What it can ground

- [`concept/symbol-grounding-problem`](../concepts/symbol-grounding-problem.md) — this page is the primary source for that concept.
- [`concept/grounding`](../concepts/grounding.md) — the binary form of the grounding requirement (Bender & Koller 2020 is the LLM-era restatement, but Harnad 1990 is the philosophical origin).
- The `grounded.perceptual` sub-tag: iconic and categorical representations are the theory of how sensorimotor grounding works.
- The `grounded.causal` sub-tag: Harnad's account of reference via sensorimotor categories is a causal-contact story (the category representation must be causally responsive to distal objects).
- Any finding page arguing that text-only LLMs lack something specifically because they have no sensorimotor loop — Harnad is the ancestral argument.

## What it cannot ground

- Claims about degree of grounding — Harnad's account is architectural and binary (grounded or not), not gradient. For gradient framing cite Lyre 2024.
- Claims about social or normative dimensions of grounding — Harnad focuses on sensorimotor, not on social embedding or joint action.
- Specific LLM behavior claims — the paper is pre-LLM and makes no predictions about large-scale text models.

## Known limits

- Harnad's "categorical representations" are meant to be implemented by connectionist networks learning invariant features; this is a 1990 view of connectionism that predates modern deep learning by decades. The mapping to current neural LLMs is indirect at best.
- The motor-grounding caveat is acknowledged but not developed: Harnad says sensorimotor grounding is important but focuses only on sensory. The `grounded.perceptual` tag therefore covers the developed argument; `grounded.causal` is the tag for the implicit causal-contact story.
- Journal-paywall: the *Physica D* published text (pp. 335–346) requires a subscription. The open-access self-archive at Southampton (scanned PDF) and arXiv HTML (cs/9906002) are the accessible copies. Tom should verify the approximate-verbatim quotes against the published text when convenient.
