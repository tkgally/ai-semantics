# Messick 1995 — Validity of Psychological Assessment

## What it is

Samuel Messick's 1995 *American Psychologist* article (50(9): 741–749), "Validity of Psychological Assessment: Validation of Inferences From Persons' Responses and Performances as Scientific Inquiry Into Score Meaning." It is the canonical statement of the **unified theory of validity**: the position that the traditional three-way division of validity into content, criterion, and construct validities is mistaken, and that validity is a single, integrated **construct-validity** concept of which content, criteria, and consequences are aspects rather than separate substitutable types. It is the source that names and defines the project's two key threat terms — **construct underrepresentation** and **construct-irrelevant variance** — and that relocates validity from the test to the *meaning of the test scores* and the *inferences drawn from them*. Like [`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md), it is a philosophy-of-measurement / methodology source — **not** a meaning-theory source and **not** a human-labeled empirical resource.

It was fetched for one specific in-repo purpose: the essay [`essay/construct-validity-without-a-criterion`](../../findings/essays/construct-validity-without-a-criterion.md) maps its own probe-design rules onto Messick's vocabulary — its **"design-out-not-model-out"** rule onto *construct-irrelevant variance*, and its **"presence-is-not-balance"** rule onto *construct underrepresentation* — but until now could only *gesture* at those terms, flagging Messick as not-yet-ingested. This page ingests Messick so the essay can state that mapping in Messick's own precise vocabulary, and so the project can check its from-scratch reconstruction against the post-1955 consensus statement of validity theory.

**This is a methodology anchor, NOT a human-comparison anchor.** It licenses no claim about any LLM. It supplies validity vocabulary and validity logic the project's probe-design discipline can be *read as* instantiating; it cannot ground any empirical finding about a model, and (being a 1995 measurement paper) it says nothing about language models, lexical gradience, or Construction Grammar.

## Key passages (verbatim, with page locators)

**Subtitle — validity as inquiry into score meaning (title page, p. 741):**

> "Validation of Inferences From Persons' Responses and Performances as Scientific Inquiry Into Score Meaning"

**Abstract — the three-type view rejected as fragmented (p. 741):**

> "The traditional conception of validity divides it into three separate and substitutable types—namely, content, criterion, and construct validities. This view is fragmented and incomplete, especially because it fails to take into account both evidence of the value implications of score meaning as a basis for action and the social consequences of score use."

**Abstract — the unified concept (p. 741):**

> "The new unified concept of validity interrelates these issues as fundamental aspects of a more comprehensive theory of construct validity that addresses both score meaning and social values in test interpretation and test use. That is, unified validity integrates considerations of content, criteria, and consequences into a construct framework for the empirical testing of rational hypotheses about score meaning and theoretically relevant relationships, including those of an applied and a scientific nature."

**Opening definition — validity is a property of score meaning, not of the test (p. 741):**

> "Validity is an overall evaluative judgment of the degree to which empirical evidence and theoretical rationales support the adequacy and appropriateness of interpretations and actions on the basis of test scores or other modes of assessment (Messick, 1989b). Validity is not a property of the test or assessment as such, but rather of the meaning of the test scores."

**Opening definition — what must be valid is the interpretation (p. 741):**

> "what needs to be valid is the meaning or interpretation of the score; as well as any implications for action that this meaning entails (Cronbach, 1971)."

**"The Value of Validity" — validity as a social value, beyond a correlation coefficient (p. 742):**

> "As a salient social value, validity assumes both a scientific and a political role that can by no means be fulfilled by a simple correlation coefficient between test scores and a purported criterion (i.e., classical criterion-related validity) or by expert judgments that test content is relevant to the proposed test use (i.e., traditional content validity)."

**"The Value of Validity" — construct validity does not equate the score with the construct (against strict operationism) (p. 742):**

> "In construct validation the test score is not equated with the construct it attempts to tap, nor is it considered to define the construct, as in strict operationism (Cronbach & Meehl, 1955). Rather, the measure is viewed as just one of an extensible set of indicators of the construct."

**"Sources of Invalidity" — the two major threats, defined verbatim (p. 742):**

> "There are two major threats to construct validity: In the one known as construct underrepresentation, the assessment is too narrow and fails to include important dimensions or facets of the construct. In the threat to validity known as construct-irrelevant variance, the assessment is too broad, containing excess reliable variance associated with other distinct constructs as well as method variance such as response sets or guessing propensities that affects responses in a manner irrelevant to the interpreted construct."

**"Sources of Invalidity" — both threats are always operative (p. 742):**

> "Both threats are operative in all assessments. Hence a primary validation concern is the extent to which the same assessment might underrepresent the focal construct while simultaneously contaminating the scores with construct-irrelevant variance."

**"Sources of Evidence in Construct Validity" — construct validity as the evidential basis for score interpretation (p. 743):**

> "In its simplest terms, construct validity is the evidential basis for score interpretation."

**"Validity as Integrative Summary" — validation as empirical evaluation of meaning and consequences (p. 747):**

> "validation is empirical evaluation of the meaning and consequences of measurement."

**"Consequences As Validity Evidence" — the two threats restated as the source of invalid adverse impact (p. 746):**

> "low scores should not occur because the assessment is missing something relevant to the focal construct that, if present, would have permitted the affected persons to display their competence. Moreover, low scores should not occur because the measurement contains something irrelevant that interferes with the affected persons' demonstration of competence."

**"Meaning and Values in Test Validation" — value implications integral to score meaning (p. 748):**

> "A central issue is whether the theoretical or trait implications and the value implications of the test interpretation are commensurate, because value implications are not ancillary but, rather, integral to score meaning."

**"Meaning and Values in Test Validation" — values intrinsic, not added (p. 748):**

> "it is simply not the case that values are being added to validity in this unified view. Rather, values are intrinsic to the meaning and outcomes of the testing and have always been."

**"Meaning and Values in Test Validation" — the closing identity of validity and value judgment (p. 748):**

> "This makes explicit what has been latent all along, namely, that validity judgments are value judgments."

**Concluding section — construct validity in every cell as the integrating force (p. 748):**

> "A salient feature of Figure 1 is that construct validity appears in every cell, which is fitting because the construct validity of score meaning is the integrating force that unifies validity issues into a unitary concept."

**Concluding section — the unifying force named as construct validity (p. 744):**

> "Hence, validity becomes a unified concept, and the unifying force is the meaningfulness or trustworthy interpretability of the test scores and their action implications, namely, construct validity."

## What this grounds

- The **precise post-1955 vocabulary** for the project's probe-design rules. The essay [`essay/construct-validity-without-a-criterion`](../../findings/essays/construct-validity-without-a-criterion.md) can now state its mapping in Messick's own terms: its **"design-out-not-model-out"** rule (do not let a probe's score absorb variance from sources extraneous to the focal construct) is, in Messick's vocabulary, the avoidance of **construct-irrelevant variance** — "the assessment is too broad, containing excess reliable variance … irrelevant to the interpreted construct." Its **"presence-is-not-balance"** rule (a construct being represented somewhere in the stimulus set is not the same as the set covering the construct) is, in Messick's vocabulary, the avoidance of **construct underrepresentation** — "the assessment is too narrow and fails to include important dimensions or facets of the construct."
- A **check on the project's reconstruction against the consensus.** The project derived its operationalization-gate discipline largely from scratch. Messick lets it test whether that reconstruction matches the unified-validity consensus that succeeded Cronbach & Meehl: in particular, the relocation of validity from the test to *score meaning* and to the *inferences drawn* ("Validity is not a property of the test … but rather of the meaning of the test scores") matches the project's refusal to read a probe's score as a property of the instrument, and Messick's "two major threats" frame the design discipline as a single balancing problem ("underrepresent the focal construct while simultaneously contaminating the scores with construct-irrelevant variance").
- The **consequential / value dimension** as a way Messick goes *beyond* [`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md). Where Cronbach & Meehl give the nomological-net logic of construct meaning, Messick folds the *social consequences of test use* and the *value implications of score meaning* into construct validity itself ("validity judgments are value judgments"; "values are intrinsic to the meaning and outcomes of the testing"). This is citable when the project's probe-design discipline touches the downstream-use or value-laden interpretation of a contrast, not only its bare measurement.
- The point that **construct validity is the unifying frame.** Messick's "construct validity appears in every cell … the integrating force that unifies validity issues into a unitary concept" is the canonical statement that, once criterion- and content-validation are subsumed, only construct validity remains — which is the same structural conclusion the Cronbach-Meehl page reaches from the no-human-subjects rule (criterion validation removed by design leaves nomological-net construct validity as the only available form).

## What it cannot ground

- **Any claim about any model.** It predates language models and reports no measurement of any LLM. It cannot be an `anchors:` resource — it is not a human-labeled empirical asset (no treebank, sense inventory, acceptability norm, or annotation). Its role is methodology framing only.

## Known limits

- **One paper in a long line.** Messick *refines* the construct-validity tradition founded by [`source/cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity`](cronbach-meehl-1955-construct-validity.md) — unifying the validity types and adding the consequential aspect — but it is not the whole literature. The realist **critique of construct validity (Borsboom)** and the **convergent/discriminant multitrait-multimethod framework (Campbell & Fiske, 1959)** remain separate and uncatalogued; Messick *cites* Campbell & Fiske for the "external aspect" but does not himself originate that vocabulary. Cite Messick as the canonical statement of *unified validity* and the source of *construct underrepresentation* / *construct-irrelevant variance*, not as the last word on validity theory.
- **1995 measurement methodology.** Its examples are educational and psychological performance assessments (placement tests, portfolios, ability/achievement scales). The *logic* transfers; the *examples* are domain-specific and predate the questions this project asks of language models.
- **Underlying text is © 1995 APA** (see Provenance/access note); the Bath mirror provides open access but not a public-domain license. Quotes kept short and attributed.
- **Two source typos reproduced in the original are not quoted.** The extracted text contains "In particluar" (p. 741) and "Comoro" / OCR debris in the photo caption (p. 742); these are artifacts and are not used inside any quoted span.
