# Ortega Andrés 2020 — "Polysemous Nouns and Copredication"

## What it is

A doctoral thesis (Tesis Doctoral) by **Marina Ortega Andrés**, supervised by **Agustín Vicente**, defended **12 June 2020** at the **University of the Basque Country (UPV/EHU)** and deposited in the university's open institutional repository **ADDI** (record `https://addi.ehu.es/handle/10810/50149`; full-text PDF `TESIS_ORTEGA_ANDRES_MARINA.pdf`, ~1.26 MB). The thesis is the long-form development of the same author's open-access *Glossa* article (Ortega-Andrés & Vicente 2019, "Polysemy and co-predication", DOI `10.5334/gjgl.564`), and it quotes that article's own definition of copredication verbatim.

This is a survey/theory source about **human language** — specifically about polysemous nouns (*school*, *book*, *city*, *bottle*, *newspaper*, …) and the phenomenon of **copredication**. It makes no claim about LLMs, supplies no human-annotated dataset, and licenses no claim about any model. The project uses it only as an a-priori interpretive frame (see *What it grounds*).

**Open-access basis (declared honestly).** The verbatim text below was extracted locally from the full-text PDF served by the UPV/EHU institutional repository ADDI (`https://addi.ehu.es/bitstream/handle/10810/50149/TESIS_ORTEGA_ANDRES_MARINA.pdf`), which is openly and directly downloadable (HTTP 200, unencrypted PDF). The ADDI record is OpenAIRE/Recolecta-validated open access; **no explicit Creative Commons license string is printed on the record page**, so the access status is recorded here as "open institutional-repository doctoral thesis, freely downloadable" rather than as a specific CC tier. The companion published *Glossa* article (diamond open-access, CC-BY) was **not** usable for verbatim extraction in this session: the `glossa-journal.org` domain is gated behind an Anubis anti-bot proof-of-work challenge that blocks automated fetching, the PhilArchive postprint PDF (`philarchive.org/archive/ORTPAC-2`) returned HTTP 403, and the Wayback snapshot had itself captured the Anubis challenge page. The thesis was the cleanest verbatim-fetchable route to the same author's copredication account.

## Why it bears on this project (the copredication-by-name gap)

Two in-repo source pages already ground neighbouring pieces of this picture but explicitly leave the **copredication phenomenon, named and treated directly** as a stated want:

- [`source/sennet-2021-ambiguity-sep`](sennet-2021-ambiguity-sep.md) grounds **zeugma / conjunction-reduction as the ambiguity diagnostic** from the philosophy-of-language side.
- [`source/pustejovsky-1991-generative-lexicon`](pustejovsky-1991-generative-lexicon.md) grounds the generative-lexicon machinery (qualia, coercion, the contrastive/complementary distinction) but its own honesty note records that **"the *term* 'copredication' and the explicit dot-object / dot-type apparatus are developed in Pustejovsky's later work … *not in this 1991 article*"**, and that the explicit copredication diagnostics "by name" remain to be ingested.
- [`source/falkum-vicente-2015-polysemy`](falkum-vicente-2015-polysemy.md) records that logical polysemy is "operationally defined as polysemy which passes the conjunction and anaphoric reference tests" — pointing at the copredication diagnostic but not developing it.

This thesis fills exactly the named gap: it (a) **defines copredication** in so many words; (b) gives the canonical copredicative examples (*school*, *book*, *city*, author/work, container/content, food/event); (c) states **copredication as a diagnostic test for polysemy** and what its success vs. failure is taken to show about sense *availability*; (d) frames the **polysemy↔homonymy distinction as a continuum** — at one end closely-related polysemous senses "activate each other, forming activation packages that facilitate copredication", at the other "meanings of homonymous words inhibit each other"; and (e) carries the project's own honesty discipline by stating that copredicability "is … a question of degree rather than an all-or-nothing matter".

**Honesty note on the word "zeugma."** The thesis develops the **copredication test** (whether a single noun can be yoked to two incompatible-seeming predicates at once) and the homonymy↔polysemy contrast, but it **does not use the term "zeugma"** in its body (the word appears only once, in its bibliography, citing Viebahn 2018). The dedicated grounding for the *term* "zeugma" and the conjunction-reduction diagnostic under that name remains [`source/sennet-2021-ambiguity-sep`](sennet-2021-ambiguity-sep.md); this page grounds the **copredication** side of the same family of tests. The two are complementary, not redundant: Sennet supplies "zeugma as the diagnostic for ambiguity", this thesis supplies "copredication as the diagnostic for *available, co-present* senses, and homonyms as the inhibitory pole that fails it."

## Key passages (verbatim, ligatures expanded and line-breaks rejoined as declared)

**Definition of copredication (§1.1.5 "What is copredication?", quoting the author's own 2019 Glossa article) — the load-bearing definition:**

> "Copredication is the phenomenon in which the same polysemous nominal expression comes along with simultaneous predications for two (or more) different meanings or senses of the word in a sentence (taken from: Ortega-Andrés & Vicente 2019)."

Immediately followed by the canonical examples (reproduced verbatim):

> "(14) The books are thick and interesting.
> (15) The school caught fire and was celebrating 4th of July when the fire started.
> (16) The city has 500,000 inhabitants and outlawed smoking in bars last year."

**Copredication used as a diagnostic test for polysemy, and what its success/failure shows about sense availability (Ch. 5, Introduction to Part II):**

> "copredication has been used as a diagnostic test for polysemy (Copestake & Briscoe, 1995, Cruse, 1986, Jezek & Vieu, 2014), which means that the availability of copredication is taken to reflect straight-forward access to the different related senses, while failed copredication tests indicate that one of the senses is currently not available."

**The polysemy↔homonymy *continuum*: closely-related senses form activation packages that facilitate copredication, homonymous meanings inhibit each other (thesis summary / Introduction):**

> "My proposal is that the distinction between polysemy and homonomy [sic] should be understood as a continuum. At one end, some senses of some polysemous words are so closely related that they activate each other, forming activation packages that facilitate copredication. At the other end, meanings of homonymous words inhibit each other."

**Copredicability is graded, not all-or-nothing — the honesty caveat the project should carry forward (Ch. 5):**

> "The incompatibility of the two apparently polysemous senses is heavily context-bound and is thus a question of degree rather than an all-or-nothing matter, which means that copredication should not be used as a test for determining whether a word is polysemous or not."

With the illustrative *dissertation* contrast (reproduced verbatim; `?` is the thesis's own infelicity mark):

> "(54) ? Judy's dissertation is thought provoking and yellowed with age.
> (55) Judy's dissertation is still thought provoking although yellowed with age."

## What it grounds in this project (relevance)

- **[`essay/layer-specialness-vs-always-resolvability`](../../findings/essays/layer-specialness-vs-always-resolvability.md) — strengthens the lexical grounding of "genuinely-unresolvable / forced-both" item.** That essay's provenance note flags as its weakest point the want for "a semantics/lexicography source on … zeugma, and co-predication". This thesis supplies the **copredication** half of that want, *by name and in full*: it grounds the contrast between a **forced-both** item (closely-related polysemous senses that are *co-present* and jointly licensed in a copredicative sentence — "activation packages that facilitate copredication") and a **rival / disjunctive** item (homonymous senses that "inhibit each other" and fail to copredicate, so committing to one reading discards the other). This is exactly the rival-vs-forced-both distinction the essay rests on. It grounds the *framing*, not any empirical re-reading of model behavior.
- **[`essay/presence-is-not-balance`](../../findings/essays/presence-is-not-balance.md) — grounds the "co-present vs. disambiguated" axis.** The copredication phenomenon is a worked human-language case where two senses are *simultaneously present* (predicated at once) rather than one being selected and the other discarded; the all-or-nothing-vs-degree caveat further grounds the essay's gradience commitment.
- **[`concept/polysemy`](../concepts/polysemy.md) — adds copredication and the activation-package account.** It sharpens the page's "related senses" notion with the copredication diagnostic and the continuum framing (related senses facilitate copredication; homonymous senses inhibit each other), complementing [`source/falkum-vicente-2015-polysemy`](falkum-vicente-2015-polysemy.md) (regular/irregular carving, conjunction+anaphora tests) and [`source/pustejovsky-1991-generative-lexicon`](pustejovsky-1991-generative-lexicon.md) (qualia/coercion machinery).

## What it cannot ground

- **Any empirical claim about LLMs.** A human-language doctoral thesis with no labeled resource. It cannot serve as the `anchors:` resource for any claim or result; it bears on *framing* only.
- **The term "zeugma" / the conjunction-reduction diagnostic under that name.** The thesis does not use "zeugma" in its argument; that grounding stays with [`source/sennet-2021-ambiguity-sep`](sennet-2021-ambiguity-sep.md). This page grounds **copredication** specifically.
- **A graded measurement instrument.** It supplies a conceptual frame (copredication; the continuum; degrees of copredicability), not a graded human-rated scale or annotations. The wedge's graded measurement still comes from the usage-similarity resources at [`resource/wic-graded-usage-similarity`](../resources/wic-graded-usage-similarity.md) (DWUG / WiC).
- **A settled formal partition.** The thesis itself argues the polysemy/homonymy boundary is a *continuum* and that copredicability is a matter of degree; treat the carving as a useful frame, not a sharp rule.

## Map/reference, not a human anchor

This page is a **reference/theory source**. It grounds *framing* — the definition of copredication, copredication as a diagnostic for sense availability, and the polysemy↔homonymy continuum (forced-both vs. rival/inhibitory poles) — that the project's lexical wedge and the two essays above presuppose. It makes **no empirical claim about LLMs**, supplies **no human-annotated resource**, and **cannot** serve as the `anchors:` resource for any claim or result.
