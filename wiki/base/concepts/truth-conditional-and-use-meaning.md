# Truth-conditional meaning vs. meaning-as-use

This page owns one axis of the meaning-theory debate: **truth-conditions ↔ use**. It is the oldest fault line under the project's lexical and grammatical wedges, and it is the one that decides what an LLM's behavior could even *be evidence for*. Compositionality, prototype/frame category structure, semantic holism, and the deflationary/eliminativist positioning each have their own sibling page and are linked, not developed, here.

## The two poles

**(a) Truth-conditional / model-theoretic semantics.** On this view, to know the meaning of a (declarative) sentence is to know the conditions under which it would be true; meaning bottoms out in *reference* (what words pick out) and *truth-in-a-model* (how the picked-out things make sentences true or false). The lineage is, in its standard textbook telling (Frege, Tarski 1933/1944, and Davidson 1967 are now **in-repo** at primary strength — see the per-author links below; the Montague characterization is now in-repo at **secondary** strength via the SEP survey [`source/janssen-zimmermann-montague-semantics-sep`](../sources/janssen-zimmermann-montague-semantics-sep.md), with the **PTQ primary itself still not in-repo / wanted**): Frege's distinction between sense and reference (`Über Sinn und Bedeutung`, 1892; **now in-repo**, [`source/frege-1892-sense-and-reference`](../sources/frege-1892-sense-and-reference.md)) gave the referential side its modern shape, with `Bedeutung` as the truth-relevant semantic value of an expression — for a declarative sentence, its `Wahrheitswerth` (truth-value), its sense the thought (`Gedanke`) expressed (Frege pp. 32-34); Tarski's recursive definition of truth for a formalized language (1933/1944; **now in-repo**, [`source/tarski-1944-semantic-conception-of-truth`](../sources/tarski-1944-semantic-conception-of-truth.md)) made "is true" a precise notion via **satisfaction** — "a sentence is true if it is satisfied by all objects" (§11) — disciplined by the material-adequacy criterion (Convention T), whose schema is "(T) X is true if, and only if, p" with the running instance "the sentence 'snow is white' is true if, and only if, snow is white" (§4); Davidson (1967; **now in-repo**, [`source/davidson-1967-truth-and-meaning`](../sources/davidson-1967-truth-and-meaning.md)) proposed that a Tarski-style truth theory could serve as a theory of *meaning* for a natural language — in his own words, "to give truth conditions is a way of giving the meaning of a sentence" (p. 310), borrowing Tarski's Convention T as the adequacy test while *reversing* its order of explanation (truth taken as the illuminating primitive rather than meaning/translation; the reversal is the standard reading, not a Davidson quote); and Montague (PTQ, 1973) showed a fragment of English could be handled with the model-theoretic machinery of intensional logic, treating natural language as a formal language with a denotational semantics — in his own words (quoted in the SEP survey, [`source/janssen-zimmermann-montague-semantics-sep`](../sources/janssen-zimmermann-montague-semantics-sep.md), §1.1), "There is in my opinion no important theoretical difference between natural languages and the artificial languages of logicians." The unifying commitment: meaning is, at its core, a *world-relation* — a function from expressions to truth-conditions or denotations — and the semantics of a complex expression is built up systematically from the denotations of its parts. (The systematicity half of that commitment is exactly [`compositionality.md`](compositionality.md)'s lane; the world-relation half is [`concept/referential-meaning`](referential-meaning.md)'s.)

**(b) Meaning-as-use / the Wittgensteinian pole.** Against fixing meaning to a determinate truth-condition, the later Wittgenstein (*Philosophical Investigations*, 1953; **now in-repo** at primary strength for the core ideas, [`source/wittgenstein-1953-philosophical-investigations`](../sources/wittgenstein-1953-philosophical-investigations.md)) urged that the meaning of an expression is the *role it plays* in the practices — the "language-games" (`Sprachspiele`) — embedded in a "form of life." His own formulation is carefully **qualified**: "for a *large* class of cases of the use of the word 'meaning' — though not for *all* cases of its use — … the meaning of a word is its use in the language" (§43, project gloss of the PD German; the heavily-memorized slogan is only the second half and drops the restricting clause). The slogan reading is "don't look for the meaning, look for the use." Two further moves matter for this project. First, **family resemblance**: many of our general words (the standing example is *game*) are held together not by a shared set of necessary-and-sufficient conditions but by a network of overlapping, criss-crossing similarities — there need be no definitional core. Second, the rejection of a fixed, fully-determinate hidden sense behind ordinary words: what an expression means is exhausted by how it is used in practice, not by a private object or a sharp boundary the use is answerable to. On this pole, a meaning theory describes regularities of use; it does not reduce them to a denotation.

The two poles are not strictly contradictory about every word — a truth-conditional semanticist can grant that *use* is the evidence we have, and a use theorist can grant that some sentences have clear truth-conditions. They come apart on **where meaning ultimately lives**: in a relation to the world (pole a) or in patterns of practice (pole b). That is the choice this page is about.

## The said / implicated layer: a finer cut inside truth-conditional content

Pole (a) is not homogeneous. Not everything an utterance conveys belongs to its truth-conditional content, and the refinement that matters here is Grice's distinction between *what is said* — the at-issue, truth-conditional content a speaker asserts — and *what is implicated* — further content the speaker conveys without asserting it. This cut is now grounded in an in-repo source: [`source/davis-2024-implicature-sep`](../sources/davis-2024-implicature-sep.md), Wayne Davis's SEP survey of implicature. Davis fixes the technical vocabulary: "Grice introduced the technical terms _implicate_ and _implicature_ for the case in which what the speaker said is distinct from what the speaker thereby meant or implied" (§1). The notational convention the entry then uses throughout is to "use parentheses to refer to the sentences in an example like (3), and brackets to refer to what the sentences express. So (3c) is the sentence "Being brave follows from being English" and [3c] is the proposition that being brave follows from being English" (§2). The upshot for this page: within the truth-conditional pole there is an *at-issue* layer (what is said) and a *not-at-issue* layer (what is implicated), and an instrument can be aimed at one without reaching the other.

Davis's survey separates two species of the implicated content, each with verbatim diagnostics.

**Conversational implicature (including the generalized / scalar case — the `some`/`all` pattern).** Its marks are (i) *cancelability*: "it is widely accepted that generalized conversational implicatures differ from semantic implicatures in being _cancelable,_ either explicitly or implicitly (contextually)" (§2); (ii) *calculability* from the Cooperative Principle — "Conversational implicatures must be capable of being worked out", where "To work out an implicature is to infer it in a specific way from the Cooperative Principle using particular facts about the meaning of the sentence uttered and the context of utterance" (§6); and (iii) the load-bearing diagnostic for this page, that such content is generally *not entailed* by what is said: "Whereas semantic implicatures are generally entailed by what is said, many believe that conversational implicatures cannot be" (§2). That last point is the standard view, not an exceptionless law, and Davis tempers it immediately — "As (1), (2), (5), and (6) illustrate, conversational implicatures are _typically_ not entailments. But exceptions have been noted" (§2) — so the page carries it as a *typical*, hedged property, not a theorem. The scalar `some`/`all` case is the running illustration: uttering "Some athletes smoke" generates the upper-bounding implicate "Not all athletes smoke", but a speaker "may do this explicitly by adding "Indeed, all do" after uttering "Some athletes smoke"" (§2) without contradiction — the cancelation that marks it as conversational, not semantic.

**Conventional implicature (the "therefore" / trigger-word case).** This species is tied to the conventional (lexical) meaning of particular words and is, in the standard telling, *implied rather than said* — not part of the at-issue truth-conditional content. Davis's worked example is (3a) "The queen is English and therefore brave": "By using (3a) to say and mean [3a], speakers implicate [3c]. That is, by using (3a) to say and mean that the queen is English and therefore brave, speakers implicate that being brave follows from being English. They _imply_ rather than _say_ that being brave follows from being English" (§2). The carrying words are lexical triggers: "Other words "triggering" conventional implicatures are _but, even, too, still, yet, already, again, stop, start, know,_ and _regret_" (§2).

Why this cut matters for the project, **at the construct level** (the arm-by-arm application is elsewhere; this section establishes only the construct and the a-priori expectation it licenses). The project's inferential indicator — 3-way NLI — is, by construction, a probe of *what is said*: a truth-conditional / entailment instrument tracks at-issue content. Davis's typology therefore predicts *a priori*, before any data, that such an instrument *should* register a closed-class swap that changes at-issue truth-conditional content, and *may be blind to* — or legitimately diverge on — one whose force is carried by a cancelable *scalar implicature*, precisely because on the standard view "many believe that conversational implicatures cannot be" entailed by what is said (§2). This is the interpretive frame the function-word essay [`essay/function-words-not-one-category`](../../findings/essays/function-words-not-one-category.md) uses to fire its revision trigger (d), and [`result/function-word-few-many-split`](../../findings/results/function-word-few-many-split.md) is the arm where such a divergence is actually observed — but the arm-by-arm reading belongs to that essay; this section asserts only the construct and the expectation. (That application is flagged here as **the project's interpretive use of Davis, not a claim Davis makes**: his entry is about human language and speakers' meaning, and asserts nothing about what an instrument or a model computes.)

Honest bounds, kept tight. **(i)** The typology is about *human language and speakers' meaning* — the semantics/pragmatics interface — not about what an LLM computes; applying it to label-behavior is an interpretive frame, and producing labels consistent with an implicature is not the same as computing one. **(ii)** The non-entailment of conversational implicature is the *standard view Davis reports*, and it is itself hedged and contested ("many believe", "exceptions have been noted", §2); cite it as a well-motivated frame, not as ground truth about what entailment must do. **(iii)** This is a cut *internal to* the truth-conditional pole — at-issue versus not-at-issue content — so it does **not** by itself settle this page's main truth-conditional-vs-use question. It refines pole (a); it does not adjudicate the metaphysics between (a) and (b).

## Where the project sits — a methodological reading (not a metaphysical verdict)

This section is an **interpretation of the project's method, flagged as such** — a working commitment, not a proven claim about what meaning *is*.

The project's primary instrument is behavioral API probing: it reads a model's "meaning" off **patterns of use** — same/different-sense ratings, graded relatedness judgments, acceptability, inference behavior under minimal pairs. That is methodologically a *use*-side stance by construction. Crucially, the project has **no verified truth-condition or world-relation for any LLM**: as [`concept/referential-meaning`](referential-meaning.md) records, "there is **no comparable in-repo resource that anchors reference**: no dataset that pins a human-agreed extension or reference relation an LLM could be tested against" (§"Live tension"). So the truth-conditional pole is not refuted here — it is *un-probed*, and un-probeable with the resources in hand.

The charter's "describe, don't litigate" posture and the project's recurring **distributional null** (apparent meaning-tracking must be shown to beat a context-similarity / form shadow before it counts) both sit closer to the use pole than to the truth-conditional one: they treat the regularity of use as the thing to characterize, and stay agnostic about an underlying denotational fact. **This is a bet the method makes**, and it should be stated plainly rather than smuggled in: probing use does not *show* that meaning is use; it shows that use is what this instrument can see. A truth-conditional theorist could agree with every measured number and still hold that the world-relation — the thing the instrument cannot reach — is where meaning really resides.

## Map to the lexical findings

- **[`result/lexical-sense-gradience-v1`](../../findings/results/lexical-sense-gradience-v1.md) — SUPPORTS the use pole over a discrete sense inventory.** (Since promoted, with its cross-date replication, into [`claim/lexical-sense-gradience`](../../findings/claims/lexical-sense-gradience.md), s176.) Across 200 DWUG EN within-period usage pairs, every panel model's graded sense-relatedness rating is strongly rank-correlated with the human DURel median: Spearman **0.68 / 0.60 / 0.80** (claude-sonnet-4.6 / gpt-5.4-mini / gemini-3.5-flash) on the 4-point DURel framing and **0.70 / 0.68 / 0.83** on the 0–100 framing — in or above the human inter-annotator range (DWUG EN human–human ρ ≈ 0.69) — and the monotonicity *survives* partialling out the model's own topic-similarity rating (gemini 0.80→0.73). The signal is **graded usage-relatedness**, not a near-binary same/different collapse onto a fixed inventory of senses. That is a *family-resemblance / meaning-as-use* picture of word sense: relatedness is scalar and read off use, not a step function over discrete denotations. The human-side echo is the Erk/McCarthy/Gaylord finding quotable via [`resource/wic-graded-usage-similarity`](../resources/wic-graded-usage-similarity.md): "We find that the graded responses correlate with annotations from previous datasets, but sense assignments are used in a way that weakens the case for clear cut sense boundaries." (citing Erk, McCarthy & Gaylord, ACL 2009, https://aclanthology.org/P09-1002.pdf). *Honest bound:* this is a behavioral rank correlation, not evidence of graded sense *representations*, and the topic control is model-internal (see the result's own caveats); it supports the use pole's picture of sense, it does not prove the truth-conditional picture false.

- **[`result/lexical-polysemy-homonymy-v3`](../../findings/results/lexical-polysemy-homonymy-v3.md) — STRAINS the classical truth-conditional picture; SILENT on its strongest form.** The classical truth-conditional/lexicographic expectation is *discrete senses with a sharp polysemy/homonymy switch* — homonyms are two entries, polysemes one, and a competent system should treat them as different *kinds*. v3 is a **powered null** on exactly that switch: the panel's graded rating does **not** separate WiC same/different-sense pairs any better for homonyms than for polysemes (AUC_homonym − AUC_polyseme ≈ 0 in all 6 model×framing cells; permutation p 0.73–0.96), which strains the "discrete switch" expectation and is consistent with a single graded-use axis (homonyms simply sitting further out on it). The one positive — homonym different-sense pairs piling at the "unrelated" floor more than polyseme-F pairs — **cannot be separated from plain graded distance** (homonyms *are* more unrelated; it is half-driven by one lemma `case`, partly a gemini scale-use quirk, and its clustering-honest CIs cross zero). *Be scrupulous:* the result page states its design **cannot distinguish a discrete sense boundary from monotone graded distance** at all. So on the *strongest* truth-conditional claim — that a sharp discrete regime exists underneath — v3 is **SILENT, not decisive**; it removes a positive sign that a discreteness advocate would have wanted, nothing more. A null is "no detectable effect, or underpowered-for-the-discrete-reading," never "discreteness proven absent."

- **The truth-conditional pole is the SILENT cell for LLMs here.** No in-repo resource anchors *truth-conditions* or a *world-relation* for any model (the gap [`concept/referential-meaning`](referential-meaning.md) flags). Truth-conditional LLM meaning is un-probed **by construction** — the project measures use, and has no yardstick for the world-relation. Every "support for the use pole" above is therefore *relative*: it is support for the use picture over a *discrete-sense* construal, not a measured defeat of a truth-conditional one.

## Tensions and where they are developed

These are flagged links, not arguments to be made here:

- Truth-conditional semantics is the home of **systematic composition** ([`compositionality.md`](compositionality.md)) and tends to be **classical about categories** (necessary-and-sufficient definition) — whereas the category-structure evidence the project touches leans graded/usage-based, which is [`frame-and-prototype-semantics.md`](frame-and-prototype-semantics.md)'s lane.
- A strong use stance shades into **semantic holism** — if meaning is role-in-a-practice, an expression's meaning may depend on indefinitely much of the rest of the language; that consequence is [`semantic-holism.md`](semantic-holism.md)'s to develop.
- The use pole connects naturally to **inferential role** ([`concept/inferential-meaning`](inferential-meaning.md)): "what an expression licenses you to infer" is one disciplined way to make "use" precise, and Piantadosi & Hill's "meaning without reference" line is the in-repo statement that meaning need not bottom out in extra-linguistic reference — "there are many terms that are meaningful to us but have no discernible referent at all, such as abstract words like 'justice' and 'wit.'" ([`source/piantadosi-hill-2022-meaning-without-reference`](../sources/piantadosi-hill-2022-meaning-without-reference.md), §"Meaning and reference"). The hard truth-conditional rejoinder in-repo is Bender & Koller's: meaning is "the relation M ⊆ E × I which contains pairs (e, i) of natural language expressions e and the communicative intents i they can be used to evoke" ([`source/bender-koller-2020-climbing`](../sources/bender-koller-2020-climbing.md), p. 5187), and form-only training "will not learn meaning" because there is "not sufficient signal to learn the relation M between that form and the non-linguistic intent of human language users" (p. 5187) — a world/intent-relation requirement the use pole denies is constitutive.
- Whether *any* of this should be read as the model having meaning at all, or merely as a useful redescription of token statistics, is the **deflationary/eliminativist** question ([`deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning.md`](deflationary-and-eliminativist-llm-meaning.md)) — the pole this page's "use" reading is most easily mistaken for, and which that page keeps distinct.

## Honest gaps

Of the truth-conditional lineage, **Frege 1892, Tarski 1944, and Davidson 1967 are now all in-repo** at primary strength: Frege ([`source/frege-1892-sense-and-reference`](../sources/frege-1892-sense-and-reference.md), session 110 — `Sinn`/`Bedeutung`, sentence-reference-as-truth-value, quoted verbatim with locators); Tarski ([`source/tarski-1944-semantic-conception-of-truth`](../sources/tarski-1944-semantic-conception-of-truth.md), session 116 — Convention T, satisfaction, object/meta-language, the Liar, §-located; the 1944 article *outlines* the satisfaction recursion the unread 1933 monograph gives in full); Davidson ([`source/davidson-1967-truth-and-meaning`](../sources/davidson-1967-truth-and-meaning.md), session 116 — truth-conditions-as-meaning, finitude, holism, Convention-T dependence, page-located). And on the use pole, **Wittgenstein 1953 is now in-repo** ([`source/wittgenstein-1953-philosophical-investigations`](../sources/wittgenstein-1953-philosophical-investigations.md), session 115 — meaning-as-use §43, language-games §§7/23, family resemblance §§66–67, with verbatim locators; primary-direct German PD for the core, English carried-via-secondary, the §201/§243 rule-following/private-language strand at lower secondary-only strength). The **Montague characterization is now in-repo at secondary strength** ([`source/janssen-zimmermann-montague-semantics-sep`](../sources/janssen-zimmermann-montague-semantics-sep.md), session 124 — the SEP survey by Janssen & Zimmermann, ten §-located quotes double-fetch-confirmed: the compositionality principle and its algebraic homomorphism form §§1.2/3.2, the model-theoretic core §§1.1/1.2, the rule-to-rule hypothesis §§1.2/2.3, context-dependence pushed to pragmatics §3.4); so references to "the model-theoretic / Montague tradition" on this page now rest on a citable in-repo source. **Only the Montague *primary* (PTQ 1973 and the 1970 companion papers) remains not-in-repo** and stays on the `wanted.md` backlog — the survey is secondary-strength and does not stand in for the primary text. The *said / implicated* typology in §"The said / implicated layer" **is** now backed by an in-repo source — [`source/davis-2024-implicature-sep`](../sources/davis-2024-implicature-sep.md), quoted verbatim with §-locators — so the verbatim quotes on this page are the three earlier in-repo ones (Bender & Koller p. 5187; Piantadosi & Hill §"Meaning and reference"; Erk/McCarthy/Gaylord via the WiC-graded resource page) **plus** the Davis implicature quotes (§§1, 2, 6). That entry grounds the *implicature* layer only, the Frege ingest (session 110) discharges the **Frege** leg of the truth-conditional gap, the Tarski + Davidson ingests (session 116) discharge the **truth-definition machinery** and the **truth-conditions-as-meaning** legs, and the Wittgenstein ingest (session 115) discharges the **use pole's founding text** for its core ideas; and the Montague characterization is now carried by the SEP survey (session 124), leaving only the **Montague PTQ/1970 *primary*** not-in-repo. The project's stance toward the two poles is a **methodological commitment**, not an established metaphysics, as §"Where the project sits" states.
