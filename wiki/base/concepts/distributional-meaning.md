# Distributional meaning

**Stub.** Will expand when a finding needs it.

The position: a linguistic item's meaning is constituted (or at minimum tracked) by its distribution over contexts. "You shall know a word by the company it keeps" (Firth 1957). Harris (1954) gives the formal statement.

For this project:

- The implicit theory behind word embeddings and next-token-prediction objectives.
- The natural anchor for any claim about LLM "meaning" that bottoms out in co-occurrence structure.
- Notably *silent* on reference and on truth — the contested boundary with `referential` and `grounded`.
