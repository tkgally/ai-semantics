# Grounding

**Stub.**

Two contrasting positions:

- **Bender & Koller (2020), "Climbing towards NLU".** Strong form: meaning requires grounding in communicative intent and the world; form-only systems (LLMs trained on text alone) cannot in principle learn meaning. The octopus thought experiment is the canonical illustration.
- **Lyre (2024), "Understanding AI: Semantic grounding in large language models".** Grounding is *gradual*, not binary; functional, social, and causal sub-grounds can be partially satisfied. This frame is the natural fit for a constructive (not adjudicative) project.

Sub-tags to use in `meaning-senses:`:

- `grounded.perceptual` — multimodal anchoring; the text+image case bites this.
- `grounded.causal` — tracking of intervention/consequence structure.
- `grounded.social` — uptake in joint action, normative status; bleeds into `relational`.
