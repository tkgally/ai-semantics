# Formal vs. functional linguistic competence

**Stub.**

Mahowald, Ivanova, Blank, Kanwisher, Tenenbaum, Fedorenko (2024), "Dissociating language and thought in large language models" (*Trends in Cognitive Sciences*).

The distinction:

- **Formal competence** — knowing the patterns of a language: morphology, syntax, lexical co-occurrence. LLMs are uncontroversially strong here.
- **Functional competence** — using language for reasoning, world-modeling, social action. The contested zone.

For this project: when a finding hinges on whether an LLM behavior is on the formal or functional side of that line, tag it with `functional-vs-formal`. This is especially useful for constructional findings, where the construction's *form* can be learned distributionally but its *function* (what speakers use it to do) may not be.
