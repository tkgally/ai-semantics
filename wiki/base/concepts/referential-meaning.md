# Referential meaning

**Stub.**

Frege's `Sinn` / `Bedeutung` distinction; the externalist tradition (Putnam, Burge, Evans). For the project's purposes, three sub-positions are worth keeping separate:

- **sense** (`referential.sense`) — mode of presentation; how the referent is given.
- **reference** (`referential.reference`) — what the expression picks out in a world or model.
- **externalist** (`referential.externalist`) — meaning fixed partially by causal-historical and social embedding (Twin Earth; division of linguistic labor).

The LLM-meaning debate's hardest cases live here: an LLM trivially tracks something like sense (distributional context), but the case for reference is contested, and the externalist conditions (causal contact, social embedding) are exactly where Bender & Koller's "form-only" critique bites.
