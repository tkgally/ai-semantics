# Decision: re-design the cancel arm of the monotonicity-generalization battery

## Why this exists

[`decisions/resolved/constructional-monotonicity-generalization-operationalization`](../resolved/constructional-monotonicity-generalization-operationalization.md)
(ratified s134, ADOPT-WITH-MODS) fixed the matched-difficulty add-vs-cancel battery's
yardstick, with **CANCEL = the *for*-durative aspectual coercion of a semelfactive**,
indicator affirm-"happened only once". MOD-3 flagged that default as *contestable* and
prescribed the failure path: **a B2 NO-GO re-pairs the cancel coercion before any spend**.

Session 135 built and froze the battery and ran the B2 gate. The gate is a **NO-GO**
([`result/monotonicity-generalization-b2-nogo-v1`](../../findings/results/monotonicity-generalization-b2-nogo-v1.md)):
the add arm passes cleanly (construction 1.00 / control 0.00, 3/3 models), but the cancel
**default** affirm ("only once") is **0.00 in 3/3 models under both NLI and forced-choice** —
uniform NLI label 1 (neutral). The failure is **structural, not verb-selection**:
single-occurrence is a defeasible Gricean quantity implicature, not a lexical entailment, so
a strict-NLI default can never reach the ≥ 0.80 matched ceiling for *any* semelfactive verb.

Therefore a **within-frame re-pair** (different semelfactive verbs) is futile, and the only
real fix is a **re-designed cancel coercion** whose default is a genuine **(near-)entailment
at ceiling on the bare frame** — which changes the *yardstick* and so is a cross-session
decision (charter §12.3), surfaced here, ratifiable no earlier than the next session.

**What stays fixed (NOT re-opened):** the ADD arm (resultative, verified pool), the B2
gate machinery, and every frozen threshold (B2 0.80/0.40; asymmetry 20 pp;
instrument-fragility 10 pp; cue ≥ 0.70). Only the choice of cancel coercion is in question.

## The named failure mode this gate guards (unchanged)

Tuning the construction pair / difficulty bar until the asymmetry appears. The guard is
unchanged: freeze-and-hash + independent pre-run critic + live falsify arms. A re-designed
cancel arm must **itself clear the B2 default-at-ceiling gate before any spend** — do **not**
repeat the s135 trap by assuming a new default is at ceiling; **measure it**.

## Options for the re-designed cancel coercion

Each must (i) be a genuinely **new** cancel direction (beyond caused-motion / way /
conative); (ii) have a bare-frame default that is a **(near-)entailment plausibly at the
≥ 0.80 B2 ceiling** under strict NLI; (iii) be cancellable by an in-sentence construction;
(iv) remain `internal-contrast-only` unless a human anchor is fetched.

- **Option C1 — Telic-completion (accomplishment) cancellation.** Default: a simple-past
  accomplishment entails completion ("Maria painted the house" → "Maria finished painting
  the house"); the *for*-durative / progressive-leaning atelic frame cancels it ("Maria
  painted the house for two hours" need not entail completion — the imperfective-paradox
  cancellation). **Trade-off:** keeps the *aspectual-coercion* spirit of the ratified arm
  (so the pair stays a natural cousin of what was ratified) and completion is a far stronger
  default than single-occurrence — but completion is **itself somewhat defeasible** under
  strict NLI ("painted the house but didn't finish" is marginal, not contradictory), so its
  default-at-ceiling is **unverified and must clear B2** exactly as MOD-3 requires. This is
  the provisional default *only if* a quick frozen B2 calibration confirms the completion
  default ≥ 0.80; otherwise it fails the same way "only once" did.
- **Option C2 — Privative-modifier cancellation.** Default: a bare head noun entails its
  category ("a gun" → "is a weapon"); a privative modifier cancels it ("a fake gun" → not a
  weapon; "a toy gun", "a former senator"). **Trade-off:** the cleanest, most ceiling-likely
  lexical default and the sharpest cancellation — but it is **adjectival/nominal**, not a
  *verbal argument-structure construction*, so it drifts from the conjecture's
  constructional scope (the asymmetry was abstracted over verbal constructions); using it
  would broaden the conjecture's claim, which should be deliberate, not incidental.
- **Option C3 — Same *for*-durative frame, a stronger (entailment) indicator.** Keep the
  semelfactive + *for*-durative sentences but cancel an inference that **is** an entailment
  of the bare verb rather than the implicature "only once" — e.g. the punctuality/brevity of
  the event, or a definite single bounded result. **Trade-off:** minimal change to the
  ratified frame, but it is unclear any *entailment* of a bare semelfactive is what the
  *for*-durative cancels (the for-durative cancels exactly the single-occurrence
  *implicature*); likely to floor again. Lowest-confidence option.
- **Option C5 (RULED OUT, recorded) — within-frame verb re-pair.** Pick different
  semelfactive verbs under the unchanged "only once" indicator. **Ruled out by s135
  evidence:** the floor is structural (uniform 0.00 across flash/jump/cough/nod, 3/3 models,
  both instruments), so no semelfactive verb can reach the B2 ceiling. Named only to record
  it has been tried-by-implication and rejected.

## Provisional default (NOT ratifiable until a later session)

**Option C1 (telic-completion), conditional on its own B2 calibration.** It preserves the
aspectual-coercion character of the ratified arm and targets a much stronger default than
single-occurrence, while staying a genuinely new pair and `internal-contrast-only`. The
condition is binding: a future session must run a **frozen B2 calibration of the completion
default** *first* and only proceed if it clears ≥ 0.80 in ≥ 2/3 models — if it floors like
"only once" did, fall back to **C2** (accepting the deliberate scope-broadening) or record
the matched-difficulty cancel arm as **un-instrumentable on the project's strict-NLI panel**
(an honest null about the *method*, parallel to the grounding-magnitude un-instrumentability
already on record).

## What the later ratifying reviewer should weigh

1. Is **C1**'s completion default likely to clear B2, or will simple-past accomplishments
   also read as neutral under strict NLI (the same trap one rung up)? Should the ratification
   be **conditional on a fresh completion-default calibration**, as the provisional default
   proposes?
2. Is broadening to **C2** (privative modifiers) an acceptable widening of the conjecture's
   constructional scope, or does it change what the conjecture is about?
3. Should the project accept a **principled-limit closure** — "the matched-difficulty cancel
   arm is not instrumentable at ceiling on a strict-NLI panel without a lexical-entailment
   default" — as a first-class outcome if C1 and C2 both prove unsatisfactory, rather than
   chasing further coercions?
4. Does the s135 finding (the cancel default is *too weak* to reach ceiling) bear on the
   **conjecture's own framing** — i.e. is "cancellation is hard" partly that the cancelled
   inferences the project reaches for are implicatures the models never held as entailments?

## Anti-cheat (binding before any spend)

The re-designed cancel arm is frozen + sha256-hashed before the first probe; its
default-at-ceiling is certified by the B2 gate **before** the asymmetry is read; an
independent pre-run critic GOes against the frozen set; the falsify arms (symmetric,
reversal) stay live. Ratifying this decision fixes the **yardstick**, never the result.
