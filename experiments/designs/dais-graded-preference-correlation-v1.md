## Why this unit is owed now — the one thing the dative line has never had

The dative line is the project's flagship information-structure result, and it is thoroughly built out:
[`result/dative-information-structure-powered`](../../wiki/findings/results/dative-information-structure-powered.md)
attaches a within-item **shift** magnitude at N=100 (claude +0.316 [0.298,0.334], gemini +0.524
[0.506,0.542], gpt +0.056 [0.039,0.074]), promoted to
[`claim/dative-information-structure-givenness`](../../wiki/findings/claims/dative-information-structure-givenness.md).
But that magnitude is a **within-model** contrast — how far *this model* moves across a discourse-context
manipulation. The line has **never compared its effect size against a human effect size.** The claim's own
Anchor section says so plainly: it is anchored on its *direction* leg only (Bresnan et al. 2007 production
corpus), with **no per-item human acceptability rating and no human effect-size anchor** for the graded
surface.

The s244 license scout and s245 ratification changed the input, not yet the measurement. DAIS is now
verified (CC BY 4.0, firsthand), mirrored, inspected, and **adopted as a scoped secondary
graded-acceptability companion** — grounding the *definiteness/length preference surface* and the
conjecture's graded-acceptability clause (see the resolved decision). What remains is the measurement the
adoption deliberately did **not** perform: run the panel and report a **human-vs-model graded correlation**.
This design is that measurement — the dative line's first human effect-size comparison, **scoped to the
definiteness/length + verb-bias preference surface** (not the discourse-context givenness shift), wanted since
founding.

## The load-bearing scope fence (carry from the s245 resolution — read before citing anything this produces)

DAIS grounds the **definiteness/length preference surface**, **not** the project's discourse-context
within-item givenness shift. The two are different instruments:

- **The project's tested claim** manipulates **discourse-context givenness** on **byte-identical** phrasings
  (the sentence letter-for-letter identical across an item's contexts; the shift is immune to length /
  definiteness / order by construction). Its anchor stays Bresnan production, direction-only. **This design
  does not touch it, and produces no magnitude for it.**
- **DAIS** varies recipient/theme **definiteness and length** on **isolated** sentence pairs (no discourse
  context). This design's model measure matches DAIS's instrument — a **bare** graded preference between the
  two phrasings, **no discourse context** — so it correlates against the definiteness/length gradient DAIS
  actually measured. It is a **NEW** measurement on a **different** surface, not a re-scoring of the
  givenness claim.

So a result from this design anchors to DAIS for the **definiteness/length / verb-bias preference surface
only**, and must state at the top that it is **not** a human-effect-size comparison for the discourse-context
givenness shift (which has none, by design). Wiring DAIS here is honest precisely because the measurement
*is* the definiteness/length surface DAIS grounds — the exact opposite of bolting a definiteness/length
anchor onto the givenness claim, which the s245 decision fenced off.

## The human gradient (committed, firsthand-inspected — no raw rows in git)

From [`resource/dais-dative-ratings`](../../wiki/base/resources/dais-dative-ratings.md) and the committed
derived tables under [`experiments/runs/2026-07-17-dais-license-scout/`](../runs/2026-07-17-dais-license-scout/)
(`inspection_manifest.json` + `verb_recipient_means.csv` — the 50,136 raw judgment rows stay gitignored;
this probe reads only the derived tables). Two human directions with **magnitude**, both firsthand-reproduced
from the raw rows at cataloguing:

- **The verb-bias surface (Arm A target).** Per-verb mean `DOpreference` over 200 verbs (100 Levin
  "alternating" + 100 "non-alternating"), 0–100 slider (0 = strong prepositional-dative, 100 = strong
  double-object, 50 = "about the same"). The alternating/non-alternating human contrast is **33.97 vs 18.92**
  (a 15.05-point gap; the paper's reported coefficient b = −15.0). This is the "verb bias" the DAIS paper is
  literally titled about (*Investigating representations of verb bias in neural language models*).
- **The recipient definiteness/length preference surface (Arm B target).** Mean `DOpreference` falls
  **monotonically** across the five recipient conditions: **pronoun 37.74 > shortDefinite 30.50 >
  shortIndefinite 25.15 > longDefinite 20.78 > longIndefinite 18.31** — pronominal / short / definite
  (given-like) recipients raise DO preference, a ~20-point slider swing pronoun→longIndefinite. This is the
  graded definiteness/length surface DAIS specifically grounds (s245 resolution).

The committed `verb_recipient_means.csv` gives the full **200 verbs × 5 recipient conditions** grid of human
means (1,000 cells, with per-cell n), which is the head-to-head target for both arms.

## The two arms — and their honest asymmetry

**Arm A — verb-bias magnitude (n up to 200; the flagship-power ρ, but confoundable).** Correlate the
panel's per-verb bare DO-preference against the human per-verb mean `DOpreference`. Spearman ρ over the
verbs is very well-powered at n≈200. **But** the per-verb human gradient is dominated by the
alternating/non-alternating split — non-alternating verbs are PO-only, so humans floor them on the DO
slider, and a model can reproduce that split from **lexical subcategorization / verb frequency** alone
(plausibly memorized), which is *not* "graded information-structure sensitivity." So Arm A's ρ is
interesting (a first human-vs-model verb-bias magnitude) but its confound is explicit: a
**frequency/classification control** (partial ρ over verb frequency rank and/or restricted to alternating
verbs) is **binding** before any "graded sensitivity beyond lexical bias" reading (the BLiMP
frequency-confound discipline, mirrored).

**Arm B — the definiteness/length preference surface (the DAIS-named grounding).** Within **alternating**
verbs (where both phrasings are licensed, so the recipient definiteness/length factor can actually move
preference), does the panel reproduce the human recipient gradient (pronoun > shortDefinite > shortIndefinite
> longDefinite > longIndefinite)? The **powered** Arm-B measure is the **per-verb monotonicity rate** over
the ~40 Arm-B verbs (the fraction whose model gradient is monotone in the human direction) — a majority test
with real N; the 5-condition rank correlation is reported only as a **direction/monotonicity check** (n = 5
condition means has near-zero resolving power and does not carry a bootstrap CI, so it is never presented as a
powered ρ).

**Arm B carries its own binding confound control — the within-length definiteness contrast.** The five DAIS
recipient conditions covary length *with* definiteness (pronoun is shortest, longIndefinite longest), so a
pure **end-weight / length-only** reader (shorter recipient → DOC) reproduces the full recipient gradient
with *zero* definiteness or information-structure sensitivity — the exact shortcut the flagship givenness
claim spent its rigor budget excluding (its end-weight dissociation control). So Arm B is **not**
confound-free: it swaps Arm A's lexical-subcategorization floor for an end-weight confound. The control is
available in the committed human data: the **within-length definiteness contrasts** hold length fixed and
move only definiteness — human `DOpreference` shortDefinite **30.50** > shortIndefinite **25.15** (+5.35 at
short length) and longDefinite **20.78** > longIndefinite **18.31** (+2.47 at long length). A
"definiteness-sensitive, not merely length-counting" reading of Arm B **requires** the model to reproduce
these within-length definiteness contrasts (≥2/3, direction-consistent); absent that, a TRACKS reading on the
raw 5-condition gradient carries an explicit **end-weight caveat** (the model may be counting length, not
tracking the definiteness/information-structure surface). This is Arm B's analogue of Arm A's binding
frequency/classification control, and it is the reason Arm B is the *definiteness*-surface test only when the
within-length contrast survives.

**Why both.** Arm A gives the powered human-vs-model verb-bias magnitude the line has never had; Arm B, with
its within-length control, gives the graded definiteness/length surface test DAIS was adopted to ground.
Reporting both, and their divergence, is the honest deliverable — a model with strong verb-bias ρ but a flat
definiteness/length gradient has *lexical* knowledge, not the *graded preference surface*; a model that
reproduces the raw recipient gradient but fails the within-length definiteness contrast is *length-counting*,
not tracking definiteness — both dissociations are first-class results.

## The contamination fence (LOAD-BEARING — DAIS public since 2020)

DAIS's items may sit in the panel's pretraining data. Two mitigations, both binding:

1. **Anchor the factor→rating relation, never lift DAIS sentences.** The probe stimuli are
   **project-constructed** (own recipient/theme lexical fillers instantiating the 5 definiteness/length
   conditions); DAIS supplies only the **verb list** and the **human ratings** (the factor→rating relation
   the correlation targets), never a `DOsentence`/`PDsentence` string. A freeze-time disjointness assertion
   confirms **no** probe stimulus string appears verbatim in DAIS's released sentences (the raw file is
   in-repo, gitignored, for this check only). This is Gate Q1, the crux.
2. **Report a high correlation as a contamination CEILING, not a competence result.** A near-perfect ρ is a
   memorization *upper bound* (the BLiMP absolute-accuracy framing), interpreted cautiously; the informative
   signal is the **pattern** — which conditions/verbs the model tracks vs. misses, the Arm-A-vs-Arm-B
   dissociation, and the cross-model spread of ρ (prediction 3: effect size decorrelates across the panel) —
   **not** the raw ρ magnitude alone.

## GATE Q1 — contamination-safe stimulus construction (the crux)

How to realize the factor→rating relation without lifting DAIS sentences (the resolution's fence #3 / the
resource page's "clean use").

- **Q1-A (provisional default) — fully project-constructed frames.** For each DAIS verb, build DOC/PD
  phrasings from a **project-chosen** theme + a recipient realized at each of the 5 definiteness/length
  conditions (the *factor levels* — pronoun / short-definite / short-indefinite / long-definite /
  long-indefinite — instantiated with **our own** nouns, not DAIS's item-specific recipients), holding the
  theme fixed across conditions so only the recipient factor moves. DAIS supplies the verb list + the human
  ratings only. Freeze-time verbatim-disjointness assertion against the raw DAIS sentences. Cleanest
  contamination posture; the definiteness/length *factor* is shared by construction (it must be — it is the
  thing being correlated), the *sentences* are not.
- **Q1-B — DAIS verbs + paraphrased DAIS frames.** Start from DAIS's frames and paraphrase (different
  theme/recipient nouns, altered word order where grammatical). Closer to DAIS's exact items; weaker
  contamination firewall (paraphrases of memorized items still cue the memorized rating).
- **Q1-C — DAIS's exact sentences, with a memorization side-probe. REJECTED up front.** Lifting DAIS
  sentences verbatim is precisely the fence the resolution drew; a "memorization control" cannot un-ring the
  bell once the model has produced a preference on a possibly-seen item. Not an option.

**Why value-laden.** The tighter the contamination firewall (Q1-A), the further the stimuli drift from
DAIS's exact factor realizations, so a *low* correlation could be "our fillers differ" rather than "the model
lacks the surface." Q1-A accepts that risk (and reports it as a fence) in exchange for a clean memorization
posture; the freeze must pin the exact recipient/theme lexicalizations so the definiteness/length contrast is
faithful to DAIS's operationalization of the factor even though the words differ.

## GATE Q2 — N, arms, cost, and the split under the $2.50 flag

- **Q2-A (provisional default) — Arm A all 200 verbs at a single fixed canonical recipient condition; Arm B
  a powered subset of ~40 alternating verbs × 5 conditions.**
  - **Arm A:** 200 verbs × 1 canonical condition (freeze-fixed, e.g. `shortDefinite` — a mid-surface,
    naturally-alternating realization) × 3 models = **600 calls**. Correlate model per-verb DO-pref vs human
    per-verb mean at the matched condition (and vs the human per-verb collapsed mean as a robustness check).
    ρ over 200 verbs (well-powered) + the binding frequency/classification control.
  - **Arm B:** ~40 alternating verbs (a powered, freeze-frozen random subset of the 100 alternating verbs) ×
    5 recipient conditions × 3 models = **600 calls**. Within-verb recipient gradient; per-condition marginal
    correlated against the human per-condition surface; per-verb monotonicity rate.
  - **Total ≈ 1,200 calls.** Pre-flight at the powered dative probe's measured ~$0.00217/call ⇒ **≈ $2.6**
    (bare-preference completions are shorter than the givenness probe's justified outputs, so likely lower;
    re-estimated at freeze). **Above the $2.50 prefer-split flag**, so the two arms are **separable runs**
    (Arm A, Arm B) that MAY be split across two UTC days if the freeze-time pre-flight exceeds the day's
    remaining headroom, or run as the day's only spend on a full-$5 day (the particle/s229 precedent). Each
    arm is an independent frozen instrument; splitting does not change either measurement.
- **Q2-B — the full 200 × 5 grid on all three models** (3,000 calls, ~$6.5). The richest joint (per-verb and
  per-condition marginals from one grid), but over the $5/day cap and unsplittable without changing the
  measurement; rejected unless a later session judges the extra power worth two full-$5 days.
- **Q2-C — a smaller pilot (≈12 verbs)**, to calibrate the elicitation before spending. Useful as a
  **liveness/format gate** (like the existing dative probe's 3-call liveness), **not** as the claim-carrying
  N (PROTOCOL §4: micro-N is for pilots, not verdicts). Folded into Q2-A as the pre-run liveness check, not a
  standalone option.

**Why value-laden.** The N × arms × condition choice trades power (Arm A wants all 200 verbs; Arm B wants
many conditions) against the $5/day cap and the $2.50 single-run flag. Q2-A keeps Arm A fully powered (n=200)
where power is cheap (one condition) and spends the second arm's budget on the confound-cleaner
definiteness/length surface, splittable if needed.

## GATE Q3 — anchor declaration, verdict map, and promotability

Unlike the internal-contrast lexical-recovery designs, this probe **is** a human-vs-model comparison against
an **adopted human resource**, so it carries a real anchor.

- **Q3-A (provisional default) — `anchor: human-anchored`, `anchors: → resource/dais-dative-ratings`, scoped
  to the definiteness/length + verb-bias preference surface, explicitly NOT the discourse-context givenness
  shift.** DAIS was adopted (s245) as the graded-acceptability companion for exactly this surface, so a
  result measuring model-vs-DAIS on it is the honest home for the anchor edge. The result's opening
  blockquote must state the scope fence (this is not a human-effect-size comparison for the givenness claim).
  **Verdict map** (direction + thresholds frozen at freeze):
  - **TRACKS-HUMAN-SURFACE** — Arm A per-verb ρ CI-LB > 0 on ≥2/3 models **AND** Arm B's per-verb
    monotonicity rate beats chance on ≥2/3 **AND** the **within-length definiteness contrast** clears
    (≥2/3) — the panel tracks the human graded verb-bias + definiteness/length surface, definiteness-sensitive
    (not merely length-counting), with a measured effect-size correspondence and its cross-model spread.
  - **LENGTH-ONLY** — Arm B reproduces the raw recipient gradient but the **within-length definiteness
    contrast fails** ≥2/3 — the panel is end-weight-counting, not tracking the definiteness/information-structure
    surface; the raw-gradient reading carries the end-weight caveat (a first-class dissociation).
  - **VERB-BIAS-ONLY** — Arm A ρ clears but Arm B's monotonicity rate is at chance / reversed ≥2/3 — the
    panel has lexical verb-bias but **not** the graded definiteness/length preference surface (a
    first-class dissociation).
  - **SURFACE-ONLY / DECOUPLED / null** — pre-named: Arm B gradient tracks but Arm A ρ collapses under the
    frequency/classification control (surface without lexical bias); or neither correlates (a human-magnitude
    divergence, itself a finding); or the arms diverge across models (reported, not resolved by fiat).
  - **CONTAMINATION-CEILING flag** — a near-perfect ρ (≥ a freeze-fixed ceiling) is reported as a
    memorization upper bound, not a competence result; the pattern/dissociation carries the reading.
  - **Frequency/classification control BINDING on Arm A** (Q3 fence): a "graded sensitivity beyond lexical
    verb-bias" reading requires the partial-ρ / alternating-only control to survive; the raw Arm-A ρ alone is
    a *verb-bias* magnitude only.
- **Q3-B — `anchor: internal-contrast-only`.** Rejected: this genuinely compares model preference against a
  human rating surface, so an internal-contrast declaration would *under*-claim (and mis-describe) the
  measurement. The comparison is the point.
- **Q3-C — promote-on-this-run.** Rejected: a single run is not a promotion. Per PROTOCOL §3 the result stays
  `proposed`; a `claim` (a human-effect-size-anchored dative claim on the definiteness/length surface) is
  earnable only via a fresh-item replication + a cross-session promotion review, held distinct from the
  existing givenness claim.

**Why value-laden.** Whether — and for which surface — DAIS anchors a *result* (vs. the s245 caution that a
blunt anchor edge over-claims), and what a graded correlation must clear to count as "tracks the human
surface" vs. a memorization ceiling. Q3-A wires the anchor exactly where the s245 resolution located DAIS's
legitimate grounding (the definiteness/length surface), and pre-commits the contamination-ceiling and
frequency-control fences so a high ρ cannot be over-read.

## Metrics + verdict map (direction fixed at freeze; thresholds tightened-not-loosened)

- **Model measure per cell:** DO-preference = DOC_points / (DOC_points + PD_points) ∈ [0,1] from the graded
  forced-choice (the existing dative `common.doc_pref`), bare (no context). Aggregated to per-verb (Arm A)
  and per-(verb,condition) (Arm B) model means.
- **Arm A statistic:** Spearman ρ(model per-verb DO-pref, human per-verb mean `DOpreference`) per model, with
  a bootstrap CI; **plus** the binding partial-ρ / alternating-only frequency-classification control.
- **Arm B statistic (powered = the monotonicity rate; the 5-point ρ is a direction check only):** the
  **per-verb monotonicity rate** over the ~40 Arm-B verbs (fraction whose model recipient gradient is monotone
  in the human direction), tested against chance; the 5-condition Spearman ρ(model, human) is reported as a
  direction/monotonicity check, **not** a powered correlation (n = 5, no CI). **Plus the binding within-length
  definiteness contrast:** the model's DOC-preference at shortDefinite − shortIndefinite and at longDefinite −
  longIndefinite, each tested for the human-direction sign — Arm B is the *definiteness*-surface test only when
  these clear (≥2/3, direction-consistent); otherwise a raw-gradient TRACKS reading carries the end-weight
  caveat.
- **Verdicts:** TRACKS-HUMAN-SURFACE / VERB-BIAS-ONLY / SURFACE-ONLY / DECOUPLED / null, per the Q3 map;
  n = 3 models, orderings/directions not pooled coefficients; CONTAMINATION-CEILING flag on a near-perfect ρ.
- **Anti-cheat fence (PROTOCOL §B):** the stimulus lexicalizations, the fixed Arm-A condition, the Arm-B verb
  subset, the ρ CI rule, the contamination-ceiling threshold, the frequency-control specification, and the
  verdict bands are **all frozen in `PREREG.md` before any model call**. No band or threshold is touched
  after the correlations are seen. VERB-BIAS-ONLY, SURFACE-ONLY, DECOUPLED, and null are pre-named
  first-class outcomes. The failure mode to guard (charter §8): do **not** re-choose the fillers or the
  canonical condition after seeing a weak ρ.

## What each outcome feeds

- **TRACKS-HUMAN-SURFACE:** the dative line gains its first **human effect-size** correspondence on the
  definiteness/length preference surface — the panel tracks not just the human *direction* but a graded human
  *magnitude*. Fills the conjecture's reserved secondary-confirm graded-acceptability slot with a measured
  correlation (still secondary/non-decisive per the conjecture's confirm criterion; it cannot convert or
  rescue the primary givenness test, which it does not touch). A replication + promotion review is the route
  to a scoped `claim` — held distinct from the givenness claim.
- **VERB-BIAS-ONLY / SURFACE-ONLY dissociation:** a first-class result on *which* graded surface the panel
  reproduces — lexical verb-bias vs. the graded definiteness/length preference — sharpening the
  distributional/constructional reading of the dative alternation and feeding the cross-model decorrelation
  story (prediction 3).
- **DECOUPLED / null:** an honest human-magnitude *divergence* — the panel shifts in the human direction
  (already shown) but its graded surface does not track the human graded surface. A first-class negative on
  the calibration record; would caution any "the panel has human-like graded dative preferences" reading.
- **Registered in [`predictions.md`](../../wiki/predictions.md) §B** — a DAIS-correlation bet, provisionally
  registered at **design (s246)** (the particle A5-third-sibling precedent for a design-stage registration),
  **finalized at freeze** once Q1/Q2/Q3 fix the exact verdict bands; co-registered with the conjecture's
  secondary-confirm graded-acceptability clause (not double-scored); outcome updated the run session.
