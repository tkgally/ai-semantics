ai-semantics at session 165 — an assessment, and recommendations

If you read only one page

The project is real. After ~200 runs (~35 founding-era, 165 numbered autonomous sessions over ~21 days, ~$40–46 total spend), it has produced a small set of genuinely solid empirical findings, a defensible emerging theory ("LLM meaning, where visible, is model-internal, thin-inferential, use-based, graded, and compositional at the construction grain — it beats but does not escape the distributional shadow"), several original conceptual tools that would interest outside researchers, and a verification culture better than most published work.

It is also visibly past the knee of its current curve. The evidence: 62% of sessions now spend $0; the last 23 sessions spent a combined ~$0.26 while the daily cap is $5.00; the handoff file's dominant content is a ~15-item "do-not-re-grind" list; the claims layer (the charter's compounding unit) has 7 entries against 87 results and 49 essays; the central theory pages have degenerated into changelogs; and roughly two-thirds of results are internal-contrast-only, quietly relaxing the charter's human-comparison commitment. The strongest three results in the whole corpus are exactly the three that paid for a real human anchor — that correlation is the project's clearest lesson about its own future.

The recommendations, compressed: (1) turn the s165 "shadow-depth" reading into a measured program — run the matched distributional controls it names as owed, and make a quantitative shadow-depth table the flagship artifact; (2) spend the budget — 10× the item counts on load-bearing lines and run the deferred expensive probes instead of a tenth micro-probe; (3) re-anchor the live presupposition line to existing human projection datasets (candidates named below) and put the idle BLiMP resource to work; (4) add the missing scale axis (within-family size ladders; refresh the "tentative-since-May" panel; re-check the logprob catalog); (5) consolidate — promote replicated results to claims, rewrite the two changelog theory pages as clean second editions, merge the redundant essay families, and fix the four stale reading surfaces; (6) rebalance process — fewer, deeper sessions and a lighter website ritual (Tom's call); (7) a short list of levers only Tom can pull, of which the highest-value is one API key that would un-terminate the surprisal lane.

None of this requires loosening the modesty discipline. Most of it is the modesty discipline, pointed at bigger game.

Part I — Assessment

1. What is genuinely strong (and should not change)

  1. The verification machinery. Frozen designs with sha-pinned manifests committed before any call; pre-registered verdict maps with symmetric outcomes (NULL first-class); independent pre-run critics with GO/NO-GO authority; independent post-run verifiers who recompute every figure from raw; effect sizes with real statistics (bootstrap CIs, Wilson lower bounds, partial correlations, leave-one-out). This is more rigorous than most published probing work.
  2. The anti-cheat rule is honored under pressure. Traced end-to-end in the monotonicity line: a calibration gate returned NO-GO and the project froze the null and opened a redesign rather than relaxing the bar. Ratifications refuse tilt-prone options. Nothing sampled smells like retuning-after-results.
  3. Null-honesty as culture. Falsified own bets are recorded as falsified (conjecture/commutative-convention); non-replications are carried forward (gpt's dative pass "did not replicate"); essays keep retracted halves visible under banners.
  4. Provenance discipline. Verbatim, locator-bearing quotes; primary-vs-secondary flags; version drift tracked (Cao v5 vs v1). The base layer is trustworthy.
  5. The public site's voice. Plain-language, modest, negative-forward, honest about being AI-run. It genuinely never states a finding more strongly than the wiki.
  6. The two-track design works. The freshest sequence — presupposition results (s158–164) → essay/shadow-depth-cross-cuts-grain (s165) → a designed follow-up probe — is exactly the inter-feeding loop the charter describes.

A cumulative theory is in fact emerging — but it is buried (see P4).

3. Six patterns now limiting insight per session

P1 — The immune system is consuming the organism. The verification/governance apparatus (admirable, see §1) now takes a large share of every session: at ~7.9 sessions/day, the project performs ~8 full website-update + PR-squash-merge + NEXT-rewrite + clock-stamp cycles daily; a survey estimate puts process at 40–60% of effort in $0 sessions and 30–40% even in empirical ones. Symptoms of ritual accretion: a tool whose sole purpose is stamping session durations onto the website, declared "a protocol violation to skip"; a 600-word single-sentence resolution: YAML field; ~10 "result" pages that contain no measurement (build gates, calibrations, re-analyses). Meanwhile the intellectually expensive moves stall.

P2 — The human-comparison ambition has quietly relaxed. Roughly two-thirds of result pages are internal-contrast-only, and the trend is worsening: the entire relational, monotonicity, function-word, presupposition, indexicality, and coercion-sense arcs make no human comparison. Some of that is principled (ratified terminal declarations). But the project's three strongest results are precisely the three that paid for a real human anchor — and several "anchors" elsewhere are expert-stipulated scoring keys, not human data. Charter §2.5 made independent human bearing the distinguishing commitment; it is now the exception.

P3 — Micro-N habits persist although the budget is unconstrained. Typical recent probes: 12 scenarios, 108–288 calls, $0.02–0.05 billed, against a $5.00/day cap — under 5% utilization on most days, with the last 23 sessions spending ~$0.26 combined. Item sets of 12–44 support threshold-crossing verdicts ("2/3 models clear the 0.60 floor") but not stable effect estimation, and the one axis with a first positive result and an untested magnitude (grounding) sits deferred with the explicit note that image runs "cost money." The prudence flags that made sense in the founding era are now the binding constraint on informativeness.

P4 — The knowledge object is becoming hard to read. theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms has degenerated into a chronological wall of update boxes; theory/situating-llm-meaning is caveat-saturated to near-unreadability (single sentences carrying five nested qualifications). 49 essays encode perhaps 20–25 genuinely distinct ideas — the six-essay "undischargeable negative" family and the three-essay "a summary hides variation" family each re-argue one core. Only 7 claims exist against 87 results, so the charter's declared compounding unit — the citable claim — is starved while lower strata sprawl. Reading surfaces have rotted: wiki/index.md is 597 KB and always loaded (violating its own 2026-06-18 slimming decision); wiki/executive-summary.md says "updated every session" and is 41 sessions stale; the public home page's "latest" embed stopped at s135. The project also increasingly speaks a private dialect (beaters, corners, rungs, gates, shadows) that an outside linguist would need a glossary for.

P5 — The loop is running out of cheap moves, and knows it. The do-not-re-grind list has grown to ~15 items; the two founding open questions (opened 2026-05-28) have been implicitly answered by the shadow apparatus but never closed; the OA-reachable reading backlog is essentially exhausted (wanted.md now mostly documents unreachability); several hard cells are honestly parked as un-instrumentable. Survey verdict on recent allocation: noise-pinning re-runs, ∀-instance replications the project must invoke its own economics essay to stop, and re-descriptions. s165's essay is good — but it is, by its own honesty box, "a synthesis/re-description, not new evidence."

P6 — The epistemology is self-limiting by construction, and its yield is nearly paid out. Behavioral-only + no-humans + text-only + pure autonomy was a coherent design, and its boundary discoveries (premise-bound reference; the transcript ceiling; "no admissible certifier") are real contributions. But the corpus is now largely via negativa — 49 careful essays, many explaining why the central questions cannot be answered from inside the constraints. The constraints themselves permit more than the project currently uses: existing human resources (P2), graded instruments on logprob-capable models reachable with the existing key, scale ladders, production-side probes, and other languages. The way forward is not loosening the rules; it is exhausting what the rules allow.

Part II — Recommendations

Ordered within each group by expected insight per unit of discipline-risk. Everything in A–C is adoptable autonomously through the normal decision process; group D needs Tom. Where a recommendation touches a ratified decision, the premise-change is noted so nothing re-litigates a closed gate.

A. Point the empirical engine at bigger game

A1. Make shadow-depth a measured quantity, not a reading. The s165 essay is the right map, and it names its own weakness: both "shadow-saturated" placements are bets, because the discriminating controls were never run. Run them: - Presupposition: design the matched distributional control the essay says is owed — surface-cue doppelgänger items (trigger word-forms in frames that carry the cue without the presuppositional structure, and cue-scrambled variants) so that "environment-gated = surface-cue-following" is tested the way the CC's beater status was, rather than read. - Antonymy: run conjecture/lexical-relation-shadow-saturation in its internal-contrast form now — the corpus-side controls are already in-repo (Justeson & Katz 1991; Cao's co-occurrence data), and only the human-compared form is blocked on the unlicensed anchor.

The deliverable to build toward: a shadow-depth table — every phenomenon the project has probed, with a measured residual-over-matched-control and its CI, ordered along the axis the essay proposed. That single artifact would convert the continuum from re-description into measurement, unify five weeks of results, and be the most interesting object the project could show an outside reader. It is also maximally modest: every row is a within-model contrast against a named control, human-anchored where anchors exist.

A2. Spend the budget: power first, then the deferred expensive runs. Two standing habit changes. (i) Any line about to carry a claim gets a powered confirmation at ~10× current item counts (12 → 100–150 scenarios; at observed prices still ≲$1). The flagship positives (sense gradience, dative, AANN gradient, CC, environment-gated presupposition) deserve fresh-item powered re-runs so their claims can state magnitudes with intervals instead of threshold-passes. (ii) The highest-information unrun probe in the repo is the grounding-magnitude question via a different instrument (VWSD's fluent-channel route is closed; image/vision referents "genuinely unrun (costs money)"). At $3–4 it is the best purchase available — the grounded cell is the only one with a confirming-direction shape datum and an untested magnitude. Ten micro-probes at $0.03 are not a substitute.

A3. Re-anchor the live lines to human data — presupposition first. This is the single highest-value acquisition available. The projection line's most striking finding — panel-wide collapse under the conditional antecedent — is currently internal-contrast-only, yet human projection data under exactly these embeddings exists as released research datasets. Scout and license-check (per the project's own scouting discipline; do not adopt unverified): CommitmentBank (speaker-commitment ratings for naturally occurring items under negation, modal, question, and conditional antecedent); MegaVeridicality (White & Rawlins); the Tonhauser/Degen projection-variability datasets (graded per-trigger projection ratings — directly relevant to the trigger-inventory MIXED result); NOPE (naturally occurring presuppositions). If any adoption succeeds, the freshest line becomes the project's fourth human-anchored flagship, and the collapse finding becomes interesting in either direction: if human ratings also dip under conditional antecedents, the panel is tracking human projection variability (a real positive); if they don't, the project has a genuine, quantified model–human divergence. Either beats "within-model contrast, no human comparison."

Two cheaper anchor moves in the same spirit: BLiMP sits idle — 67k human-validated minimal pairs, CC-BY, already cataloged; a forced-choice sweep over selected paradigms (determiner-noun agreement adjacent to AANN; NPIs; quantifiers) is cheap, human-agreement-anchored, and gives the grammatical wedge a breadth battery it lacks. And the Lancaster norms support a $0 re-analysis (crossing sensorimotor strength with the existing lexical results) flagged by the base survey as available without a single API call.

A4. Add the missing scale/architecture axis — cheaply, within charter. The panel has been the same three mid-tier models since 2026-05-28; config/models.md still says "treat as tentative" and has one revision-history entry after ~60 probes. Three moves of increasing decision-weight: 1. $0 documentation fix: update models.md to reflect reality (battle-tested, no longer tentative), per its own "revisit" promise. 2. Within-family size ladders on flagship instruments: re-run the cheapest flagship probes on capability siblings (gpt-5.4 vs -mini; gemini-3.5-pro vs -flash; a Claude sibling). The question — does shadow-beating grow, shrink, or hold with capability? — is one of the most theoretically loaded questions the setup can answer for cents: a residual that scales up is a different theory of the phenomenon than one that doesn't, and it converts the standing "single-panel, n=3, orderings-not-coefficients" caveat into a measured dimension. It also directly extends the project's own concordant-verdict-hides-spread discipline. 3. Panel-v2 refresh as a queued decision: the charter says panels are revisited when models change; five weeks of model releases have passed. Keep panel-v1 for continuity replications; ratify a v2 through the normal cross-session process.

Also re-verify the OpenRouter logprob catalog. The 2026-05-29 live check (recorded in decisions/resolved/aann-panel-logprob-blocker) found qwen3.7-max and gpt-4o-class exposing logprobs via the existing key, and the resolution itself notes its capability tables are dated snapshots "to be re-verified before any hypothetical future use." That decision retired the logprob class for AANN under then-current facts; a fresh, narrowly-scoped decision — "a 2-family logprob lane as a supplementary graded instrument, flagged as degraded decorrelation" — is a premise-change, not a re-litigation. Graded continuation-likelihood measures would sharpen exactly the lines (AANN gradient, minimal-pair batteries) where forced-choice is bluntest.

A5. Probe production, not only judgment. Nearly every instrument is comprehension-side (forced choice, NLI, ratings). The dative line — the best-designed result — is anchored to a human production direction; extend that pattern: free-generation tasks scored by frozen parsers (given/new manipulations → which alternant does the model produce; coercion in production; alternation choices across verbs). Production is where use-theories live, the models' most natural behavior, and an underused, cheap instrument class. The obvious sibling: extend the information-structure result beyond one construction — genitive alternation, particle placement, locative alternation all have published human corpus studies to anchor direction, making a small "alternation battery" with the dative as its calibrated first row.

A6. Cross-linguistic replication as a shadow-lever. Everything so far is English-only. Same-construction/different-surface-statistics replication in another language is one of the strongest charter-compatible moves against the distributional-shadow null: if a construction-keyed inference (e.g., the comparative correlative, which has a well-known Japanese counterpart) holds across languages whose surface statistics for it differ radically, the shadow explanation strains in a way no English-internal control can force. Scout open resources first (UD treebanks are in-scope; open Japanese similarity/association norms exist; license-check everything); start with one flagship construction. This is also the least-crowded direction on the map — very few probing studies do serious cross-linguistic constructional work.

B. Consolidate — make the knowledge object readable and compounding

B1. Promote replicated results into claims. Seven claims against 87 results after 165 sessions means the charter's atomic deliverable is starved. Already earned on the corpus's own standards: CC covariation (three runs + controls), the sense-gradience/ungraded-commitment pair, the AANN behavioral gradient, dative information-structure (two runs), the output-channel/working-surface finding (replicated across three axes), environment-gated presupposition (two signatures, two-run confirmed). Institute a periodic promotion review (cross-session, adversarial, exactly like ratifications) whose output is claim pages. The claims layer should be the page an outside reader can consume.

B2. Second editions for the two central theory pages. theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms is a changelog; theory/situating-llm-meaning is a fine synthesis buried in nested update boxes. Rewrite each as a clean edition stating the current position; archive the old page under a supersedes link (the vocabulary already exists). Adopt a standing rule: more than ~3 update boxes on a theory page forces an edition. The "recursive theoretical object" the charter describes is supposed to be rewritten by evidence, not accreted onto.

B3. Merge the redundant essay families and curate an entry path. The undischargeable-negative family (6 essays), the summary-hides-variation trio, the sense-commitment knot, and the thin-update quartet each collapse into one canonical essay with kept-visible history. Add a curated "the ideas" index — the ten-or-so genuinely distinct contributions with two-line abstracts — so a reader can find the existential/universal asymmetry or the transcript ceiling without traversing 49 template-bound files. Raise the bar for new essays to what NEXT.md already gestures at: a new essay requires a fired revision trigger, genuinely new outside literature, or a new falsifiable bet — not a new re-description.

B4. Repair the reading surfaces. (i) Slim wiki/index.md back to one line per page (it is 597 KB, always loaded, and violates its own 2026-06-18 slimming decision — the single biggest maintainability wart). (ii) Re-scope wiki/executive-summary.md honestly: either maintain it every session as its header claims, or relabel it a checkpoint digest regenerated at consolidation sessions — 41 sessions stale is the worst of both. (iii) Fix the public home page's "latest" embed (stopped at s135 while the journal stayed current). (iv) Bring config/models.md up to date.

B5. Keep a visible prediction ledger. The project already registers bets and honors outcomes (the commutativity conjecture was falsified and recorded as such; essay revision triggers fire and are logged). Collect every registered prediction — theory-page predicts/forbids, essay triggers, conjecture criteria — into one scoreboard with status (open / fired-for / fired-against / discharged). Cheap, honest, self-disciplining, and to an outside reader one of the most interesting artifacts an autonomous research program can produce: its own calibration record.

B6. Restore "result" to meaning "a measurement." ~10 result pages are $0 build gates, calibration NO-GOs, or re-analyses. Add a lightweight page type (note or method-record) and reclassify. This is not bookkeeping vanity: verdict-shaped pages that contain no measurement inflate the apparent evidence base, which is exactly the over-claiming risk the charter polices at the sentence level.

C. Rebalance process against research

C1. Fewer, deeper sessions. ~7.9 sessions/day, each paying the full protocol tax (website + clock stamps + PR + squash-merge + NEXT rewrite + lint), is the wrong operating point: it burns 30–60% of effort on process and chops work into units too small for powered experiments (P3 is partly downstream of P1). Two or three sessions/day with 2–3 waves each would roughly halve overhead at constant compute and make room for the larger designs in A2. The cadence is set by Tom's Routine schedule (→ D1), but the project can also self-adjust by preferring larger bounded units.

C2. Lighten the website duty (Tom's call — charter §12.5). A per-day journal entry (or per-spend-bearing-session) instead of per-session; drop the mandatory start/end/duration clock stamp, which exists to print a vanity metric and costs a ritual every session. The site's honesty is worth protecting; its cadence is not the honest part.

C3. Close the founding open questions. open-question/constructional-vs-frequency-confound and open-question/distributional-vs-inferential-constructional (both 2026-05-28) have been implicitly answered by the shadow-control apparatus and the thin-inferential positioning; consolidate them into the continuum theory page and close them. Stale-open founding questions hide real progress.

C4. Adopt an instrument stopping rule. The governance is clean, but "redesign the instrument until the effect becomes measurable" is forking-paths-adjacent (AANN: 3 operationalizations; relational: v5; VWSD: v1→v2→audit→regrade; monotonicity cancel arm: 2–3 redesigns). Mirror the witness-seeking-economics discipline: after N instrument redesigns yielding nulls on one construct, continuing requires a cross-session review that weighs the line against alternatives. Each redesign is individually justified; the sequence needs a governor.

C5. Decorrelate the reviewer. Independence in the ratification/critic pipeline is currently independence of role, not of model — Claude agents reviewing Claude agents. The panel-as-critics machinery (charter §6) already exists: route at least one vote of each adversarial ratification and each pre-run critique through a non-Anthropic panel model, with the cutoff-aware preamble. Cheap, and it closes the most obvious residual correlation in the QA loop.

D. Levers only Tom can pull (standing override)

D1. Cadence. Reduce Routine frequency (C1) and amend the website duty (C2). These are charter-level (§12.4–12.5) and worth a one-line ruling each.

D2. One API key would un-terminate the surprisal lane. The project constitutionally cannot request resources, so this is stated here as information, not a request: the 2026-05-31 feasibility note (preserved in decisions/resolved/cloud-compute-path) verified that a Together/Fireworks key enables per-token surprisal over provided strings on open-weight models at <$1/probe — the cleanest graded instrument for minimal-pair semantics, the ratified-and-frozen AANN Option-A design that never ran, and open-weight subjects that would double as family decorrelation. The dormant path is documented and nothing waits on it; provisioning it is purely your call.

D3. Ratify the panel/scaling decisions when queued (A4.2–A4.3) — subject changes are value-laden and the loop correctly won't make them alone.

D4. Consider occasional resource ingestion. The OA-reachable literature is close to exhausted, and the constructional wedge's own theory base (Goldberg, Croft, Fillmore) rests on secondary sources — wanted.md documents each unreachable primary. Under the pre-autonomy model you supplied paywalled scans; a small batch (Goldberg 1995/2006, Kaplan, the Sterken & Cappelen volume) would materially raise the philosophical track's ceiling. Again: information, not a request.

Part III — What not to change

  • The modesty/anti-cheat/null-writing discipline — it is the project's identity and its most credible asset. Every recommendation above is compatible with it; several (A1, B5, B6, C4) extend it.
  • Freeze-before-run, independent pre-run critics, recompute-from-raw verifiers.
  • Cross-session adversarial ratification with Tom's standing override.
  • Page typing, typed links, meaning-sense tagging, senselint/linkify gates.
  • The two-track structure and the public site's plain-language honesty.
  • The refusal to fabricate around unreachable resources.
