MODAL-ARM-WIDENING (session 71). Tests essay revision trigger (a): does the will->would modal null generalize across modal types? REUSES the ratified instrument + matching discipline (run-v2). {fw}/{Fw}=function word (premise only). {cw}=content word (premise AND hyp). base/fn=predicted 3-way NLI label (0 ent,1 neu,2 con). pairs=[out,in]; build.py drops any pair failing freq(b,c)+SIGNED-length(d) matching vs its function pair. ARMS: because/some/will REUSED verbatim from run-v2 (because+some = in-run positive controls proving the NLI instrument flips on a real inferential change; will = replication of the run-v2 modal null). shall->should (deontic obligation->advisory) and must->might (deontic necessity->epistemic possibility) are the NEW modal arms; both use an 'is required to' hypothesis so the swap is predicted to flip ENT->NEU (shall/must = required; should=advisory, might=possibility, neither = required). Content swaps matched to the MODAL pair within |dLg10WF|<=0.10 at both ends, signed dlen==function dlen, content gap>=function gap (certify.py). shall rests on one content pair (buy->give) -- the gap-0.759 supply ceiling, flagged like run-v2's single-out-word arms.
