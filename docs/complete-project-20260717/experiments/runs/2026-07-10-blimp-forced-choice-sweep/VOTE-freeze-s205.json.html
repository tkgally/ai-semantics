1. **GO-WITH-CONDITIONS** 2. **Fatal flaw(s):** - **Possible construct leakage in “depth” stratification.** Your “shallow vs deep” split is not purely structural if the deep bucket mixes **NPI licensing + quantifiers + islands + filler-gap**, which are heterogeneous phenomena with different difficulty and error modes. That does **not** make the probe invalid, but it **does** make R2/R2h interpretation weaker than the label “depth” suggests. This is a design risk, not a stop-the-run flaw. - **R2/R2h are sensitive to category composition, not just depth.** Because the shallow bucket is only local agreement (plus medium distractor) while deep includes multiple distinct constructions, any mean-gap result can be driven by category mix rather than a true depth effect. Again, not fatal, but it is a real identifiability issue. - **Excluding low-agreement paradigms for data availability can bias human anchoring.** You say they are excluded for 404/data availability rather than value, but if those paradigms are systematically harder, removing them could inflate human-baseline coherence. Since they’re frozen out for access reasons, this is acceptable only if you explicitly treat R1 as a **40-paradigm observed-set** metric, not as “full on-axis BLiMP.” Otherwise it’s a definitional mismatch. 3. **Biggest residual risk + one freeze condition:** - **Biggest residual risk:** the probe’s conclusions may be dominated by **category-composition effects** and **test-set idiosyncrasies** rather than the intended “shallow vs deep” construct. - **Freeze condition to add:** precommit that **all interpretation of R2/R2h is limited to “this exact frozen panel”** and not generalized as a universal depth effect; additionally, report **per-category means** alongside the shallow/deep aggregate so readers can see whether one category is carrying the result. 4. **Anti-cheat:** - **None** in the instrument as written. The prompt is neutral, the order-balancing is appropriate, and I do not see any obvious setup aimed at forcing a preferred outcome. The only caveat is that the **panel composition itself** could favor a desired narrative if someone later selectively emphasizes the shallow/deep aggregate over the category breakdown.
