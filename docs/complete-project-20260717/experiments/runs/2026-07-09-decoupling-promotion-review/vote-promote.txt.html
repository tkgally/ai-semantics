REFUSE

- Strongest FOR-argument I’m overriding: two replications in two corpus families with disjoint cues, plus the POS boundary explains the failure on adjectives. That is good evidence of a noun-specific phenomenon.
- But the standing-unit bar is higher than “replicated absence of association.” Here the core result is a twice-agreeing **n=6** rank-correlation null, not a robust positive effect or a mechanism.
- The powered item-level arm is not the same claim: it is within-cue and near-zero, while the proposed positive replacement (IS-A depth) is thin and only partially replicated. So there is no stable promoted-positive anchor to absorb the absence.
- A claim page should state what the evidence licenses in a compact, transferable way. “Cue-strength does not rank-predict recovery for nouns” is too close to a descriptive result, not a compounding claim.
- The safest current framing is a **result** with explicit scope: nouns, WordNet relations, the specified corpus families, and the two replicated Spearman analyses.
- What would earn promotion: a third independent noun run with the same null direction, or a stronger pre-registered aggregation across the two corpora that still shows the same near-zero/weak relationship with a tighter CI, plus a replicated positive replacement if you want the claim to be about mechanism rather than absence.
- Single strongest rule for the future claim if promoted later: it must **not** say “cue-strength is generally unrelated to recovery” or imply cross-POS generality.
