Keep Q1 animacy. Frequency-collinearity is exactly why you need the strongest human-anchored semantic constraint, not a weaker surrogate. Don’t swap to end-weight just because it’s “cleaner” for the model; that would change the yardstick. B1–B3 are not airtight. Residual cheat surfaces: - RARE/NONCE possessor lemmas still leave lexical idiosyncrasy and lemma-specific register effects. - The frozen marginal covariate helps, but a single partialled-out scalar cannot eliminate higher-order confounding with animacy, lexical class, and genre. - The confirm-vs-shadow rule is a decision rule, not a causal firewall. It can classify “confirm” without proving the model tracked animacy rather than some correlated cue. So I would not ratify the claim that the control stack “closes every way” a surface reader could pass. It materially reduces risk, but it does not eliminate it. Direction-only human anchoring is honest, given the license limit. Just don’t overstate it: you have an external sign anchor, not a per-item gold gradient. That’s fine if the paper is explicit that the comparison is directional, not human-level. Net: acceptable with a stronger caveat on residual confounding and a narrower interpretation of the control as corroboratory, not definitive. But if your default text implies “distributional shadow defeated,” that overclaims. VOTE: ADOPT-WITH-MODIFICATION
