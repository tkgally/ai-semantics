chief_cost_statement
The v5 can never say 'models pick the singular agreement the way humans do' — only that the construction shifts the within-model singular-agreement choice relative to a matched bare-plural control, in the direction the published AANN semantics predicts. This is a within-model contrast, not a human-comparison claim.
expected_agreement_key_provenance
EXPERT-STIPULATED. For an AANN measure-phrase subject the singular ('was') is the AANN-licensed agreement; for the bare-plural control the plural ('were') is the ordinary agreement. This is the design author's coding of the published AANN semantics (Solt 2007 unit-coercion; Dalrymple & King 2019; Bylinina & Nouwen 2018 — named as the analyses the stipulation rests on, NOT quoted; they are not in-repo). It is a scoring key, NOT a behavioral-human anchor.
