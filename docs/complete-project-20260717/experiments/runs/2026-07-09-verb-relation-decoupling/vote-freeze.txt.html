NO-GO

- **B1 honesty:** The **0.50 CORE-4 bound is pre-committal enough on its face**, but **using CORE-4 while excluding mechanically-included `cause` is borderline cherry-picking**. It is not obviously fraud, because you say `cause` was added by a frozen rule and the bound was fixed before inspection; still, the *verdict gate* should be interpreted as anchored to the preplanned backbone, not retrofitted to preserve a result. This is **fragile, not clean**.

- **Anti-cheat leak:** Biggest concern is **post-hoc interpretability drift**, not an explicit leak. I do **not** see a direct threshold-tuning leak from the text, but the combination of:
  - a mechanical relation inclusion rule,
  - a degeneracy bound defined on a subset,
  - and a special note that the wider spread “rides on cause + antonymy”
  
  makes the analysis easy to narrate opportunistically. That’s not proof of cheating, but it is **too easy to game in presentation**.

- **Dropped relation / silent omission:** You explicitly say **the corpus Hearst arm is dropped** because no clean verb troponymy frame exists. That’s fine if pre-registered, but it is still a **material design subtraction**. I would want this flagged in the prereg as a structural exclusion, not justified after seeing the data.

- **Byte-freeze claim:** “BYTE-IDENTICAL to the frozen noun-run build” is strong, but I would not trust it blindly unless the build artifact hash, command line, and environment hash are frozen too. As written, it is **asserted**, not independently auditable.

- **Fresh-cue disjointness:** “asserted 0 overlap” is acceptable only if the check was computed against the exact prior lemma list, with normalization rules frozen. Otherwise it’s a potential **lexical loophole**. Not a stop by itself, but I would verify before spending money.

- **Stop conditions:** I would **stop** if any of these are true: the CORE-4 denominator was chosen after looking at spread; `cause` was exempted from the degenerate-bound logic for convenience rather than prereg; the byte-identical claim lacks hashes; or the fresh-cue disjointness was checked with any mutable preprocessing.
