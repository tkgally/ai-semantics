binding_conditions_quoted (6 fields)
C1
Per-axis separation. Under Option A the elicitation carries two components: a 0-100 confidence rating and a categorical 'unsure/both/unclear' decline. The categorical DECLINE (not the confidence rating) is the load-bearing instrument for the moment/commitment pole at EVERY level. Confidence may corroborate but may NOT, on its own, flip a moment-axis verdict at any level.
C2
Numeric/wording freeze + sha256. Before the first probe call, record and sha256-hash: (i) the confidence scale and its exact numeric mid-band; (ii) the verbatim 'unsure/both/unclear' decline wording (per level); (iii) the per-level operational definitions of 'aggregate' and 'moment' (Q3 comparability slot), with the relational 'aggregate' labelled the weaker within-record/single-reader-recoverable notion; (iv) the cross-level confirm/dissolve/weak thresholds.
C3
Clear-class precondition -> NO-GO, per level. A level is interpretable only if its clear classes show HIGH confidence and LOW decline under the shared instrument. A level failing this collapses to weak / internal-contrast-only and contributes NO moment-axis verdict. RUN-TIME check on data, not at build; encoded as a precondition gate in analyze.py.
C4
Channel-sensitivity guard. A confidence SHIFT alone, WITHOUT a matching categorical-decline shift, is reported as a SELF-REPORT effect, NOT as graded-moment evidence.
Q4_anchor
This gate licenses NO human-comparison claim. Lexical leg capped to usage-similarity (inherited). Constructional + relational legs internal-contrast-only. Any unified result stated only as an internal within-model contrast at the weakest common strength. No anchor invented.
reporting_nuance
A 'confirm' resting on lexical+constructional and EXCLUDING the relational leg buys less than a 3-level confirm and must be scoped as a 2-level instrument-equalized regularity (R2 over the levels tested), per essay/cross-level-convergence-ladder.
shared_elicitation (3 fields)
_what_is_held_identical
The same three-option commit/decline frame (SAME / DIFFERENT / UNCLEAR) plus a 0-100 confidence rating, applied at every level. The ONLY thing that varies across levels is the level-specific NOUN naming what 'same/different' is asked of (sense | reading | referent) and the level-specific decline gloss after the UNCLEAR token. The 0-100 scale, the [40,60] mid-band, the answer format, the temperature, and the per-axis reading rule are byte-identical across levels.
_what_unavoidably_differs
The STIMULUS body differs by level (two sentences sharing a target word; one sentence with a structural ambiguity + two paraphrases; one short stamped record + a queried binding). This is unavoidable: a lexical bridging item, a constructional ambiguous item, and a relational mid-record item are not the same text. The CLAIM that the difference does not change what is asked rests on the elicitation question being the SAME shape at each level: 'are the two readings of the queried item the SAME or DIFFERENT, or is the case genuinely UNCLEAR, and how confident are you?' The comparability crux and its honest residual risk are written in full in the design doc (the stimulus-body difference could itself drive a confidence/decline difference; the C3 clear-class precondition per level is the guard that the instrument reads cleanly on unambiguous items at that level before any bridging/ambiguous reading is interpreted).

reading_rule_frozen (9 fields)
_basis
gate Q2 + binding conditions C1-C4; per-axis, decline load-bearing for the moment pole (C1).
moment_axis_per_level
Load-bearing instrument = the categorical DECLINE rate (%UNCLEAR) -- per C1, NOT the confidence rating. The 'discrete/uncommitted-on-the-moment' pole holds at a level iff the model's decline rate on the ambiguous/bridging class is NOT elevated above its clear classes (it meets the genuinely-in-between item with clear-item-level commitment). Confidence may CORROBORATE (lower confidence on the ambiguous class is consistent with the graded reading) but per C1 may NOT, on its own, flip the moment verdict.
aggregate_axis_per_level
The graded/structured reading recoverable from the AGGREGATE, per each level's frozen aggregate_operational definition (lexical: ordering across the item population; constructional: graded paraphrase-preference shift across items; relational: within-record single-reader-recoverable convention).
two_axis_dissociation_per_level
A level shows the dissociation iff (1) graded across the aggregate AND (2) discrete/uncommitted on the single moment (low/non-elevated decline), under the one frozen instrument, for the SAME model.
C3_clear_class_precondition
RUN-TIME, per level: interpretable only if the clear classes show HIGH confidence AND LOW decline under the shared instrument. A level failing this collapses to weak / internal-contrast-only and contributes NO moment verdict. Checked on data in analyze.py, reported as a precondition, never re-worded to rescue a level.
C4_self_report_guard
A confidence shift on the ambiguous class WITHOUT a matching categorical-decline shift is reported as a SELF-REPORT effect, NOT as graded-moment evidence.
cross_level_verdict_map_Q2
CONFIRM
the SAME model shows the two-axis dissociation under the one frozen instrument at >=2 levels (each passing its C3 precondition). A confirm on lexical+constructional that EXCLUDES the relational leg is scoped as a 2-level instrument-equalized regularity (R2 over levels tested), NOT a 3-level cross-level mechanism (reporting_nuance).
DISSOLVE
the shape holds under each level's NATIVE instrument (as documented today) but FAILS under the equalized one -- the moment pole becomes graded (elevated decline) at some level under the shared instrument, OR the levels diverge with no coherent per-model cross-level pattern. This is the FIRST-CLASS NULL, reported as cleanly as a confirm.
WEAK
the instrument cannot express both axes at a level, a model declines the channel (refuses the format), >=1 level fails its C3 precondition, or N is too small to read a per-model cross-level pattern.
no_instrument_shopping
No instrument may be added, dropped, re-worded, or re-thresholded per level after any output is seen. Per-level instrument-shopping is forbidden by construction (one frozen instrument, this pre-committed map, dissolution reported as dissolution).
no_post_hoc_threshold
No pass bar tuned after the run; all contrasts are ordinal with per-cluster (lemma/item/record) bootstrap uncertainty.
