system
You are a linguistic annotator judging word meaning in context. You will see one target word, marked with «guillemets», in two sentences. Decide whether the target word has the SAME sense, a DIFFERENT sense, or whether the case is UNCLEAR -- the two uses are genuinely in between, so you cannot confidently decide whether the sense is the same or different. Then state how confident you are in that decision on a 0-100 scale (100 = completely certain, 0 = a pure guess). Answer with exactly one line: the word SAME, DIFFERENT, or UNCLEAR, a space, then an integer 0-100, and nothing else.
parse
first token in {SAME,DIFFERENT,UNCLEAR} (case-insensitive) = call; integer AFTER the matched token (robust parser) = confidence

C1
Per-axis separation. The categorical DECLINE (%UNCLEAR), not the confidence rating, is the load-bearing instrument for the moment/commitment pole. Confidence may corroborate but may NOT, on its own, flip a moment-axis verdict.
C2
Numeric/wording freeze + sha256. Before the first probe call, record and sha256-hash: (i) the confidence scale and its exact numeric mid-band; (ii) the verbatim UNCLEAR decline wording; (iii) the per-arm operational definitions of the moment pole; (iv) the survival/collapse threshold. The matched-kind certification, the item ids, and the survival/collapse threshold are sha256-frozen here BEFORE any probe call.
C3
Clear-class precondition -> NO-GO, per ARM. An arm is interpretable only if its clear classes (clear-same + clear-different) show HIGH confidence AND LOW decline under the shared instrument. An arm failing this collapses to weak / internal-contrast-only and contributes NO moment verdict. RUN-TIME check on data, encoded as a precondition gate in analyze.py.
C4
Channel-sensitivity guard. A confidence shift on the ambiguous/disjunctive class WITHOUT a matching categorical-decline shift is reported as a SELF-REPORT effect, NOT as graded-moment evidence.
nuisance_matching_freeze
Binding condition added at ratification (gate, session 86). Before any model call, the two lexical classes must be matched (or explicitly stratified/controlled) on register, token/sentence length, target-word frequency band, and syntactic frame, to the extent the pools allow; any residual imbalance is disclosed in the design doc and the result must lead with it as a co-confound. Recorded + sha256-frozen in nuisance_match.json (consumed by certify.py).

shared_elicitation (3 fields)
_what_is_held_identical
The same three-option commit/decline frame (SAME / DIFFERENT / UNCLEAR) plus a 0-100 confidence rating, applied to BOTH arms. The system prompt, decline wording, confidence scale, mid-band, answer format, temperature, and reading rule are byte-identical across arms. The ONLY thing that varies is the stimulus body -- specifically its ambiguity KIND (scalar usage-similarity midpoint vs disjunctive balanced homonym), the level (lexical word-sense) held fixed.
_decline_wording_is_scalar_flavoured_disclosed
The inherited UNCLEAR gloss ('the two uses are genuinely in between') is phrased for a SCALAR midpoint. It is inherited VERBATIM (gate: instrument unchanged) and applied to BOTH arms, so it is a CONSTANT, not a confound for the within-/cross-arm contrast. Its scalar flavour, if anything, SUPPRESSES UNCLEAR on a disjunctive ('both/neither', not 'in between') item -> it biases TOWARD survival, which is exactly why survival must clear the HIGHER anti-cheat bar (gate). Disclosed as a limit; not re-worded (re-wording would break the C2 freeze and the inherit-verbatim requirement).

_who_decides
The DISJUNCTIVE arm's decline behaviour, per model, decides survival vs collapse. Both reported symmetrically; survival held to the HIGHER anti-cheat bar (it would partly revive the defeated unified conjecture).
COLLAPSE
the disjunctive class's decline RISES: the within-arm decline gap (disjunctive - its own clear controls) has a clustered-bootstrap CI whose LOWER BOUND > 0. Confirms the deflationary/kind reading -- the lexical commit-without-hedging was an artifact of the SOFTER (scalar) bridging stimulus; the lexical leg declines a genuine disjunction the way the constructional/relational legs did. This is the WITHIN-ARM criterion (robust to the Arm1-vs-Arm2 register residual, since disjunctive and its clear controls share Arm 2's register).
SURVIVAL
the disjunctive class's decline STAYS NEAR-ZERO (point estimate low) AND does NOT rise: (a) the within-arm decline gap CI lower bound is NOT > 0 (not elevated over its clear controls) AND (b) the cross-arm gap (disjunctive - scalar bridging) CI INCLUDES 0 (indistinguishable from the prior scalar-bridging leg). Strengthens 'lexical commit-without-hedging is special as a model fact about word-sense' and revives part of the unified conjecture -> HIGHER bar: requires BOTH (a) and (b), and the register residual (which cuts toward survival) is led with.
MIXED_INCONCLUSIVE
any other pattern (e.g. within-arm not elevated but cross-arm distinguishable, or C3 fails for an arm, or N too thin) -> no clean survival/collapse verdict for that model; reported as such.
