Ostertag 2025 — Language Models and Externalism: A Reply to Mandelkern and Linzen

What it is

A single-author reply by Gary Ostertag (Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai & The Graduate Center, CUNY), published in Computational Linguistics 51(2):651–659 (2025; ACL Anthology 2025.cl-2.8; DOI 10.1162/coli_a_00551), responding directly to source/mandelkern-linzen-2024-do-words-refer (M&L). It is the project's third pole in the LM-reference debate, and the published reply that argues M&L's specific externalist route fails on its own terms.

A correction to the framing in which this source was queued is needed up front, and is itself the page's main finding. Ostertag is not a defender of M&L's externalist line, and he is not an internalist. He is an externalist who attacks M&L's particular version of externalism — what he labels "naïve externalism," the thesis "that the natural history of a name grounds its reference" (§1) — using a tool internal to the philosophy of explanation (Lipton's contrastive-explanation account). His verdict: "the natural history of a term, in the austere sense assumed by Mandelkern and Linzen, cannot ground the reference relation" (§Introduction), so "Mandelkern and Linzen's quick route to the referentiality of LM-generated texts fails" (Abstract). He explicitly accepts the more careful conditional — that if LMs can have referential intentions, or if deference is not essential to reference, then there is no principled barrier to LM reference (§4) — and disputes only the antecedents and the naïve-externalist shortcut. So his relation to M&L is contradicts (he makes their positive conclusion less likely), not supports.

This is a philosophical interlocutor, not a human-annotated resource. Its evidence base is conceptual argument (contrastive-explanation theory applied to the Twin Earth case); it makes no measurements and cannot anchor any claim or result. Its in-repo role is to add the third published position on whether LM words refer: M&L say "may refer" (externalist, positive); source/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder say "no, not even narrowly" (internalist, negative); Ostertag says "the externalist route M&L take does not get there, though a better-grounded externalism might" (externalist, conditional / route-blocking). Note that Ostertag does not cite or engage Baggio & Murphy — see "What it cannot ground."

Key passages (verbatim, page locators from the publisher PDF)

Abstract — the central negative thesis:

"Appealing to the literature on contrastive explanation, I show that a term's natural history does not generally ground its referential properties. Thus, Mandelkern and Linzen's quick route to the referentiality of LM-generated texts fails."

Introduction (p. 651) — the stakes, stated in his own voice:

"(If they cannot, then a large chunk of generated discourse—indeed almost all of what we care about—will be meaningless.)"

Introduction (p. 651) — the thesis he will defend, with the "austere" qualification on M&L's externalism:

"As I argue, however, the natural history of a term, in the austere sense assumed by Mandelkern and Linzen, cannot ground the reference relation."

§1 (p. 652) — the Kripkean correction: intentions are ineliminable, which the naïve route drops:

"Referential intentions, however they are ultimately construed, are thus an ineliminable part of what grounds proper-name (and natural-kind-term) reference."

§1 (p. 653) — the label for the target view (M&L's reconstructed position), quoting M&L's own page 1194:

"Let's call this view—that the natural history of a name grounds its reference—naïve externalism."

§2 (p. 654) — his stated aim against M&L's optimism:

"My claim in what follows is to show that their optimism is misplaced: Naïve externalism gives us no grounds for holding that LMs' outputs refer."

§2 (p. 655) — the decisive contrastive question the naïve externalist cannot answer (his core counterexample):

"Q4. Why does water refer to H2O, and not to oxygen, among Earthians?"

§2 (pp. 655–656) — why the naïve difference-maker fails on Q4:

"But, although H2O is implicated in the causal history of water, and thus partially grounds that reference, oxygen is equally implicated in the causal history of water. We thus fail to meet "the difference condition" (Lipton)."

§3 (p. 658) — the bottom line of the argument:

"The upshot is that externalism, as understood by Mandelkern and Linzen, cannot ground reference."

§4 Conclusion (p. 658) — the conditional he accepts, isolating exactly where he and M&L diverge:

"We can express these options with the following conditional: if we can make sense of LMs possessing referential intentions, or if deference to usage is not essential to reference, then there is no principled barrier to LMs' outputs referring. In this case, my concern is not with the conditional, which I accept, but with the antecedent, about which I have doubts."

§4 Conclusion (pp. 658–659) — the closing verdict:

"In any case, as I hope to have shown, the path to the claim that LMs' outputs refer remains daunting—a far cry from the easy route that the naïve externalist promised."

What this grounds / bears on

  • source/mandelkern-linzen-2024-do-words-refer — the principal connection: a direct, published contradicts, but a narrow one. Ostertag grants the more careful externalist conditionals M&L flag (intentions, deference) and targets only the naïve-externalist shortcut he reconstructs from M&L's pp. 1194–1195 — that the bare natural history of a form suffices to ground reference. His argument: completing M&L's Twin Earth "Why?" question contrastively yields not just Q2 (H2O vs. XYZ, which the naïve externalist can answer) but also Q4 (H2O vs. oxygen, which it cannot, since oxygen is equally implicated in water's causal history). Hence "externalism, as understood by Mandelkern and Linzen, cannot ground reference" (§3) and their "quick route … fails" (Abstract). This makes M&L's positive verdict less likely via their stated mechanism, so the link is contradicts. Crucially it is not the internalist contradicts of Baggio & Murphy: Ostertag stays inside the externalist family and faults the grounding mechanism, not the externalist programme. This usefully confirms the exact wording and page numbers of M&L's load-bearing claims, since Ostertag quotes M&L's Computational Linguistics version-of-record (CL 50(3):1191–1200) with page citations (1191, 1193, 1194, 1195) — see "Known limits" for what this lets a later session fix.
  • concept/referential-meaning — it sharpens the externalist sub-axis with an intramural critique. That concept page builds referential.externalist on Putnam's Twin Earth and the division of linguistic labor. Ostertag is a published demonstration that the externalist machinery, even granted, does not deliver LM reference on the naïve reading, and that propping it up (deference to chemists, A2–A4) runs into the context-sensitivity of expert usage (he cites Weisberg 2006, "Water is not H2O"). The link is depends-on (the reply presupposes exactly the externalist apparatus the concept page lays out). The concept page's standing note — that the externalist primaries (Putnam 1975, Kripke 1980) are not-in-repo — is unchanged: Ostertag cites Putnam 1975, Kripke 1980, Lipton 2004, Cappelen & Deutsch 2024, Cappelen & Dever 2021, none in-repo. (Correction, 2026-07-05, s183: Putnam 1975 has since been ingested at primary strength — source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning, session 110 — and the concept page updated; Kripke 1980 and the others remain wanted per wanted.md.)
  • The three-pole picture of the LM-reference debate is now in-repo complete. With M&L (positive externalist), Baggio & Murphy (negative internalist), and Ostertag (route-blocking externalist) all catalogued, a finding that turns on which kind of objection one makes to LM reference can cite all three and distinguish "wrong programme" (Baggio & Murphy) from "right programme, wrong grounding mechanism" (Ostertag). This services the same revision-trigger region of the sixth essay essay/reference-as-premise-bound that Baggio & Murphy fed, but from the externalist side: the pro-reference pole is contested even by externalists.
  • concept/inferential-meaning (lighter touch). Ostertag's restoration of "referential intentions" as "an ineliminable part of what grounds … reference" (§1) re-introduces a speaker-side, mental-state requirement that sits adjacent to conceptual-role/internalist pictures, though he does not use inferentialist vocabulary and his own commitments stay externalist. Co-tagged inferential only for that adjacency; no typed link, as the connection is thematic.

What it does (and does not) establish — for this project

Honestly bounded: this is a conceptual rebuttal of one externalist argument, not evidence about any model. It establishes that M&L's specific "natural-histories-suffice" route is contested in the published literature on its own externalist terms — that contrastive-explanation theory exposes a difference-maker the naïve account cannot supply (Q4: H2O vs. oxygen). It does not establish that LMs do not refer (Ostertag is explicit that he is "neutral" on whether a properly Kripkean externalism, or a deference-free externalism, could ground LM reference — §4), and it does not establish any empirical fact about any model. The project's descriptivist stance ("describe, don't litigate") means this should be cited as one strongly-argued position, sharpening where the externalist route is vulnerable, not as a verdict the project endorses. It is orthogonal to the project's grounding nulls (perceptual-channel results), which concern a different (perception-vs-history) sense of grounding entirely.

What it cannot ground

  • Any empirical claim about any model. No experiments, probes, or measurements; not a human-labeled resource — cannot serve as an anchors: resource for a claim or result.
  • A settled verdict that LMs do not refer. Ostertag explicitly blocks only the naïve route and accepts the broader conditional; cite him as a route-critic, not as establishing that LM words fail to refer.
  • Any contradicts relation to source/baggio-murphy-2024-internalist-rejoinder. Ostertag does not cite, name, or engage Baggio & Murphy anywhere in the paper (verified: zero occurrences of "Baggio," "Murphy," "internalist," "rejoinder," or "arXiv" in the full text). The two replies arrive at overlapping skepticism by different routes (Ostertag: intramural externalist / contrastive-explanation; Baggio & Murphy: internalist / mind-brain), but there is no textual basis for a typed link between them, and the brief's hypothesized contradicts relation against Baggio & Murphy is not asserted. Any prose comparing the two replies (as this page does) is the project's own juxtaposition, not Ostertag's.
  • Anything about lexical gradience or Construction Grammar. Like M&L and Baggio & Murphy, it works at the level of names and natural-kind terms ('Nixon', 'Peano', 'water'); it does not bear on graded word sense, constructional form–meaning pairing, or acceptability methodology.
  • The relational/between-agents axis. Its picture is a single model's outputs and their (lack of) referential grounding, not meaning constituted between agents.

Known limits

  • Single conceptual argument, one author. Its standing is "published reply, version of record" (CL 51(2), peer-reviewed: it carries an Action Editor and review dates); the verdict is Ostertag's argued position, not a consensus. Note the Acknowledgments thank "Matt Mandelkern," so the exchange is collegial.
  • The argument leans on one framework (contrastive explanation / Lipton). Ostertag himself flags this in footnote 6: one could "take issue with my appeal to the contrastivist framework" and deny that Q1a is elliptical for the contrastive Q2; he offers a fallback intuition-based argument there, but the central case rests on contrastivism. Report the verdict with that conditionality.
  • It targets a reconstructed "naïve externalism," not necessarily M&L's considered view. Ostertag is careful that M&L "do not explicitly endorse naïve externalism" (§2) but hold it "provides a promising route"; the rebuttal bites the route, and a defender could say M&L never owed the naïve thesis. Cite the scope precisely.
  • Externalist primaries not in-repo. Kripke 1980, Putnam 1975, Lipton 2004, Cappelen & Deutsch 2024, Cappelen & Dever 2021, Weisberg 2006 are cited by Ostertag but are not in-repo; this source is a worked application, not a substitute for those primaries (Putnam 1975 and Kripke are already queued in wanted.md). (Correction, 2026-07-05, s183: Putnam 1975 is now in-repo — source/putnam-1975-meaning-of-meaning, session 110; Kripke 1980 and the rest remain not-in-repo.)
  • Useful side-benefit for citation hygiene. Because Ostertag quotes M&L's Computational Linguistics version with page numbers (CL 50(3):1191–1200; quotes located to pp. 1191, 1193, 1194, 1195), a later session can use this source to confirm the M&L page's currently-flagged "CL venue unverified / section-only locators" note (see source/mandelkern-linzen-2024-do-words-refer provenance). This page does not edit the M&L page; it records that the means to confirm it now exist in-repo.
