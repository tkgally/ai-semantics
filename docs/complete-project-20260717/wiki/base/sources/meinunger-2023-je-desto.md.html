The German comparative correlative — je … desto/umso

Ingested: 2026-07-12 (session 213), to satisfy freeze condition (viii) of decisions/resolved/cross-linguistic-cc-replication-scope: the German CC replication's items must be self-audited against an ingested grammar source, the no-human-subjects-compliant substitute for a human-auditor check. This page records the construction's form (what a well-formed German CC minimal pair looks like) with page-level provenance, so the frozen design/comparative-correlative-german-v1 items rest on a verifiable source, not on unaudited generation.

Provenance depth (honest). Two sources were consulted this session: a scholarly analysis (Meinunger 2023, metadata + abstract verified from the publisher page — the full article was not read, paywalled) and a pedagogical grammar reference (verbatim form + examples, freely readable). The item-authoring form facts below are standard, uncontroversial German grammar; both sources corroborate them. Where a claim rests on the abstract only, it is labelled.

Scholarly citation (form-confirming; abstract-level provenance)

Title: "Je-Desto, Je-Umso: An Analysis of the German Comparative Correlative Construction" Author: André Meinunger. Journal: Journal of Germanic Linguistics 35(2), 148–204 (June 2023; online 11 May 2023). DOI: 10.1017/S1470542722000101.

From the publisher abstract (verbatim-summarized this session; full paper not read): Meinunger argues that German je-desto sentences function as regular verb-second structures — "normal, regular prefield structures" rather than left-dislocation or construction-specific verb-third realizations. This confirms the desto/umso-clause is an ordinary main clause (finite verb in second position), consistent with the pedagogical description below. (The paper's finer theoretical claims about je-clause attachment are not relied on for item authoring and were not read in full.)

Construction form (the authoring anchor)

je + comparative … , desto (or umso) + comparative … — the German equivalent of English the … , the …. Two clauses, each carrying a comparative form; together they express a proportional covariation between two scales.

  • je-clause (comes first): a subordinate clause → finite verb goes to the END.
  • desto/umso-clause (comes second): a main clause → finite verb in SECOND position (the fronted desto+comparative counts as the first element, so the verb follows it).
  • desto and umso are interchangeable in the second clause.
  • The finite verb (esp. the copula) is frequently omitted in short/nominal CC clauses; both full-verb and verbless CC forms are well-formed standard German.

Verbatim examples with the finite verb present (pedagogical grammar reference)

"Je früher man kauft, desto billiger ist das" = "The earlier you buy, the cheaper it is" "Je weniger es gibt, desto größer wird der Preis sein" = "The less there is, the higher the price will be." "je weniger es gibt, umso größer wird der Preis sein" = "The less there is, the higher the price will be"

(Grammar reference: The je…desto conjunction in German, germanveryeasy.com/je-desto — a freely readable pedagogical German grammar reference, read verbatim this session. Corroborated by multiple pedagogical references, e.g. Lingoda, CORE Languages, and the Meinunger abstract above; the "je-clause verb-final subordinate / desto-clause verb-second main / desto≡umso" description is uniform across them.)

What this source is used for (and its bounds)

  • Used for: authoring well-formed German CC items (the cc-positive / cc-inverse forms) and their same-word non-CC controls (two independent declaratives; a single comparative clause) — i.e. fixing the form so the construction-isolation contrast is between the CC syntax and its absence, with the scalar vocabulary held constant.
  • Not used for: any claim about German CC frequency, acquisition, or human processing — none of which this page establishes. The frequency/co-occurrence control uses UD German-GSD (a separate, license-verified resource); this page fixes only construction form.
  • Not a human anchor. This is a grammar/construction-form source, not a human-judged CC dataset; the German replication is internal-contrast-only (no non-English CC human dataset exists per the scout).
