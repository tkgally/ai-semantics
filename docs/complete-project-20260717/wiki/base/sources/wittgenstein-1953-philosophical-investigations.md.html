Wittgenstein 1953 — Philosophische Untersuchungen (Philosophical Investigations)

What it is

Ludwig Wittgenstein's Philosophische Untersuchungen (Philosophical Investigations), written across the 1930s–40s and published posthumously in 1953 (Wittgenstein died 1951). It is the founding primary text of the meaning-as-use pole of meaning-theory — the "Wittgensteinian pole" of concept/truth-conditional-and-use-meaning (pole b). Against fixing an expression's meaning to a determinate truth-condition or a hidden definitional core, the later Wittgenstein urged that the meaning of a word is the role it plays in the practices — the "language-games" (Sprachspiele) — embedded in a "form of life" (Lebensform). The book is also the ancestor of prototype theory's family-resemblance (Familienähnlichkeit) categories: many general words (the standing example is game / Spiel) are held together not by necessary-and-sufficient conditions but by a network of overlapping, criss-crossing similarities.

It is a primary philosophy-of-meaning text — not a human-labeled empirical resource, and so not a human anchor: it grounds the theory the project's concept pages characterize (the use pole), never a result about any model. (See "What it cannot ground.")

Before this page, concept/truth-conditional-and-use-meaning cited the Investigations only as "(not in-repo; characterization)" and its "Honest gaps" section flagged that "every characterization above is in this page's own words, with no quote or page locator." This page closes that gap for the meaning-as-use, language-game, and family-resemblance ideas, with verbatim locators (§43, §7, §23, §66, §67). The rule-following / private-language strand (§§201, 243) is carried only via a secondary source and at lower strength (see provenance and "What it cannot ground").

Key passages

1. Meaning-as-use — the §43 neighborhood

The load-bearing formulation. The German is primary-direct; note that Wittgenstein qualifies the claim — it is offered for a large class of cases of the use of the word "meaning," explicitly not for all cases (the project's gloss preserves his italics):

"Man kann für eine große Klasse von Fällen der Benützung des Wortes »Bedeutung« – wenn auch nicht für alle Fälle seiner Benützung – dieses Wort so erklären: Die Bedeutung eines Wortes ist sein Gebrauch in der Sprache." (§43)

(Primary-direct, German PD, wittgensteinproject.org; the italics on große/alle are Wittgenstein's, carried as ''…'' markup in the fetched source.)

Project gloss (not the Anscombe translation): "For a large class of cases of the use of the word 'meaning' — though not for all cases of its use — one can explain this word thus: the meaning of a word is its use in the language."

The English short form, carried-via-secondary, corroborates the same locator:

"the meaning of a word is its use in the language" (PI 43)

(Carried-via-secondary: reproduced via SEP "Ludwig Wittgenstein," which attributes it to PI 43.)

The qualifying clause matters and is easy to lose: the heavily-memorized slogan is the second half of §43; Wittgenstein's own framing restricts it to a large class of cases, not a universal definition of "meaning."

2. Language-games — meaning embedded in practice (§§7, 23)

Wittgenstein introduces the term Sprachspiel (language-game) — primary-direct:

"Ich will diese Spiele »Sprachspiele« nennen, und von einer primitiven Sprache manchmal als einem Sprachspiel reden." (§7)

(Primary-direct, German PD, wittgensteinproject.org. Provenance nuance: the opening clause "Ich will diese Spiele »Sprachspiele« nennen" was returned verbatim by the fetch; the continuation "und von einer primitiven Sprache manchmal als einem Sprachspiel reden" is the standard §7 wording and was not separately reproduced in-tool — re-confirm the full sentence against the German original before leaning hard on the continuation.)

Project gloss: "I will call these games 'language-games', and will sometimes speak of a primitive language as a language-game."

That language is bound up with non-linguistic activity — the "form of life" point — is the §23 line, carried-via-secondary (English):

"the word 'language-game' is used here to emphasize the fact that the speaking of language is part of an activity, or of a form of life" (PI 23)

(Carried-via-secondary: reproduced via SEP, attributed to PI 23.)

This is the pole-(b) core: meaning is read off the role an expression plays in a rule-governed but not strictly-systematized practice, not off a world-relation.

3. Family resemblance — the "game" example (§§66, 67)

The standing example is Spiel (game): looking at the things we call games, one finds no single feature common to all, but a web of overlapping similarities. Primary-direct (the §66 "don't think but look" injunction and the "network of similarities" image):

"Denn wenn du sie anschaust, wirst du zwar nicht etwas sehen, was allen gemeinsam wäre, aber du wirst Ähnlichkeiten, Verwandtschaften, sehen, und zwar eine ganze Reihe. Wie gesagt: denk nicht, sondern schau!" (§66)

"Wir sehen ein kompliziertes Netz von Ähnlichkeiten, die einander übergreifen und kreuzen." (§66)

(Both primary-direct, German PD, wittgensteinproject.org; italics on allen are Wittgenstein's.)

Project gloss: "For if you look at them you will not see something that is common to all, but similarities, relationships, and a whole series of them at that. As said: don't think, but look!" / "We see a complicated network of similarities overlapping and criss-crossing."

The English short forms, carried-via-secondary, attribute both to PI 66:

"Don't think, but look!" (PI 66)

"a complicated network of similarities overlapping and criss-crossing" (PI 66)

(Carried-via-secondary: reproduced via SEP, attributed to PI 66.)

And the term itself, Familienähnlichkeiten (family resemblances), is introduced in §67 — primary-direct:

"Ich kann diese Ähnlichkeiten nicht besser charakterisieren als durch das Wort »Familienähnlichkeiten«" (§67)

(Primary-direct, German PD, wittgensteinproject.org.)

Project gloss: "I can think of no better expression to characterize these similarities than 'family resemblances'."

This is the direct ancestor of prototype/frame-semantics category structure: a concept with no definitional core, held together by criss-crossing similarity — the picture concept/truth-conditional-and-use-meaning ties to the project's graded-sense findings.

4. Rule-following and private language (§§201, 243) — carried-via-secondary only, at lower strength

Two further moves are reachable only via the secondary in this session (no primary-direct German fetched for them); they are recorded at lower strength and should be re-verified against a primary before being leaned on hard.

"This was our paradox: no course of action could be determined by a rule, because every course of action can be made out to accord with the rule. The answer was: if everything can be made out to accord with the rule, then it can also be made out to conflict with it." (PI 201)

"words … are to refer to what only the speaker can know—to his immediate private sensations …" (PI 243)

(Both carried-via-secondary: reproduced via SEP, attributed to PI 201 and PI 243 respectively; PI 243 quoted with the SEP's own ellipses. No primary-direct German confirmation was fetched for these two.)

These ground, at characterization strength, the rejection of a fixed private/determinate sense behind ordinary words — the second pole-(b) move the concept page leans on. Treat the §201/§243 wording as secondary-attributed, unlike the §43/§7/§66/§67 quotes which are confirmed against the PD German.

Bearing on this project

  • concept/truth-conditional-and-use-meaning (pole b). That page's pole (b) opens by attributing to "the later Wittgenstein (Philosophical Investigations, 1953; not in-repo; characterization)" the meaning-as-use thesis, language-games-in-a-form-of-life, and family resemblance. This page supplies the verbatim primary for all three: meaning-as-use (§43, with its restricting "large class of cases" clause), Sprachspiel (§7) as part of "an activity, or of a form of life" (§23), and Familienähnlichkeit (§§66–67). The "Honest gaps" line — "every characterization above is in this page's own words, with no quote or page locator" — is now closed for Wittgenstein specifically (the Tarski/Davidson/Montague legs of that page's gap remain not-in-repo).
  • The use / distributional lineage. Meaning-as-use is the philosophical ancestor of the project's distributional stance (concept/distributional-meaning) and of its methodological "describe use, don't litigate truth-conditions" posture: a meaning theory on this pole describes regularities of use, it does not reduce them to a denotation. The meaning-senses: tags distributional and inferential reflect this — "use" is made precise in the project either distributionally (patterns of co-occurrence/substitution) or inferentially (what an expression licenses), per meaning-senses.md.
  • Family resemblance → graded sense. §§66–67 are the conceptual root of the graded, no-definitional-core picture of word sense that concept/truth-conditional-and-use-meaning maps onto the lexical-sense-gradience result: relatedness as scalar and read off use, not a step function over discrete denotations.

What it can ground

  • The verbatim meaning-as-use formulation with its qualifying "large class of cases" clause (§43) — for any page needing the founding statement rather than the bare slogan, and crucially to correct the over-strong memorized slogan (Wittgenstein restricts it; it is not a universal definition).
  • The verbatim Sprachspiel / language-game-in-a-form-of-life thesis (§§7, 23) and the Familienähnlichkeit / "don't think, but look" / "network of similarities overlapping and criss-crossing" passages (§§66, 67), as the primary for pole (b) and for the family-resemblance ancestor of prototype categories.
  • The historical-conceptual placement of the Investigations as the founding text of the meaning-as-use pole, contrasted against Frege's referential/truth-conditional pole (source/frege-1892-sense-and-reference).

What it cannot ground

  • Any claim about LLMs, embeddings, or neural models. The book predates all of it; it grounds the theory a model may or may not instantiate, never a result about a model. It is not an anchors: resource — not a human-labeled empirical asset (no treebank, sense inventory, acceptability norm, or annotation); citing it discharges no empirical claim's human-anchor obligation.
  • The Anscombe English translation verbatim. That translation is in copyright and was not sourced. English here is either the SEP's carried-via-secondary short quotes or the project's own marked glosses of the PD German — neither may be cited as the published Anscombe wording.
  • The rule-following / private-language argument at primary strength. §§201 and 243 are present here only carried-via-secondary (SEP); no primary-direct German was fetched for them this session. A finding leaning on the rule-following paradox or the private-language argument should reach the primary (PD German, or a legitimate English edition) and re-verify before citing those § numbers at primary strength.
  • The book's broader argument (the Augustinian picture, ostension, "seeing-as," the beetle-in-a-box, aspect perception, etc.): only the §§7, 23, 43, 66, 67 core and the §§201/243 secondary fragments were fetched. Do not cite other § numbers on the strength of this page.

Known limits

  • Two-route provenance, mixed strength. §§43/7/66/67 are confirmed against the PD German on wittgensteinproject.org (primary-direct) and partly corroborated in English via SEP; §23 and §§201/243 are English-only carried-via-secondary (SEP). The German host is an open-access transcription, not a page image of the 1953 Blackwell edition; a session quoting at length should re-verify against the German original (or a critical edition) — especially §23, which was characterized but not fetched verbatim in German here.
  • Glosses are the project's own, not a published translation. Every English rendering of a German quote above is the project's paraphrase, supplied because the Anscombe translation is in copyright; do not cite a gloss as Anscombe's or any other published wording.
  • Section numbering. Locators are PI/PU § (Bemerkung) numbers, the standard citation unit for Part I of the Investigations; they are stable across editions for Part I. No Part II (later "Philosophy of Psychology — A Fragment") material is cited here.
  • Bedeutung. §43 uses Bedeutung ("meaning"/"significance") in its ordinary sense — Wittgenstein is talking about the meaning of the word "meaning" — which is not Frege's technical referential Bedeutung (source/frege-1892-sense-and-reference, where Bedeutung = reference/truth-value). The same German word carries the two poles' opposed readings; flagged to avoid the unqualified-"meaning" lint trap.
