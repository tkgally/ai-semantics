The Japanese comparative correlative — 〜ば〜ほど (-ba … hodo)

Ingested: 2026-07-13 (session 215), to satisfy freeze condition (ii) of decisions/resolved/cross-linguistic-cc-replication-scope — "source-verify the Japanese -ba…hodo form before authoring Japanese items" — and, by the same logic as the German arm, condition (viii): the Japanese CC replication's items must be self-audited against an ingested grammar source, the no-human-subjects-compliant substitute for a human-auditor check. This page records the construction's form (what a well-formed Japanese CC minimal pair looks like) with provenance, so the frozen design/comparative-correlative-japanese-v1 items rest on a verified source, not on unaudited generation.

Provenance depth (honest). The form facts below (the ば…ほど frame, the predicate-repetition signature, the conjugation of the antecedent predicate for the three predicate classes, the proportional "the more X, the more Y" reading) are standard, uncontroversial Japanese grammar, verified this session against a freely readable pedagogical grammar reference read verbatim (JLPT Sensei, N3 ば〜ほど) and corroborated across three further reference descriptions consulted the same session (Hanabira; practice-japanese.com; JapaneseProfessor) — all uniform on the form. Two academic linguistics sources were located, not read in full and are cited below only as corroboration that the construction is a recognized object of study; no item-authoring fact rests on them. Construction form (the authoring anchor — verified firsthand)

〜ば〜ほど expresses a proportional covariation between two scales: "the more X, the more Y" (equivalently "the -er X, the -er Y"). Its signature — and what makes it typologically distant from the English the … the … and German je … desto correlatives — is that Japanese has no overt comparative morpheme (no -er / more / mehr / desto): the "more" is carried entirely by the ば…ほど frame plus repetition of the antecedent predicate, in an SOV, agglutinative, verb-final clause. This is exactly the surface-statistics distance the A6 Japanese arm exists to exploit (freeze condition vii, Q1-C: the stronger anti-template lever).

Antecedent clause (the X scale): [Pred‑ば] [Pred] ほど — the predicate is stated once in its ば-conditional form, then repeated in its plain/attributive form, then ほど. Conjugation by predicate class (verified verbatim from JLPT Sensei; corroborated across the other references):

  • Verbs: V‑ば + V(plain) + ほど. E.g. 聞けば聞くほど (kikeba kiku hodo, "the more one listens"); 勉強すればするほど (benkyō sureba suru hodo, "the more one studies").
  • い-adjectives: A‑ければ + A‑い + ほど. E.g. 高ければ高いほど (takakereba takai hodo, "the higher/more expensive"); 早ければ早いほど (hayakereba hayai hodo, "the earlier").
  • な-adjectives / nominal predicates: NA‑なら(であれば) + NA‑な + ほど. E.g. 静かなら静かなほど (shizuka nara shizuka na hodo, "the quieter"); the 〜であればあるほど variant is also standard.

Consequent clause (the Y scale): the ordinary main clause, stated last (verb-final), whose own scalar predicate rises (〜くなる / 〜になる / 増える / 上がる …) or falls (an antonym predicate, or 減る / 下がる / 〜なくなる …). Because the whole sentence is verb-final, the Y-predicate closes the item.

Verbatim examples (JLPT Sensei, read this session)

ビートルズの音楽は聞けば聞くほど好きになります。 = "The more I listen to The Beatles, the more I come to like their music." 勉強すればするほど、日本語が上手になりますよ。 = "The more you study, the better your Japanese gets." お金はあればあるほど安心です。 = "The more money you have, the more at ease you feel."

Two-scale (different-X-and-Y) CC, the form this instrument uses

The pedagogical examples above vary a single predicate (X = Y in kind: listen→like). The construction equally licenses two distinct scales — the shape the English/German instrument uses (Je dicker der Roman, desto geduldiger der Leser / the thicker the novel, the more patient the reader). In Japanese:

小説が厚ければ厚いほど、読者は辛抱強くなった。 = "The thicker the novel was, the more patient the reader became." — antecedent X = 厚い (thickness, repeated 厚ければ厚いほど); consequent Y = 辛抱強い (patience) rising via 〜くなった. The inverse swaps Y for a fall: … 読者は飽きっぽくなった ("… the more easily bored the reader became") — the same X frame, Y decreasing.

What this source is used for (and its bounds)

  • Used for: authoring well-formed Japanese CC items (the cc-positive / cc-inverse forms) and their same-word non-CC controls (two independent declaratives; a single scalar clause) — fixing the form so the construction-isolation contrast is between the ば…ほど syntax and its absence, with the scalar vocabulary held constant across forms.
  • Not used for: any claim about Japanese CC frequency, acquisition, or human processing — none of which this page establishes. The frequency/co-occurrence control uses UD Japanese-GSD (CC BY-SA 4.0, license verified firsthand this session; a separate resource); this page fixes only construction form.
  • Not a human anchor. This is a grammar/construction-form source, not a human-judged CC dataset; the Japanese replication is internal-contrast-only (no non-English CC human dataset exists per the scout, Q3-A).
