Relational meaning

Relational (or interactional) meaning is meaning constituted between agents — between a model and a person, or between two models — rather than computed inside a single agent and then read off. It is the second of the charter's three candidate axes for where LLM-meaning lives, and the one the charter singles out as "genuinely under-explored" (PROJECT.md §1). The controlled vocabulary states the wedge tersely: relational meaning is "Meaning constituted between a model and a person, or between models — the under-explored second axis from the charter. Treat this as the project's distinctive wedge: the multi-agent literature is about coordination, not meaning-constitution" (meaning-senses.md, §relational). The phrase to hold onto is constituted between: the claim is not that two systems happen to coordinate, but that the content at issue exists in the interaction and not antecedently in either party.

Why this is the charter's distinctive wedge

The opportunity is precisely that the existing multi-agent literature does not occupy this space. Work on communicative agents, debate, negotiation, and emergent communication studies whether agents coordinate — whether they reach a shared label, converge on a strategy, or improve task success — but coordination is silent on constitution. Two agents that each independently compute the same convention from the same evidence would look perfectly coordinated without anything being constituted between them. So "the multi-agent literature is about coordination, not meaning-constitution" (meaning-senses.md, §relational) is not a dismissal of that literature; it is the statement of an unfilled niche. A lexicographer's question — whose meaning is this, and where does it live — is exactly the question that coordination metrics skip. The charter flags the same point and the same reason it is open: the relational axis is "genuinely under-explored (the multi-agent literature is about coordination, not meaning-constitution) and is a strong opportunity" (PROJECT.md §1).

The contrast with model-internal meaning: a second ladder

Relational meaning is defined against model-internal meaning — content located in a single model's behavior or representations. Every rung of the project's current evidence ladder is model-internal: the constructional probes (dative, AANN, way, caused-motion, conative, comparative-correlative) all ask what one model knows about a form–meaning pairing. The synthesis page states the relation between the two loci explicitly: "The relational axis is not a higher rung of the same ladder; it is a second ladder whose bottom rung is not yet defined" (theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms, §"The under-explored axis"). The metaphor is load-bearing. Relational meaning is not "more meaning" stacked above internal meaning on the same scale; it is a different kind of object, measured differently, with its own floor that has not yet been built. The same page records the consequence honestly: "Until a relational pilot exists, claims on this page are scoped to single-model constructional meaning" (theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms, §"The under-explored axis" — the first edition, quoted as written; superseded s177 by theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms-v2, and the pilot has since run — see the Live-tension section below; the relational axis now has its own synthesis, theory/relational-meaning-in-llms). This concept page names the second ladder; the Live-tension section below records how far it has since been climbed.

The defining difficulty is operational, and the open question states it exactly: distinguishing "meaning constituted BETWEEN agents" from "meaning computed in each, then averaged" (open-question/relational-meaning-pilot). A serious relational claim has to make those two stories come apart in a measurement, rather than assuming the first because coordination was observed.

The empirical anchor direction (both anchors since fetched — see the update box)

The human order-/path-sensitivity comparison anchor remains a genuine IOU (the fetched pages ground convergence/compression and the theoretical backdrop, not an order-sensitivity gradient) — see the Live-tension section's caution, which stands.

The relational axis needs a human-generated dyadic-interaction resource to anchor any empirical claim (always-on rule 5; PROJECT.md §2.5). The anchor shortlist has been ratified (decisions/resolved/relational-anchor-shortlist, Option A):

  • Clark & Wilkes-Gibbs 1986, "Referring as a collaborative process" — the canonical referential-communication / tangram-naming study, in which dyads collaboratively coin and compress a shared label for hard-to-name figures over repeated rounds. It is the ratified empirical anchor: it supplies the human convergence/compression baseline and a documented paradigm the relational pilot directly instantiates.
  • Pickering & Garrod 2004, "Toward a mechanistic psychology of dialogue" — the interactive-alignment framework, the most-cited mechanistic model of dialogue alignment-across-levels. It is the ratified theoretical backdrop only: useful for what alignment predicts and, importantly, for what it does not claim about meaning-constitution.

Both papers are not yet in the repository. They are queued in base/wanted.md (P1 and P2 respectively) and have not been fetched, so this page describes them at the conceptual level only and quotes neither. No page number, quotation, or finding is attributed to either work here, and none may be until Clark & Wilkes-Gibbs 1986 is fetched and read — at which point a source/ page with page-level provenance becomes the citable ground, and this concept page can be linked to it with an anchors relation. Until then the relational axis carries an explicit anchor IOU, exactly as the resolved decision records: "no relational result may be promoted until the anchor is fetched" (decisions/resolved/relational-anchor-shortlist).

How the project operationalizes the tag: trajectory-dependence

The relational tag earns its keep only if there is a measure that separates "constituted between" from "computed within and averaged." The candidate the project has sharpened is trajectory-dependence, defined in open-question/relational-meaning-pilot: if a coined convention is genuinely constituted in the interaction, then one agent's later interpretation of the coined term should depend on the ordered history of the exchange — the particular sequence of repairs and refinements that produced it — and not merely on the set of utterances that happened to occur. The discriminating contrast is live dialogue vs. shuffled-history replay: hold the content of prior turns constant but destroy their ordering, and ask whether interpretation survives unchanged. A live-vs-shuffled gap is the operational signature of a convention built between rather than within; its absence is a relational deflation. That live/shuffled contrast is the project's candidate bottom rung of the second ladder — the minimal design whose result would count as evidence for or against relational meaning-constitution.

Crucially, the deflationary alternative here is itself a distributional story, which is why this page co-tags distributional and depends on concept/distributional-meaning. Two next-token predictors conditioned on overlapping recent context will drift toward a shared label and will show entrainment — length compression and rising token overlap — purely from co-occurrence content, and that convergence would survive order-scrambling. So lexical entrainment alone is not sufficient evidence for the relational tag; it is explicable by distributional structure that would equally appear in the shuffled control. Relational meaning-constitution requires a surplus over distributional convergence, and the live-vs-shuffled gap is exactly that surplus (open-question/relational-meaning-pilot). This is the relational analogue of the discipline that runs through the constructional pages: the eye-catching convergence is the floor, and only the discriminating contrast climbs.

Open distinction: where does grounded.social belong?

The typology carries an unresolved seam directly adjacent to this concept, which is why this page refines concept/grounding. The meaning-senses page asks: "Is grounded.social a sub-tag of grounded or a separate top-level sense closer to relational? Plausibly the latter for this project" (meaning-senses.md, §Open issues). Social grounding — uptake in joint action, normative status between participants — is hard to distinguish from relational meaning, because both locate content in what happens between parties rather than inside one. The concept/grounding page flags the same seam from its side, noting that until the placement decision is taken "a finding that turns on social grounding should consider co-tagging relational." This concept page does not resolve the question; it records that the grounded.social-vs-relational boundary is open, that a finding straddling it should co-tag both, and that the placement should not be treated as settled.

Live tension for this project. Relational meaning is the project's most distinctive claim and its least evidenced. The axis's first in-repo result (2026-05-31) — the iterated dyadic reference-game pilot (result/relational-reference-game-v1) — was a first-class null (the closest in-design outside neighbor, source/zeng-2026-lvlms-ground-differently, reaches a compatible reading on the same paradigm — LVLM dyads fail to coin-and-compress where human dyads in the same task do — though it runs no order-scramble control, so the live-vs-shuffled discriminator remains this project's own wedge): homogeneous LLM dyads coordinated and used coined conventions, but those conventions were order-insensitive in that setting (recovered from the content set, not the ordered trajectory), so no live-vs-shuffled surplus appeared. Update (2026-06-16): a later, decisive arm changes the picture without overturning that null. When physical text-position is neutralized and a coined term is reassigned across stamped rounds so that the content-set is symmetric and only recency disambiguates, both panel models recover the term by its most-recent binding spontaneously and at ceiling (result/relational-spontaneous-recency-a; claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment). So the recovered convention is order-sensitive (non-commutative) where order carries disambiguating information — the bottom rung of the second ladder is partly climbed. But only the bottom rung: the order-sensitivity is thin (a latest-binding-wins / convention-update rule), which is not a convention constituted between agents through the live trajectory. The v1 null is not wrong — it is bounded: commutativity is operationalization-dependent (present where order carries no disambiguating signal, absent where it does). Update (2026-06-17): an implicit-reassignment control removed the most obvious deflationary worry about this positive — that the models tracked recency only because the prompt told them a reassignment had occurred. With that explicit sentence dropped and everything else byte-identical, both models still recovered the most-recent binding at ceiling (result/relational-implicit-reassignment-control), so the thin order-sensitivity is not a wording artifact. This strengthens the bottom-rung finding without lifting it: still thin latest-wins, still short of constitution. Update (2026-06-17, rung ii): a non-overwrite-repair probe (result/relational-integration-rung-ii) tested the next rung — whether an earlier, non-terminal turn survives when it is compatible with the latest (refinement, not replacement). It does: when only the conjunction of an earlier shape and a latest pattern is unique, both models compose the two at ceiling (overwrite rate 0.000), so the update rule is supersede-on-conflict, compose-on-compatibility. This occupies a second, stricter rung — but still on the thin side: the composition observed is exactly what a single reader of the record performs (intersect the stated constraints), and the symmetric design shows the earlier turn survives, not that the composition is order-sensitive. Two thin rungs are now climbed; the rich upper ladder (path-dependence, between-agent constitution) stays unbuilt (see essay/update-is-not-constitution). Update (2026-06-18, rung iii closure): the gap rung (ii) left open — is the composition itself order-sensitive (non-commutative), or only survival of a compatible turn that a commutative conjunction passes equally? — was tested directly with Option C of the ratified rung-(iii) decision (result/relational-order-composition-c). A coined token moves on a track under two stamped, non-commuting operations (STEP/FLIP), so the two stamp orders reach different end states and order is load-bearing by construction. The verdict splits: claude orders the two moves by their round stamps spontaneously and at ceiling (COMP 1.000, DIRECT on-demand gate 0.861 PASS) — genuine order-sensitive, non-commutative composition, a stricter dependence than the rung-(ii) integration (which showed only survival of a compatible turn, a commutative conjunction passing equally); gemini cannot compose the two moves even when told the order (DIRECT 0.583 < 0.80 → UNINTERPRETABLE), so this rung is occupied by one model, not the panel. Two points carry the calibration. First, the thin side gained a stricter rung but is still thin — adjudicated before the run as single-reader-recoverable ("respects operation order," not rung iii). This is not the live-vs-shuffled surplus discussed above: the operation order here is itself written into the record's content (the stamped move-list), so a non-commuting-operation gap is something a single reader applies in stamp order, not arrival-order surplus carried outside what the record states. Second, the rich side — path-dependence proper (rung iii) — is now documented structurally CLOSED for text-only stimuli. Because a transcript is a final content+stamps record, no text design can carry arrival-order surplus beyond what the record states; the strongest order-load-bearing text design the project can build (Option C, two genuinely non-commuting operations) was built and run, and even claude's perfect gap is thin. Per decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence (§A5) this documented closure is a fully promotable outcome, not a failure, and it sharpens this page's standing point that the rich upper ladder (path-dependence, between-agent constitution) stays unbuilt: it is unbuilt because the medium forecloses it. The closure is medium-bound — a different medium (an interaction log with normative observables) is untested and out of scope — and it corroborates essay/transcript-ceiling. The honest scope stays narrow: this finding is internal-contrast-only (no human comparison; the in-repo human convergence baseline is resource/hawkins-tangrams for compression only, and the ratified anchor Clark & Wilkes-Gibbs 1986 (since fetched, s134 — resource/clark-wilkes-gibbs-1986-referring) grounds convergence/compression, not order-sensitivity), and it does not assert that any model exhibits constituted relational meaning. Two cautions still travel with the concept. First, the human side of the order-/path-sensitivity contrast remains unanchored in-repo, so any human-comparison statement is an IOU, not a finding. Second, the deflationary distributional reading of entrainment is the default that any relational constitution claim must beat; the thin latest-wins rule is order-sensitive but is not that surplus — treating it as constitution would be the relational version of the distributional null this project keeps insisting on writing.
