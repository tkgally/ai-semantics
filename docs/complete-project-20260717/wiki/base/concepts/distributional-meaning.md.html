Distributional meaning

The distributional view holds that a linguistic expression's meaning is constituted — or at minimum reliably tracked — by its pattern of co-occurrence across contexts. The canonical slogan is Firth's: "you shall know a word by the company it keeps" — reliably attributed (via secondary sources) to source/firth-1957-synopsis, which carries the line through Brunila & LaViolette (2022) and Quote Investigator rather than from the Firth primary, so it is cited here as attribution, not as a primary reading. The formal distributional hypothesis is Harris's, read here from the primary (Caltech scan): "difference of meaning correlates with difference of distribution" (source/harris-1954-distributional-structure, p. 156). In the computational tradition the view is instantiated in word embeddings and the next-token-prediction objective: a model trained to predict context implicitly encodes distributional structure, and that structure is often treated as a proxy for lexical or compositional distributional meaning.

For this project the distributional tag underwrites the null hypothesis against which constructional and functional-competence claims are tested. When an LLM tracks a construction's characteristic form and statistical environment, that is distributional competence. The live empirical question is whether the tracked structure also encodes the construction's meaning — form-meaning pairing in the CxG sense — or only its formal recurrence. Weissweiler et al. (2023) flag exactly this as the central confound: a model may reproduce collocational patterns without encoding "the extent of the construction" or the inferential role of its slots (source/weissweiler-2023-cxg-insight, §3.1).

A critical constraint on citing distributional meaning: distributional structure is, by itself, silent on reference and on truth. The controlled vocabulary in meaning-senses.md (§distributional) makes this explicit. Bender & Koller (2020) argue that no amount of distributional pattern-learning yields meaning proper because meaning is defined by a relation to communicative intent and the world — a relation that form-only training cannot supply (source/bender-koller-2020-climbing, p. 5185). Piantadosi & Hill (2022) contest this, arguing that systematic inferential-role structure — which distributional training plausibly produces — constitutes meaning in the conceptual-role sense, without requiring extra-linguistic reference (source/piantadosi-hill-2022-meaning-without-reference, abstract). The tension between these two positions is live for every empirical finding in this project that involves an LLM.

Historiographic caveat: "the" distributional hypothesis is two ideas, not one. Following Brunila & LaViolette (2022, arXiv:2205.07750) as reported in source/firth-1957-synopsis ("Bearing on this project" / "What it can ground"), Firth and Harris — routinely bundled as co-founders of distributional semantics — in fact diverge: Harris's distribution is internal to linguistic form, whereas Firth's "collocation" is only one mode of meaning among several and is always situated in a broader "context of situation." The consequence worth stating is that a probe instantiating Harris-style form-internal distribution is not thereby instantiating Firth's wider contextual/situational notion; and NLP's appropriation of Firth arguably narrows his notion to exactly the form-internal residue that is silent on reference and on truth. This is a secondary-sourced historiographic observation (via Brunila & LaViolette), not an empirical result — but it sharpens the silent-on-reference point above: the residue the embedding tradition inherits from Firth is precisely the part that does not hook onto the world. The essay essay/two-distributional-hypotheses develops the consequence — that an LLM's distributional competence instantiates the Harris (form-internal) hypothesis and not the Firth (situated) one, so a distributional success re-labels rather than resolves the grounding question.

Live tension for this project. Distributional training is also the route by which formal competence is acquired (Mahowald et al. 2024; source/mahowald-2024-dissociating): the distributional objective produces strong formal-linguistic competence — structural acceptability, morphological generalization — without demonstrably producing functional competence. A distributional success therefore does not settle the formal/functional question, and it does not settle the grounded/ungrounded question. Findings tagged distributional should specify which of those further questions they bear on, or explicitly mark themselves as silent on them. Co-tagging with inferential is appropriate when the finding turns on whether the distributional representations also encode inferential roles.

What the project's findings say about the distributional null

The distributional null above is not only a definition; it is the baseline the project's empirical loop actually tests against. A recurring design — does the model track a meaning gradient that beats the distributional shadow? — puts a phenomenon's behavior against a matched distributional control (a co-occurrence statistic, a frequency covariate, or a same-material contrast) and asks whether anything survives. This section is a pointer to where that null now lives; every number sits on the cited page at its stated strength, not here.

The flagship measured backbone. theory/shadow-depth-table-v1 (draft; internal-contrast-only as an arrangement — it adds no new measurement or human comparison) collates the project's controlled probes into one object sorted by shadow-depth: how much of a phenomenon is already written into surface co-occurrence. Each beater row is a residual over a named distributional control with a 95% CI — this table is the operationalization of "beat the distributional shadow." Each saturated corner is a phenomenon placed at the deep-shadow end by an argued reading. The table's own honest one-line summary is that the project has measured that the shadow can be beaten at both the word and construction grains, while the "deep corner at each grain" half stays a reading (and one such corner has since been measured — see the s186 result below). Read the two honesty boxes there before citing: the beater rows use two non-commensurable residual forms (a within-model matched-material gap vs. a human-gradient partial correlation), so no cross-row magnitude ranking is made or implied.

The promoted beater claims — each a within-model residual over a named distributional control, read at its own scoped strength:

  • claim/comparative-correlative-covariation (supported) — the panel asserts a covariation direction for ~100% of comparative-correlative items but for only ~12% of matched same-word non-CC controls: a construction-isolation gap ≈87 pp (86.8 / 88.2 / 86.8; 95% CI lower bound ≈78 pp) at powered N. That gap is a within-model magnitude (internal-contrast-only legs); the claim is scoped to a direction/ordering statement on a near-ceiling instrument, not a magnitude-vs-human or general-competence claim.
  • claim/dative-information-structure-givenness (supported) — a within-item information-structure shift whose two scored phrasings are byte-identical across the item's two discourse contexts, so the distributional control is by construction (length/order/position immune). Powered N=100, CONFIRM 3/3, but the per-model magnitudes decorrelate ~9× and gpt-5.4-mini's is a tiny +0.056; human-anchored on direction only (Bresnan production corpus).
  • claim/lexical-sense-gradience (supported) — the panel's graded sense-relatedness rating rank-tracks the human DWUG DURel median (ρ 0.60–0.83, in/above the human inter-annotator range) and survives partialling out the model's own topic-similarity rating (the lexical analogue of the constructional shadow), partial ρ|topic 0.52 / 0.50 / 0.73. Cross-date replicated 3/3 on fresh pair-disjoint items; scoped to direction/agreement, the topic control model-internal and modest.
  • claim/aann-behavioral-gradient (supported, scoped) — prompted AANN acceptability rank-tracks the human Exp-2 MTurk gradient (cell ρ 0.68–0.75) and survives a Zipf word-frequency partial (0.69 / 0.66 / 0.74) plus a noun-class marginal guard. Cross-date replicated for the graded gradient; but held-out productivity is noun-class-dependent and the temporal stratum fails (uniformly negative).

The newest, most directly on-point datum — about the null's reach. result/lexical-relation-shadow-saturation-v1 (s186; proposed; internal-contrast-only) built an explicit contrastive-frame co-occurrence (G²) control over six WordNet noun relations and scored it against the panel's relatum recovery. Two results bear straight on the distributional null: (a) even the most-cued relation's recovery is largely un-explained by the control — antonymy, top of corpus cue-strength, still carries one of the largest residuals (HIT@3 +0.61–0.67), not the smallest; and (b) corpus cue-strength does not predict which relations get recovered (across-relation Spearman ≈ −0.086, 3/3 models; hypernymy is best-recovered, not the most-cued antonymy). A pre-registered calibration gate fired (the control recovers almost none of any relation's relata), so the residual arm is descriptive-only — but the direction of that news is that this co-occurrence control's predictive reach over what the panel recovers is slight.

What none of these findings claim — the modest frame that binds them all. A residual over a co-occurrence control does not show the model escaping distribution. Per essay/two-distributional-hypotheses, the next-token objective instantiates the Harris (form-internal) hypothesis, so "beating the shadow" is graded inside form-internal distribution, not against a situated or world-relating baseline. And per essay/shortcut-vs-competence-mis-cut (ratified s151), a residual over any one chosen co-occurrence statistic grades local-vs-transferable distributional generalization — the model getting the answer where the local cue is thin, by an abstracted contrast that travels — not distribution vs. non-distribution. A surviving residual is the shadow's transferable grade, not evidence of competence beyond distribution. So each finding above is a within-distribution result: it sharpens which distributional achievement is shown and leaves the reference/grounding questions (the distributional caveat's "silent on reference and on truth") exactly where the sections above put them.
