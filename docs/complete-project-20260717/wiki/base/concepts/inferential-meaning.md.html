Inferential meaning

The inferential view holds that a linguistic expression's meaning is constituted by its inferential role: a word or construction means what it licenses you to infer to and from. Brandom's inferentialism (1994/2000; not in-repo) is the systematic philosophical statement — content is fixed by an expression's position in a web of material inferences and incompatibilities, not by any prior relation to objects. Conceptual-role semantics in philosophy of mind is the broader family: the content of a representational state is its causal-functional role among other states. On either telling, to grasp a concept is to be disposed to make the inferences it underwrites, and the unit of analysis is the relational structure, not the isolated symbol.

For this project the inferential tag marks findings whose force is that an LLM tracks the inferences a word or construction licenses — that it preserves or breaks the right entailments, not merely that it reproduces a characteristic distribution. The contrast with the distributional tag is deliberate. Distributional structure is co-occurrence; inferential structure is what follows from what. The two are entangled in an LLM — next-token prediction is implicitly inferential, and meaning-senses.md flags the open question of whether the two senses should collapse — but they are not definitionally the same, which is why this page refines rather than restates concept/distributional-meaning. A finding earns the inferential tag when the indicator is inference-preservation, not similarity or surprisal alone.

Piantadosi & Hill's "meaning without reference"

The strongest pro-LLM-meaning position in the current literature is Piantadosi & Hill (2022), who argue from conceptual-role semantics that meaning constituted through inferential relationships among a system's internal states is genuine meaning, and that LLMs plausibly achieve it. The central thesis is explicit:

"Contrary to claims that LLMs possess no meaning whatsoever, we argue that they likely capture important aspects of meaning, and moreover work in a way that approximates a compelling account of human cognition in which meaning arises from conceptual role. Because conceptual role is defined by the relationships between internal representational states, meaning cannot be determined from a model's architecture, training data, or objective function, but only by examination of how its internal states relate to each other." (source/piantadosi-hill-2022-meaning-without-reference, §Abstract)

Two moves do the work. First, meaning is relational rather than referential — it "comes from the way concepts relate to each other" (§"Conceptual role theory"). Second, reference is therefore not necessary: "there are many terms that are meaningful to us but have no discernible referent at all, such as abstract words like 'justice' and 'wit.'" (§"Meaning and reference"). The illustrative case is logical vocabulary: "A symbol like AND only means logical conjunction if it interacts (composes) with others symbols like TRUE and FALSE in the appropriate way—i.e. when it has the right conceptual role" (§"Discovering conceptual role"). The upshot for LLMs is a positive claim stronger than mere distributional competence — that an LLM whose internal states stand in the right relations already "share[s] the foundation of how our own concepts get their meaning" (§"Conclusion").

This is the direct counter to the grounding requirement, which is why this page contradicts concept/grounding. Bender & Koller (2020) hold that form-only training cannot yield meaning because meaning is a relation to communicative intent and the world that distributional form does not supply (source/bender-koller-2020-climbing). Piantadosi & Hill deny the premise: if inferential role is sufficient for meaning, then the absence of extra-linguistic grounding is not a disqualification.

How the project operationalizes the tag

The inferential indicator is inference behavior, not distribution. Concretely: entailment / NLI behavior (does the model accept what its premises entail and reject what they exclude?); acceptability and behavior under inference-licensing constructions (constructions co-tagged constructional because constructions license characteristic inferences); and systematic substitution that preserves or breaks inferential role — swapping a term for an inferential equivalent should leave licensed inferences intact, while swapping for a non-equivalent should break them. A model that passes the first and fails the second is exhibiting inferential structure rather than surface mimicry. Piantadosi & Hill supply the philosophical backing for treating such a result as semantically significant, but they do not supply the evidence: the source page is explicit that it "cannot ground … empirical claims about whether specific models actually exhibit the right inferential role structure" — that requires a human-anchored resource on the finding page itself.

Live tension for this project. The genuine open question is whether systematic inferential-role structure produced by distributional training actually constitutes meaning. Piantadosi & Hill say yes; Bender & Koller's grounding requirement says form-only training never yields meaning regardless of how systematic its inferential structure looks. This project does not adjudicate that dispute — it sets up findings so the contrast is visible, and a page that pits "model tracks the inferential role" against "model is form-only" must cite both sides. Two honest caveats temper any triumphalism. First, the source page's "Known limits" warns that the conceptual-role account advocated there "is not the only version of inferentialism; it should not be conflated with Brandom-style normative inferentialism or with NLI-task performance alone" — so a strong NLI score is not by itself the philosophical conclusion. Second, all Piantadosi & Hill quotes here are section-level, not page-level (the ar5iv HTML rendering has no pagination); this is expected for this source and noted on its page, but it means citations locate by heading, not page number.

Brandom's normative inferentialism (and why it is not what the project measures)

The previous section's caveat names a fork that matters more than its single sentence suggests. There are (at least) two inferentialisms, and the project measures only the thinner one. The systematic non-Piantadosi-Hill branch is Robert Brandom's normative inferentialism (Making It Explicit, 1994; Articulating Reasons, 2000; not in-repo; characterization), with Wilfrid Sellars's image of "the space of reasons" (Empiricism and the Philosophy of Mind, 1956; not in-repo; characterization) as its lineage. The contrast with the conceptual-role account already developed above is not a quibble; it is a category difference, and getting it wrong would let a strong NLI score masquerade as a philosophical conclusion it cannot earn.

Four features characterize the Brandomian position (characterized, not quoted — these works are not in the repository):

  1. Material inference. For Brandom the primitive notion of inferential goodness is material, not formal: the inference from "Pittsburgh is to the west of Princeton" to "Princeton is to the east of Pittsburgh," or from "it is raining" to "the streets will be wet," is a good inference in virtue of the conceptual content of its non-logical terms (west/east, rain/wet), not in virtue of logical form. Logically valid inference is a derivative, made-explicit special case, not the foundation. The content of a concept just is its role in the web of such material inferences and incompatibilities.
  2. The game of giving and asking for reasons. Concept use is modeled as a social practice — a discursive game in which participants make assertions, challenge them, and demand and supply reasons. To grasp a concept is to be able to play one's part in this game: to know what one's assertion commits one to and what would entitle one to it.
  3. Content as a normative/social status. This is the decisive divergence. For Brandom, conceptual content is constituted by deontic statuses — commitments and entitlements — that are instituted by the practice and tracked between interlocutors (the "deontic scorekeeping" picture). Content is a normative status in a social practice, not a causal-functional property of one head. What you mean is fixed by what you would be committed and entitled to, where commitment and entitlement are social-normative, not merely dispositional, facts.
  4. The priority of the inferential and propositional over the representational. Brandom inverts the usual explanatory order: rather than explaining inference in terms of antecedently-given representational contents, he explains representation (reference, aboutness) as something made explicit by inferential-expressive vocabulary downstream of a prior practice of giving and asking for reasons. ("Making it explicit" is the program's name.)

The bearing on this project is a sharp, load-bearing distinction. Piantadosi & Hill's conceptual role is internalist and causal-functional: meaning is constituted by inferential relations among one system's internal representational states, with the locus explicitly inside the model — recall their thesis that meaning can be found "only by examination of how its internal states relate to each other" (source/piantadosi-hill-2022-meaning-without-reference, §Abstract). Brandom's conceptual role is social and normative: meaning is constituted in deontic statuses instituted between interlocutors in a practice. These are different theories of where inferential content lives — within a single causal-functional economy, versus between agents in a normative-social one. The source page makes the non-conflation an explicit limit of its own scope:

"The conceptual-role account the paper advocates is not the only version of inferentialism; it should not be conflated with Brandom-style normative inferentialism or with NLI-task performance alone." (source/piantadosi-hill-2022-meaning-without-reference, §"Known limits")

So when this page tags a finding inferential, it should be read against the Piantadosi-Hill internalist sense by default — the sense the in-repo source actually defends. A Brandomian inferentialist would say the project's indicators do not reach the deontic-social fact that, on his view, constitutes content at all. That is not an objection the project can wave away with a higher accuracy number; it is a disagreement about what would even count as evidence. (Whether the holism common to both branches is itself a liability is a separate matter, developed at concept/semantic-holism, not here.)

What the project's inference-licensing results do and don't show

The caveat becomes concrete when set against the project's own Tier-4 (inference-licensing) results. These are the strongest behavioral evidence in the repo that current decoders track construction-contributed inferences — and they make the P&H "Known limits" caveat vivid using the project's own data: they operationalize a thin, NLI / forced-choice notion of inferential role (inference-preservation behavior on frozen minimal pairs), not Brandom's normative-social articulation of content.

What the results show (behaviorally, at their stated bars):

  • The way-construction — all three models draw the path-traversal entailment onto non-motion verbs (Mia whistled her way down the hall ⊨ she moved), at 77.8–100%, in 3/3 models on both instruments, with the idiomatic-way over-generalization guard at 0% (result/way-construction-traversal-v1).
  • Caused-motion — the construction's added causation-of-motion entailment is affirmed at 90–100% (gap 70–100 pp over controls, 3/3 models), and crucially withheld when the object moves by another cause (result/caused-motion-minimal-pair-divergence-v1).
  • The conative — the at-frame's cancellation of completed contact surfaces as a forced-choice gap of 42–88 pp across all three models (result/conative-minimal-pair-divergence-v1).
  • The comparative correlative — multi-step covariation composes at 100% (3/3 models, both instruments), including the diagnostic multi-bears item where two negative steps compose to a positive relation (negative × negative = positive), which a single-clause heuristic gets wrong (result/comparative-correlative-covariation-v2; since promoted, with its sibling runs, into claim/comparative-correlative-covariation, s168).

What they do not show is anything about Brandom's notion of inferential role. They are forced-choice and NLI judgments about whether a sentence licenses a target inference — inference-preservation, a within-model behavioral signature. They say nothing about commitments and entitlements instituted between interlocutors, about the giving-and-asking-for-reasons practice, or about content as a social-normative status. A model that aces every cell of the tables above has demonstrated the thin inferential-role competence and is silent on the thick Brandomian one. This is exactly the P&H caveat — "should not be conflated with … NLI-task performance alone" — made concrete: the strongest inference-licensing scores the project has are precisely "NLI-task performance," and the caveat warns against reading them as the philosophical conclusion.

There is a second, sharper twist the project's own evidence forces. Even the thin measure is instrument-dependent, so a strong NLI score is not by itself even the behavioral conclusion, let alone the philosophical one. The conative case is the clean demonstration: gpt-5.4-mini fails the conative entirely under NLI — it calls all 24 conative items "entailment," reading kicked at the ball as entailing made contact — yet partly recovers under forced-choice (gap +42 pp), the same model on the same frozen items in the same run (result/conative-minimal-pair-divergence-v1). Whether measured "inferential competence" is a stable property of the model or partly an artifact of the elicitation instrument is a standing methodological question with a typed home: open-question/instrument-sensitivity-constructional-inference, which has since separated two sources of instrument sensitivity (a gpt-5.4-mini-specific logical slip; a general NLI-vs-forced-choice pragmatics/entailment effect) and bounded their pervasiveness. The upshot for this concept page is conservative: a strong NLI score is not, by itself, the philosophical conclusion — first because the thick Brandomian notion is untouched by it, and second because the thin notion it does index is itself elicitation-fragile. (INTERPRETATION of the project's results in light of the concept; the results' own calibrated readings and "does-not-license" lists are on their pages, and several lead with the standing "ceiling on relatively easy controls is weak evidence for any strong deep-processing reading" caveat.)

The relational bridge

Brandom's is precisely the version of inferentialism on which content would be constituted between agents — in the deontic scorekeeping of a shared practice — rather than inside one head. That makes the project's relational axis (concept/relational-meaning, the charter's "constituted between" wedge) the natural place where a Brandomian inferentialism would, if anywhere, get empirical purchase: if these models' inferential content were normatively/socially instituted in the Brandomian sense, one would expect it to show up as something constituted in interaction and not recoverable from one agent's internal computation alone.

The one in-repo result that bears on this is the relational reference-game pilot (result/relational-reference-game-v1), and it points the other way. Homogeneous LLM dyads coordinate and use a coined convention, but the convention proved order-insensitive: giving the matcher the content of the prior turns lifts accuracy (history-lift +0.25 to +0.42), but destroying their order does not (ordered − shuffled +0.06 to +0.11, and no model's CI excludes 0). The convention is recovered from the set of prior turns, not their ordered trajectory — coordination, not constitution. Read against the two inferentialisms, this is (weak, bounded) evidence that whatever inferential structure these models exhibit looks model-internal — the internalist causal-functional kind P&H describe, recoverable from the content each agent conditions on — rather than relationally/normatively instituted in the Brandomian, between-agents sense, which would have predicted a surplus that survives only in the ordered interaction. (No longer the only bearing result — s183 back-annotation: the later recency-disambiguation regime found the panel's referential conventions order-sensitive — claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment, supported 2026-06-16. Its own scope limits keep this page's reading intact: the order-sensitivity is consistent with a thin latest-binding-wins convention-update rule, single-reader-recoverable, and expressly does not establish a convention constituted between agents — so the internalist, model-internal reading above stands, now bounded by one more datum rather than resting on the pilot alone.)

This reading must be marked twice over. First, it is INTERPRETATION of a bounded null, not a result: the pilot is at pilot scale (n=12 coined terms/model), detects only large consistent order effects, was, until its 2026-06-12 ratification (decisions/resolved/relational-pilot-operationalization), contingent on an open operationalization decision, and its own headline is a deflationary relational null whose force is internal-contrast-only on the load-bearing trajectory measure — it does not assert that any model lacks relational meaning, only that this probe finds no evidence of constitution. Second, the inference from "no order-sensitivity" to "model-internal rather than Brandomian-normative" is mine on this concept page, not a claim the result page makes; it is the kind of weak abductive bridge that the holism objection (which applies to both branches of inferentialism alike) could also reframe — and that development belongs at concept/semantic-holism, which already absorbs this same result cautiously and to which the holism question is handed. What is fair to say here is narrow: the project's first relational probe is more consistent with the internalist conceptual-role picture than with a relationally-constituted one, which is a small piece of friction against expecting the Brandomian version to be the right description of these systems — and nothing stronger.
