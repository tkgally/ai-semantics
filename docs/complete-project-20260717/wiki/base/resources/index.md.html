Human-anchored resources — catalog

This is the project's reservoir of independent human bearing on empirical claims about LLM meaning (charter §2.5). Every empirical claim about LLM meaning must anchors: a resource catalogued here.

A resource page belongs here when the artifact:

  1. Was produced by humans, with human judgment in the loop (treebank, sense-annotated corpus, acceptability norming, dictionary, construction inventory, etc.).
  2. Has a documented method and a stable identifier.
  3. Can be inspected (in repo or via a stable URL) by a later run.

Format per page (under base/resources/<id>.md):

---
type: resource
id: universal-dependencies
title: Universal Dependencies
status: catalogued | verified | partial | external-only | scouting
url: https://universaldependencies.org/
local-path: data/ud/   # if mirrored locally
license: ...
---

Plus a short prose description of:

  • What the resource is and how it was made.
  • What kinds of claims it can ground (be specific — UD can ground claims about dependency structure across languages; it cannot ground claims about word sense disambiguation).
  • Known limits.
  • Pointer to the specific feature(s) most likely to be cited by claims.

Catalogued

  • mahowald-2023-aann-stimuli.md — Mahowald 2023 EACL AANN stimulus suite (templates + slot fillers) plus MTurk acceptability ratings, released at github.com/mahowak/aann-public. Status: external-only. Grounds: conjecture/aann-construction and experiments/designs/aann-construction-v1.md.
  • scivetti-2025-cxnli-dataset.md — Scivetti et al. 2025 CxNLI / CxNLI-Distinction constructional NLI dataset (435 + 99 human-annotated NLI triples over 8 / 5 phrasal constructions; native-speaker baseline ≈0.90 / ≈0.83). Status: partial (de-anonymized repo inspected 2026-05-29: per-item construction labels + a single gold answer per item; not mirrored). Ratified anchor (2026-05-29) for conjecture/caused-motion-construction, conjecture/conative-construction, conjecture/way-construction, and conjecture/comparative-correlative-construction — an answer-key comparison + aggregate baseline, not a multi-rater gradient. Cannot ground AANN.
  • wic-graded-usage-similarity.md — Usim graded usage-similarity ratings (Erk, McCarthy & Gaylord 2009/2013), with WiC (Pilehvar & Camacho-Collados 2019, binary, CC BY-NC 4.0) as a binary cross-check. Status: external-only (Usim file verified-but-unfetchable + unlicensed; counts/scale verified from primary sources). Surfaced as the candidate gradience anchor for conjecture/lexical-sense-gradience, but the lexical-sense-gradience-anchor decision resolved 2026-05-29 to Option B — not Usim; a replacement graded anchor (DWUG / CoSimLex) is to be identified+verified. Page retained for the record + the WiC cross-check.
  • dwug-usage-graphs.md — DWUG (Diachronic Word Usage Graphs; Schlechtweg et al.): graded human usage-similarity judgments (DURel/Usim tradition). Evaluated 2026-05-30 as the ratified Option-B graded-anchor candidate for conjecture/lexical-sense-gradience; see the page for the license / scale / counts / fetchability / fit verification breakdown.
  • lancaster-sensorimotor-norms.md — Lancaster Sensorimotor Norms (Lynott, Connell, Brysbaert, Brand & Carney 2020; Behavior Research Methods 52(3)): human perceptual/action strength ratings (0–5) on 11 sensorimotor dimensions for 39,707 English words, ~3,500 raters. Status: catalogued (CC BY 4.0 confirmed; aggregate CSV fetched + sha256-pinned this session, full CSV gitignored, small derived 42-lemma join table committed — recipe-not-corpus posture). The text-side perceptual-grounding moderator for prediction 1 of conjecture/multimodal-lexical-grounding-divergence; anchors result/lexical-perceptual-grounding-moderation-v1. Word-level (not sense-level); bears on prediction 1 only, not the image predictions. (created 2026-05-30; multimodal axis)
  • semeval2017-pun-corpus.md — SemEval-2017 Task 7 pun corpus (Miller, Hempelmann & Gurevych 2017): ~1298 homographic + ~1098 heterographic puns with human per-item gold of the two WordNet 3.1 senses each pun evokes, on the actual sentence — human sense co-activation labels for genuine forced-both items. Status: catalogued (mixed CC BY 4.0 / CC BY-NC 4.0; fetched + sha256-hashed + inspected firsthand 2026-06-23). Anchor for the forced-both lexical line (gate Q4) + the matched-ambiguity Option-A homonym sense-anchor — anchor RATIFIED for Q4 co-activation only (session 93) (decisions/resolved/sense-coactivation-anchor-semeval-puns; Q-A adopt / Q-B-1 separate dominance step still owed / Q-C-1 puns as forced-both stimuli, homonym subset, frozen). Honest limit: certifies co-presence, not graded balance/dominance. (created 2026-06-23, session 92)
  • homonym-meaning-dominance-norms.md — Homonym meaning-dominance norms (British eDom, Maciejewski & Klepousniotou 2016, 10.5334/jopd.28; + spoken-ambiguity norms, J. Cognition 10.5334/joc.194): per-word human sense-balance/dominance (D≈0 = balanced) for selecting balanced homonyms. Status: catalogued (both CC BY 4.0; licenses + OSF reachability + schema verified by direct fetch 2026-06-23; data not yet fetched). The human-anchored balance/dominance step the decisions/resolved/sense-coactivation-anchor-semeval-puns Q-B-1 calls for (ratified session 93 — co-activation does NOT discharge Q1-ii, so this separate dominance step is owed); a CC-BY upgrade over the SemCor general-balance proxy. Honest limit: general-usage, not the specific sentence's balance. (created 2026-06-23, session 92)
  • wordnet-sense-inventory.md — Princeton WordNet (Miller 1995 / Fellbaum 1998): the expert-lexicographer synset sense inventory for English (117,659 synsets / 82,115 noun synsets, WordNet 3.0; verified via NLTK). Status: verified (WordNet 3.0 License, permissive/BSD-style, verbatim from the NLTK-shipped LICENSE). The categorical same/different-sense backbone the op-gate Q2 + v2/v3 stratification assume; anchors the constructed pairs in result/multimodal-grounding-image-v1 (gold = same vs. different synset). Honest grain: categorical lexicographic partition, not graded (DWUG) nor per-item human labels (WiC). (created 2026-05-31)
  • wic-word-in-context.md — WiC (Word-in-Context; Pilehvar & Camacho-Collados 2019, NAACL): binary lexicographer same/different-sense labels over two contexts of a target word — 7,466 items (50/50, N+V, 2,345 targets; the pilehvar package ships test gold labels). Negatives are homonymy-enriched (closest-polyseme pairs pruned) — the discreteness contrast DWUG EN lacked. Status: verified (CC BY-NC 4.0; package fetched + inspected live 2026-05-31, sha256-pinned). Anchors the clause-(b) lexical-v3 (design/lexical-polysemy-homonymy-v3) and the image probe (design/multimodal-grounding-image-v1). Scope limit: binary, not graded. (created 2026-05-31)

  • hawkins-tangrams.md — Hawkins/Stanford tangrams repeated-reference corpus (Hawkins, Frank & Goodman 2020, Cognitive Science 44(6)): a record of human dyads playing an iterated tangram reference game, with ordered per-dyad referring-expression histories (role/success/length) — the human entrainment/compression curve. Status: verified (GitHub license: null + the authors' published "open corpus" statement → recipe-not-corpus posture; raw tangrams.csv fetched + sha256-pinned this session, gitignored, derived aggregate entrainment table committed). Catalogued per decisions/resolved/relational-fetchable-anchor (Option A); anchors the human convergence baseline only for open-question/relational-meaning-pilot — the pilot's novel live-vs-shuffled trajectory-dependence measure is not anchored by Hawkins. (created 2026-05-31)

  • subtlex-us-frequency.md — SUBTLEX-US word-frequency norm (Brysbaert & New 2009, Behavior Research Methods 41(4):977–990): 74,286 American-English word forms from a 51M-word film/TV subtitle corpus, with raw + per-million + Lg10WF (log10) frequencies; the frequency measure best predicting human lexical-decision RTs among the standard norms. Status: catalogued (plain-text list fetched + sha256-pinned 2026-06-21; no formal license string on the source page — open-for-research + citation request → recipe-not-corpus posture: full list gitignored, prep.py + a derived 56-row seed table committed). The Q1 frequency-matching floor for conjecture/function-word-substitutability, governed by decisions/resolved/function-word-anchor-design (ratified 2026-06-21). Grounds the matching control only — not the indicator (no PoS in the fetched file, no acceptability/entailment); word-form frequencies aggregate over senses. (created 2026-06-21)

  • blimp.md — BLiMP (Benchmark of Linguistic Minimal Pairs; Warstadt, Parrish, Liu, Mohananey, Peng, Wang, Bowman 2020, TACL 8): 67 paradigms × 1,000 = 67,000 template-generated English acceptability minimal pairs (acceptable vs unacceptable sentence) over 12 phenomena, with 96.4% aggregate human agreement (distinct from the 89% baseline quoted in source/mahowald-2024-dissociating). Status: catalogued (CC-BY, README-prose-only — no standalone LICENSE file; 67 data files fetched + field schema inspected firsthand 2026-06-21; a 10-line sample sha256-pinned, full 67k not committed — recipe-not-corpus). The optional Posture-2 human-acceptability upgrade named in decisions/resolved/function-word-anchor-design (overlaps the swap targets via determiner-noun agreement / quantifiers / NPI licensing / subject-verb agreement) and an adjacent baseline for the AANN line. Grounds acceptability only — cannot anchor entailment/implicature/sense-similarity (the entailment-flip indicator wants a human NLI set); template-generated (contamination caveat). (created 2026-06-21)
  • languageR-dative-corpus.md — Bresnan et al. (2007) dative-alternation corpus (languageR::dative, CRAN languageR 1.6): 3,263 attested ditransitive clauses (Switchboard spoken + WSJ written) hand-coded for 15 variables (semantic class, length, animacy, definiteness, pronominality, discourse accessibility) with the NP-vs-PP realization as outcome — human production choices, not ratings. Status: catalogued (GPL-2 | GPL-3, verified on CRAN 2026-06-20). Anchors conjecture/dative-alternation-information-structure and the dative claims built on it (claim/dative-information-structure-givenness, claim/dative-pronominality-partial-reach). (created 2026-06-20)
  • vwsd-semeval-2023.md — Visual Word Sense Disambiguation, SemEval-2023 Task 1 (Raganato et al. 2023): human-gold 1-of-10 image selection for an ambiguous target word in minimal context — EN gold test 463 items (+16 trial; the 12,869-item training split is silver/auto, not an anchor). Status: catalogued (CC-BY-NC 4.0 stated on the task website only; image-redistribution unconfirmed — images fetched at runtime, sha256-pinned, never committed). Anchors the grounding-headroom probes result/vwsd-grounding-headroom-v1 and result/vwsd-grounding-headroom-v2. Honest limit: gold is binary correct-image selection, not a graded relatedness signal. (created 2026-06-24, session 97)
  • clark-wilkes-gibbs-1986-referring.md — Clark & Wilkes-Gibbs 1986, "Referring as a collaborative process" (Cognition 22(1):1–39): the originating human tangram referential-communication study — the collaborative present/refashion/accept model, the 41→~8 words-per-figure compression curve, the Principle of Mutual Responsibility. Status: verified (primary-direct full text from H. H. Clark's Stanford self-archive, fetched session 134, 2026-06-28; 8 quotes char-for-char with journal pagination). Shortlist to catalogue when a finding first cites them

These are the resources the founding conjectures point to. They become full pages in this directory when first cited.

  • Universal Dependencies (UD) — multi-lingual dependency treebanks; for any claim about argument structure across languages.
  • PropBank / FrameNet / VerbNet — predicate-argument and frame-semantic annotation; for argument-structure constructions and verb-class semantics.
  • CoNLL acceptability / BLiMP / SyntaxGym — minimal-pair acceptability sets; canonical anchors for behavioral semantics/syntax probes.
  • WordNet / OntoNotes / SemCor — sense inventories and sense-tagged corpora; for any claim about lexical sense.
  • CxG construction inventories — e.g., the Berkeley Construction Inventory, ConstructiCon (Russian, English etc.); for any constructional claim.
  • Norming / rating studies — concreteness, age-of-acquisition, semantic feature norms (McRae et al.; Brysbaert) — when a claim turns on a graded human judgment about a lexical item.

When a run first cites one of these, write the resource page (the catalogue entry), check the specific feature actually bears on the claim, and anchors: to it. Do not pre-write empty resource pages.
