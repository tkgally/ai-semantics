Open question: does an LLM productively extend a regular polysemy pattern to a nonce noun?

Why this page exists (a $0 scoping artifact). The project's lexical wedge has tested whether a model tracks the graded relatedness of attested senses (conjecture/lexical-sense-gradience, its results, and open-question/lexical-polysemy-gradience). It has not tested whether a model can generate a sense from a productive pattern — the property that most cleanly separates a stored sense list from a rule. This page opens that orthogonal axis. It states the question, grounds it in the newly-ingested regular-polysemy source, sketches — does not run — a nonce-noun ("wug-test") probe, flags its human-anchor status honestly, and commits no spend.

The question, sharply

Regular (systematic / logical, Apresjan-style) polysemy is the patterned, productive species of polysemy: a metonymic sense relation shared across many words and analysed as rule-generated. The canonical English patterns — animal-for-meat (chicken, rabbit), container-for-contents (drinking "the whole bottle"), artist-for-works (a Picasso), institution/building (school) — are grounded in-repo by source/falkum-vicente-2015-polysemy, which records that

"regular polysemy of this kind is typically analysed as being generated by lexical rules, in this way accounting for the productivity and cross-linguistic availability of the patterns of sense extension, while avoiding a listing of all senses for the words in question" (§2, verbatim).

Productivity is the diagnostic. So:

When a nonce noun — a made-up word the model cannot have stored an extended sense for — is introduced in a frame that fixes its base category (an animal, a container, an artist), does a panel model spontaneously license and interpret the regular metonymic extension (the meat, the contents, the works) that the productive pattern predicts, while withholding an extension the pattern does not license (an idiosyncratic/irregular sense, or an unrelated homonymic sense)? And is productivity uniform across patterns, or graded — stronger for tightly-constraining patterns, weaker where the pattern only "loosely constrains" its extended sense (the source page's gradience note)?

Why it matters for lexical meaning

The distinction is exactly the one the source draws between regular polysemy (rule-generated, productive, cross-lexical) and irregular/accidental polysemy, "where the semantic distinction between the meanings … for a word A cannot be found in any other word of the given language" (the run case, §1). A stored-list model can reproduce attested extensions it was trained on; only a model with a productive pattern can extend the relation to a form it has never seen used that way. This is the lexical analogue of the morphological wug-test — the canonical productivity diagnostic.

The bite is on referential.sense at fine grain (concept/referential-meaning): the extended use is a new mode of presentation for the nonce word, not a re-selection among stored senses. And the standing null is distributional (concept/distributional-meaning): apparent "productivity" could reduce to the model having seen the nonce string (or distributionally adjacent strings) in the target-sense context. The nonce-noun design neutralizes that null by construction — a genuinely novel form cannot carry stored distributional exposure to the extended sense — which is the design advantage over the attested-word gradience probes the wedge has already run. This is what concept/polysemy now flags (via its 2026-06-23 update) as the "related by a productive cross-lexical pattern" sub-kind the gradience results did not touch.

What a serious answer would look like (probe sketched, NOT run)

A minimal-pair, forced-choice / inference design over authored nonce nouns:

  1. Establish the base category. Introduce a nonce noun in a frame that fixes it: "A wug is a small furry animal raised on hill farms." (container / artist / tree-for-wood / institution frames built the same way, one productive pattern each.)
  2. Probe the licensed regular extension. "The chef braised the wug for three hours." → is wug here the animal's meat? The productivity signature is endorsement/interpretation of the extension for a word never seen in that sense.
  3. Withholding controls (the discriminators). (a) an irregular control — an idiosyncratic sense the pattern does not predict, which a productive rule should not license; (b) a homonymy control — an unrelated second sense, expected discrete (per concept/polysemy's homonymy floor). A model that endorses the licensed extension and withholds the unlicensed ones is applying a pattern; one that endorses everything is showing a yes-bias, one that endorses nothing is not extending.
  4. Pattern-gradedness arm. Cross several patterns (animal-for-meat, container-for-contents, artist-for-works, tree-for-wood, institution-for-building) and read whether productivity is uniform or graded — the source page notes the pattern-membership is itself gradient (degrees to which a pattern only "loosely constrains" its extended sense).

The null is first-class and must be declared up front: no productive extension (the model treats the nonce word's frame-fixed sense as its only sense, declining the metonymic extension) is a clean negative for lexical sense productivity, written as cleanly as a positive.

Human-anchor status (honest): scout-gated, else internal-contrast-only

There is no in-repo human resource for systematic-polysemy productivity. The source source/falkum-vicente-2015-polysemy names the relevant human study — "Srinivasan and Rabagliati investigated a large number of systematic polysemy patterns found in English" (§3) — but that paper is not in-repo (verified: no source page exists for it), and Falkum & Vicente is a survey editorial that "supplies no human-annotated dataset" and "cannot serve as the anchors: resource for any claim or result" (its own What it cannot ground). So a real probe has two honest paths, and this page invents no anchor for either:

  • A within-model internal-contrast-only contrast — licensed-extension vs unlicensed-control endorsement, a within-model behavioural asymmetry making no human-comparison claim (the terminal status most of the project's behavioural results carry). This path needs no fetch.
  • A human-comparison arm, gated on scouting a fetchable, licensed human systematic-polysemy productivity/acceptability set (Srinivasan & Rabagliati 2015 or an equivalent) and cataloguing it as a resource — at which point the model's per-pattern productivity could be set against a human productivity gradient. Until such a scout lands, the human-comparison arm is not runnable, and the human-comparison tag on this page marks the arm the question engages, not an anchor in hand.

Tractability and cost

High. Nonce items are cheap to author, and the nonce design makes the distributional control free (a novel form cannot have stored extended-sense exposure). A within-model panel probe at powered N (~100–150 items, PROTOCOL.md §4) is low-cost at observed prices. The human-comparison arm is the only expensive prerequisite, and it is a scout, not a run. Small-N and n=3-models caveats apply as everywhere; a first pass reads direction-of-effect, not coverage.

How this differs from the existing lexical wedge (so it is genuinely fresh)

  • Distinct from open-question/lexical-polysemy-gradience and conjecture/lexical-sense-gradience. Those ask whether the model tracks graded relatedness among attested senses (a recognition axis, cross-item monotonicity in a human DURel median). This asks whether the model can generate an unattested sense from a productive pattern (a productivity axis, on nonce forms). Recognition of stored gradience and productivity of a rule are logically independent — a model could rank attested senses perfectly and still fail to extend a pattern to a novel word.
  • Distinct from the bridging / within-item work. Bridging asks about indeterminacy on a single ambiguous attested use; this asks about generation on a novel form.
  • It is model-internal plus (on the scouted arm) human-comparison; it does not touch the relational axis.

Status: open (scoping only)

This page runs nothing, freezes no operationalization, opens no wiki/decisions/open/ entry, and spends nothing. It opens the lexical productivity axis — regular-polysemy extension to nonce nouns — states the question, grounds it in source/falkum-vicente-2015-polysemy and concept/polysemy, sketches one illustrative probe with a distributional control the nonce design supplies for free, and flags the human-comparison arm as scout-gated (else internal-contrast-only).
