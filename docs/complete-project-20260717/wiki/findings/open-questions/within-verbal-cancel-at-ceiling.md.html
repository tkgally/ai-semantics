Open question: is a NEW within-verbal cancel default at ceiling findable — or is the matched verbal cancel arm a principled limit?

RESOLVED (session 141, 2026-06-28) — the within-verbal confirm is OBTAINED; the principled-limit hypothesis is REJECTED. The matched within-verbal battery this page's two prior updates built toward has run (result/monotonicity-within-verbal-battery-v1, $0.06882, internal-contrast-only): the frozen resultative ADD arm + the s140 progressive CANCEL arm, both verbal, read by the frozen s134 thresholds → B2 GO; asymmetry +0.50/+0.33/+0.67, ≥ 0.20 in 3/3 models; fragility 3/3; cue robust; leave-one-out robust at the verdict level. So the answer to this page's question is settled: a NEW within-verbal cancel construction at ceiling is findable AND the matched within-verbal asymmetry confirms — the matched verbal cancel arm is not a principled limit. The M2 domain mismatch the weak C2 confirm carried is discharged. Persisting caveats (carried into the conjecture, not this page): suppression is partial/verb-uneven (shatter resists; gpt at the margin); no human comparison. This open question is therefore answered; the live threads it leaves (human comparison; defeasance competence vs strict-NLI labeling) belong to the conjecture, not here. Sections below are the original (pre-battery) scoping; read them through this resolution.

UPDATE (session 140, 2026-06-28) — the cancel CONSTRUCTION is measured and it WORKS; the matched within-verbal battery is now buildable. The progressive cancel-suppression calibration the s139 update called for has run (result/monotonicity-causative-progressive-cancel-v1, $0.02586, internal-contrast-only). In the matched conflicting-cue paradigm, the progressive suppresses the B2-confirmed result entailment in all three models (NLI affirm drops from 100% to 33/33/67%, suppression 66.7/66.7/33.3 pp; FC to 0/0/50%) and an explicit result-consequence cue re-licenses it (positive shift in all 6 model×instrument cells) — the conative-shaped cancel pattern, and on NLI cleaner than the original conative (gpt suppresses here where it failed there). So the first NEW within-verbal cancel arm beyond the conative is in hand, and the matched within-verbal add-vs-cancel battery (frozen ADD arm 80bd4b60b55a3e60 + this progressive cancel arm, read by the frozen s134 thresholds) — which would deliver the clean within-verbal generalization confirm that discharges M2 — is buildable next (not yet run; this calibration pairs no ADD arm, so the conjecture is untested by it). Honest caveat: per-verb suppression is uneven (dissolved suppresses cleanly everywhere, shattered resists in all NLI files — punctual-verb progressives sometimes read as culminated), so the battery must keep the per-verb breakdown visible. This question stays open until that battery runs.

UPDATE (session 139, 2026-06-28) — path (ii) executed; the "scarcity" horn's un-findability reading is REFUTED, the question re-narrows. The cheap B2 calibration survey this page called for has run (result/monotonicity-verbal-cancel-survey-v1, $0.02482, internal-contrast-only). All three NEW candidate verbal families clear B2 at ceiling — NLI affirm 1.00 in 3/3 models each: implicative ("managed to V" ⊨ "V-ed"), factive ("realized that P" ⊨ P), and causative-inchoative ("broke the vase" ⊨ "the vase broke"). So held-at-ceiling verbal lexical defaults are findable — the s135/C1 floors were specific to aspectual/quantity (Gricean/defeasible) defaults, not verbal defaults in general, and the un-findability reading of the "principled limit for want of a held verbal default" horn below is falsified (the stronger distributional form — held defaults being rare among constructionally-cancellable ones — is not settled; these three families were chosen as a-priori near-categorical inference types). The limit that remains is on the cancel-CONSTRUCTION side: of the three B2-passers, implicative and factive are cancelled only by a lexical-class swap / scopal flip (not a constructional add-vs-cancel of the conative type), while causative-inchoative supplies a genuine same-verb constructional cancel — the progressive/imperfective paradox ("was breaking the vase" ⊭ "the vase broke"). The question therefore narrows to: does the causative-inchoative progressive actually suppress the held result entailment in the matched paradigm (un-measured; the progressive is aspectual and so exposed to the C1-style weakness — measure, don't assume)? On a clean suppression signal, the matched within-verbal battery (frozen ADD arm + causative-inchoative progressive cancel) would deliver the clean within-verbal confirm. The survey ran nothing beyond the calibration; the within-verbal confirm remains un-obtained. Sections below are the original (pre-survey) scoping; read them through this update.

Why this page exists. The conjecture/constructional-monotonicity-asymmetry is now status: tested on a weak confirm (result/monotonicity-c2-battery-v1), but that confirm was bought at the cost of a verbal-add / nominal-cancel domain mismatch (the ratified M2 scope-broadening): its only B2-passing cancel default was taxonomic ("a gun" ⊨ "a weapon"), a noun-category entailment, paired with a verbal resultative add arm. The two verbal cancel directions the project built for the generalization battery — the s135 for-durative single-occurrence default and the C1 telic-completion default — both failed their B2 ceiling gates. So the cleanest form of the bet — a within-verbal add-vs-cancel contrast at matched ceiling — has not been obtained, and a strong (un-caveated) confirm would need a verbal cancel default the panel holds as a categorical entailment at ceiling. This is a scoping page: it states that question precisely, lays out what the three B2 calibrations have already established, surveys candidate verbal cancel directions and their B2 prospects (without re-grinding the ruled-out ones), and assesses whether this is a near- principled limit for the verbal case — parallel to the grounding-magnitude un-instrumentability (open-question/grounding-magnitude-instrument). It runs nothing, ratifies nothing, and spends nothing.

The question, sharply

The monotonicity asymmetry's strongest within-verbal cancel leg is the conative: the transitive's completed-contact entailment is at near-ceiling (91.7–100%) and the conative frame suppresses it only partially and instrument-fragilely (result/conative-cancel-direction-v2). That is a genuine within-verbal cancel arm with a held entailment to push against — so a within-verbal cancel-at-ceiling default is not un-found in the absolute. But the conative is one of the three original constructions the asymmetry abstracts over, not a generalization of it. The conjecture's own confirm criterion demands the asymmetry "generalizes to new add-vs-cancel construction pairs beyond caused-motion / way / conative … tested at matched difficulty and ceiling" (conjecture/constructional-monotonicity-asymmetry, §"What would confirm / falsify"). The generalization battery therefore needed a new pair — and every new verbal cancel default tried for it has floored the B2 gate. So:

Is there a NEW (generalization-eligible, not the original conative) within-verbal cancel default that the strict-NLI panel holds as a categorical entailment at ceiling — one a construction could then be shown to suppress less reliably than a matched verbal add arm accumulates? Or is the matched within- verbal cancel arm a principled limit on this instrument: the verbal defaults the project can reach for are disproportionately implicatures/defeasible defaults, so a clean within-verbal generalization confirm may be un-instrumentable without a categorically-held verbal lexical entailment to cancel?

This is a live, tractable scoping question, and it is itself a near-principled-limit candidate finding (in the spirit of essay/nothing-to-cancel's case-(i) precondition). It is not a commitment to a re-run, and — per the do-not-re-grind discipline below — not a re-attempt on the two verbal directions already ruled out.

What the three B2 calibrations have already established

Across the cancel-arm redesign the project ran three frozen B2 default-at-ceiling calibrations, which now rank which cancel-side defaults the panel holds as categorical entailments. The ladder (quoted from the C2-privative result, which tabulates all three):

cancel default kind of inference affirm (NLI, 3 models) held at ceiling? domain
single-occurrence ("flashed" → "only once"), s135 Gricean quantity implicature 0.00 / 0.00 / 0.00 no verbal
completion ("built the house" → "finished building it"), C1 aspectual default (defeasible) 0.25 / 0.375 / 0.75 no verbal
category membership ("a gun" → "a weapon"), C2 taxonomic lexical entailment 1.00 / 1.00 / 1.00 yes nominal

(Numbers verified verbatim against note/monotonicity-c2-privative-calibration-v1, §"The contrast this sharpens"; the domain column is this page's own annotation, not part of the source table.) Two facts fall out:

  1. The only cancel default that cleared B2 at ceiling is nominal (taxonomic noun→category), not verbal. So the matched battery that confirmed the asymmetry crosses a domain difference — "some of the gap could be verbal-vs-nominal, not add-vs-cancel" (result/monotonicity-c2-battery-v1, §"What this does and does not license").
  2. Both verbal cancel defaults tried for the generalization floored, and one structurally: s135 calls the single-occurrence floor structural — "no choice of semelfactive verb can lift this default to the ≥ 0.80 ceiling" (note/monotonicity-generalization-b2-nogo-v1, §"Why this is structural, not a bad verb pool") — because single-occurrence is an implicature, not an entailment; and C1 completion read as "defeasible-not-categorical … 0/3 models reach the ≥ 0.80 ceiling" (note/monotonicity-c1-completion-calibration-v1, §"One-line finding"), even though, by standard event semantics, a quantized simple-past accomplishment does entail culmination. So a categorically-held verbal cancel default is, so far, scarce on this panel: the conative's completed-contact (91.7–100%) is the one clear instance, and it is the original construction, not a generalization.

What a clean within-verbal generalization cancel arm would require

The same case-(i) precondition the B2 gate enforces, now specialized to "verbal and new":

  • (a) A verbal lexical default that clears B2 at ceiling (≥ 0.80 affirm in ≥ 2/3 models, strict NLI) on the bare frame — a held entailment, not a Gricean implicature (s135 trap) nor a defeasible aspectual default (C1 trap). This is the binding scarcity: the panel reads many natural verbal defaults as defeasible.
  • (b) A construction that genuinely cancels that verbal default — i.e. a coercion the panel will read as suppressing the held entailment, placed in the matched conflicting-cue paradigm of result/conative-cancel-direction-v2, so add and cancel are compared at the same task structure.
  • (c) Genuinely new — beyond caused-motion / way / conative — so a positive result is a generalization of the asymmetry, not a re-reading of the three constructions it already abstracts over.
  • (d) No domain smuggling — to deliver a within-verbal confirm it must pair the verbal cancel arm with a verbal add arm (the frozen resultative qualifies), avoiding the M2 verbal/nominal mismatch the C2 battery had to accept.

Requirements (a) and (c) are in tension exactly the way (a) and (b) were on the grounding-magnitude page: the verbal defaults that are categorically held (completed contact under the conative) are largely the ones already used; the new verbal coercions the project reaches for (aspect, quantity) target defaults the panel treats as defeasible. An arm that satisfies both at once is what is being scouted.

Candidate verbal cancel directions and their B2 prospects (survey, $0)

A scoping survey only — no model is run, and the two ruled-out directions are not re-tested. Each candidate is judged on whether its verbal default is plausibly a categorical entailment the panel would hold at ceiling (requirement (a)), reading off the pattern the three calibrations already revealed.

  • Aspect / telicity (e.g. for-durative single-occurrence; telic completion). Ruled out — both forms tried floored B2 (s135 structural; C1 0/3 at ceiling). Aspectual and quantity defaults are read as defeasible under strict NLI. Do not re-grind. Prospects for other aspectual coercions: poor, by the same diagnosis (the panel declines to force bare-past → culmination, and quantity implicatures floor).
  • Selectional / contact defaults (the conative family). The conative works (contact 91.7–100% held; off-ceiling suppression). But it is one of the original three, so a new member would have to be a different verb class whose object-affectedness entailment is at ceiling and is cancellable by a frame (e.g. other Levin "conative"-eligible or affected-object alternations). Most promising direction for a B2-passing verbal default, precisely because completed-contact is the one verbal default already shown categorical — but "new vs. a re-read of the conative" (requirement (c)) is the live risk: the closer it is to the conative, the less it generalizes.
  • Factive / implicative verb entailments (e.g. "managed to V" ⊨ "V-ed"; "forgot to V" ⊭ "V-ed"). Implicative-verb entailments are classically near-categorical and might clear B2; the cancel side would be a non-implicative or negative-implicative frame. Untested, plausibly B2-eligible, but whether the contrast is a constructional add-vs-cancel of the conative type (rather than a lexical-class swap) needs design scrutiny — it may not be a clean construction-cancels-a-verb-default shape at all.
  • Causative/inchoative and other Levin alternations with an entailment that flips. Possible, but each must pass the same (a) gate before any asymmetry is read — measure, don't assume (the standing s135 lesson). Untriaged here.

The survey's honest upshot: the conative-adjacent affected-object family is the most likely place a NEW verbal cancel default would clear B2, because completed contact is the lone categorically-held verbal default on the ladder — but that very adjacency puts requirement (c) (genuine generalization) under pressure. Implicative-verb entailments are the most likely non-conative B2-eligible verbal default, but their constructional add-vs-cancel framing is unproven. Aspect/quantity is closed.

Do-not-re-grind discipline (binding)

  • The s135 for-durative / semelfactive single-occurrence cancel arm is ruled out (structural B2 NO-GO; within-frame re-pair futile). Do not rebuild or re-run it.
  • The C1 telic-completion cancel arm is ruled out (B2 NO-GO, 0/3 at ceiling). Do not re-pair completion verbs within the frame.
  • The frozen s134 thresholds and the frozen B2-passing resultative ADD arm (80bd4b60b55a3e60) are not to be re-tuned or rebuilt — any future verbal cancel arm reuses the add arm verbatim, exactly as the C2 battery did.
  • A re-attempt owes a genuinely new verbal cancel direction with its own B2 calibration first — not a re-grade or re-prompt of a ruled-out arm.

Honest bottom line

  • A within-verbal cancel default at ceiling is not absolutely un-found — the conative's completed-contact (91.7–100%) is one — but a NEW (generalization-eligible) within-verbal cancel default at ceiling has not been found, and two attempts floored B2. The weak C2 confirm therefore stands on a nominal cancel arm across a domain mismatch; the cleanest within-verbal generalization confirm the conjecture wants remains out of reach.
  • This may be a near-principled limit for the verbal case, not merely a gap to fill. The recurring pattern across s135 and C1 — the project's natural new verbal cancel targets (aspect, quantity) are defaults the strict-NLI panel reads as defeasible-not-categorical — is itself the candidate finding: on this instrument, categorically-held verbal lexical entailments that a construction can cancel are scarce, so a clean within-verbal cancel arm may be hard to instrument in principle (parallel to the grounding-magnitude un-instrumentability, open-question/grounding-magnitude-instrument). This is a fact about the instrument (strict NLI) and the panel, not yet about defeasance competence (per essay/nothing-to-cancel).
  • What would resolve it, either way: (i) a B2-passing NEW verbal cancel default — most plausibly in the conative-adjacent affected-object family or among implicative-verb entailments — run through the matched battery with the frozen verbal add arm, would deliver the clean within-verbal confirm and discharge the M2 caveat; or (ii) a small structured survey that triages a handful of candidate verbal defaults through cheap B2 calibrations and, if they too floor, writes the principled limit explicitly (the matched within-verbal cancel arm is un-instrumentable at ceiling on a strict-NLI panel for want of a categorically-held cancellable verbal default). Either is a legitimate future unit; neither is forced.

Status: open (scoping only)

This page runs nothing and ratifies nothing. It states the within-verbal generalization question, shows that the only B2-passing cancel default so far is nominal while both new verbal directions floored, surveys where a B2-passing verbal default might still be found (conative-adjacent affected-object; implicative verbs) and where it will not (aspect/quantity), and flags the matched within-verbal cancel arm as a near-principled-limit candidate. The clean within-verbal generalization confirm of conjecture/constructional-monotonicity-asymmetry remains un-obtained; the conjecture's tested status rests on a weak, domain-mismatched confirm.
