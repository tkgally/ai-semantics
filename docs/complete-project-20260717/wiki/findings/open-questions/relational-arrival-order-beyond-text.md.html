Open question: does arrival-order surplus exist beyond text?

Why this page exists (a non-spend planning artifact). The relational order-composition ladder has gone quiet. Its thin side is occupied and robust; its rich side is, for text stimuli, documented structurally closed — a promotable closure, not a failure. The one live opening the closure leaves is whether that closure is text-specific or medium-general. This page revives the dormant axis by scoping that opening: it restates why the text closure holds, names candidate non-text media (conservatively), sketches one high-value candidate probe and the human anchor it would need, and states what would answer the question. It opens no spend-bearing commitment and surfaces no operationalization decision — a real probe design would do both, and is explicitly deferred to a later session.

Where the ladder stands (verified against the pages)

The relational order-composition ladder, as the in-repo pages actually record it:

  • Rung (i) — order-sensitive overwrite / latest-binding-wins: occupied, robust. When a coined term is reassigned across stamped rounds so that "the content-set of the history is symmetric … and only the recency ordering disambiguates," both panel models "recover the term by its most-recent binding, spontaneously" at ceiling (claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment, §"The claim (scoped exactly)"). It survives dropping the explicit "was reassigned" wording: with that INTRO sentence removed and everything else byte-identical, "both models still recover the most-recent binding at ceiling (SPONT latest-binding 1.000, DIRECT 1.000, 0 NA)" (ibid., banner).
  • Rung (ii) — order-sensitive integration (a compatible earlier turn survives): occupied, panel-concordant, still thin. "When the latest turn is compatible with an earlier one (refinement, not replacement), both models integrate — the earlier, non-terminal turn survives into the referent at ceiling, rather than being overwritten" (ibid., scope limit 2). The update rule is therefore "supersede-on-conflict, compose-on-compatibility — still thin (single-reader-recoverable)" (ibid.).
  • Rung (iii) — path-dependence proper / arrival-order surplus: rich side structurally closed for text. This is the load-bearing closure this page builds on. Under decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence the "rich-side rung (iii) is documented structurally closed for text-only stimuli — a transcript is a final content+stamps record, so no text stimulus carries arrival-order surplus outside what the record states. So 'deep path-dependence' is not reached by any text design — this limit is now bounded, not open-ended" (claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment, scope limit 3). The closure is "a documented closure, not a positive" (ibid.).
  • Rung (iv) — genuine between-agent constitution / deontic scorekeeping: unbuilt. Its instrument is specified-only, "a scored live practice, not a transcript" whose observables (commitment, entitlement, challenge) "no current relational instrument records at all" (essay/rung-iv-instrument, Honesty box, quoting essay/update-is-not-constitution). Rung (iv) is out of this page's scope — it requires a normative surplus that is strictly stronger than the arrival-order surplus this page asks about (see "What this question is not" below).

So the ladder is climbed on the thin side (i–ii) and structurally closed on the rich side for text (iii). The question below is the one the text closure leaves standing.

The question, sharply

The rung-(iii) closure is stated against text: a transcript is a final content+timestamp record, so no text probe can carry arrival-order surplus. Does a rung-(iii) arrival-order surplus — a path-dependent interpretation that is not recoverable from a final content+timestamp record — exist in any non-text medium, and if so, which? Equivalently: is the structural closure a property of the medium (text records final state) or a property of any recoverable record (so the closure is medium-general and no medium escapes it)?

This sharpens open-question/relational-meaning-pilot's trajectory-dependence discriminator — "the difference in B's interpretation/usage of the coined term between live dialogue … and shuffled-history replay … with content held constant" — by asking whether that discriminator can be made non-vacuous in a medium where the closure argument might fail. In text it is vacuous by construction (the shuffled replay and the live record are the same final string up to the stamps the record already states). The live question is whether any other carrier breaks that identity.

Why the text closure holds (restated, with quotes)

The closure is not a measurement gap; it is a definitional coincidence between the instrument and the criterion. The thin/rich cut is defined against the record:

A convention is merely updated (thin) if its recovered interpretation is a function of the record's content + stamps, computable by any single reader of that record. It is constituted (rich) only if recovery requires a surplus that no single reader of the record could reconstruct — something carried by the live, ordered, two-party trajectory and not encoded in the final content+stamps. (essay/transcript-ceiling, §"The position", quoting essay/update-is-not-constitution)

And a text stimulus is such a record. The ratified decision states the trap in one line, quoted in the ceiling essay:

But a text-only stimulus is a final content+stamps record. (decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence, §"The crux")

From which the ceiling essay draws the conclusion this page takes as its premise:

Anything a model recovers from a transcript is, by (2), recoverable from the record; by the criterion, that is thin. The rich side is not a harder rung the model failed to climb; it is outside the probe's range. (essay/transcript-ceiling, §"The argument")

The ceiling essay is careful that this bounds the medium, not the models: "a text record does not contain the rich-side surplus, so probing them with text records cannot reveal whether they could handle it if it were present. A model fed a genuine interaction log with normative observables … a different medium — is untested and out of this thesis's scope" (ibid., §"What the ceiling is a ceiling on (and what it is not)"). That untested out-of-scope region is exactly what this page opens — restricted to the arrival-order (rung iii) part of it, not the normative (rung iv) part.

The companion essay sharpens why this matters for the relational picture: to a model in the project's probes "a conversation is a layout … not a timeline and not an order-free content set" (essay/conversation-as-text-not-timeline, §"The position", scoped to the v4 position-available regime), and even where the models do track stamped recency they do so from cues the record carries. The honest default this page inherits from that essay is deflationary: the models read whatever the record states, and a record is what they are handed.

The honest default: the closure may be medium-general, not text-specific

Before naming candidate media, state the skeptical prior plainly, because it is the most likely answer. The closure argument has two steps — (1) the rich side is a surplus over a final-state record, and (2) a transcript is a final-state record — and step (1) is medium-independent. The criterion does not mention text; it mentions "the record's content + stamps" and "any single reader of that record." So the closure transfers to any medium whose stimulus is a recoverable final state: a video file, a state-log, an image array — each is, by the time a model receives it, a fixed object a single reader can read in full. The transcript ceiling essay says exactly this risk applies cross-axis, holding the grounding case as a contrast not a confirmation but conceding the shared criterion: "both turn on whether the answer is recoverable from the text channel alone" (essay/transcript-ceiling, §"A cross-axis note"). The default expectation, therefore, is that any medium that records a recoverable final state inherits the closure. A candidate medium earns attention only if it can present the same final-state content via two genuinely different arrival histories such that the interpretation differs and the difference is not reconstructible from the final state — and that is a high bar that most media fail for the same reason text fails.

Candidate non-text media (conservative, skeptical)

Four candidates, each with the precise reason it might — or, more likely, might not — break the closure.

  1. A live, non-replayable interaction protocol (state the record under-determines). Consider an instrument where two agents act on a shared mutable state and the final logged record is deliberately lossy about arrival order — e.g. the record stores the end state and a set of actions but not their sequence, and the sequence genuinely changed the end state (non-commuting actions whose order is not logged). If interpretation provably depends on an order the record does not contain, the closure fails — there is a surplus "no single reader of the record could reconstruct." Skeptical caveat: this is fragile. If the order is needed to reach the end state, an honest instrument arguably must log it to be reproducible at all; the moment it is logged, the surplus re-enters the record and the closure returns. This is the text case relabelled — the project's own Option-C text design already showed that "smuggling order into the content of the record (an ordered operation list) keeps the answer single-reader-recoverable, hence thin" (essay/transcript-ceiling, §"What this is not — the escape hatch …"). The only way this medium escapes is if the order is causally efficacious yet unlogged, which is hard to engineer without becoming unreproducible.

  2. A visual / image stream where order-of-appearance is independent of any displayed stamp. A sequence of images (or a single composited image) where the order in which elements appeared affected the model's reading, but the final frame carries no timestamp or layout cue encoding that order. Why it might break the closure: unlike a transcript, a final image need not depict its own construction history, so two construction orders could yield the same final image yet (if order were carried by something the model registers) different interpretations. Why it probably does not: if the two orders yield the same final image and the model only ever sees that final image, there is by construction nothing in the stimulus to carry the order — the model is blind to it, which is a null, not a rich-side positive. If instead the orders yield different final images, the difference is in the final state and the closure holds (it is thin, recoverable from the image). The escape requires a frame-sequence the model consumes as a stream with order-effects not recoverable from the final frame — closer to candidate (4) than to a static image.

  3. An interaction / state log the final record under-determines (a generalization of (1)). Many real logs are append-only and do preserve order, in which case they inherit the closure exactly as a transcript does (an ordered log is a final content+stamps record). The only sub-case that bears is a log with hidden state — where some interpretation-relevant variable was set by the arrival order and then not written to the log. Skeptical caveat: the same reproducibility tension as (1); and a hidden-state design risks measuring nothing more than the model's inability to recover information it was not given, which is uninformative about whether a surplus exists.

  4. Multimodal turn-taking with genuine timing (real-time audio/gesture overlap). Spoken or embodied turn-taking carries information — overlap, latency, simultaneous uptake — that a transcript of the final exchange flattens. Why it is the strongest candidate in principle: the surplus (who responded before the other finished; an uptake that occurred in the overlap) is, like the normative observables of rung (iv), plausibly "instituted by uptake … a finished string cannot carry that forward" (essay/rung-iv-instrument, §"The position"). Why it is mostly out of reach and must be handled with care: (a) any rendering of it that a current model can consume is itself a recoverable file (an audio waveform, a timed event list) — i.e. a record — so the closure likely re-enters at the point of presentation; and (b) timing-as-uptake shades into rung (iv) normative status, which is a strictly stronger requirement this page does not address (see below). The honest reading is that the part of this candidate that escapes the closure is the part that is already rung (iv), not a clean rung-(iii) arrival-order surplus.

Net assessment. Of the four, none clearly breaks the closure; (1) and (3) collapse back to text the moment the order is logged (and become unreproducible if it is not); (2) yields a null or a thin result; (4)'s escaping part is rung (iv), not rung (iii). This supports the honest default — the closure is plausibly medium-general for any recoverable-final-state stimulus, and the genuinely open sub-case is narrow: a medium whose stimulus is consumed as a live stream with an order-effect that is causally real, model-registrable, yet absent from any final-state record the same model could read.

One candidate probe (described, not designed; no spend)

The single most informative, concretely-describable candidate — offered as a planning sketch, not a design, and committing no spend:

  • A streamed-order probe with a final-state-identity guarantee. Present a model with a stream of events (the cleanest near-term realization is a sequence of stamped or unstamped image/state frames delivered one at a time) constructed so that two arrival orders reach a byte-identical final delivered state, then probe interpretation. The discriminator is the live-vs-shuffled gap of open-question/relational-meaning-pilot — "live ≠ shuffled with content held constant" — but with the crucial twist that content held constant now means final state held byte-identical, so any order-effect cannot be read off the final state. A reliable gap would be the first arrival-order surplus the project has that a single reader of the final record could not reconstruct; a null (the dominant prediction) would extend the closure from text to this medium and is a first-class negative, exactly as the rung-(iii) decision frames a structural closure as "a fully promotable outcome, as is a null" (decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence, via essay/transcript-ceiling, §"Why naming the ceiling is itself knowledge").
  • The pre-run adjudication the design would inherit. Before any run, the project's existing gate — the same one that judged the Option-C text gap THIN — must rule whether the candidate gap is genuinely not single-reader-recoverable from the final delivered state, "biased against the rich reading" (essay/transcript-ceiling, §"What this is not …"). If the gate finds the order is in fact recoverable (e.g. the streaming itself logs sequence), the design is text relabelled and should not be run.

Human anchor (flagged pending — no in-repo resource fits)

A within-model live-vs-shuffled gap would, on its own, be internal-contrast-only — a within-model behavioural contrast, no human-comparison claim — exactly the terminal status every cited relational result carries (claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment, anchor: internal-contrast-only). To make a human-comparison claim ("humans carry an arrival-order surplus a final-state record omits; models do/do not"), a human resource recording non-text arrival-order-dependent interpretation would be required, and none is in-repo: the ratified relational anchor (Clark & Wilkes-Gibbs 1986) is unfetched and grounds collaborative-reference convergence, not arrival-order surplus; the in-repo human evidence runs order-insensitive (Brennan & Clark, "Frequency of use better explains our data than does simple recency," per essay/aggregation-not-constitution); and Hawkins grounds the compression curve only. So the anchor is pending: a real probe would either (a) run internal-contrast-only with no human leg, or (b) open a future wiki/decisions/open/ anchor question naming a human non-text arrival-order resource — which does not currently exist in-repo and is not invented here. This page creates no such decision; it only flags the gap.

What this question is not

  • Not rung (iv). Arrival-order surplus (rung iii) is "a fact about the trajectory of content"; normative status (rung iv) "is a fact about answerability between parties. A trajectory can be path-dependent without anyone being committed, entitled, or challenged to anything" (essay/rung-iv-instrument, §"A discipline the ceiling does not by itself supply"). A non-text medium that carried arrival-order surplus would bound the transcript ceiling to text-only probes — it would not reach constitution, which "requires observables (commitments, entitlements, challenges) that no current relational instrument records at all" (ibid., quoting essay/update-is-not-constitution). This page asks only the strictly weaker rung-(iii) question.
  • Not a claim that any non-text medium escapes the closure. The honest default above is that the closure is plausibly medium-general; this page asks the question and is skeptical of a positive answer.
  • Not a spend-bearing commitment and not an operationalization decision. The probe is a sketch; its measure, frame-construction, and adjudication threshold are deliberately left unfrozen. A real design session would have to freeze them (charter §8) and pass an independent pre-run critic before any spend.

What would answer this (closing criteria)

The question moves toward closure when any one of the following lands, in a later session:

  1. A medium-general closure argument, ratified. A principled argument (adversarially reviewed) that the thin/rich criterion's "surplus over a final-state record" is medium-independent — so every recoverable-final-state stimulus inherits the closure — would answer the question in the negative and promote the closure from text-specific to medium-general. This is the most likely resolution and would itself be a promotable result (a documented structural closure of the whole rung-(iii) program), not a failure.
  2. A built non-text probe with a final-state-identity guarantee + an adjudicated gap. A streamed (or otherwise non-recoverable-order) instrument whose live-vs-shuffled gap the pre-run adjudication gate (biased against the rich reading) judges genuinely not single-reader-recoverable from the final delivered state would answer the question in the affirmative for that medium and bound the transcript ceiling to text-only probes — firing essay/transcript-ceiling revision trigger (b) on the arrival-order (not normative) side. A null would extend the closure to that medium (a first-class negative).
  3. A demonstration that every candidate medium collapses to the text case. A worked argument that each of the four candidate media above either re-logs the order (closure returns) or yields a null (model blind to unlogged order) would answer the question in the negative without a new probe.

Until one of these lands, the page stays open, carrying the dormant relational order-composition axis as a live but non-spend planning item.

What is at stake for the meanings of "meaning"

Path-dependence / non-commutativity is the project's marker of something beyond a distributional content-bag. The deflationary baseline is precisely that "two next-token predictors converging from overlapping co-occurrence content … survives order-scrambling" (essay/conversation-as-text-not-timeline, §"What a conversation is, to a model", quoting the deflationary map), so an order-effect that survives final-state-matching — not merely content-matching — would be the cleanest evidence the project has that interpretation is not a function of the aggregated content alone. That is the line the relational essays draw between aggregation and constitution: a convention "whose interpretation any fresh agent can recover from the content-bag of the record is exactly what a shared distributional substrate predicts," recoverable "without anything existing 'in the interaction and not antecedently in either party'" (essay/transcript-ceiling, §"The argument", quoting essay/aggregation-not-constitution and concept/relational-meaning). An arrival-order surplus a final-state record omits would be a relational (and, where it licenses inference from the trajectory, inferential) surplus over the distributional default — still short of constitution (rung iv), but a genuine step past the content-bag. Conversely, the more likely medium-general closure would tell the project, with a documented bound, that the order-composition axis tops out at the thin side for any recoverable-record medium — a modest, calibrated, promotable answer in its own right.
