Essay: updating a convention is not constituting one

Status: revised (2026-06-17). A philosophical-track essay arguing in the project's own voice. Its original contribution is conceptual, not empirical: it builds the structure the relational concept page and the two prior relational essays said was missing — a ladder of distinct rungs between a thin update-rule and a convention genuinely constituted between agents, each with a candidate behavioral signature, and a sharp thin/rich criterion for telling them apart. The project now has firm, replicated evidence for two rungs — rung (i), the bottom (overwrite on conflict; claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment, supported), and, as of 2026-06-17, rung (ii) (the earlier turn survives when compatible — composition, not blind overwrite; result/relational-integration-rung-ii). Both are on the thin side of the criterion. Rung (iv) remains a proposed discriminating design, not a reported result; rung (iii) was operationalized (Option C) and its rich side is now documented structurally CLOSED for text-only stimuli (2026-06-18, result/relational-order-composition-c) — bounded, not crossed (see the revision log and the rung-(iii) discussion). The thesis is unchanged and better-instanced: even with two rungs climbed (and a stricter thin rung now occupied by the whole panel given a working surface, generalizing across two non-commuting operation pairs — see the 2026-06-19 revision-log entry), both sit below the thin/rich cut, the rich side of rung (iii) is closed for text rather than approached, so the distance to constitution stays measurable — and large. There is no human comparison anywhere in this essay (the cited results are internal-contrast-only; the human leg of any order/path contrast is unanchored in-repo). Read the revision triggers before citing.

The position

The relational track now has a defined floor. When a coined referential term is reassigned across stamped rounds so that the content-set is symmetric and only recency disambiguates, both panel models recover the term by its most-recent binding, spontaneously and at ceiling (claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment; result/relational-spontaneous-recency-a), and that behavior survives removal of the explicit reassignment flag (result/relational-implicit-reassignment-control). The concept/relational-meaning page records the consequence in a single phrase: the second ladder's "bottom rung is partly climbed," but only the bottom rung, because the order-sensitivity is "thin (a latest-binding-wins / convention-update rule)," not "a convention constituted between agents."

This essay argues the thesis compressed in its title: updating a convention is not constituting one. An order-sensitive result is not, on its own, evidence of between-agent constitution — and the gap between the two is not a vague "more research needed," but a structured gap with nameable rungs and a decidable criterion. The prior relational essays asserted the bound ("thin, not rich / bottom rung"); none of them said what the rungs above the bottom one are, or what observable surplus each would require. The concept/relational-meaning page describes the relational axis as "a second ladder whose bottom rung is not yet defined" — naming the ladder but leaving its higher rungs unspecified. This essay builds that structure. It is a map of unbuilt terrain, offered precisely so the project does not mistake the firm floor for nearness to the top.

The deflationary core of the positive: why order-sensitivity is not constitution

Start with what the bottom rung actually is, stated as deflationarily as the evidence permits. Latest-binding-wins is order-sensitive — but it is order-sensitive in the weakest possible way: the recovered interpretation is a function of the single maximum-stamp binding. Given the content of the record (which figures the term was used for) and the round stamps (when), the answer is the figure with the largest stamp. Nothing else in the trajectory bears on it. The first binding, the middle bindings, the path by which the term moved from one figure to another — none of it changes the output. The result page says exactly this: order-sensitivity here "is consistent with a thin convention-update / overwrite rule ('the latest agreement supersedes earlier ones')," which "is not the deep concept/relational-meaning notion of a convention constituted between agents through the live trajectory" (result/relational-spontaneous-recency-a, §"What this shows").

Here is the move that makes "order-sensitivity ≠ constitution" precise. An overwrite rule is fully recoverable by a single reader from the record. The relational concept page names the deflationary default that any constitution claim must beat: a convention "whose interpretation any fresh agent can recover from the content-bag of the record is exactly what a shared distributional substrate predicts," and predicts "without anything existing 'in the interaction and not antecedently in either party'" (essay/aggregation-not-constitution, §"What the null deflates", quoting concept/relational-meaning). Latest-binding-wins barely escapes the cleanest version of that default — the order-insensitive bag of turns — but it does not escape the default's deeper point. Take the bag of turns and add to each turn its stamp. A single reader, handed that augmented record, computes max-stamp and reads off the answer. No second party is required; no live exchange is required; the ordering is recoverable from content+stamps that sit inert in the text. This is why the finding co-tags model-internal and is consistent with distributional structure: a next-token predictor conditioned on a linear, stamped context can produce a recency-weighting "for free" from the prompt geometry and the training objective (concept/distributional-meaning), with no constitution anywhere in the loop. Order-sensitivity raised the floor above the order-free bag; it did not lift the result out of the space of things one reader can compute from the record alone. That space — single-reader-recoverable — is the home of mere updating. Constitution, if it exists, must live outside it.

A ladder of rungs on the second ladder

With that core in hand, the rungs become legible. Each is a stricter demand on what the recovered interpretation must depend on; each comes with a candidate behavioral signature and a discriminating design. The project has evidence for rungs (i) and (ii) (the latter in its survival sense, as of 2026-06-17; plus an order-sensitive sense — one-model under a forced format as of 2026-06-18, panel-wide given a working surface as of 2026-06-19; see the revision log), both on the thin side. Rungs (iii)–(iv) are conjectural — the essay is proposing their signatures, not reporting them. (Update 2026-06-18: rung (iii)'s text operationalization has since been run, with its rich side documented structurally closed for text-only stimuli — bounded, not crossed; see that rung below. Its rich-side signature is still not occupied.) Where a rung connects to a real open backlog unit, that is noted.

Rung (i) — order-sensitive OVERWRITE (latest-binding-wins). The rung now climbed. Interpretation is a function of the maximum-stamp binding. Signature: in a reassignment design where only recency disambiguates, recovery tracks the latest binding above the shortcut chance floor. This is the established positive — SPONT latest-binding rate 1.000 in both models, replicated under the implicit control (result/relational-spontaneous-recency-a; result/relational-implicit-reassignment-control). It is order-sensitive and it is real. It is also, by the argument above, single-reader-recoverable: thin.

Rung (ii) — order-sensitive INTEGRATION / non-overwrite repair. The rung now occupied (in its survival sense). The recovered convention reflects the combination of earlier and later agreements, not just the last state. A later turn that refines an earlier one ("the DAX — the spiky one — actually, the spiky one on the left") should yield an interpretation that carries information from both turns, not one that simply discards the earlier. Signature: recovery that is a function of more than the max-stamp binding — measurably sensitive to a non-terminal turn whose content an overwrite rule would throw away. Proposed discriminating design: hold the final turn fixed and vary an earlier refining turn; an overwrite reader (rung i) is invariant to that change, an integrating reader is not. This is now tested (result/relational-integration-rung-ii, 2026-06-17): when the latest turn (a pattern) is compatible with an earlier turn (a shape) and only their conjunction is unique, both panel models recover the both-match figure at ceiling (INTEG target 1.000; the pure-overwrite "latest only" reading taken 0.000), beating the 0.50 single-attribute ceiling that any non-integrating reader is pinned to. The earlier, non-terminal turn survives; agreements compose. With rung (i) established for conflicting turns, the rule is: supersede-on-conflict, compose-on-compatibility. Two limits the result carries (both binding on this paragraph): the design's constraints are symmetric, so the result shows the earlier turn survives, not that the composition is itself order-sensitive (a commutative AND of two compatible facts passes the test equally) — so the "order-sensitive" in this rung's title remains the aspiration, with only the survival half occupied; and the conjunction is trivially easy (the on-demand check is also at ceiling), so the result is decisive evidence against blind overwrite but only weak evidence of genuinely spontaneous composition. One horn of that harder-load worry — "more turns" — was tested 2026-06-17 and bounded one step in the claim's favour: with three compatible turns and the earliest buried under two later ones, both models still retain it at ceiling and the "drop-the-oldest" reading is taken 0.000 of the time (result/relational-integration-depth) — so integration is not a depth-1 artifact, though that result is itself saturated (robust to one further turn of depth, not arbitrary depth) and leaves the spontaneity reading exactly as weak (DIRECT still at ceiling). Integration is in any case still single-reader-recoverable — a single reader given the full record could combine the turns — so rung (ii) is a stricter form of dependence but, even occupied, not by itself constitution. It matters because it was the first rung an overwrite rule fails: it separates "the latest agreement supersedes" from "the agreements compose," and the models fall on the compose side. Update 2026-06-18: the survival-vs-order-sensitive gap this paragraph names was tested and partly closed — for one model. The Option-C probe (result/relational-order-composition-c) made the composition genuinely non-commutative (two stamped moves whose two orders reach different end states). claude-sonnet-4.6 orders the moves by their stamps spontaneously at ceiling (COMP 1.000; DIRECT on-demand gate 0.861 PASS) — genuinely non-commutative composition, occupying the stricter half this rung's title aspired to; gemini-3.5-flash cannot compose the two moves even on demand (DIRECT 0.583 < 0.80 → UNINTERPRETABLE), so the stricter rung is occupied by claude, not the panel. It remains thin (adjudicated single-reader-recoverable before the run: the stamped move-list is in the record) — "respects operation order," not rung (iii). Update 2026-06-19: the "by claude, not the panel" reading is superseded — the stricter rung is now occupied by the WHOLE PANEL given a working surface. Changing only the response format from a forced single token to a working surface flipped both gemini (DIRECT 0.656→1.000) and gpt (0.250→0.969) to RESPECTS-ORDER at/near ceiling, spontaneously ordering the two moves by their stamps (result/relational-order-composition-c-reasoning-scaffold), and the same panel-concordant result replicated on a genuinely different non-commuting pair (CYCLE/SWAP / A4) (result/relational-order-composition-c-altpair). So the forced-format split was an artifact of the output channel (the essay/output-channel-confound lesson), not a capability split: spontaneous order-sensitive composition is a panel-wide capacity across two operation pairs. It stays thin — the working surface broadens which models and which pairs occupy this stricter rung; it does not move the rung across the thin/rich cut.

Rung (iii) — PATH-dependence proper. Interpretation depends on the trajectory of repairs — the ordered sequence by which the convention was reached — and not merely on the final multiset of bindings plus their stamps. Two records with the same set of stamped turns but a different order of arrival should yield different interpretations. Signature: a live-vs-shuffled gap — hold the content and the stamps of prior turns constant, destroy their ordering, and ask whether interpretation survives unchanged (concept/relational-meaning, §"How the project operationalizes the tag: trajectory-dependence"). The relational concept page names this as the operational surplus over distributional convergence: "a live-vs-shuffled gap is the operational signature of a convention built between rather than within; its absence is a relational deflation." Crucially, rung (iii) is where single-reader-recoverability begins to fail: if the answer depends on a trajectory that the final content+stamps do not encode, no single reader of the record can reconstruct it. This is a proposed contrast, not a result — and the project does not currently have an instrument that separates rung (i) from rung (iii) (see the criterion section). It is the threshold rung: below it, updating; at it, something the record alone cannot carry.

Update 2026-06-18 (prior framing above kept visible). The instrument was, in fact, built and run — the Option-C non-commuting-operation probe (result/relational-order-composition-c), the strongest order-load-bearing text design the project can construct. The honest outcome is not a rung-(iii) crossing but a documented structural closure for text-only stimuli: because a transcript is a final content+stamps record, any order-load-bearing text design has its gap adjudicated single-reader-recoverable (thin) before the run — and Option C's gap was so adjudicated, so even the model that cleared it perfectly (claude, COMP 1.000) supplies only a stricter thin rung, not arrival-order surplus. The rich side of rung (iii) is therefore bounded, not merely unmeasured: for text stimuli it is closed by construction (a fully promotable closure per decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence §A5, internal-contrast-only), medium-bound — a non-text medium remains untested. So the "proposed contrast, not a result" framing above stands for what was originally available, but the contrast has now been operationalized and its text-only rich side documented closed.

Rung (iv) — genuine BETWEEN-agent constitution. Content that exists in the interaction and not antecedently in either party — the defining phrase of concept/relational-meaning. The reference instance is Brandom's deontic scorekeeping: conceptual content as commitments and entitlements instituted and tracked between interlocutors, "a normative status in a social practice, not a causal-functional property of one head" (concept/inferential-meaning, §"Brandom's normative inferentialism", characterized-not-read in-repo). Signature: content recoverable only from the two-party normative trajectory — who committed to what, who is entitled to what, which challenges were issued and answered — with no observable on which a single reader of the transcript could reconstruct it. Nothing in the project measures this. As the prior essay records, the relational pilots "made no assertions [the agents] could be held to, issued and faced no challenges, kept no score of anyone's entitlements" (essay/aggregation-not-constitution, §"What the null does not touch"). Rung (iv) is the top of the ladder and is entirely unbuilt.

The shape of the climb is the point. Rungs (i) and (ii) are firm (rung ii in its survival sense, as of 2026-06-17). Rungs (iii) and (iv) are each strictly more demanding, and the project has run neither of them — and crucially, both occupied rungs sit on the thin side of the criterion below. The two rungs being real does not foreshorten the rest of the ladder; it makes the unclimbed length of it — the rich side, where constitution would live — visible.

What would distinguish thin from rich: the criterion

The ladder needs a single decidable cut, and the deflationary core supplies it.

Thin/rich criterion. A convention is merely updated (thin) if its recovered interpretation is a function of the record's content + stamps, computable by any single reader of that record. It is constituted (rich) only if recovery requires a surplus that no single reader of the record could reconstruct — something carried by the live, ordered, two-party trajectory and not encoded in the final content+stamps.

Rungs (i) and (ii) sit on the thin side: an overwrite rule and an integration rule are both single-reader-recoverable (one reads off max-stamp; the other composes the turns), even though (ii) is a stricter dependence than (i). The 2026-06-17 rung-(ii) result confirms this placement empirically — the models compose compatible turns, but the composition the design measures is exactly the kind a single reader of the record performs (intersect the stated constraints), so occupying rung (ii) moved the models to a stricter rung on the same side of the cut, not across it. Rungs (iii) and (iv) cross to the rich side: path-dependence proper requires information about the order of arrival that the final multiset+stamps does not contain, and between-agent constitution requires normative status that no transcript-reader can reconstruct alone. The criterion is what makes "order-sensitivity ≠ constitution" non-negotiable: latest-binding-wins is order-sensitive and single-reader-recoverable, so it is thin by definition, not by interpretive charity — and so is the integration just observed.

Honesty demands stating plainly what the current instrument can and cannot do. It cannot separate rung (i) from rung (iii). This is the claim's own scope limit 3, stated verbatim: "'Latest-binding-wins' may be a shallow update heuristic; this claim does not separate thin order-sensitivity from deep path-dependence" (claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment, scope limit 3). The reassignment design makes recency the sole disambiguator, but it does not vary the trajectory while holding the final state fixed, so a max-stamp reader and a genuinely path-dependent reader produce the same answer on it. The design that could separate them is the live-vs-shuffled contrast the concept page names: present the same final multiset of stamped bindings via two different ordered histories and test whether interpretation differs. A null there (interpretation invariant to the shuffle) would confirm the convention is thin even where one might have hoped for path-dependence; a clean gap would be the first evidence for rung (iii). That design's operationalization is a ratified decision (decisions/resolved/relational-rung-iii-path-dependence, ratified 2026-06-17: adopt Option C — non-commuting operation semantics — under a pre-run adjudication gate that decides, biased against the rich reading, whether an operation-order gap even counts as rung iii), which states honestly that the rung may be structurally unable to produce a rich-side result with text-only stimuli (any difference a single reader of the final record could reconstruct is thin by construction) — so a documented closure is a live, promotable outcome alongside a positive or a null. Update 2026-06-18: that design has now been run, and the promotable outcome it anticipated landed — documented structural closure for text-only stimuli. The Option-C probe (result/relational-order-composition-c) was the strongest order-load-bearing text design buildable; its gap was adjudicated THIN before the run, and even claude's perfect gap (COMP 1.000 — and, as of 2026-06-19, the whole panel's at/near-perfect gap given a working surface, see the revision log) is single-reader-recoverable, because a transcript is a final content+stamps record — so no text stimulus can carry the arrival-order surplus rung (iii) requires. The rich side of rung (iii) is thus closed by construction for text, not merely unmeasured (per the decision §A5, internal-contrast-only), and medium-bound: a non-text medium is untested. So the "proposed here, not run" / "cannot separate (i) from (iii)" framing is superseded for text: the separation was attempted and the rich side documented closed, not crossed. And even a clean rung-(iii) gap — were a non-text medium ever to produce one — would not reach rung (iv), which requires observables (commitments, entitlements, challenges) that no current relational instrument records at all.

Revision triggers (read before citing)

This essay's empirical floor is firm (two thin rungs occupied) but its rich upper ladder is a proposal; specific evidence would force a rewrite.

The essay is revised. (The null that would have sharpened the thin overwrite verdict — recovery insensitive to the non-terminal turn — did not occur; the result instead moved the models up one thin rung.)   • (b) A thin/rich separation result (rung iii). — PARTLY RESOLVED 2026-06-18 (text-only branch: documented closed). A live-vs-shuffled / non-commuting-operation design that holds the final stamped multiset constant and varies trajectory: a clean gap would be the first crossing to the rich side and would falsify the essay's central deflationary reading of the current positive's neighborhood (though not the positive itself, which is scoped to rung i); a null would confirm single-reader-recoverability where path-dependence was the alternative.   • (c) Any between-agent observable (rung iv). A relational instrument that records commitments/entitlements/challenges (a scorekeeping observable) — currently absent in-repo — would let rung (iv) be tested rather than only characterized, and would require replacing the "entirely unbuilt" description with whatever it finds.
  • (d) A human order/path anchor. The human leg of every order/path contrast here is unanchored in-repo; fetching a human resource that bears on order- or path-sensitivity (none is in-repo) would, for the first time, license a human comparison — currently forbidden by the internal-contrast-only status of the cited results.

What this essay is not

Not a new empirical claim — its original contribution is the ladder and the thin/rich criterion, a conceptual scaffold; every empirical assertion in it cites the in-repo page that carries it, at that page's stated strength. Not a claim that the project is near constitution — it argues the opposite, that the firm bottom rung makes the distance to constitution measurable and that the distance is large and mostly unmeasured.
