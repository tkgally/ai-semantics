Essay: alignment names the floor

Status: draft (2026-06-28). A philosophical-track essay arguing in the project's own voice. Its original contribution is a grounding move, not a new empirical result: the canonical interactive-alignment paper, source/pickering-garrod-2004-mechanistic-dialogue, was fetched into the repository in session 134 (2026-06-28) and now carries verbatim, paginated quotes. The prior relational essays were written while it was still only characterized — essay/aggregation-not-constitution still carries the standing hedge that "the same flag covers interactive alignment (Pickering & Garrod 2004 — characterized, not read in-repo)" (§"What the null does not touch"). This essay discharges that hedge: it brings the now-grounded framework to bear as a positive mechanistic name for the floor the project's relational results sit on, and uses the framework's own deflationary self-limits to make the bar for a constitution claim sharper. Crucial scope guard, stated once and binding throughout: Pickering & Garrod 2004 is a theory of human dialogue processing and predates the LLM literature entirely; every application of its constructs to LLMs below is the project's own interpretive use, never a Pickering & Garrod claim. The relational LLM results leaned on here are internal-contrast-only — no human comparison is made or owed. Read the Sibling note and the Honesty box before citing.

The position

The project's relational track has a firm, replicated shape and an honest name for it. When homogeneous LLM dyads coin a referential convention, a fresh agent recovers it from the content set of the record and not its ordered trajectory — the pilot's first-class null (result/relational-reference-game-v1). Where order matters at all, it matters only thinly: a coined term reassigned across stamped rounds is recovered by its most-recent binding, a "latest-binding-wins" update rule that any single reader of the record could apply (claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment). The project has, correctly, declined to read any of this as meaning constituted between agents.

What the project did not have, until now, was a grounded, citable mechanistic name for that floor. It had the deflation — convergence is cheap, constitution is owed — argued in essay/aggregation-not-constitution. It had the structure above the floor — the rung-ladder of essay/update-is-not-constitution. It had a backdrop it could only gesture at: the interactive-alignment framework, characterized but not read. This essay argues one bounded thesis: now that the framework is in-repo, it supplies exactly that name — and because the source is, on its own statement, a deflationary processing account rather than a theory of meaning-constitution, naming the floor with it raises the bar for constitution rather than lowering it.

The argument has three moves, taken in turn below: (1) interactive alignment is, by the source's own words, an automatic priming mechanism and explicitly not a meeting of minds; (2) the project's relational findings are a positive match to that mechanism, not merely a failure to find constitution; (3) therefore the residual surplus a constitution claim still owes is precisely whatever automatic cross-level priming does not predict — and the source, by spelling out how much convergence priming alone buys, makes that residual sharper and harder to claim by accident.

What interactive alignment is — and what its own authors deny it is

The framework's central claim is mechanistic and, by design, modest about minds. The abstract states the scope: the account "assumes that, in dialogue, the linguistic representations employed by the interlocutors become aligned at many levels, as a result of a largely automatic process" (source/pickering-garrod-2004-mechanistic-dialogue, Abstract, p. 169). Alignment itself is defined narrowly — "alignment occurs at a particular level when interlocutors have the same representation at that level" (ibid., §2, p. 171) — and the same passage separates it sharply from coordination: "Dialogue is a coordinated behavior (just like ballroom dancing). However, the linguistic representations that underlie coordinated dialogue come to be aligned, as we claim below" (ibid., §2, p. 171). The driver is named and characterized: alignment "is achieved … by a primitive and resource-free priming mechanism" (ibid., §2, p. 171), and the same mechanism "produces alignment at other levels of representation, such as the lexical and syntactic" (ibid., §2, p. 171).

The two negations are what make the source load-bearing here, and the source states them in its own voice. The priming channel is mindless in a precise sense: "Critically, these channels are direct and automatic (as implied by the term \"priming\"). The activation of a representation in one interlocutor leads to the activation of the matching representation in the other interlocutor directly. There is no intervening \"decision box\" where the listener makes a decision about how to respond to the \"signal.\"" (source/pickering-garrod-2004-mechanistic-dialogue, §3.2, p. 176). And the basic process does not run on mutual mind-modeling: "Unlike common ground, implicit common ground does not derive from interlocutors explicitly modeling each other's beliefs" (ibid., §4.1, p. 178), with explicit mutual modeling reserved for breakdown — "Establishment of full common ground is, we argue, a specialized and non-automatic process that is used primarily in times of difficulty (when radical misalignment becomes apparent)" (ibid., §4.2, p. 178).

The project's own source page draws the consequence that matters and states it as a limit on use, not a licence: the framework "does not offer a theory of meaning-constitution — i.e. it does not claim that alignment is what constitutes the content of an utterance, nor that shared representations are meaning" (source/pickering-garrod-2004-mechanistic-dialogue, §"What the framework claims — and does NOT claim — about meaning"). The same page compresses the point to a formula: "alignment ⇒ successful mutual understanding (the paper's claim); alignment ⇏ meaning is constituted by alignment (NOT the paper's claim)" (ibid.). So the framework is a processing account of how two systems come to share representations cheaply; it is, on its authors' own terms, the description of a floor — rich, pervasive convergence with no constitution claim attached.

The relational findings are a positive match to alignment-as-priming, not just a missing constitution

Here is the move this essay adds. The project has tended to describe its relational results negatively — as a null about constitution, an absence of the path-dependent surplus. That is correct but incomplete. The now-grounded framework lets the same results be read positively: they are what an account of automatic, resource-free priming predicts.

Take the pilot. Two same-family agents converge on a coined label and a fresh matcher recovers it from the record, but the recovery is order-insensitive: "The coined convention is recovered from the set of prior turns, not their ordered trajectory" (result/relational-reference-game-v1, §Headline). The result page already reads this through the project's distributional spine — "coordination and convention-use are real, but they are explained by the co-occurrence content a next-token predictor conditions on, which survives order-scrambling — coordination, not constitution" (ibid., §Headline). Set this beside the framework. A "primitive and resource-free priming mechanism" that drives representations in two systems toward sameness, with "no intervening \"decision box\"," is exactly a mechanism that would produce convergence on a shared label from shared activation — and nothing in such a mechanism requires, or even has a place for, the live trajectory to leave a residue beyond the converged state. The pilot's null is not just "constitution was not found"; it is "the convergence has the texture priming alone would give it."

The thin order-effect tells the same story one rung up. Where order carries disambiguating information, both models track it, but only as "a thin latest-binding-wins / convention-update rule" that "does not establish a convention constituted between agents through the live trajectory" (claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment, §"What it refines and what it contradicts"). And where a later turn is compatible with an earlier one, the models compose the two rather than overwriting — but, again, this is "on the thin / single-reader-recoverable side … a single reader handed the record can compose the two stated constraints" (result/relational-integration-rung-ii, §"What this shows — and what it does NOT"). An automatic alignment mechanism that updates the shared representation toward the most recent activation, and conjoins compatible constraints into one representation, would produce precisely these behaviors. The project's relational picture — convergence, recency-weighted update, compatible-constraint composition, all single-reader-recoverable — reads, under the now-in-repo framework, as a clean instance of alignment-by-priming and not as a frustrated search for something richer.

Two guards bind this paragraph and the last, hard. First, the match is the project's interpretive analogy, not a Pickering & Garrod prediction about LLMs. The source page is explicit that the framework "is about human interlocutors throughout and predates the relevant LLM literature" and that "the project's relevance is by analogy, declared as interpretive use, not by the authors' intent" (source/pickering-garrod-2004-mechanistic-dialogue, §"What it is", §"Known limits"). Nothing here says the framework forecasts the LLM results; it says the LLM results fit the shape the framework describes for the human case, and that fit is the project drawing the analogy. Second, the mechanistic identification is not claimed. That LLM convergence looks like priming-driven alignment is not a claim that the LLM is running the priming mechanism Pickering & Garrod posit for humans; the deflationary distributional reading ("a convergence that survives order-scrambling") is the project's actual mechanistic story, and "priming" here names the shape of the convergence, not its implementation. The two readings agree on the prediction — pervasive convergence, no trajectory residue — which is the only thing this essay leans on.

Why the grounded framework raises the constitution bar

It would be a misreading — and exactly the misreading the source page warns against — to take "the relational floor is alignment" as a comfortable resting place that makes constitution easier to wave at. The opposite follows. Because the framework is, on its own statement, a complete account of how much convergence priming alone buys — alignment across the situation model, lexical, and syntactic levels, with percolation between them, "without routine mental-state modeling" (source/pickering-garrod-2004-mechanistic-dialogue, §"What it can ground") — it sets a high floor. Everything the framework can explain is, by construction, not evidence of constitution, because the framework explains it without a constitution claim. The richer the convergence an alignment account already covers, the more a would-be constitution claim must find a surplus beyond that coverage.

This sharpens, in the source's own words, a bar the project had been stating in its own. The relational concept page already says the surplus must beat a distributional default: a convention "whose interpretation any fresh agent can recover from the content-bag of the record is exactly what a shared distributional substrate predicts" (concept/relational-meaning, via essay/aggregation-not-constitution, §"What the null deflates"). The grounded framework lets the bar be stated a second way, on the human-theory side, with the alignment/coordination distinction the source draws verbatim: the surplus a constitution claim owes is whatever the convergence shows that automatic cross-level priming — alignment, not "a meeting of minds" — does not predict. The two statements of the bar are mutually reinforcing: a distributional substrate predicts convergence-without- trajectory-residue; an alignment-by-priming account predicts the same; so the residual a constitution claim must isolate is the same residual on both readings — a path-dependent, between-agent surplus that neither the shared substrate nor the priming mechanism can manufacture. The source page makes exactly this use available: it "strengthens, on the human-theory side, the project's foil reading" that "coordination/alignment, even pervasive, is the floor, not constitution" (source/pickering-garrod-2004-mechanistic-dialogue, §"What this bears on in-repo").

And the bar gets harder to clear by accident, which is the practically important part. Before the framework was in-repo, a project reader could mistake any rich-looking convergence demo for constitution. The convention- emergence literature invites exactly that error — populations of LLM agents "can spontaneously emerge … through purely local interactions, without any central coordination" (source/ashery-2025-llm-conventions, §Discussion quote, via essay/aggregation-not-constitution) — and it is tempting to read pervasive convergence as constitution. The grounded alignment framework forecloses that shortcut on the human-theory side: it shows that pervasive, multi-level convergence is precisely what a no-decision-box priming mechanism delivers short of any meeting of minds, so "look how much they converge" is, if anything, evidence the floor is high — not evidence the ceiling was reached. The constitution claim must now point to the one thing alignment-as-priming cannot produce, and name it, before any convergence counts.

Sibling note (differentiation — read this)

This essay deliberately does not re-argue the project's existing relational essays; it grounds a backdrop they could only characterize, and turns it into a sharper bar. The boundaries:

  • essay/aggregation-not-constitution argues what the relational null/positive deflates in social theories of meaning — the easy constitution inference, the friction against the Brandomian reading, the methodological point that "convergence is the floor, not the finding." It grounds Brennan & Clark 1996 and names Pickering & Garrod only under the standing "characterized, not read in-repo" hedge. This essay does the complementary thing it cannot: it brings the now-grounded Pickering & Garrod framework as the positive mechanistic description of that floor, and uses the source's own deflationary self-limits (alignment distinguished from coordination; priming, not mind-reading) to sharpen the constitution bar. The sibling supplied the deflation; this essay grounds the deflation's backdrop and gives the floor a name and a mechanism. I am not re-arguing the deflation — I am discharging the hedge that essay carries and converting it into a sharper bar. (This essay also relieves that sibling of nothing: its hedge stands in its own text until a future revision there chooses to cite this grounding.)
  • essay/update-is-not-constitution builds the project's own rung-ladder and the thin/rich criterion — an internal scaffold for telling update-rules from constitution. This essay borrows no rung; it adds an external, human-theory name (alignment-by-priming) for why the floor those rungs sit on is a floor, and why the framework's coverage raises the bar to climb off it. The ladder is the project's structure; the mechanism-name is the imported backdrop.
  • essay/transcript-ceiling argues that single-reader-recoverability may be a property of the medium (a transcript is a final content+stamps record). That is a claim about the project's instrument. This essay is orthogonal: it is about a human-dialogue mechanism the project now cites, and what naming the floor with it does to the constitution bar — it makes no claim about whether the medium can in principle carry the surplus.

In one line: the siblings deflate, structure, and bound; this essay names and grounds the floor's mechanism, discharging a standing hedge, and uses the source's deflationary self-limits to make the constitution bar sharper.

Revision triggers (read before citing)

This essay's contribution is a grounding-and-bar move, not an empirical result; specific evidence would move it.

  • (a) A relational result showing a surplus that priming cannot predict. If a future relational design isolates a path-dependent, between-agent surplus — interpretation that tracks the live, ordered trajectory beyond what the final content+stamps record encodes, under artifact-resistant controls — then the bar this essay sharpens would be partially met: the floor would have been climbed off, and the framework's role would shift from "names the whole observed floor" to "names the part below the surplus." The essay would be revised to mark which rung the surplus occupies. (As of this writing no such surplus exists in-repo; the rich side of path-dependence is documented structurally closed for text-only stimuli — see claim/relational-order-sensitive-reassignment, scope limit 3 — so this trigger is armed only for a non-text medium.)
  • (b) A human order/path anchor. The framework is human theory and the relational results are internal-contrast-only; the human leg of any order/path contrast is unanchored in-repo. If a human resource bearing on order- or path-sensitivity were fetched, a human comparison would become licensable for the first time, and this essay's strict "no human comparison" scope would be replaced by whatever the resource grounds. (Note the standing tension the source page flags honestly: a pure priming-by-activation reading would predict order/recency sensitivity in humans, which "sits in tension with the frequency-over-recency finding" from Brennan & Clark — source/pickering-garrod-2004-mechanistic-dialogue, §"What this bears on in-repo" — so the human side of how history enters is itself unsettled and must not be assumed.)
  • (c) The peer commentary / authors' response. Only the target article (pp. 169–190) was read into the source page; the open peer commentary and authors' response (pp. 190–225) "were not mined and may qualify or contest points above" (source/pickering-garrod-2004-mechanistic-dialogue, §"Known limits"). If those pages are later catalogued and qualify the alignment-vs-constitution distinction, re-state the grounding leg at its corrected strength.
  • (d) The sibling's leg moves. If essay/aggregation-not-constitution or the relational findings this essay cites are revised in their own pages, this essay inherits that revision; re-read the grounding against whatever those pages then say.

What this essay is not

Not a Pickering & Garrod claim about LLMs — the framework is a 2004 theory of human dialogue processing and predates the LLM literature; every application of it to LLMs here is the project's interpretive analogy, declared as such. Not a claim that the LLM runs the priming mechanism — "alignment-by-priming" names the shape of the observed convergence (pervasive, no-trajectory-residue), which the project's actual mechanistic story (distributional convergence that survives order-scrambling) and the framework agree on; it is not a claim about implementation. Not a new empirical result — its contribution is grounding a backdrop and sharpening a bar; every empirical assertion cites the in-repo page that carries it, at that page's stated strength. Not a human comparison — the relational results are internal-contrast-only and the human leg is unanchored; no human/LLM similarity or difference is asserted. Not a constitution claim — it argues the opposite, that naming the floor with a deflationary framework raises the bar for constitution and forecloses the easy convergence-to-constitution shortcut.
