Essay: when the default eats the construction

Status: draft (2026-06-13). The project's second original essay — the philosophical track arguing in its own voice. Its empirical leg rests on one ceiling-bounded null (result/aann-inferential-v3) and the open question that null walked straight into (open-question/distributional-vs-inferential-constructional). The thesis is a claim about measurability and method, not a claim about whether the models "have" the inference; the Revision triggers section states in advance exactly what future evidence would revise or retract it. Read the triggers before citing the argument.

The position

This essay argues one bounded methodological thesis. For a next-token model, a construction's licensed inference can be behaviorally indistinguishable from the phrase's distributional default; and where the two coincide, a shift-from-control design cannot reach the inferential question at all. The AANN inferential probe is the worked instance: it returned a null not because the models fail to draw the unification inference, but because they read the ordinary plural phrase (three beautiful days) as a unified evaluated stretch just as readily as the construction (a beautiful three days) — so there was no headroom for the construction to shift anything. The contribution is to name what then compounds, and what it costs the project's evidence ladder: the top rungs of that ladder — inferential use — may be unreachable by within-model contrast for exactly the constructions whose meaning is most entrenched as a default. That is a limit on the instrument, not a verdict on the models. The title states it: where the inference is the default, the default eats the construction, and the measurement has nothing left to see.

The evidence, stated at its actual strength

The v3 inferential probe (result/aann-inferential-v3) asked whether the AANN construction makes a model draw the unification / whole-evaluation inference (a beautiful three days = one unified evaluated stretch, not three separately-evaluated days), measured only as the AANN-vs-control shift. The pre-registered verdict was NULL: "No model produced an AANN-vs-control shift on the primary paraphrase arm that clears the frozen bar (τ = +0.20 with bootstrap-CI-lower > 0)" (paraphrase shift +0.174 / +0.043 / 0.000 for claude-sonnet-4.6 / gpt-5.4-mini / gemini-3.5-flash; result/aann-inferential-v3, §Headline).

The load-bearing fact is why the shift is null. The result page is explicit, and the essay takes nothing beyond it: "The unification paraphrase and the unification/whole-evaluation NLI hypotheses are chosen/affirmed at near-ceiling rates for both the AANN premise and its plural control" — paraphrase control raw rates already 0.78 / 0.96 / 1.00; NLI control 0.87 / 0.83 / 1.00. "The models read three beautiful days as describing a unified, whole-evaluated stretch just as readily as a beautiful three days. The construction therefore has no headroom to shift the reading" (result/aann-inferential-v3, §"What the null is, and what bounds it", point 1). The design's pre-registered defence — an under-pressure subset where the distributive paraphrase is the locally-fluent continuation — "did not rescue the arm: the under-pressure shift is +0.20 for claude … and 0.00 for both gpt and gemini" (same section). And the result page draws its own limit in a sentence the essay leans on verbatim: it is "a measurement null with a precise, named cause, not as 'the construction carries no inferential force'" (result/aann-inferential-v3, §Bounds). Everything below must be read against that grain of evidence and no finer.

That this would happen was predicted, not discovered after the fact. The open question had already named the trap: "The two senses predict the same answer in the easy cases," and "the distinguishing cases are the rare ones," because "inference and likelihood are entangled by the objective" (open-question/distributional-vs-inferential-constructional, §"Why it is hard"). The result is the open question's own diagnosis made concrete; the open question's update records it as "exactly the predicted wall" and stays open (open-question/distributional-vs-inferential-constructional, §Update). (So it stood when this essay was written; the founding question was since closed — status: answered, s170, its closure argued in theory/lexicon-grammar-continuum §"Closing the two founding open-questions" — with exactly this wall carried into the closure as the reason the answer is "thin.")

The argument: two limits that compound

The essay's claim is that this null sits at the intersection of two distinct limits, and that their compounding is what makes it interesting — and what the project had not previously named in one place.

Limit (1): can't-say-like-humans. The v3 result is anchor: internal-contrast-only. Its binding chief-cost statement is verbatim: "the v3 can never say 'models draw the inference the way humans do' — only that the construction shifts inferential behaviour relative to a matched control, in the direction the published semantics predicts" (result/aann-inferential-v3, §header box). There is no human AANN-inference dataset; the expected-inference key is expert-stipulated (a theorist's coding of the published semantics), not human judgment data (same box). So the strongest thing this instrument could ever report is a within-model contrast — never a human comparison. That ceiling is fixed independently of any result.

Limit (2): can't-separate-from-the-default. Even the within-model contrast — the one thing limit (1) leaves on the table — is blocked when inference and default coincide. A shift-from-control design measures the construction's contribution by subtracting a baseline; when the baseline is already at the unification ceiling, the subtraction has nothing to register. The governing decision states the mechanism plainly: "The AANN-vs-control shift design subtracts a baseline that is already at the unification ceiling, so there is no headroom to detect the construction's contribution" (decisions/resolved/aann-inferential-default-coincidence, §"Why this exists").

The compounding is the point. Limit (1) says the probe can at best deliver a within-model contrast. Limit (2) says that for this construction, even that within-model contrast is unreachable, because the inference the construction is supposed to license is already the phrase's distributional default. So the two limits do not merely stack — they close off both the ceiling and the floor of what the design could have shown. The honest description of the null is therefore narrow and precise: at this instrument, the construction's unification inference is not separable from the plural phrase's default unification reading. It is not a finding that the models lack the inference (the result page is emphatic on this; §Bounds), and it is not a human-comparison claim (it cannot be). It is a statement about what this kind of measurement can and cannot reach.

What this means for the evidence ladder

The constructional evidence ladder (theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms (superseded s177 by theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms-v2, which carries the ladder unchanged in structure — cited here as the edition this essay engaged)) orders evidence from form-acceptability up to Tier 4 — inference-licensing, "the strongest text-internal rung and the one that earns the inferential tag in Piantadosi & Hill's sense." The ladder's own update records the v3 run as "the ladder's first encounter with the limit [the open question] warned of: at Tier 4 the inferentially-licensed reading and the distributional default coincide, so a shift-from-control design cannot separate them unless the control's default is engineered to be distributive" (theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms, §"The evidence ladder", v3 box).

The essay draws the structural consequence the ladder gestures at but does not state as a thesis: the top rung of the ladder may be unreachable — by within-model contrast — for precisely the constructions whose contributed reading is most entrenched as a distributional default. This is not a paradox; it is a selection effect. A within-model shift design can only measure a contribution the model does not already make for free. The more thoroughly a construction's meaning has soaked into the surrounding distribution — the more "default" its inference has become — the less a shift-from-control design can see of it. The constructions for which Tier 4 is easiest to climb by this method are, by this argument, the ones where the construction's inference is not the default. Which is exactly where the ladder's add-direction positives already live: the way-construction and caused-motion add an inference the lexical verb does not carry, so the baseline is off-ceiling and the contrast has somewhere to register (theory/constructional-meaning-in-llms, §"Where the existing wedge sits"). The method's reach and the construction's entrenchment trade off against each other.

This sharpens, rather than weakens, the deflationary reading the project already carries. The recurring AANN pattern is "strong on form, unproven on inferential use" (result/aann-inferential-v3, §"Relation to the conjecture and the theory"). The essay's amendment: for AANN, the "unproven" is partly a property of the instrument under entrenchment, not only of the model. We do not get to read the v3 null as "the construction carries no inferential force" — but neither do we get to read it as evidence the model has the inference. The within-model contrast that would adjudicate is the very thing the coincidence forecloses.

Joining the lexicon–grammar continuum

The same pattern lives at the other end of the form–meaning cline. theory/lexicon-grammar-continuum argues that the lexical and grammatical wedges are "not two tracks but one," tested by a single skeleton — "does a meaning gradient beat the distributional shadow?" — and that the grounding (multimodal) results saturate "because the text-only panel already separates clear homonyms perfectly (AUC = 1.000 in every cell) … text saturates and the image has nothing to add" (theory/lexicon-grammar-continuum, §"The prospective third axis"). That is the lexical form of the present essay's claim: the test is "least sensitive exactly where it looks cleanest," because the text distribution already carries the distinction the probe is reaching for (same section).

The AANN inference/default coincidence is the grammatical-side analogue of that lexical saturation. In both, the model's distribution already supplies the very signal the probe was built to detect, so a contrast-against-the-default design has no residual to measure. The continuum page's own framing — that a context-similarity shadow (lexicon) and a frequency shadow (grammar) "are the same deflationary hypothesis at two grains" (theory/lexicon-grammar-continuum, §"What this page predicts and forbids") — extends cleanly: "the default eats the construction" is a unifying pattern across both ends of the cline. Where text already carries the distinction (lexical homonymy) or already licenses the reading (the AANN unification default), the instrument saturates, and the question it was built to ask becomes unreachable by that instrument. This essay claims that join as a structural observation, not a new measurement; the measurements are on the two pages it leans on. (The continuum page itself does not yet record the AANN coincidence; this essay proposes the bridge, and the continuum page would be the place to fold it in on a future revision.)

The one signal that moved — and why it sharpens, not undercuts, the thesis

There was a lone positive in v3, and it matters precisely because it is the exception that proves the rule. gpt-5.4-mini showed a grammaticalized singular-agreement reflex: it "picks singular was for the AANN frame (0.96) but plural were for the matched control (0.22 was), a +0.74 shift with a CI well clear of zero" (result/aann-inferential-v3, §"What the null is", point 2). The result page is careful about what this is — "the construction's singular agreement on a plural head … is the one place the distributional-shadow story predicts the opposite of the inferential story (singular agreement on a plural noun is distributionally dispreferred in general text)" — and the pre-registered headline-gating rule refused to count it as inference: gpt-5.4-mini is recorded NULL on the inferential question, the agreement reflex "carried as a noted exception" (same section).

The essay reads this exactly as the design intended, and uses it to sharpen the thesis. The agreement reflex is a form reflex, not the inferential discriminator — but notice where it could move at all: it moved because, on the agreement dimension, the default points the other way. Singular agreement on a plural head is distributionally dispreferred, so the control baseline is off-ceiling (0.22), and the contrast has headroom. This is limit (2) read in the mirror: the one signal that moved is the one place the default does not coincide with the construction's effect. Far from undercutting the thesis, the positive confirms its shape — a within-model contrast registers precisely when, and only when, the construction's contribution diverges from the distributional default. The design correctly declined to launder a form reflex into an inference claim; the essay declines to launder it into a counterexample.

Revision triggers (read before citing)

This essay is a claim about measurability. The following concrete future evidence would revise or retract it:

  • (a) An Option-A v4 distributive-default control that DOES register a shift. The governing decision adopted Option A — "engineer a distributive-default control" whose baseline unification rate is demonstrably off-ceiling (target ≤ 0.30), with a mandatory within-design lexical-cue control arm so a measured shift cannot be a lexical-cue artifact (decisions/resolved/aann-inferential-default-coincidence, §Resolution and §"Six binding conditions"). If a v4 meets the headroom precondition and registers a clean AANN-vs-control unification shift that survives the lexical-cue arm, then for AANN the inference and default did not coincide once the baseline was moved off-ceiling — and this essay's worked instance is overturned (the general methodological point would survive as a statement about the v3 design specifically, but the headline claim "the AANN inference is not separable from the default" falls). The essay moves to status: revised with an in-page log entry.
  • (b) A human AANN-inference dataset. Limit (1) ("can't-say-like-humans") rests on the stated absence of human inference data for this construction (result/aann-inferential-v3, §header box). If such a resource is found or built and a probe anchors to it, limit (1) is lifted and the compounding argument loses one of its two legs; the essay's "two limits compound" framing would need rewriting to a single-limit (separability-only) claim.
  • (c) A construction where inference and default diverge, producing a clean separable inferential result. The decision's binding fallback is Option B — redirect to a cancel-direction construction (the conative), "where the construction must suppress a lexically-default entailment" and "the default points the other way" (decisions/resolved/aann-inferential-default-coincidence, §Options B). The conative minimal-pair probe already separated the inference from the default precisely because the default runs the other way (result/conative-minimal-pair-divergence-v1; result/conative-cancel-direction-v2). A clean, separable Option-B inferential result does not overturn this essay — it bounds its scope: it shows the unreachability is specific to inference/default coincidence, not a general ceiling on Tier 4. The essay should then state explicitly that its claim is confined to the coincidence case and does not generalize to divergence constructions.
  • (d) Representational evidence. This essay is behavioral throughout. If probing-classifier or causal-intervention evidence (the open question's move 5, open-question/distributional-vs-inferential-constructional, §"What a serious answer would look like") were to separate an inference-predictive internal signal from a default-predictive one for a coincidence construction, the behavioral-unreachability claim would be relativized to behavioral instruments — the within-model behavioral contrast stays blocked, but a within-model representational contrast might not be. The essay's scope tightens to "by within-model behavioral contrast."

What this essay is not

Not a verdict on whether the models have the unification inference — the result it rests on is emphatic that the null "does not show the models lack the unification inference" (result/aann-inferential-v3, §Bounds), and this essay claims nothing stronger. Not a human-comparison claim of any kind — the v3 anchor is internal-contrast-only and no human inference data exists; the essay inherits that ceiling and does not breach it. Not a claim that Tier-4 inference-licensing is generally unreachable — the ladder's add-direction positives (way, caused-motion) show it is reachable exactly where the construction's inference diverges from the default; the unreachability claimed here is specific to coincidence. And not a finished result: it is an argument built on a single ceiling-bounded null and the decision that responds to it, with the triggers above naming in advance what would move it.

Honesty box

  • The essay's original contribution is the argumentative shape — naming the two-limit compounding, drawing the ladder's selection-effect consequence, and joining it to the lexical saturation pattern as one cline-spanning phenomenon — not any new empirical claim. Every empirical assertion cites the in-repo page that carries it.
  • The strongest sentence the evidence supports is conditional and method-scoped: where a construction's licensed inference coincides with the phrase's distributional default, a within-model shift-from-control design cannot separate the construction's inferential contribution from the default, so the inferential question is unreachable by that instrument — independently of whether the model draws the inference. The v3 null supports the antecedent for AANN unification, at one instrument, for three 2026 commercial models. Nothing here outruns that.
  • Weaker-than-evidence flag: the claim that this is "the same deflationary pattern" as the lexical multimodal saturation is an analogy the essay draws, resting on the structural parallel (text/distribution already supplies the probed signal), not on a measurement that tested both ends with one instrument. It is offered as a unifying observation, explicitly weaker than the within-cline unification the continuum page already established for the "beat the distributional shadow" positives.
