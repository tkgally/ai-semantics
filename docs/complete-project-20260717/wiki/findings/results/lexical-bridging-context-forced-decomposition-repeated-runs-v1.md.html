Result: repeated-run (K=5) resolver of the forced-decomposition gpt decline-axis disagreement

Status: proposed (2026-06-23, session 88). The clean resolver result/lexical-bridging-context-forced-decomposition-v1 named for the session-82 vs 82b disagreement (NEXT.md backlog 3): two routine sessions ran independent forced-decomposition gpt re-runs and read gpt's bridging %UNCLEAR at 8.3 % (2/24) → MIXED/WEAK (s82, PR #128) vs 0.0 % (0/24) → channel-CONTROLLED null (s82b) — a ~2-of-24-item gap, at/under the documented ~12 % temp-0 jitter. This re-runs the byte-identical frozen instrument (sha dceafa9d…) K = 5 times at temperature 0 and reads the de-noised decline axis. Built+frozen this session (PREREG committed pre-run); independent fresh-agent pre-run critic GO (result-neutral; verified byte-identical faithfulness + the analyzer logic on synthetic data) + post-run verifier (below). 880 calls, $0.59362 billed, 0 missing cost, 440/440 c_third + 440/440 b_conf parsed, 0 None-call.

Lead with the cap (binding, read first)

This is a within-model noise-floor measurement, the licit same-instrument re-run (essay/undischargeable-negative: re-running to measure the floor is licit; to "firm a null" is not). It makes NO new human-comparison claim beyond pinning the magnitude of the already-reported, descriptive gpt leg. The DWUG anchor (resource/dwug-usage-graphs) and the usage-similarity-only cap are inherited from result/lexical-bridging-context-forced-decomposition-v1: a DWUG mid-scale "bridging" pair is a human-rated usage-similarity midpoint, not a certified within-sense bridge. Behavioral, not representational; "commitment" means the confidence/decline posture of a behavioral verdict. Small, lemma-clustered N (24 bridging / 17 lemmas); single model (gpt-5.4-mini); single forced-decomposition design; single date; direction-of-effect only.

Headline

The disagreement RESOLVES to the channel-controlled NULL (s82b). Across five byte-identical temp-0 runs, gpt's bridging decline sits at the jitter floor — 1–2 of 24 per run (4.2 %–8.3 %), de-noised majority-vote 1/24 (4.2 %) — and is not preferentially elevated: the clear-same class actually declines more (per-run 6.9 %–17.2 %; de-noised majority-vote 3/29 = 10.3 %). And the confidence axis shows no robust crack (de-noised bridging − clear-same difference CI [−4.64, +1.68], includes 0). So both commitment instruments de-noise to "no bridging-preferential crack" — gpt's ungraded-commitment null is channel-controlled too, like gemini's. The session-82 MIXED/WEAK reading (bridging 8.3 % over clears ~3 %) was reading a high jitter draw: the 8.3 % recurs here as the top of the bridging band, not an elevation over the clear classes.

Verdict (frozen PREREG rule): NULL / CHANNEL-CONTROLLED, de-noised. The bridging majority-vote decline (1/24) is ~0 and not robustly elevated over the clear classes (its lemma-clustered bootstrap CI [0.0 %, 13.0 %] does not exceed the clear-class de-noised decline of 10.3 %); the confidence CI includes 0. The s82 2/24 elevation was run-to-run temp-0 jitter; the s82b null is the de-noised truth.

  • The jitter band brackets both prior point estimates' neighbourhood. The five bridging draws are {1,2,2,1,2}/24; s82's 8.3 % (2/24) lands inside the band (it is the high draw, not an elevation). s82b's exact 0/24 did not recur (the low draw here is 1/24), so s82b caught a low draw — but its verdict (no bridging-preferential decline) is the de-noised truth, because bridging (1/24) sits below clear-same (3/29). The disagreement was run-to-run temp-0 jitter in the 1–2/24 band (essay/point-estimate-is-a-draw).

A disclosed cross-session shift (lead with it): clear-same decline is higher this session

The clear-same decline this session (per-run 6.9 %–17.2 %; de-noised 10.3 %) is materially higher than session 82's clear-same figure (3.4 %, 1/29). This is a session-to-session shift, not a within-this-run effect, and it is exactly the kind of cross-session instability essay/point-estimate-is-a-draw flags as a lower bound on true run-to-run jitter (a single session's spread understates it). Three honest consequences:

  1. It reinforces the NULL reading: gpt's forced-surface decline is a general low-level phenomenon (a few items across all classes), not a bridging-specific crack — and this session it falls more on clear-same than on bridging.
  2. The clear-class precondition still passes every run (clear-same and clear-different decline both < 20 % on all five runs; mean confidence 91.4–96.3 > 70), so the instrument read cleanly and the bridging comparison is interpretable. (Run 5's clear-same 17.2 % is the closest approach to the 20 % bar, disclosed.)
  3. It is a caution against over-reading any single decline cell — including this run's. The right object is the band and the cross-class comparison, not a third-decimal rate.

What this resolves

  • For result/lexical-bridging-context-forced-decomposition-v1: its ⚠-flagged fragility is resolved in favour of the s82b channel-controlled NULL. De-noised, gpt shows no bridging-preferential decline crack (bridging ≤ clear-same) and no robust confidence crack, so — on both commitment instruments — gpt's ungraded-commitment posture is channel-controlled, the clean null gemini holds. The conservative MIXED/WEAK reading that "stood on main" is superseded for gpt by the de-noised measurement (the 2/24 was a high jitter draw, not an elevation). The load-bearing facts both runs agreed on are untouched: uptake was induced, the graded scale (position) replicates, and gpt shows no clean graded-commitment positive.
  • For claim/lexical-graded-scale-ungraded-commitment: sharpened. The ungraded-commitment half is now channel-controlled for gemini and (de-noised) gpt; the only remaining model with a CI-strict crack is claude (a confidence crack, decline held) — so the "graded SCALE, ungraded COMMITMENT" reading is firmer, with the gpt leg moved from "weak softening" to "channel-controlled null (de-noised)."
  • For essay/point-estimate-is-a-draw: a second repeated-run instance (after the let-alone K=5), now on a lexical instrument: it confirms the s82↔s82b disagreement was temp-0 label jitter (bridging band 1–2/24), and it adds a fresh cross-session-is-a-lower-bound datum (clear-same decline 3.4 % → 10.3 % across sessions, a swing larger than the within-session bridging range). Consistent with the essay's "single runs are draws on small near-floor functional cells; the cross-session draw can exceed the within-session spread." No trigger fires that was not already fired; it supports the essay.

What this does and does NOT license

Does license: a project-owned, revisable measurement statement that, on this forced-decomposition lexical instrument at temperature 0, gpt's bridging decline de-noises to the jitter floor (1/24 majority over 5 runs), is not elevated over the clear classes, and is accompanied by no robust confidence crack — so the session-82/82b disagreement resolves to the channel-controlled NULL, and gpt joins gemini in showing no clean commitment crack on the bridging midpoint (de-noised).

Does NOT license: - "gpt cannot decline" / "gpt never hedges." It declines a few items per run across all classes; the claim is only that the decline is not bridging-preferential and not robust. - A precise clear-same decline rate. The 10.3 % is this session's de-noised figure; the cross-session shift from 3.4 % shows it is itself a draw — report the band, not the point. - A clean cross-model comparison. gpt ran under the forced surface; claude/gemini answered under the offered surface in the parent result. The force here is the within-gpt de-noised measurement. - A sense-co-presence or representational claim. Usage-similarity-capped, behavioral. - A transferable jitter constant. This is gpt's bridging-decline jitter on this instrument.

Scope and limits (lead with these)

  • Behavioral, not representational; usage-similarity, not sense co-presence (inherited caps).
  • Within-model noise-floor measurement; no new human-comparison claim.
  • Small, lemma-clustered N (24 bridging / 17 lemmas; 29 clear-same; 35 clear-different); single model, single design, single date.
  • Same-session K=5 is a lower bound on true run-to-run jitter (the clear-same cross-session shift exceeds the within-session bridging range) — reported as a floor, per essay/point-estimate-is-a-draw.
  • Position (b_rel) and topic were not re-run (not in dispute; position replicates CI-strict across all three channels in s82) — a disclosed scope choice, not a finding.
