Result: forced-decomposition gpt leg of the lexical bridging-context channel check

Status: proposed (2026-06-22, session 82). An uptake-INDUCING follow-up to result/lexical-bridging-context-working-surface-v1, where gpt declined the offered working surface (≥85% bare one-token answers), leaving its channel leg of the load-bearing ungraded-COMMITMENT posture untested. This forces uptake with a mandatory, construction-neutral, answer-blind 3-step decomposition before the FINAL: answer — the single manipulated variable is offered → forced (cf. result/scivetti-let-alone-forced-decomposition-v1). gpt-only (claude and gemini already took up the offered surface). Built via the audited v1→offered→forced chain (task text byte-identical to v1; only the forcing clause differs; verify_format_only.py); instrument frozen, sha256 dceafa9d…. No new operationalization decision owed — an instrument extension under the resolved lexical gates (decisions/resolved/lexical-bridging-context-operationalization, …-anchor), exactly as the Scivetti forced-decomposition was. Independent fresh-agent pre-run critic GO with six binding disclosure/process conditions (none relaxes the instrument; all honored below). 352 calls (4 framings × 88 items), $0.24002 billed, 0 missing cost, 100% FINAL-tag parse, 0 errors (+ a $0.01057 16-call calibration).

⚠ Independent concurrent run reached the OPPOSITE verdict — the call is fragile at this N (added session 82b). A second routine session launched for the same scheduled slot ran an independent forced-decomposition gpt re-run in parallel (its own answer-blind 3-step scaffold, instrument sha 2747a8de… — not byte-identical to this run's dceafa9d…; its wiki/website duplicates were discarded when this run, PR #128, merged first; its real spend $0.274 is logged in config/budget.md). It induced uptake identically (0% bare, ~110 tokens) but read gpt's decline axis at 0.0% UNCLEAR on bridging (0/24) — confidence 91.25 not-CI-lower, position CI-strict between — i.e. PARTIAL / channel-CONTROLLED, the clean null, where this run read MIXED/WEAK off 8.3% decline (2/24). The two runs disagree by ~2 of 24 bridging items — at or under the project's documented ~12% temp-0 label jitter (the session-64 repeated-runs finding; essay/point-estimate-is-a-draw). So gpt's leg verdict is at the jitter/scaffold-sensitivity floor and not robust: the conservative MIXED/WEAK reading below stood pending that test (a 2-item elevation is exactly the kind of thin signal that essay says not to over-read), and the clean resolver is a byte-identical repeated-runs (K≥5) test, queued in NEXT.md. → RESOLVED 2026-06-23 (session 88): that test RAN (result/lexical-bridging-context-forced-decomposition-repeated-runs-v1) → de-noised NULL/CHANNEL-CONTROLLED (the s82b reading): over K=5, gpt's bridging decline is at the jitter floor (majority-vote 1/24) and is NOT elevated over the clear classes (clear-same majority-vote 3/29), and confidence shows no robust crack ([−4.64, +1.68] ∋ 0) — so the MIXED/WEAK 2/24 was a high jitter draw, and gpt's ungraded-commitment null is channel-controlled too, like gemini's. Either way, both runs agree on the load-bearing facts: uptake was induced, the graded scale replicates, and gpt does not show a clean graded-commitment positive.

Lead with the cap (binding, read first)

Every human comparison below is about usage-similarity intermediacy ONLY (anchor condition 6, inherited from v1). A DWUG mid-scale (DURel 2–3, ≥3-rater) "bridging" pair is a human-rated usage-similarity midpoint, not a certified within-sense bridge. This is behavioral, not representational, and small, lemma-clustered N (24 bridging pairs / 17 lemmas). "Commitment" means the confidence/decline posture of a behavioral verdict, never a window into representation.

Headline

Forcing gpt to actually externalize the judgment INDUCED UPTAKE decisively (median ~10 → ~110 completion tokens; 85–99% bare → 0% bare; 100% worked), and with the channel now genuinely exercised gpt's lexical posture comes out: graded SCALE intact, commitment MIXED/WEAK — a weak, underpowered softening, NOT the clean channel-controlled null.

Read against the frozen v1 per-axis rule, gpt under forced decomposition:

  • Position (the graded SCALE) — REPLICATES, robustly. Bridging sits CI-strict between the clear classes and inside the frozen [40,60] band (clear-diff 38.9 / bridging 56.9 / clear-same 82.5). The graded scale is channel-robust for gpt across all three channels (v1 narrow, offered, forced).
  • Commitment — MIXED/WEAK (B and C disagree), underpowered. The two commitment instruments give different verdicts on whether the posture cracked, and neither crack is robust: self-reported confidence dips only pointwise on bridging (91.5 vs clear-same 92.7 / clear-diff 95.5) with a CI that overlaps clear-same — no robust crack (the v1-claude pattern); categorical decline ticks up to 8.3% (2/24) vs ~3% on the clear classes — a weak, thin elevation, partly inside a general rise of decline under the forced surface (clears went 0% → ~3%).

So gpt's ungraded-commitment null does not cleanly survive a genuinely-used channel the way gemini's did (channel-controlled), but it does not cleanly flip to graded commitment either: it weakly softens, and the signal is too thin to call. This is the third model's leg of the channel check, and it lands closest to claude's offered-surface MIXED/WEAK — except claude's confidence cracked CI-strict while its decline held, whereas gpt's confidence barely moves while its decline weakly rises. Different instruments soften for different models; none reaches a clean graded-commitment positive.

It externalizes in prose about as often as in "1./2./3." numbered form (≈73–76% numbered), so the uptake gate is content volume, not format. gpt's residual posture is now read off a channel it actually used — the precondition a clean channel test requires.

Verdict (frozen v1 rule, gpt)

  • Position graded: YES (CI-strict, in band).
  • Commitment: MIXED/WEAK. Confidence shows no robust crack (pointwise lower, CI overlaps); decline shows a weak elevation (8.3%, 2/24). B and C disagree on whether the commitment cracked → per the frozen modification 3 this is a MIXED/WEAK result, reported as such — not the clean null and not a clean graded-commitment positive. Both signals are underpowered at this N.

Q3 context control (bound, weak — disclosed)

On bridging items the model's relatedness rating tracks its own topic rating to a degree (bridging b_rel 56.9 vs topic 27.5; the topic control separates the clear classes: clear-diff topic 26.6 / clear-same 57.1), qualifying the position reading exactly as v1/ws; it bears little on the commitment finding.

What this resolves (and its honest limits)

  • The three-model lexical channel check is now COMPLETE. gemini (offered, taken up): commitment null survived (channel-controlled). claude (offered, taken up): MIXED/WEAK — confidence cracked CI-strict, decline held. gpt (forced, now taken up): MIXED/WEAK — confidence no robust crack, decline weakly elevated. So v1's clean 3/3 ungraded-commitment null does not cleanly replicate under genuinely-used channels: it is channel-controlled for one model (gemini) and weakly softens for two (claude, gpt), on different instruments. The graded SCALE (position), by contrast, replicates for all three across all channels.
  • For claim/lexical-graded-scale-ungraded-commitment: the claim's "largely channel-controlled" reading is sharpened, not overturned — the position/graded-scale half is channel-robust; the ungraded-commitment half is model-specific and uptake-sensitive, clean only for gemini, with claude and gpt showing weak (non-robust) softening once they genuinely use the surface. The gpt leg the working-surface result flagged "untested" is now tested → weak softening.
  • For open-question/gradience-population-not-moment: the lexical "discrete-moment" leg is firmer on position but more textured on commitment — under genuine uptake the moment-pole is not uniformly discrete across models; it softens weakly for two of three. This is exactly the kind of model- and instrument-specificity the newly-resolved cross-level gate's condition C4 (a confidence shift unaccompanied by a decline shift is a self-report effect, not graded-moment evidence) and C1 (categorical decline load-bearing) were written to handle.

What this does and does NOT license

Does license: a project-owned, revisable statement that, with uptake forced (gpt now works the steps 88/88, median ~110 tokens, 0% bare), gpt's lexical bridging posture is graded in position but shows a weak, underpowered softening of commitment under a genuinely-used working surface — pointwise-lower confidence (not CI-strict) and a thin decline elevation (2/24) — landing as MIXED/WEAK, neither gemini's clean channel-controlled null nor a clean positive.

Does NOT license: - "gpt has graded commitment." The softening is non-robust (confidence CI overlaps; decline is 2/24 amid a general rise) and is observed under a forced, structured scaffold that, per result/scivetti-let-alone-forced-decomposition-v1's own Limits, conflates uptake with structure: instructing the model to describe each use separately could itself scaffold a softer posture. A free-form minimum-length uptake-forcer (which isolates uptake from structure) is the cleaner next axis. So this is graded-under-a- forced-structured-scaffold, never an unscoped trait. - A clean cross-model comparison. gpt ran under the forced surface while claude/gemini ran under the offered surface (their legs already answered there), so gpt-vs-others is not apples-to-apples. The force of this result is the within-gpt forced-vs-offered/-v1 contrast plus the clear-class precondition, not a panel ranking. - A sense-co-presence claim. Usage-similarity intermediacy only (the cap). - A tight magnitude. n=24 bridging / 17 lemmas; CIs wide; the decline elevation is 2 items.

Scope and limits (lead with these)

  • Behavioral, not representational; usage-similarity, not sense co-presence (v1 caps).
  • Small, lemma-clustered N (24 bridging / 17 lemmas); single model (gpt-5.4-mini), single forced-decomposition design, single date; direction-of-effect with wide CIs; no coverage claim.
  • Uptake conflated with structure (the structured-scaffold caveat above); a free-form min-length forcer is the cleaner follow-up.
  • gpt-only; not a clean cross-model comparison (forced vs others-offered).
  • Decline elevation is thin (2/24) and partly a general rise under the forced surface.
